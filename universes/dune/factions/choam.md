# CHOAM

## Overview

CHOAM — the Combine Honnete Ober Advancer Mercantiles — is the universal trading company of the Imperium, and the mechanism through which the economic power of the Known Universe is held, distributed, and contested. Every Great House, the Emperor, the Guild, and significant organizational players hold CHOAM directorships — shares of the trading concern whose profits flow from all commerce across all planets. To control CHOAM is to control the economic lifeblood of civilization.

Control of Arrakis, therefore, is not simply political dominance of a desert planet. It is control of the most valuable single commodity in existence — spice melange — which the Guild requires to navigate, which the Bene Gesserit use in their Reverend Mother rites, which extends human life in high doses, which is produced exclusively on Arrakis. Whoever controls Arrakis controls the spice supply. Whoever controls the spice supply controls CHOAM. Whoever controls CHOAM controls the Imperium.

This is not metaphor. This is the actual mechanism of power.

## Key Facts

- **Structure:** A trading company with directorship shares distributed among the Great Houses, the Emperor's family (the Corrinos hold significant shares), and other major entities. Profits from all commercial activity in the Imperium flow through CHOAM. [book_dune]
- **Political function:** CHOAM directorships are political instruments. Giving or withholding shares is a mechanism of Imperial favor and punishment. A House stripped of CHOAM directorships loses its economic foundation. [book_dune]
- **The spice connection:** Spice melange is the most profitable commodity in existence. CHOAM's profits depend substantially on spice commerce. Control of Arrakis is therefore control of a critical share of CHOAM's revenue stream. [book_dune]
- **The Emperor's position:** The Corrinos hold the Imperial throne partly because of their CHOAM share position. A family or House that could accumulate enough CHOAM influence could challenge Imperial economic authority without firing a shot. This is part of why House Atreides's growing Landsraad support concerned the Emperor — it suggested a potential path to economic power that could undermine Corrino dominance. [book_dune]

## After Paul's Victory

Paul's seizure of Arrakis does not merely take a planet — it seizes the controlling position in CHOAM's most valuable revenue stream. His ability to threaten the spice supply gives him leverage over every CHOAM directorship in existence. The economic underpinning of the Imperium is, in one stroke, held by a young man who spent two years in the desert learning to ride sandworms.

The Great Houses understand this. The Emperor understands this. The Guild understands this. This is why the Emperor concedes. The military victory is real but the economic leverage is decisive. [book_dune]
