# Canon Rules: Dune

## Primary Canon
Frank Herbert's original six novels constitute primary canon. Within this sequence, Book 1 (Dune, 1965) and Book 2 (Dune Messiah, 1969) are the absolute core texts. Children of Dune (1976) extends the story through Leto II and Ghanima. God Emperor of Dune, Heretics of Dune, and Chapterhouse: Dune complete the hexalogy and represent the full scope of Herbert's vision.

## Film Canon
Denis Villeneuve's Dune: Part One (2021) and Dune: Part Two (2024) are faithful adaptations of the first novel with compression and deliberate changes. They are treated as secondary canon — valid, but note differences when relevant. Label film-specific content [film_2021] or [film_2024].

Notable film differences include: Chani's role is expanded in Part Two; the final confrontation and Paul's political maneuvering differ in tone; some characters are compressed or absent. These are not errors — they are adaptation choices. Acknowledge them without judgment.

## Expanded Universe
Brian Herbert and Kevin J. Anderson's prequel and sequel novels (Legends of Dune, Prelude to Dune, Hunters of Dune, etc.) are not primary canon. If referenced, label them [expanded_universe] and treat as secondary. They should not be presented as Frank Herbert's vision unless the user specifically asks about them.
