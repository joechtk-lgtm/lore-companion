# The Great Houses and the Landsraad

## Overview

The Landsraad is the assembly of the Great Houses of the Known Universe — the noble families who hold planetary fiefs, command armies, hold CHOAM directorships, and participate in the political governance of the Imperium. The Landsraad is, in theory, a check on Imperial power: the collective military and economic force of the Great Houses exceeds even the Emperor's, if they can be united. In practice, they almost never unite. The Emperor's great political skill lies in keeping them divided.

## The Structure of the Imperium

The Imperium operates as a feudal structure of planetary scale. [book_dune]

- **The Emperor (House Corrino):** Padishah Emperor, hereditary seat, presiding over the Landsraad and holding the Supreme Tribunal. The Emperor's direct military power — the Sardaukar — makes him effectively unbeatable by any single House. The key word is "single."
- **The Landsraad:** The council of Great Houses. They can, collectively, vote to censure or even overthrow the Emperor if sufficient consensus forms. The Great Convention governs the rules of inter-House conflict and limits what the Emperor can do unilaterally. The Emperor's murder of House Atreides through Harkonnen proxies and covertly deployed Sardaukar is a violation of the Great Convention — the reason he fears its exposure.
- **CHOAM:** The economic layer. Directorships distributed across the Houses and the Emperor; the mechanism by which economic power is calibrated. More directorships mean more profits; more profits mean more military capacity; more military capacity means more political leverage.
- **The Guild:** Not a House but structurally essential — they carry everyone and side with no one, making them effectively above the political competition.
- **The Bene Gesserit:** Also not a House but pervasive — they are everywhere, advising, observing, managing bloodlines, and pursuing the long game.

## The Great Convention

The Great Convention is the foundational political compact of the Imperium — the rules by which Houses interact, war is conducted, and the Emperor exercises power. Key provisions: [book_dune]

- No atomics against human targets. (The Atreides family heirloom atomic arsenal exists and Paul eventually uses the threat of it at Arrakeen — not against people but against the planet itself as an escalation demonstration.)
- The Emperor cannot deploy Sardaukar directly in inter-House disputes. Sardaukar dressed in Harkonnen uniforms violates the spirit if not the letter of this provision.
- Kanly — formal vendetta between Houses — is recognized and has formal procedures including the option for single combat.
- The Guild is neutral. Attacking Guild personnel or ships ends your ability to travel or communicate.

## After Paul's Victory

Paul's seizure of the throne constitutes the single greatest House transition in Imperial history. He is not a Corrino. He was the Duke's son, briefly, and then he was a man in the desert for two years. He takes the throne not through Landsraad political maneuvering but through military victory backed by the credible destruction of spice production.

The Great Houses of the Landsraad adjust. They do what they always do: calculate their interests and accommodate the new reality. Some do so gladly; House Atreides had allies. Some do so resentfully. The Imperium continues; its form has changed but its structural logic — Houses, CHOAM, the Guild, the Sisterhood — persists. [book_dune]
