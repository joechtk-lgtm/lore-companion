# The Cat School (School of the Cat)

## Overview

The Cat School is one of the Continent's witcher schools, known for a fighting style built around speed and agility, a more morally flexible interpretation of the witcher's code, and a historical antagonism with the Wolf School that has occasionally turned lethal. Cat witchers are fast, light-armored, and often willing to take contracts and use methods that Wolf School witchers decline on principle or professional grounds. They are not universally corrupt or villainous — they are a product of a different training tradition with different philosophical foundations. The Wolf School finds them dangerous and untrustworthy. The Cat School finds the Wolf School self-righteous. Both assessments have some truth to them.

## Key Facts

- **Location:** The Cat School's keep is in the mountains — specific location less precisely established than Kaer Morhen, associated with southern or central mountain ranges [game_w3]
- **Symbol:** Cat medallion (vibrates in the presence of magic, like Wolf medallions) [game_w3]
- **Combat philosophy:** Speed over strength. Light armor, rapid attacks, priority on avoiding hits rather than blocking them. Cat witchers dodge and reposition rather than tanking damage with heavy equipment. [game_w3]
- **Signs:** Cat witchers use Signs but with less emphasis than Wolf School. The speed-focused style means they often can't spare concentration for sustained Sign use. Quick Sign deployment (fast Igni, instinctive Aard) is the Cat School approach. [game_w3]
- **Moral code:** Less rigid than the Wolf School's claimed neutrality. Cat witchers are more willing to take contracts involving humans (assassinations, political work), work as spies or infiltrators, and interpret "professional monster hunter" broadly enough to include people who are being monstrous. [game_w3] [books]
- **Status:** Functioning but diminished. The Cat School's keep has not suffered the same massacre as Kaer Morhen, but the general decline of the witcher population affects all schools. [game_w3]
- **Gear:** Cat School gear is findable in Witcher 3 — light armor emphasizing agility and Sign intensity. One of the game's strong gear sets. [game_w3]

## The Wolf-Cat Antagonism

The historical bad blood between Wolf and Cat schools runs deep:

**The philosophical conflict:** Wolf School witchers claim neutrality and a professional code that distinguishes them from mercenaries. Cat School witchers regard this as self-aggrandizement — witchers are mercenaries, and pretending otherwise is dishonest. The Wolf School's code is real but it is also the basis of their pride, which the Cat School finds irritating.

**The contract conflict:** Cat witchers taking contracts on humans — particularly contracts that overlap with territory where Wolf witchers operate — has caused violence. A Cat witcher who assassinates someone a Wolf witcher was also hired to protect creates an obvious problem. These conflicts have happened. They have not always ended without deaths on both sides.

**The gear conflict:** There are historical incidents involving Cat witchers acquiring Wolf School techniques or equipment through methods that were not entirely above board. The exact incidents are matters of school tradition rather than documented history, but they contribute to the baseline distrust.

**What both schools agree on:** Monsters need killing. The Continent needs witchers, whatever the disagreements about method. In practice, when forced to cooperate (common threat, common employer), the schools can work together. They simply don't prefer to. [game_w3] [books]

## Gaetan — The Honorton Incident

The most prominent Cat witcher in Witcher 3 is Gaetan, encountered in the quest "Where the Cat and Wolf Play" (near the village of Honorton, in Velen).

**What happened at Honorton:** Gaetan had a contract with the village — monster hunting, standard arrangement. The village decided not to pay. More specifically, the village decided to avoid payment by reporting Gaetan to the local authority (whoever that was in Velen's chaotic occupation), intending to have him arrested and eliminated. It is not clear whether they intended his death specifically or merely to avoid the debt.

Gaetan killed the entire village in response.

**The moral complexity:** What Gaetan did was a massacre. He killed civilians, including children, in retaliation for a payment dispute. This is monstrous by any reasonable standard. He knows it. He doesn't fully apologize for it but he knows what it was.

His grievance was real — he did the work, he was owed payment, the village tried to have him killed to avoid the debt. The Cat School's less rigid code means this was, by his own philosophy, an acceptable response to treachery. By Wolf School standards — by any normal human standard — it was mass murder.

**Geralt's choice:** Geralt arrives after the massacre, with a young girl who survived (she was in hiding). He investigates, finds Gaetan, and can:
- Kill Gaetan (most straightforward response to a witcher who just killed an entire village)
- Let Gaetan go (he committed the massacre partly as a response to genuine betrayal; he's leaving Velen anyway)

There is no clean option. The girl who survived wants justice. Gaetan's grievance was real. He also murdered children. The game gives Geralt the choice without declaring the right answer. [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

Cat witchers are occasionally present in Velen and the surrounding region — contract work takes them where the contracts are. The Honorton incident (Gaetan) is the only direct Cat School encounter in the Novigrad/Velen arc.

Cat School gear diagrams are findable in the world — they are part of the scavenger hunt gear quest system that runs throughout the game. [game_w3]

## DLC Content

Cat School gear diagrams (enhanced, master, grandmaster levels) are part of the gear upgrade system available throughout the DLCs. No specific Cat School characters appear in Hearts of Stone or Blood and Wine.
