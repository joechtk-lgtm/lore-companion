# The Viper School (School of the Viper)

## Overview

The Viper School is a witcher school from the southern kingdoms — closer to Nilfgaard's territory than any other school — that specializes in assassination, poison use, and infiltration. It occupies the darkest edge of what a witcher school can be: monsters are a loose category for Viper witchers, and the distinction between a professional monster hunter and a professional assassin is one they have made peace with. The school was effectively destroyed by Nilfgaard — used as wet work tools for the Empire's intelligence operations and then eliminated when they became a liability. The most prominent surviving Viper witcher is Letho of Gulet, the central antagonist of Witcher 2 and an optional ally in Witcher 3.

## Key Facts

- **Location:** Southern kingdoms, closer to Nilfgaard than other schools. The keep's exact location is southern mountains. [game_w1w2] [game_w3]
- **Symbol:** Viper medallion [game_w1w2] [game_w3]
- **Combat philosophy:** Efficiency over style. Poison use is standard practice. Assassination techniques — approaching from concealment, eliminating targets quickly, managing multiple opponents through terrain and positioning rather than direct confrontation. [game_w1w2] [game_w3]
- **Moral code:** The most permissive of the major schools. Viper witchers take contracts on humans regularly. The definition of "monster" in their code is functional — if a human has become monstrous in their behavior, they qualify. [game_w1w2]
- **Poison specialty:** Viper witchers are both more resistant to poisons than other witchers (already highly resistant due to mutations) and significantly more skilled in poison preparation and application. Their blades carry poisons as a matter of course. [game_w3]
- **School destruction:** Nilfgaard's Emperor (Emhyr var Emreis) used the Viper School for operations he didn't want traced to the Empire, then had the school's keep destroyed and most members eliminated once the operations were complete. The school no longer has an active training structure. [game_w1w2] [game_w3]
- **Gear:** Viper School gear is findable in Witcher 3 (Hearts of Stone adds enhanced versions). It emphasizes poison resistance, poison damage, and the combat style of someone who wants their enemies dead before the fight becomes extended. [game_w3]

## Letho of Gulet

The principal Viper witcher in the games.

**Witcher 2 role:**
Letho is the main antagonist of Witcher 2 — he assassinated King Demavend of Aedirn and King Foltest of Temeria, actions that destabilized the Northern Kingdoms significantly and assisted Nilfgaard's strategic position. His motivation was not purely mercenary: he made a deal with Nilfgaard (Emhyr var Emreis) to obtain the witcher formulas — the knowledge needed to create new witchers — in exchange for the assassinations. His purpose was to re-establish a safe haven for the remaining Viper witchers, a place where witchers could exist without being hunted, using the formulas to restore the school's ability to create witchers.

His companions in Witcher 2: Serrit and Auckes, two other Viper witchers who worked with him. Their fates depend on Witcher 2 player choices.

**The moral accounting:**
Letho is not simply a villain. The assassinations were genuinely harmful — Foltest's death particularly plunged Temeria into chaos. Letho's motivation was the survival of his people — a goal Geralt could understand, having made similarly extreme choices for people he cared about. The execution of the goal is indefensible. The motivation is comprehensible. Witcher 2's ending allows for Geralt to understand this and either execute Letho or let him go. [game_w1w2]

**Witcher 3 appearance (optional):**
If the player carried over a Witcher 2 save where Letho survived, or made specific choices in the Witcher 3 opening sequence, Letho appears in Velen. He is surviving — not comfortably, but surviving. He can be recruited as an ally for the Battle of Kaer Morhen. His presence in the battle is tactically significant; a Viper witcher of his capability is a substantial asset.

Whether Geralt trusts him, and whether their old conflict (Letho killed people Geralt cared about through the political chaos he caused) can be set aside for the immediate threat of the Wild Hunt, is the substance of their Witcher 3 interactions. Letho is honest about what he did and does not particularly apologize for it. [game_w3]

## Serrit and Auckes

The other named Viper witchers from Witcher 2. They worked as Letho's companions during the assassination operations. Their fates depend on Witcher 2 player choices — they can be killed, allowed to escape, or various other outcomes depending on how Geralt navigated Witcher 2's branching plot.

If they survived Witcher 2, they are not specifically encountered in Witcher 3 (unlike Letho). [game_w1w2]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

The Viper School's presence in Witcher 3 is primarily Letho's optional storyline in Velen. The school itself no longer has an active keep to refer to.

Viper School gear diagrams are part of the base game's scavenger hunt system. The gear is findable in southern areas, consistent with the school's geographic origin. [game_w3]

[spoiler_tier: kaer_morhen]
## Kaer Morhen Events

If Letho was encountered in Velen and recruited, he fights in the Battle of Kaer Morhen. His combat capability is extreme — a Viper witcher of his experience and size is one of the most effective individual fighters available. Having him present significantly helps the defense.

The dynamic of Letho at Kaer Morhen — fighting alongside Wolf School witchers he has complicated history with — is handled with appropriate awkwardness. He is professional. The other witchers are professional. Nobody pretends this is comfortable.

After the battle, he leaves. He is not a Kaer Morhen member; he was a temporary ally for a specific fight. [game_w3]

## DLC Content

**Hearts of Stone:**
The Viper School receives significant gear content in Hearts of Stone — enhanced, master, and grandmaster Viper gear diagrams are findable in the DLC's geographic area (the Oxenfurt-adjacent region). The gear is among the game's strongest available.

Letho does not appear in either DLC.
