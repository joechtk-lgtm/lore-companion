# The Wild Hunt (Wraiths of Mörhogg / Dearg Ruadhri)

## Overview

The Wild Hunt — known also as the Wraiths of Mörhogg in Skellige and as the Dearg Ruadhri (Red Riders) among themselves — is in truth the elite cavalry of the Aen Elle, the elves of the parallel world Tir ná Lia. What the people of the Continent have interpreted for centuries as a supernatural omen — a procession of spectral riders that appears before catastrophes and steals the living — is actually a disciplined military force from another world, using interdimensional travel and weather manipulation to move through the Continent as raiders and abductors. They have taken people across worlds for generations. The myth of the Wild Hunt is built on real terror with a misunderstood source.

## The Aen Elle and Their World

The Aen Elle are a parallel branch of elven civilization, distinct from the Aen Seidhe (the elves of the Continent). They inhabit Tir ná Lia, a world separate from — but traversable from — the Continent. The Aen Elle once had the ability to move freely between worlds; they lost reliable access and have since maintained it through Caranthir's navigational abilities and abducted humans with dimensional gifts.

Their world is dying. The White Frost — Tedd Deireadh — is consuming it. Eredin's campaign to capture Ciri is a survival effort: use her Elder Blood power to either stop the Frost or lead his people to a new world entirely. [books] [game_w3]

## The White Frost (Tedd Deireadh) Connection

The White Frost is not merely a metaphor — it is a real magical catastrophe consuming multiple worlds sequentially. The Aen Elle have watched their world's edges freeze and die. Eredin's plan, in crude terms: obtain Ciri, use her dimensional traversal ability, either find a solution to the Frost or abandon Tir ná Lia and invade the Continent. Either option requires controlling Ciri. [books] [game_w3]

## The Origin of the Myth

The Wild Hunt myth in the Northern Kingdoms is documented in Witcher 3 as: spectral riders appear before great disasters, harvest the living, and disappear. In Skellige, they are Wraiths of Mörhogg — Mörhogg being a mythological concept connecting to death and cold. The truth is that the Aen Elle have raided the Continent for centuries, taking people for labor. Natural disasters and the Hunt's appearance correlate not because the Hunt causes disaster but because the Hunt appears during the extreme cold and storms they generate for concealment. [books] [game_w3]

## Relationships to Other Factions

- **Avallac'h:** The Sage and former servant of King Auberon. His relationship with the Hunt is complicated — he understands Ciri's power better than Eredin does and has his own agenda regarding how it should be used. He manipulates the Hunt as much as he serves it. [game_w3] [books]
- **The Aen Seidhe (Continent's elves):** A separate population. The Aen Elle regard the Aen Seidhe as lesser relatives who adapted to living under human dominion. The Aen Seidhe have no formal relationship with the Hunt. [books] [game_w3]
- **Ciri:** The objective. Her Elder Blood power includes the ability to open stable portals between worlds — exactly what the Hunt needs and lacks. [game_w3] [books]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

The Wild Hunt's direct presence in Velen and Novigrad is limited — they operate mostly by tracking and intelligence rather than open assault in populated areas. Their previous contact with Ciri has included attacks on Kaer Trolde (Skellige) and the original emergence of the Hunt that drove Ciri to open her first uncontrolled gate. In Velen, the cold — unusual cold, frost in seasons that shouldn't have it — is a sign locals associate with the Hunt's passage.

The Crones of Crookback Bog are aware of the Wild Hunt; their relationship with ancient forces is complicated and they have their own reasons for protecting Ciri temporarily. The Hunt does not assault Crookback Bog directly.

In Novigrad, the Hunt would not risk operating in a densely populated human city. Their power base is environmental — they need cold, darkness, and space to manifest effectively. The urban setting limits them. [game_w3]

## Skellige and Beyond

The Hunt attacked Kaer Trolde to capture Ciri when she was sheltered with Crach an Craite's family. The assault was direct and devastating — Skellige's warriors and defenses were not prepared for an interdimensional cavalry raid. The Hunt moved through the cold and ice that the islands' winter provides naturally.

The Elder Blood explosion Ciri triggered on Skellige — the one that left a trace Yennefer investigated — was caused by a Wild Hunt pursuit. Ciri's power erupted as she was cornered, the explosion was massive, and it drove the Hunt back while she escaped with Avallac'h.

The investigation of Avallac'h's laboratory on Skellige reveals the Hunt's full purpose and the history of the Aen Elle's interest in Elder Blood going back to Lara Dorren. [game_w3]

## Kaer Morhen Events

The Battle of Kaer Morhen is the Wild Hunt's most direct assault in the game — a full organized attack on a fortified position to capture Ciri. Imlerith leads. The Hunt brings heavy forces: footsoldiers (Aen Elle warriors), cavalry, ice magic, and overwhelming pressure.

The defense holds longer than it should, thanks to the witchers' knowledge of the terrain and the magical support. But the Hunt is too strong to be fully repelled. Vesemir dies. Ciri's power erupts and the Hunt retreats.

After Kaer Morhen, the balance of force begins to shift. Imlerith is dead (Geralt kills him on Bald Mountain shortly after). Caranthir is killed during the Skellige naval battle. Ge'els is turned. Eredin faces the final confrontation significantly weakened. [game_w3]

## Endings and Resolution

**The Sunstone plan:**
The Sunstone — a Skellige artifact — can be used to summon the Wild Hunt to a chosen location. This is Geralt and Avallac'h's strategy: force the Hunt to fight on chosen ground rather than chase Ciri across worlds. The naval battle near Skellige is where the Hunt is lured and partially destroyed before the final gate to Tir ná Lia opens.

**The battle in Tir ná Lia:**
Geralt leads the strike force through the gate into Tir ná Lia, the Aen Elle world. The Wild Hunt fights on home territory but has been weakened by the loss of its generals. Geralt kills Eredin in single combat. The Hunt dissolves as a coherent force — without Eredin, the organization collapses.

**The White Frost:**
Ciri enters the Frost. If she survives (positive ending), the Frost is halted. The Aen Elle's world still faces whatever future they have without the Wild Hunt as an effective force and without Eredin's leadership. Ge'els was the most thoughtful of them; what he builds in Eredin's absence is left unaddressed. [game_w3]

## DLC Content

The Wild Hunt does not appear in Hearts of Stone or Blood and Wine. Their threat is fully resolved in the main game.

The DLC threats — Gaunter O'Dimm and the vampire Dettlaff — represent different supernatural categories. O'Dimm is arguably more purely dangerous than Eredin because his motives are opaque and his power seems absolute; Eredin had comprehensible goals and could be defeated by force. O'Dimm operates on different rules entirely. [game_w3]
