# The Kingdom of Redania

## Overview

Redania is the last significant military power opposing Nilfgaard's northern campaign and the dominant political force in the Northern Kingdoms by the start of Witcher 3. Under King Radovid V (known as Radovid the Stern), Redania has absorbed the remnants of neighboring kingdoms, won military engagements that other Northern rulers could not manage, and built a church-state alliance with the Church of the Eternal Fire that has given it a brutal but effective internal ideological coherence. The cost has been steep: Radovid is brilliant and increasingly paranoid, the anti-mage purges are becoming indistinguishable from mass murder, and the kingdom's foreign policy is driven partly by genuine strategic thinking and partly by Radovid's personal obsessions. Redania might win the war against Nilfgaard. What it becomes in doing so is a different question.

## Key Facts

- **Capital:** Tretogor [books] [game_w3]
- **Major city:** Novigrad (technically a Free City but within Redanian political orbit) [game_w3]
- **Current ruler:** Radovid V, called Radovid the Stern [books] [game_w3]
- **Military status:** Strongest remaining Northern army. Radovid has consolidated the Kaedwen and other remnant forces under Redanian command. [game_w3]
- **Intelligence service:** The Redanian Intelligence, formerly headed by Dijkstra, now in organizational flux following his effective exit from official service. [books] [game_w3]
- **State religion partnership:** The Church of the Eternal Fire is Redania's preferred religion and political partner. The witch hunters of Novigrad operate with Radovid's effective sanction. [game_w3]
- **Anti-mage policy:** Radovid has turned anti-mage sentiment into state policy. Mages who do not serve Nilfgaard have been hunted, captured, imprisoned, and executed in the Northern Kingdoms under Redanian occupation. This includes the Lodge of Sorceresses specifically. [game_w3] [books]
- **Anti-nonhuman policy:** Increasingly targeting elves, dwarves, and halflings as scapegoats. The racial persecution in Novigrad under Eternal Fire influence is Redanian-backed. [game_w3]

## Radovid V — The King

Radovid V is the most complicated ruler in Witcher 3:

- **Military ability:** Genuinely impressive. He has won battles that his predecessors could not have managed. He understands strategy, logistics, and the political component of warfare. [books] [game_w3]
- **Paranoia:** Real and increasing. He was politically shaped in a court that tried to use him as a puppet after his father's death — advisers who thought a young king could be managed. He learned, violently, that the way to not be managed was to remove managers. He has been removing potential threats ever since. Philippa Eilhart was one such threat. [books] [game_w3]
- **The mage hatred:** Not merely political — Radovid genuinely believes mages are dangerous and self-interested to a degree that makes them permanently untrustworthy. He is not entirely wrong about this. He has drawn conclusions from this belief that go significantly further than the evidence warrants. [books] [game_w3]
- **Mental stability:** Questionable by Witcher 3. His behavior in the late game suggests something beyond political calculation — he is, in the vocabulary of the setting, going mad in the specific way of people who have too much power and not enough opposition. [game_w3]
- **Physical presence:** Young for a king, not yet middle-aged. Blonde, typically dressed in Northern military fashion with enough royal ornamentation to mark his status. He radiates controlled intensity. [game_w3]

## Novigrad's Relationship to Redania

Novigrad is technically a Free City — it has its own governance structure, its own guild system, and its own merchant-class power structure independent of any kingdom's nobility. In practice, Radovid's Redania has enough influence over Novigrad through the Eternal Fire and through military proximity that the city operates under Redanian political dominance without being formally annexed.

The witch hunters who operate in Novigrad are not the city's own watch — they are Eternal Fire operatives working with Radovid's effective sanction. The city's actual guards (the Redanian soldiers stationed there) enforce Redanian priorities. The Free City governance is a legal fiction maintained because it's convenient for everyone involved. ## Velen and Novigrad Context

Velen — the area between Nilfgaard's occupied territories and Redania's frontier — is technically contested and practically lawless. Redanian soldiers are present but limited. Nilfgaard holds the south. The center is No Man's Land in the literal sense.

Novigrad under Eternal Fire influence is Redania's most visible domestic policy in action: witch hunters arrest, torture, and burn mages, elves, and anyone associated with magical practice. The Eternal Fire's temple serves as both religious center and political authority. The Dike district warehouses nonhuman prisoners. The stake in the city's square is not a figure of speech.

The underground mage evacuation network that Triss runs is a direct response to Redanian state policy. Every mage she gets onto a ship leaving Novigrad is someone who would otherwise be burned. [game_w3]

**Radovid's agents:** Redanian Intelligence operates in Novigrad through official and unofficial channels. Dijkstra's bathhouse network is the unofficial channel — still gathering intelligence even outside official employ, because information is power and Dijkstra knows better than anyone how to gather it. The official Redanian presence is the soldiers and the witch hunters working in coordination.

## Skellige and Beyond

Redania has attempted to influence the Skellige succession. The islands are difficult to control militarily but diplomatically significant — a hostile Skellige jarl could threaten Redanian shipping and coastal territory. Radovid prefers a neutral or friendly Skellige to an actively hostile one.

His agents' involvement in the Skellige succession is background context for why some candidates in that arc have external backing. [game_w3]

## Kaer Morhen Events

Redania is not involved in the Battle of Kaer Morhen. The Wild Hunt is not Radovid's problem — he has a war to fight and a kingdom to secure. The witchers and their troubles are peripheral to Redanian policy.

## Endings and Resolution

**Radovid's assassination:**
The quest "Reason of State" involves a conspiracy to kill Radovid. The conspirators include Vernon Roche, Thaler, and potentially Dijkstra, with Geralt as an active participant if he chooses to engage.

The assassination itself — Radovid is ambushed in a spot Geralt helped identify, killed by Roche or similar — is surprisingly undramatic for an act of regicide. The man who kept the Northern military together against Nilfgaard is killed on a street corner. The focus is immediately on what comes next.

**If Radovid dies:**
- Northern military cohesion fractures without his leadership. Nilfgaard advances more effectively.
- The anti-mage purges end. Surviving mages have breathing room.
- The power vacuum is contested between Dijkstra (Redanian intelligence) and the Temerian resistance.
- Outcome depends on whether Geralt helps Roche survive Dijkstra's subsequent betrayal.

**If Radovid lives:**
- He wins the war against Nilfgaard. The Northern Kingdoms survive under Redanian dominance.
- Anti-mage purges continue and intensify. Witch hunters have more authority, more territory, more support.
- Nonhuman persecution intensifies.
- The North "wins" in the military sense. What it becomes politically is a matter of degree of tyranny.

**Redania's future:**
Either Nilfgaard wins the war (Radovid dead, power vacuum) or Radovid wins the war (Radovid alive, continued purges). There is no ending where Redania is a healthy, free state. The game does not offer that option. This is intentional — the choice is between different flavors of bad. [game_w3]

## DLC Content

Redania does not feature directly in Hearts of Stone or Blood and Wine. The DLCs are set in Oxenfurt-adjacent territory (Hearts of Stone) and Toussaint (Blood and Wine), both of which are outside the immediate Novigrad-Redania political sphere in relevant ways. The aftermath of the main game's political choices is background context. [game_w3]
