# The Lodge of Sorceresses

## Overview

The Lodge of Sorceresses is a secret organization of female mages formed in the aftermath of the Thanedd Coup — the political catastrophe on Thanedd Island that fractured the Brotherhood of Sorcerers and left mages politically homeless and physically endangered across the Northern Kingdoms. The Lodge was founded primarily by Philippa Eilhart and Margarita Laux-Antille to preserve mage influence and ensure the survival of its members in a world increasingly hostile to magic users. It operates without official recognition, through webs of influence, blackmail, mutual protection, and the kind of coordination that only people who genuinely believe they understand what is at stake better than everyone else can sustain.

## Key Facts

- **Founded:** After the Thanedd Coup (between Witcher 2 and 3 timeline) [books] [game_w3]
- **Founders:** Philippa Eilhart and Margarita Laux-Antille primarily [books]
- **Nature:** Secret organization; not a formal institution. Members coordinate but maintain separate agendas. Not a sisterhood — a coalition. [books] [game_w3]
- **Base of operations:** No fixed location. Meetings held at safe houses. [books]
- **Primary agenda:** (1) Survival of its members. (2) Preservation of mage political influence in the Northern Kingdoms. (3) Managing Ciri — her Elder Blood represents power that the Lodge has strategic interest in not letting any single party (Nilfgaard, the Wild Hunt, anyone) control. [books] [game_w3]

## Members (during Witcher 3)

- **Philippa Eilhart** — Co-founder. Former Redanian spymaster's adviser (effectively the power behind Vizimir's intelligence apparatus). Turned owl by Radovid. In hiding in Novigrad. The Lodge's most dangerous member. [game_w3] [books]
- **Margarita Laux-Antille** — Headmistress of Aretuza academy. Imprisoned in Oxenfurt during Witcher 3. Must be freed to participate in the final alliance. [game_w3] [books]
- **Yennefer of Vengerberg** — Reluctant member. Her loyalty to Geralt and Ciri has repeatedly conflicted with Lodge politics. She cooperates because the Lodge's survival interests overlap with hers, not because she defers to its collective judgment. [books] [game_w3]
- **Triss Merigold** — Member. Her Temerian loyalties and personal relationships (Geralt, Ciri) created friction. Operating independently in Novigrad during Witcher 3. [books] [game_w3]
- **Keira Metz** — Member. Has largely broken away from Lodge involvement by Witcher 3 and is operating independently in Velen. [game_w3]
- **Fringilla Vigo** — Nilfgaardian mage. Lodge member but openly serving Nilfgaard's interests. Considered a double agent by other members. [books] [game_w3]
- **Ida Emean aep Sivney** — Elven sorceress. Lodge member with deep ties to elven political interests. [books]
- **Sile de Tansarville** — Former member; her role in Witcher 2 resulted in estrangement from the Lodge. [game_w1w2] [game_w3]

## The Thanedd Coup (Background)

The Conclave of Mages on Thanedd Island, held on the island of the same name where Aretuza is located. Nilfgaard had embedded agents among the Brotherhood. The conclave became a confrontation. Mages killed each other. Several prominent members died. Yennefer was captured and imprisoned. Geralt was present. The aftermath reshaped everything: the Brotherhood dissolved as a functioning institution, mages scattered, some fled to Nilfgaard, some went into hiding. The Lodge formed in the vacuum. [books]

## Relationships to Other Factions

- **The Brotherhood of Sorcerers:** Predecessor organization; effectively dead after Thanedd. The Lodge is its informal successor for the North's female mages. [books]
- **Aretuza:** The academy where many Lodge members trained. Margarita Laux-Antille runs it. It is the Lodge's closest thing to an institutional base. [books] [game_w3]
- **Radovid's Redania:** Hostile. Radovid turned on the Lodge specifically — he imprisoned Philippa, sent witch hunters after Lodge-affiliated mages, and views organized mage power as a threat to his rule. This is not without reason; the Lodge did manipulate Redanian politics. [game_w3]
- **Nilfgaard:** Ambiguous. Fringilla Vigo serves Nilfgaard. Other members resist it. The Lodge as an institution tries to play both sides and has largely failed to maintain this position. [books] [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

By the start of Witcher 3, the Lodge is scattered and diminished:

**Philippa Eilhart:** In hiding in Novigrad, transformed into an owl by Radovid's mages — a curse that is both imprisonment and humiliation. Geralt can find her and, through a series of steps involving another mage (Margarita, if freed) and specific ritual components, restore her human form. She is part of the Novigrad investigation connecting the witch hunter threat, the Lodge's remnants, and the political chaos of the city. Finding Philippa is a significant portion of the secondary Lodge questline in Novigrad.

**Triss Merigold:** Operating the underground mage evacuation network. Not engaging in Lodge business directly — focused on saving individual people from the purges. Her Lodge membership is background context.

**Witch hunter purges:** The Eternal Fire's witch hunters are specifically targeting Lodge members and associates alongside all mages. The purges are systematic and brutal. This is the direct threat the Lodge cannot respond to collectively because it no longer has the coordination to do so. Individual members are surviving individually. [game_w3]

**Keira Metz:** In Velen, operating independently as a wise woman-adjacent presence near Midcopse. The quest "A Greedy God" and the related Keira quests establish her current situation. She has knowledge Geralt needs (specifically about Ciri's movements and a plague affecting Velen) and her own agenda (finding notes from Avallac'h that she believes will give her leverage for her own protection). Geralt can send her to Kaer Morhen or warn her to go to Kovir — the choice has consequences for the Kaer Morhen battle. [game_w3]

## Skellige and Beyond

The Lodge has minimal direct presence on Skellige. Ermion (druid, not Lodge-affiliated) is the magical authority in the isles. Yennefer is present on Skellige but in her capacity as Geralt's ally, not as a Lodge operative.

The Lodge's agenda regarding Ciri — managing her Elder Blood, preventing any single party from controlling her — aligns with Geralt's interest in keeping Ciri safe but has historically conflicted with actually respecting Ciri's autonomy. This tension is not directly addressed during the Skellige arc but informs how Yennefer navigates her Lodge obligations. [game_w3]

## Kaer Morhen Events

Geralt's ally-recruitment arc functions as a partial Lodge reconstruction: getting Philippa's human form restored, freeing Margarita from Oxenfurt, keeping Keira alive and willing to help — these pieces are the Lodge's surviving membership coming together under extreme circumstances.

At Kaer Morhen, the Lodge members (Yennefer, Keira if present, possibly Philippa and Margarita with more effort) work with the witchers on the battle's magical components. They are capable under pressure. The Lodge, as a collective, is better at crisis than at the sustained manipulation and politics that are its normal mode of operation.

After the battle, the Lodge is still not reformed. The members have cooperated; they have not reestablished the organization. What the Lodge becomes after Witcher 3 depends on the political ending. [game_w3]

## Endings and Resolution

The Lodge's future is tied to the political resolution of the Northern war:

**If Radovid is killed:** The Northern Kingdoms' most active anti-mage force is removed. Mages have room to breathe and eventually rebuild political influence. The Lodge, or something like it, could re-emerge.

**If Radovid lives and wins the war:** His anti-mage campaign continues and intensifies. The Lodge's members who remain in the North face ongoing danger. Philippa in particular is likely to flee or fight — she will not submit.

**If Ciri becomes Empress:** A witcher-empress who was trained by Lodge members and cares about Yennefer is unlikely to pursue anti-mage policies. The Lodge's interests under a Ciri regency are served better than under almost any other outcome. [game_w3]

## DLC Content

The Lodge does not appear directly in Toussaint. The duchy has its own magical ecosystem (Duchess Anna Henrietta's court mage, the vampire population, ancient pre-Conjunction entities) that intersects with continental politics but is separate from the Lodge's Northern Kingdoms focus.

**Hearts of Stone:**
No Lodge involvement. The conflict in Hearts of Stone (Gaunter O'Dimm, Olgierd von Everec) is personal and supernatural rather than political. [game_w3]
