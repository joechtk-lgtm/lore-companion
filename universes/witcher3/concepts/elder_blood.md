# Elder Blood (Hen Ichaer)

## Overview

Elder Blood — Hen Ichaer in Elder Speech — is a rare genetic trait carried by specific descendants of Lara Dorren aep Shiadhal, an Aen Elle elf and Sage of the pre-Conjunction era. It gives its bearer access to an innate power over space and time that operates outside the rules of trained magic: the ability to traverse between parallel worlds, perceive and partially influence temporal flows, and discharge raw force at a scale that normal magic cannot replicate. In Witcher 3, Elder Blood is the reason Ciri is hunted by the Wild Hunt, sought by Nilfgaard, studied by Avallac'h, and feared by anyone with sufficient knowledge of what she can do. It is also, on a personal level, the thing that Ciri cannot fully control and cannot choose to set aside — a power that is part of her whether she wants it or not.

## Key Facts

- **Elder Speech name:** Hen Ichaer [books] [game_w3]
- **Origin:** Lara Dorren aep Shiadhal, an Aen Elle Sage who mated with the human mage Cregennan of Lod [books]
- **Inheritance:** Primarily through maternal lines, though the trait can skip generations [books]
- **Rarity:** Extremely rare. The lineage has been diluted over centuries; most descendants show no manifestation. [books]
- **Ciri's lineage:** Lara Dorren → unnamed daughter (raised by human foster parents) → generations of diluted descent → Calanthe's line → Pavetta → Ciri [books]
- **The Ithlinne Prophecy:** An elven oracle's prediction about a bearer of Elder Blood who will either save or destroy the world. The prophecy is deliberately ambiguous but points toward Ciri as its subject. [books] [game_w3]

## What Elder Blood Does

**World traversal (dimensional portals):**
The most strategically significant Elder Blood ability. Ciri can open gates between parallel worlds — not just between locations in one world (which trained mages can do through portal magic), but between entirely separate dimensional spheres. This ability is why the Wild Hunt needs her: the Aen Elle have been struggling with unreliable world-traversal since Caranthir's method requires specific conditions, while Ciri's gates are more powerful and more stable. [books] [game_w3]

**Temporal perception:**
Elder Blood gives Ciri a fractured relationship with linear time. She experiences visions of potential futures, can sometimes perceive events displaced in time from her present, and during moments of power use may experience time non-linearly. This is not controlled precognition — she cannot simply decide to see the future. It intrudes on her perception. [books] [game_w3]

**Raw force discharge:**
When emotionally overwhelmed or under extreme stress, Ciri's Elder Blood power erupts as raw force. The explosion at Kaer Morhen following Vesemir's death — the blast that drove the Wild Hunt back — is this mechanism at full expression. It is destructive, uncontrolled, and proportional to her emotional state. She cannot sustain it deliberately; it discharges when containment fails. [game_w3]

**The White Frost capability:**
The most extreme application of Elder Blood power. Ciri is the only entity in the game's world with power sufficient to enter the White Frost (a dimensional apocalypse consuming multiple worlds) and potentially act against it. This is not simply "more of the same power" — it is a qualitative difference in what her Elder Blood enables. Avallac'h's centuries of research have confirmed this; the Ithlinne Prophecy anticipated it. [books] [game_w3]

## The Lineage — Lara Dorren

**Who Lara Dorren was:**
Lara Dorren aep Shiadhal was an Aen Elle Sage — a practitioner of the pre-Conjunction magical tradition of the Aen Saevherne (the Knowing Ones). She was betrothed to Avallac'h. She fell in love with Cregennan of Lod, a human mage. The union was unprecedented — Aen Elle and human, forbidden by Aen Elle cultural and political norms.

She chose Cregennan over Avallac'h. This was an enormous personal betrayal of the man who loved her, and a political rupture in the Aen Elle community.

She became pregnant. Cregennan died (killed by Aen Elle agents, according to some accounts). Lara was driven into exile by humans who feared what the union represented. She died giving birth to a daughter in a snowbank, having been refused shelter by the humans she turned to for help. The daughter survived, found by human foster parents.

This daughter carried the Elder Blood. Her descendants include Ciri. [books]

**Avallac'h's obsession:**
Avallac'h lost Lara to a human. He watched her die for that choice. He has spent centuries studying what her legacy — the Elder Blood lineage — can do and what it is for. His relationship with Ciri is shaped by everything he felt about Lara, which is too tangled and old to describe simply. [books] [game_w3]

## Why the Trait Skips Generations

Elder Blood is not a dominant trait that manifests in every descendant. The specific genetic combination required for full manifestation is rare, and the trait can be carried silently across multiple generations before re-emerging. This is partly why the lineage was hard to track and partly why Calanthe (who seems to have had some trace of the power) didn't fully manifest it, while Pavetta (who manifested it violently at the betrothal feast) and Ciri (who manifests it most fully of all) each show increasing expression of the trait.

**Pavetta's manifestation:**
At the Cintra betrothal feast where Geralt first encountered Duny (the future Emhyr var Emreis), Pavetta's power erupted when Duny was threatened. She hadn't known she had it. It emerged in an emotional crisis and was wild and uncontrolled. It levitated everyone in the room and created a vortex of power that lasted until Geralt and others broke her concentration. Avallac'h recognized this event when he learned of it later — it confirmed the Elder Blood was reactivating in the line. [books]

**Ciri's fuller expression:**
Ciri's Elder Blood is the fullest expression in the lineage's known history. Avallac'h attributes this partly to the specific crossing of the lineage at this point, partly to destiny (the Ithlinne Prophecy), and partly to Ciri's specific life — trauma and crisis have repeatedly pushed her power into eruption, which may have developed it through stress in the way intense physical training develops muscle. [books] [game_w3]

## How It Differs From Trained Magic

**Source:** Trained magic (sorcerers, sorceresses, the Brotherhood) draws on Chaos — the magical force that flooded the world after the Conjunction. Elder Blood draws on something older: the Aen Saevherne tradition, which predates the Conjunction. [books]

**Control:** Trained magic is controlled through technique, preparation, and the mage's understanding of what they're doing. Elder Blood is wild and partially instinctive — Ciri cannot fully direct it. She has learned to suppress it and, in moments of clarity, channel it. She has not learned to aim it precisely the way Yennefer aims a spell. [books] [game_w3]

**Power ceiling:** Conventional magic has a ceiling — the most powerful sorceress alive (Yennefer, Philippa, Vilgefortz before his death) operates at a certain maximum. Elder Blood's ceiling is unclear and potentially much higher. The White Frost capacity suggests it can interact with forces that trained magic cannot. [books] [game_w3]

**Yennefer's training:**
Yennefer trained Ciri not to use her Elder Blood directly but to work around it — to have enough magical theory and control technique that she could suppress the power rather than being overwhelmed by it. The training was about containment and understanding, not instruction in Elder Blood use specifically. Yennefer cannot teach what she doesn't have. [books] [game_w3]

## The Ithlinne Prophecy

Ithlinne Aep Aevenien was an elven oracle of antiquity. Her prophecy about Elder Blood is the textual basis for Avallac'h's preparation and the Wild Hunt's urgency:

The prophecy speaks of:
- The "Eye of Yr Hegedus" — a comet or celestial body associated with the time of the prophecy's fulfillment
- The "White Frost" and "time of contempt" — the dimensional apocalypse and the era of political and moral collapse
- "A seed sown in blood, Elder Blood, Blood of the Elder" — the lineage
- "The one who will not die, the one from the blood of the elves, daughter of chaos" — the Elder Blood bearer
- A choice: save or destroy the world

The prophecy is typical oracle-speak: true in broad strokes, useless for specific planning, retroactively obvious. Avallac'h has spent centuries trying to interpret it correctly. His conclusion: Ciri is the subject, the White Frost is the crisis, and the outcome is not predetermined — it depends on who Ciri is when the moment comes. This is why her emotional state, confidence, and sense of self matter: the prophecy's uncertainty is located in the bearer, not the event. [books] [game_w3]
