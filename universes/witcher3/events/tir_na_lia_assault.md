# The Assault on Tir ná Lia

## Overview

The assault on Tir ná Lia — the Aen Elle elves' world, home of the Wild Hunt — is the final major military engagement of Witcher 3's main story. It is the culmination of everything: the Sunstone plan, the allies assembled at Kaer Morhen, the political maneuvering in Novigrad and Skellige, and Geralt's months of hunting for Ciri. The battle has two phases: a naval engagement near Skellige that draws and occupies the Wild Hunt, and then the strike force's assault through Avallac'h's gate into Tir ná Lia itself. Geralt kills Eredin Bréacc Glas. Ciri enters the White Frost. Everything that happens after depends on choices made throughout the entire game.

## Key Facts

- **Location:** Tir ná Lia — the Aen Elle world, a parallel dimension [game_w3]
- **Mechanism:** Avallac'h opens a gate using the Sunstone as a lure and specific ritual knowledge [game_w3]
- **Phase 1:** Naval battle near Skellige — the Sunstone draws the Wild Hunt to a chosen battlefield; Skellige warriors and their ships engage while the gate is opened [game_w3]
- **Phase 2:** Strike force assault through the gate into Tir ná Lia [game_w3]
- **Primary objective:** Kill Eredin Bréacc Glas. Enable Ciri to reach the White Frost. [game_w3]
- **Result:** Eredin killed by Geralt. Wild Hunt dissolved as coherent force. Ciri enters the White Frost. [game_w3]
- **Outcome:** Depends on player choices throughout the game. [game_w3]

## Preparation — The Sunstone

The Sunstone is a Skellige artifact — a magical object that resonates with specific frequencies that the Wild Hunt can detect and are compelled to respond to. Finding it requires a questline in Skellige involving Ermion (the druid) and research into pre-Conjunction Skellige magic.

With the Sunstone, Geralt and his allies can choose when and where the Wild Hunt arrives — forcing the confrontation on terms they control rather than waiting for the Hunt to attack at a time and place of their choosing. This is the strategic core of the endgame plan: stop running, make the Hunt come to a chosen battlefield, and hit them while they're committed to that engagement. [game_w3]

## Phase 1 — The Naval Battle

The Sunstone is activated near Skellige's waters. The Wild Hunt materializes for the engagement. Skellige's ships and warriors fight the Hunt's forces in the confrontation Geralt's alliance has prepared.

This phase is a delaying action: the naval battle occupies and weakens the Wild Hunt while Avallac'h opens the gate. The Skellige forces (whichever jarl is in power, with forces appropriate to their leadership style) take serious casualties. The Wild Hunt is not easily stopped at sea — their cold, spectral cavalry operating in Skellige's natural conditions is near-optimal for them.

**Caranthir's death:** Caranthir — the Hunt's navigator and the Aen Elle general responsible for maintaining dimensional pathways — is killed during the naval phase. Ciri kills him. His death removes the Hunt's most reliable method of interdimensional traversal. Without Caranthir, the Hunt is pinned to Tir ná Lia and can no longer pursue across worlds. [game_w3]

**The gate opening:** Avallac'h uses the confusion and the Sunstone's active resonance to open a gate to Tir ná Lia. The gate is large enough for the strike force to pass through. This is the moment everything has been building toward.

## Phase 2 — Inside Tir ná Lia

Tir ná Lia is extraordinary. The Aen Elle world — at least the parts Geralt moves through — is vast, alien, and beautiful in the specific way of something that doesn't follow the Continent's aesthetic rules. Ancient architecture, colors that aren't quite right, a sky that isn't quite the right sky. The Wild Hunt's home is their world, and in their world they fight with full advantage.

**The city:** Geralt moves through Tir ná Lia's outer defenses toward the confrontation with Eredin. The battle is not a straightforward corridor — the Aen Elle city has multiple fighting environments and the Wild Hunt's forces fight through them all. [game_w3]

**Ge'els's effect:** If Geralt successfully completed the quest to reach Ge'els in Tir ná Lia — showing him evidence of how Eredin poisoned King Auberon and took the throne — Ge'els's loyalty to Eredin has cracked. He does not actively fight for Geralt, but he withdraws his active support from Eredin. The Hunt's leadership cohesion is weakened before the final confrontation. [game_w3]

## Geralt vs. Eredin

The final duel.

**Eredin's state:** He has used Caranthir's death — absorbing the dimensional energy from his dying general — to augment his own capabilities. He is wounded from the naval phase but fighting through it.

**The mechanics:** Eredin portals during the fight — using dimensional stepping to reposition, appear behind Geralt, avoid sustained melee. The fight requires Geralt to track Eredin's portal destinations rather than simply maintaining close-range pressure. Yrden is particularly effective for limiting Eredin's movement.

**The kill:** Geralt kills Eredin. The fight is the hardest in the main game.

**Eredin's dying words:** He accuses Avallac'h of having orchestrated everything — used the Wild Hunt's pursuit of Ciri, used Geralt, used all of them to get Ciri to the White Frost in a way that served Avallac'h's own ancient agenda. He may be telling the truth, or a partial truth, or speaking from dying spite. The game does not resolve this. Avallac'h does not deny it in terms that close the question. Eredin dies. [game_w3]

## Avallac'h's Escape

With Eredin dead, Avallac'h moves through the aftermath without accountability. He has what he came for — Ciri is at the White Frost. Whether his intentions were pure (saving multiple worlds) or manipulative (achieving the outcome he designed decades ago, using everyone including Ciri as instruments) is left ambiguous.

He escapes consequences. This is deeply consistent with who he is. The most knowledgeable person in the room always leaves when the room becomes dangerous. [game_w3]

## Ciri and the White Frost

While Geralt fights Eredin, Ciri has gone ahead toward the White Frost — the dimensional apocalypse that is the existential threat of the entire game.

**What she does:** She enters the White Frost. Alone. The experience of entering a dimensional apocalypse — a force that consumes worlds — is not depicted directly. What it required of her is a function of player choices.

**The outcome:**
- Positive choices throughout the game → Ciri's Elder Blood power is sufficient. She returns. She survives. The White Frost, at least temporarily, is stopped from consuming more worlds.
- Negative choices → She doesn't come back. The last image is her medallion in the snow. Geralt goes after her. Both of them are implied not to return.

The White Frost is not permanently destroyed in any ending — Ciri can stop its immediate advance but the nature of the entity suggests it is a persistent cosmic phenomenon rather than something that can be ended with one act. Whether it resumes its advance eventually, in other worlds, on other timescales, is not addressed. The game concerns itself with the characters and their choices, not the ultimate resolution of cosmic apocalypse. [game_w3]

## What Determines the Outcome

The ending Ciri faces in the White Frost is determined by the cumulative weight of choices Geralt made throughout the game about Ciri's confidence, agency, and emotional state:

**The positive path (Ciri survives):**
Choices that reinforce Ciri's confidence — the snowball fight at Kaer Morhen, going with her to Bald Mountain, commemorating Skjall's grave, allowing her to make choices about her own life rather than steering her toward outcomes Geralt thinks are best.

**The negative path (Ciri dies):**
Choices that undermine her confidence or fail to support her — the pattern of treating her as someone who needs to be guided rather than supported, protecting her from decisions rather than helping her make them.

**The Empress path (different survival, different outcome):**
A third path where Ciri is taken to meet Emhyr var Emreis (requires Geralt to have taken her there during the game). She survives the White Frost and chooses the Imperial throne. The Frost was stopped. Her life is constrained in a different way. [game_w3]
