# The Thanedd Coup

## Overview

The Thanedd Coup — the violent political confrontation at the Conclave of Mages on Thanedd Island — is the event that broke the Brotherhood of Sorcerers as a functioning institution and reshuffled the magical political landscape of the Northern Kingdoms into the configuration that Witcher 3 begins with. Nilfgaard had embedded agents among the mages, including Vilgefortz of Roggeveen — one of the most powerful mages alive — who triggered the confrontation at the moment that maximized damage to the Northern mage establishment. Mages killed mages. Key figures died or were captured. Geralt was present and was nearly killed. Yennefer was captured. Ciri escaped through a portal that took her directly into the Wild Hunt's reach. The aftermath: the Lodge of Sorceresses formed in the vacuum, Geralt's memory loss (via the Rivian pogrom) occurred in the chaos of the second war's conclusion, and the political ground Witcher 3 stands on was established.

## Key Facts

- **Date:** During the Second Nilfgaard War (between Witcher 2 and 3 timelines) [books]
- **Location:** Thanedd Island — specifically Aretuza, the mages' academy, and the fortress of Garstang above it [books]
- **Trigger:** Nilfgaard's embedded agents (led by Vilgefortz) moved against the Brotherhood during a scheduled conclave [books]
- **Primary traitor:** Vilgefortz of Roggeveen — a mage of exceptional power who had been working for Nilfgaard [books]
- **Geralt's role:** He was at Thanedd to find Yennefer. He ended up in the middle of the fighting, broke Dijkstra's legs in a confrontation, and barely survived. [books] [game_w3]
- **Major casualties:** Tissaia de Vries (suicide), multiple Brotherhood members killed in the fighting [books]
- **Captured:** Yennefer of Vengerberg (by Nilfgaard) [books]
- **Escaped:** Ciri (through a portal — this exit led directly to complications with the Wild Hunt) [books]
- **Aftermath:** Brotherhood of Sorcerers dissolved. Lodge of Sorceresses formed. The political power of mages in the Northern Kingdoms was permanently diminished. [books] [game_w3]

## The Conclave

The Brotherhood of Sorcerers met at Thanedd Island to address the political crisis of the ongoing Northern war. The conclave was intended to determine whether mages would maintain their tradition of political neutrality or actively intervene against Nilfgaard (as the Sodden Hill mages had against the First War).

This was already a politically fractured gathering. Some mages were Nilfgaardian sympathizers (or outright agents). Some were committed to the North. Most were primarily committed to the Brotherhood's institutional survival and their own personal positions. The conditions for a betrayal were well-established. [books]

## Vilgefortz of Roggeveen

The traitor and the most significant individual figure in the coup:

- Vilgefortz was widely considered one of the two or three most powerful mages alive at the time — his magical ability was extraordinary.
- He had been a Nilfgaardian agent for years, presumably recruited or motivated before the coup.
- He initiated the violent phase of the coup, killing Brotherhood members who would have opposed Nilfgaard's plan.
- He personally confronted Geralt during the chaos. In the books, their first fight: Geralt lost. Badly. He was beaten efficiently by a man who was operating at a level Geralt could not match with preparation. He survived, barely, because the fight was interrupted.
- Their second confrontation occurs at Stygga Castle in the book saga's climax. Geralt survived the first meeting and prepared specifically for Vilgefortz. The second fight, with preparation and alchemical support, goes differently.
- Vilgefortz also held Yennefer captive and conducted experiments on her during her imprisonment. The specifics are unpleasant.
- He was obsessed with Ciri specifically — he wanted her Elder Blood for his own purposes, which diverged even from Nilfgaard's plans. He was ultimately a self-interested actor using Nilfgaard's resources toward his own ends, not a loyal Nilfgaardian agent. [books]

## Dijkstra's Legs

During the chaos of Thanedd, Geralt and Dijkstra came into conflict. The exact circumstances are not elaborated in detail in-game — what is established is that Geralt broke Dijkstra's legs during the confrontation. Dijkstra survived. He recovered. He brings it up with precisely the controlled frequency of someone who has decided not to make it personal while also not letting it go. In Witcher 3, this history is referenced but not dwelt upon — it is part of the texture of their complicated relationship. [books] [game_w3]

## Tissaia de Vries

Tissaia de Vries was the Rectoress of Aretuza — Yennefer's mentor and the most senior woman in the Brotherhood. She was deeply invested in the Brotherhood as an institution and in the idea of mage service to the North. At Thanedd, she discovered the scope of the betrayal — that people she had trusted, including Vilgefortz, had been working against everything she built. She killed herself (took poison) rather than survive the collapse. Her death at Thanedd is one of the book saga's genuine losses — she was formidable, difficult, and right about most things that mattered. [books]

## Ciri's Exit and Its Consequences

Ciri was at Thanedd (brought there for political reasons connected to her safety and future). During the coup's violence, Geralt helped her reach a portal — but the portal, in the chaos, did not go where it should have. Ciri's exit from Thanedd brought her into a location where the Wild Hunt could reach her. The Hunt found her. She was taken to Tir ná Lia.

This is the beginning of the years she spent as a prisoner and fugitive in the Aen Elle world before the events of Witcher 3. The Thanedd coup, directly and accidentally, put Ciri in the Wild Hunt's hands. [books]

## Aftermath and Witcher 3 Context

The Thanedd coup is background in Witcher 3 — it happened before the games' events and its consequences are the world the game starts in:

- **No more Brotherhood:** The Lodge is the replacement, built from necessity by its survivors.
- **Yennefer's position:** Her capture and imprisonment shaped her current wariness about Nilfgaard and her pragmatic approach to working under Emhyr's authority in Witcher 3. She knows what being Nilfgaard's prisoner means.
- **Dijkstra's grudge:** The broken legs are a specific texture in his relationship with Geralt throughout Witcher 3.
- **The Lodge's founding:** Philippa and Margarita built the Lodge specifically because Thanedd destroyed the alternative. The Lodge is a response to the coup.
- **Ciri's imprisonment:** The years Ciri spent in Tir ná Lia, which shape who she is and what the Wild Hunt wants with her, trace directly to Thanedd's chaotic exit. [books] [game_w3]
