# The Blood and Wine Murders (The Beast of Beauclair)

## Overview

The Blood and Wine murders are the central mystery of the Blood and Wine DLC — a series of killings in Toussaint, each of which targets a notable knight or figure and is staged to reference one of the Five Knightly Virtues of Toussaint: Valor, Generosity, Wisdom, Compassion, and Honor. The murders are attributed to the Beast of Beauclair — a monster operating in Toussaint's fairy tale landscape. The truth is more complicated: a Higher Vampire named Dettlaff van der Eretein has been manipulated into becoming an instrument of revenge by a woman who understands exactly how to weaponize his love and his nature. Geralt is hired to solve the murders and stop the Beast. Regis complicates everything by being Dettlaff's friend. The Duchess's estranged sister complicates everything further by being the one who started it.

## Key Facts

- **Location:** Toussaint — a duchy formally attached to Nilfgaard but culturally independent, wine country with a chivalric code [game_w3]
- **Employer:** Duchess Anna Henrietta of Toussaint (Beauclair) [game_w3]
- **The Beast:** Dettlaff van der Eretein — a Higher Vampire [game_w3]
- **The architect:** Syanna — Duchess Anna Henrietta's estranged younger sister [game_w3]
- **The pattern:** Each victim violated one of the Five Knightly Virtues that form Toussaint's cultural code [game_w3]
- **Regis's role:** Geralt's friend from the books; knows Dettlaff personally; essential to negotiation [game_w3]
- **Resolution:** Multiple endings depending on player choices throughout the DLC [game_w3]

## Toussaint — The Setting

Toussaint is Witcher 3's most distinctive location. It is wine country — rolling vineyards, a pastel city (Beauclair), a warm climate that the Continent proper doesn't have, and a culture organized around the Five Knightly Virtues: Valor, Generosity, Wisdom, Compassion, Honor. Everything in Toussaint is organized around the pretense that it is a fairy tale kingdom — chivalric tournaments, beautiful architecture, excellent wine, courtly love as an institution.

The darkness underneath is the point: Toussaint is a fairy tale with corruption and monsters below the surface. Vampires have been integrated into Toussaint's society for centuries (as "echinopsae" — the term locals use, politely). Political intrigues have real consequences. The Duchess's pleasant exterior hides a history with her sister that produced the current murders.

The DLC's thesis is that the prettiest places have the darkest secrets, which is a thesis it earns. [game_w3]

## The Five Knightly Virtues and the Victims

**Valor:** The first victim. A knight who violated valor — specifically cowardice in battle, hidden by reputation. Killed in a manner symbolic of the virtue violated.

**Generosity:** A man known for generosity who was privately avaricious. Killed with that hypocrisy as the signature.

**Wisdom:** A man valued for his counsel who gave destructive advice. Killed with that failure as the theme.

**Compassion:** A man who profited from others' suffering while projecting compassion. The murder stages his hypocrisy.

**Honor:** The final virtue. The pattern builds to this.

Each victim wronged Syanna specifically — they were people involved in her exile and the events surrounding it. Syanna constructed a revenge scheme using Toussaint's own code as the message: these men violated the virtues they claimed to represent, and they are judged for it. She used Dettlaff as the execution mechanism. [game_w3]

## Dettlaff van der Eretein

Dettlaff is a Higher Vampire — ancient, powerful, and emotionally volatile in ways that Higher Vampires can be when they care deeply about something. He loved Syanna. He believed she was in danger and that the murders were what was needed to save her. He is not a monster who kills for pleasure — he is a person who was manipulated through his love into becoming a weapon.

**His relationship with Syanna:** They had a relationship before the DLC's events. Syanna disappeared. When she reappeared and told him she needed the murders done, he believed her version of events entirely. He is not naive — he is in love, which functions similarly.

**His relationship with Regis:** They are old acquaintances in the Higher Vampire community. Regis's return (from the Rivian pogrom/regeneration) means he knows Dettlaff and can reach him. This is Regis's value to the investigation: he can get Geralt a conversation with Dettlaff rather than simply a fight.

**What Dettlaff actually is:** A very powerful Higher Vampire with the ability to command lesser vampires (bruxae and others), extremely dangerous in direct combat, and genuinely capable of the kind of grief and fury that makes a Higher Vampire into an apocalyptic threat if pushed past a certain point. If Dettlaff is pushed into full fury — if the situation reaches the point where he stops trying to get what he came for and starts destroying — Beauclair would be in serious danger. [game_w3]

## Syanna — Anna Henrietta's Sister

Syanna was born under a "Cursed Star" — an astrological event in Toussaint's tradition associated with ill fate and dangerous individuals. The local superstition around this led to her exile as a child. She spent years outside Toussaint, surviving, developing the skills and the anger that made the revenge scheme possible.

She returned with a plan. She found Dettlaff, used his love for her to make him her instrument, and set the murders in motion with the Five Virtues framework as both cover story and message.

**What she wants:** Revenge on specific individuals who participated in her exile and its consequences. Not redemption, not reconciliation — revenge, specifically and precisely calibrated.

**Her relationship with Anna Henrietta:** The Duchess does not know, initially, that her sister is involved. The revelation of Syanna's role — that the murders are connected to Anna Henrietta's own family history — is one of the DLC's significant plot turns. [game_w3]

## Regis and the Investigation

Regis appears to help Geralt because he knows Dettlaff and the situation. His presence complicates the investigation in productive ways:

- He can identify what Dettlaff is doing and why, based on knowing him
- He can potentially get Geralt into a conversation with Dettlaff before it becomes a fight
- He does not want Dettlaff dead if there is any alternative
- He will not lie to Geralt about what Dettlaff is capable of or what will happen if pushed

This creates a genuine moral tension: Regis is Geralt's friend, asking Geralt to pursue a resolution that doesn't end with Regis having to watch his other friend die. Geralt can pursue this. It requires more work than simply killing Dettlaff. [game_w3]

## The Fairy Tale Realm — Launeddas

One of the DLC's stranger elements: there is a pocket dimension accessible from Toussaint that functions as a fairy tale realm — complete with fairy tale characters, fairy tale logic, and characters from the stories that Toussaint's culture loves. Geralt can access this realm to find a specific item (a ribbon) that becomes critical to the best ending.

**The ribbon:** Without going into all the mechanics, obtaining this ribbon before the final confrontation is the key to the best possible outcome. It requires engaging with the fairy tale realm as a quest rather than ignoring it.

The realm itself is a delight and a contrast — pure fairy tale aesthetic inside the game's realistic world. [game_w3]

## The Endings

**The good ending (most complex to achieve):**
- Geralt got the ribbon from the fairy tale realm
- Syanna can survive Dettlaff's attack during the final confrontation
- Dettlaff is convinced to stand down (or killed if necessary — but the confrontation doesn't require his death if Syanna survives)
- Duchess Anna Henrietta is reconciled with Syanna
- Regis doesn't have to watch his friend die
- Geralt receives Corvo Bianco as his estate

**The Dettlaff dies ending:**
Dettlaff is killed. Regis accepts this with quiet grief. The investigation is resolved. The political situation in Toussaint is stabilized. The emotional cost is Regis watching his friend die. [game_w3]

**The Syanna dies ending:**
Dettlaff attacks and kills Syanna (no ribbon). The Duchess is devastated. The situation resolves badly at the human level even if Dettlaff is also stopped. [game_w3]

**Duchess's execution of Geralt:**
A specific ending where Anna Henrietta, in her grief over Syanna's death, sentences Geralt to execution (he failed to protect her sister). This is avoidable. [game_w3]

## Corvo Bianco

Whatever the ending, Geralt receives (or can receive) Corvo Bianco — a vineyard estate in Toussaint as payment for his work. The estate becomes his retirement home in the game's final epilogue. The gardener (Marlene, restored from a curse in a side quest), the beehive, the horse, and whoever Geralt ended up with complete the image. A witcher, with a home. [game_w3]
