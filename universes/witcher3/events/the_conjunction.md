# The Conjunction of the Spheres

## Overview

The Conjunction of the Spheres is the foundational cosmological event of the Witcher universe — the moment, approximately fifteen hundred years before the events of Witcher 3, when multiple parallel worlds (or "spheres") collided or briefly overlapped, allowing creatures, races, and materials from different realities to pass through into each other's worlds. The Continent's world received monsters from other dimensions. It also received humans. The event permanently altered the world's metaphysical landscape, created the portals and dimensional instabilities that mages later learned to exploit, and is ultimately responsible for every witcher, sorcerer, and monster that exists in the setting.

## Key Facts

- **Time:** Approximately 1500 years before Witcher 3 (c. ~200 BCE in the setting's internal calendar) [books] [game_w3]
- **Nature:** A catastrophic overlap of multiple parallel worlds — the "spheres" — that lasted long enough to allow permanent migration between them [books] [game_w3]
- **Primary arrivals on the Continent:**
  - **Humans** — from another sphere, arriving as refugees/migrants who eventually displaced the indigenous elder races [books] [game_w3]
  - **Monsters** — creatures from multiple dimensions: ghouls, wraiths, vampires (Higher Vampires came from their own world), and numerous other species that did not exist on the Continent before [books] [game_w3]
- **What was already here:** Elves (Aen Seidhe), Dwarves, Gnomes, and the other elder races had inhabited the Continent before the Conjunction [books] [game_w3]
- **Permanent changes:** The Conjunction left behind "rifts" — instabilities in the dimensional fabric that skilled practitioners (mages) can exploit to teleport. It also left behind monsters that became permanent population. And it left behind humans who became the dominant species. [books] [game_w3]
- **Chaos and Order:** The Conjunction released Chaos — the magical force mages draw on — into the world in dramatically increased quantities. Pre-Conjunction magic is qualitatively different from post-Conjunction magic; the raw power available increased and the rules changed. [books]

## The Races

**Before the Conjunction:** The Continent was inhabited by:
- **Aen Seidhe (Elves)** — the most prominent elder race, with advanced civilization, long lives, and magical aptitude. They called their homeland Aedirn, Redania, and the other territories now named after human kingdoms. [books] [game_w3]
- **Dwarves** — mountain-dwelling, master craftsmen, traders. They integrated with human civilization better than elves over the centuries. [books] [game_w3]
- **Gnomes** — oldest recorded inhabitants, master engineers. [books]
- **Halflings** — another elder race, farmers and traders. [books]

**The Conjunction arrivals:**
- **Humans** — appeared with no civilization footprint already on the Continent. Came in waves. Reproduced faster than elves. Expanded relentlessly. Within centuries, were the dominant species politically and militarily. [books] [game_w3]
- **Monsters** — multiple categories: ghouls and nekkers (from another world), vampires (Higher Vampires came from their own sphere and are not native Continent monsters at all), various undead-adjacent creatures, werewolves, some drowners, and others. The full taxonomy of which monsters are Conjunction arrivals versus which were already present is incomplete in-universe. [books] [game_w3]

## Higher Vampires — A Special Case

Higher Vampires arrived in the Conjunction from their own world — a world they describe as dead and cold. They are not undead humans; they are a separate species that merely resembles the folk-mythological vampire. Higher Vampires:
- Do not need to feed on blood to survive but can choose to
- Can take human form perfectly
- Are extremely difficult to kill (regeneration, need specific methods)
- Are effectively immortal unless destroyed by very specific means
- Have a complex culture and philosophical tradition entirely their own

Regis — Emiel Regis Rohellec Terzieff-Godefroy — is the most prominent Higher Vampire in the Witcher narrative. He traveled with Geralt in the books, died at Stygga Castle, regenerated over decades, and appears in Blood and Wine. [books] [game_w3]

The distinction between Higher Vampires (intelligent, person-like) and lesser vampires (brutal, feral — the bruxae, ekimara, alps, katakans) is significant. Lesser vampires are degenerate offshoots or a different species entirely. [books] [game_w3]

## Magic and the Conjunction

The Conjunction dramatically altered the metaphysical environment:

**Before:** Magic existed but was rarer, more structured, tied to specific Elder races (the Sages — Aen Saevherne — were the pre-eminent magical practitioners). The power available was more limited.

**After:** Chaos flooded in. The raw material of magic became vastly more abundant. Humans, arriving in the Conjunction, proved to have significant aptitude for this new kind of magic — the kind that draws on Chaos. The Brotherhood of Sorcerers and later the magical academies (Aretuza, Ban Ard) teach human-adapted magic, which draws on Chaos. Elder Blood magic, conversely, is older and draws on something different — the inheritance of the Aen Saevherne, predating the Conjunction. This is part of why Ciri's power operates differently from trained sorcery. [books] [game_w3]

**Portals:** The Conjunction left permanent dimensional instability. Mages can exploit this to teleport — essentially using the residual cracks in the dimensional fabric. The most skilled mages (Yennefer, Triss, Philippa) use portals freely. The Wild Hunt uses portals for a different reason: their ability to traverse worlds is related to the same instabilities, though their technology is distinct from human portal magic. [books] [game_w3]

## The Conjunction and the Witcher Profession

Witchers exist because of the Conjunction. The monsters that arrived needed to be killed. The Trial of the Grasses, the witcher mutations, the school system — all developed as a response to a monster population that proliferated after the Conjunction flooded the Continent with creatures that had no natural predators here.

The Witcher profession is explicitly a response to the Conjunction: "After the Conjunction of the Spheres, when monsters entered our world, mankind needed protectors. Witchers were created to fill that need." The irony — extensively explored in the books — is that as humanity spreads and monsters are hunted out of safe territories, the need for witchers decreases even as individual monsters become harder to find. Witchers are an answer to a problem that is slowly being solved, leaving them progressively obsolete. [books] [game_w3]

## Relevance in Witcher 3

The Conjunction is the cosmological bedrock of the entire game:

- The White Frost (Tedd Deireadh) that Ciri must face is connected to the same dimensional instabilities that the Conjunction created — it is the dimensional apocalypse from the other direction, multiple spheres dying rather than one sphere opening.
- The Wild Hunt (Aen Elle cavalry) crossing between worlds uses the same cracks in dimensional fabric that the Conjunction left behind.
- Gaunter O'Dimm (Hearts of Stone) is connected to the pre-Conjunction supernatural order — entities that predated and survived it.
- The question of whether humans "belong" on the Continent — a recurring political tension between humans and elder races — comes back to the Conjunction. Humans are immigrants who became conquerors. The elves know this and cannot forget it. Humans mostly choose not to think about it. [game_w3] [books]

## In the Context of Velen and Novigrad

The Eternal Fire's theology in Novigrad is partly a response to the Conjunction — a human-centric religion that places human civilization as the rightful order and treats elder races and monsters as threats to be purged. The logic is: after the Conjunction brought chaos, humanity has the right and duty to impose order. The witch hunters are the violent arm of this ideology applied to people (mages, nonhumans) who represent the "chaos" that the Eternal Fire opposes. [game_w3]

## DLC Context

**Blood and Wine:**
Toussaint's monster population is explicitly Conjunction-related — the vampire ecology of the region (dozens of higher and lesser vampires integrated into local society over centuries) is a legacy of Conjunction arrivals who never left. The events of Blood and Wine explore what happens when that legacy becomes visible and violent. [game_w3]
