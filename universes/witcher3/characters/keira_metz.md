# Keira Metz

## Overview

Keira Metz is a sorceress, a former member of the Lodge of Sorceresses, and one of the more pragmatic survivors of the magical persecution that has made the Northern Kingdoms dangerous for people like her. She has set herself up in Velen — as far from Novigrad's witch hunters as she can get while still being in a somewhat settled territory — posing as a "wise woman" for the nearby village of Midcopse, dispensing minor magical assistance in exchange for discretion and the pretense that she is not what she obviously is. She is self-interested, sharp, occasionally funny, and running a survival calculation at all times. She is also in possession of information about a plague affecting Velen and notes from Avallac'h's laboratory that she intends to leverage into personal protection. Her plan is probably not as good as she thinks it is. Geralt can fix this, if he's quick.

- **Personality:** Pragmatic to a fault, dry humor, capable of genuine warmth when she drops the self-preservation posture. She's been frightened for a long time and has gotten good at not showing it. [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

Keira is Geralt's first significant magical ally in the Velen arc. Her quests are threaded into the early Velen investigation and reveal both her capabilities and her precarious situation.

**The plague investigation:** Near Midcopse, Velen villages are being affected by a magical plague — people are dying in ways that suggest enchantment rather than natural disease. Keira has been investigating but limited in what she can access. Geralt helps her investigate the source, which leads to a ruined tower and the laboratory of an old mage who worked before the Northern Wars. The laboratory contains research relevant to both the plague and Avallac'h's notes (she found the notes here or in proximity).

**The ghost:** In the tower, Geralt and Keira encounter the spirit of the mage who used it — a man who worked with Avallac'h generations ago, who understood some of what the Sage was researching. The encounter is both a ghost-investigation and an information dump about Avallac'h's early research into Elder Blood and the plague that was used to test the limits of Elder Blood immunity.

**The magic lamp:** Keira gifts Geralt a magic lamp that allows him to communicate with spirits in specific locations — a useful tool for the Velen investigation.

**Her personal agenda:** Keira has the Avallac'h notes and plans to take them to Radovid. She genuinely believes this will buy her safety — that Radovid will value the information enough to extend protection to her rather than simply torture the information out of her and then burn her. This is extremely wrong. Radovid does not make deals with mages; he makes examples.

**"A Favor for a Friend":** The quest where Keira's plan is at its most exposed. She reveals her intention. Geralt can:
- Let her go to Radovid (she dies — Radovid interrogates and executes her)
- Convince her to take the notes to Kaer Morhen instead (she lives, helps at the battle, develops relationship with Lambert)
- Various other outcomes depending on dialogue choices

The right answer is clearly Kaer Morhen. Getting Keira there requires Geralt making the argument that Radovid will not honor an agreement with a mage, which is true and demonstrable if she thinks it through. She is smart enough to be convinced if Geralt frames it correctly. [game_w3]

## Kaer Morhen Events

If Geralt successfully redirected Keira to Kaer Morhen:

She arrives with the Avallac'h notes and her own magical capability. She is not initially comfortable in the witchers' fortress — it is not a comfortable place — but she is professional enough to set personal preferences aside for what is clearly a necessary fight.

Her relationship with Lambert develops during the preparation period and through the battle. The dynamics of two self-interested pragmatists who find each other more interesting than expected is well-executed in the game's brief interactions.

She survives the battle. [game_w3]

## Endings and Resolution

If Keira survived (Kaer Morhen path):
She and Lambert are together by the game's end. She presumably continues practicing magic somewhere safer than Novigrad — with Radovid dead (if that happened) or with a lower profile in Temerian territory. The notes she brought contributed to the broader information pool that informed the endgame plan.

If Keira died (Radovid path):
She is not present at Kaer Morhen. Lambert notices. He doesn't say much about it but he noticed. [game_w3]
