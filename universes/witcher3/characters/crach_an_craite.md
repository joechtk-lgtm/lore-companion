# Crach an Craite

## Overview

Crach an Craite is the Jarl of Clan An Craite, holder of Kaer Trolde on Ard Skellig, and the most powerful of Skellige's clan leaders at the time of Witcher 3. He is also an old friend — of Queen Calanthe of Cintra, of Geralt, and of Ciri, who he sheltered when she came to Skellige ahead of the Wild Hunt. He is a large, loud, straightforward man who fights well and thinks more carefully than the exterior suggests. He loved Calanthe, in whatever terms that friendship took, and the fact that she is dead and her kingdom is gone and her granddaughter is running for her life across worlds is the kind of grief Crach carries without discussing.

## Key Facts

- **Title:** Jarl of Clan An Craite, de facto First Among Jarls of Skellige at Witcher 3's start [game_w3]
- **Seat:** Kaer Trolde, the great fortress on Ard Skellig (the largest island) [game_w3]
- **Age:** Middle-aged by Skellige standards — experienced, still fighting-capable, highly respected [game_w3]
- **Appearance:** Large, heavily built, red-bearded. Looks like what he is: a Skellige warrior who has been doing this for decades. [game_w3]
- **Political standing:** After King Bran's death, Crach is the most senior and respected jarl. His children (Cerys and Hjalmar) are the primary succession candidates. [game_w3]
- **History with Calanthe:** He knew the Lioness of Cintra well. The friendship was real and influenced his instinct to protect Ciri when she came to Skellige. [game_w3] [books]
- **History with Geralt:** A genuine friendship that extends back to their shared connection through Calanthe and the Continent's political world. [game_w3]

## Relationships

- **Ciri:** He sheltered her when she arrived on Skellige. He did not know the full scope of what she was or why she was being hunted; he knew she was Calanthe's granddaughter and that was sufficient. [game_w3]
- **Cerys an Craite:** His daughter. Intelligent, politically thoughtful, quietly fierce. One of the succession candidates. Crach respects her judgment even when he'd prefer Hjalmar's straightforwardness. [game_w3]
- **Hjalmar an Craite:** His son. Bold, warrior-first, leader-by-example. The other succession candidate. Crach loves him and worries about his tendency to charge ahead of preparation. [game_w3]
- **Geralt:** Old friendship maintained across years and distance. Crach treats Geralt as a peer and someone whose judgment in a fight he trusts. [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

Crach an Craite is not present in Velen or Novigrad. He is at Kaer Trolde on Ard Skellig, managing the aftermath of King Bran's death and the succession crisis.

News of the political situation reaches him — Nilfgaard is winning, the Northern Kingdoms are fragmenting, and Skellige is navigating whether to engage in the continental war or maintain its island independence. Crach's instinct is engagement: Skellige is not an island that stays out of things. But engagement requires a jarl with sufficient support to commit the clans. [game_w3]

## Skellige and Beyond

Geralt's arrival on Skellige puts him in contact with Crach immediately. Crach is one of the first people Geralt should speak with — he knows what happened to Ciri on Skellige, has been grieving and trying to understand the aftermath of the Wild Hunt attack, and can provide both information and practical support.

**What Crach knows:** Ciri came to Skellige. She was sheltered at Kaer Trolde. The Wild Hunt attacked. Crach's people fought. The attack was devastating — Skellige warriors are formidable, but the Wild Hunt is not a conventional enemy. The battle killed significant numbers of Crach's household guard and warriors before Ciri escaped.

**Crach's death:** During the Wild Hunt's assault on Kaer Trolde, Crach fights directly. He is killed in the battle — he does not survive to the game's later arcs. His death happens during the events Geralt pieces together while investigating what happened to Ciri on Skellige. By the time Geralt arrives at Kaer Trolde, Crach is dead and his children are dealing with the aftermath of both his death and the succession question.

**The succession crisis:** With Crach dead, the An Craite clan's leadership passes to Cerys or Hjalmar. The Thing — the assembly of jarls — must choose a new king. Geralt's assistance to either candidate affects which of them gains the throne and therefore what kind of leader Skellige has going forward. [game_w3]

## Kaer Morhen Events

Crach an Craite does not survive to the Kaer Morhen arc. His legacy is present: his children's involvement in the fight (or lack thereof, depending on Skellige choices), his clan's role in the alliance, and the personal history he represented between Geralt and Skellige. [game_w3]

## Endings and Resolution

Crach's fate is fixed — he dies in the Wild Hunt attack on Kaer Trolde during events that precede Geralt's arrival on Skellige. What varies is his children's outcomes:

- **If Cerys becomes Queen of Skellige:** Cerys leads with political intelligence, trying to build Skellige's future on sustainability rather than raids. She is the better choice for long-term stability.
- **If Hjalmar becomes King:** He leads with warrior energy and military aggression, better for immediate warfare but less certain for peace.

Either way, Crach's clan holds Kaer Trolde and the An Craite legacy continues. It is not nothing for a man who cared about his people and his island. [game_w3]
