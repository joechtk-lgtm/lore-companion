# Triss Merigold

## Overview

Triss Merigold of Maribor is a sorceress, a former member of the Brotherhood of Sorcerers, and one of Geralt's most trusted friends — which is complicated by the fact that she was also his lover for several years during the period of his amnesia, a period she is aware ended with his memory recovery and his return to Yennefer. She is considered the Fourteenth of the Hill — a title of respect for surviving the Battle of Brenna, where fourteen mages died and she did not, despite fighting the entire battle with a suppurating wound because she had used all of her healing magic on soldiers. She uses it as a joke. She is genuinely brave and she finds it embarrassing.

## Key Facts

- **Full name:** Triss Merigold of Maribor [books] [game_w3]
- **Title:** The Fourteenth of the Hill (from the Battle of Brenna) [books]
- **Profession:** Sorceress, formerly affiliated with the Brotherhood of Sorcerers, Lodge of Sorceresses [books] [game_w3]
- **Origin:** Maribor, Temeria — which shapes her sympathies and political loyalties significantly [books]
- **Appearance:** Chestnut-red hair, blue eyes. Tends toward practical clothing with ornamental touches. Often wears her hair loose or simply braided rather than formally styled. [game_w3] [books]
- **Magical specialty:** General sorceress with noted skill in healing magic and teleportation. Her ability with healing is partly why she used all of it at Brenna and fought the rest of the battle injured. [books]
- **Political alignment:** Temperamentally sympathetic to the Northern Kingdoms over Nilfgaard; specifically to Temeria, her home territory. She has Temerian loyalties that go beyond purely self-interested calculation. [books] [game_w3]
- **Lodge status:** Member, but her loyalty to Geralt and Ciri repeatedly created tension with the Lodge's agenda — which prioritized its own institutional survival over individual relationships. [books]
- **Allergy:** Triss is allergic to magic-based healing applied to herself. A witcher or healer cannot use standard potions or spells on her. This has caused complications in combat situations. [books]

When Geralt recovered his memory, he returned to Yennefer. Triss accepted this, though it is unresolved. Her feelings for Geralt are real; the ethics of how their relationship began are complicated. ## Velen and Novigrad Context

Triss is operating covertly in Novigrad when Geralt arrives. The city is under Eternal Fire influence and witch hunters are actively hunting mages — arresting, torturing, and burning them. Triss has organized an underground network to help mages escape Novigrad via the sewers and the docks, connecting hunted mages with ships that will take them to relative safety.

She is hiding her own identity. A sorceress of her reputation and power would be a high-value target for the witch hunters. She wears mundane clothing, avoids obvious magic use, and moves carefully through the city.

**The quest "Now or Never":** This is the critical Triss questline in the Novigrad arc. Geralt can help Triss evacuate the last group of mages trapped in Novigrad — getting them through the sewers to Bart the troll's ferry point and onto a ship. The quest involves fighting through witch hunters in close quarters and eventually getting the evacuees aboard a vessel.

At the end of the quest, Triss is on the docks preparing to leave. If Geralt chooses to express genuine romantic interest and asks her to stay, she does and this pushes the relationship toward the Triss romance ending. If he says nothing, she leaves for Kovir.

She is also a key witness to Ciri's time in Novigrad — she helped Ciri move through the city and can fill in gaps in Geralt's understanding of what happened. [game_w3]

## Skellige and Beyond

If the "Now or Never" quest ended with Triss staying (romance path), she remains loosely in contact with Geralt. If she left for Kovir, she is still reachable but not present on Skellige.

Triss is not part of the Skellige investigation arc. Her role at this point is as an available ally — someone Geralt can call on when assembling forces for the final confrontation.

[spoiler_tier: kaer_morhen]
## Kaer Morhen Events

Triss participates in the Battle of Kaer Morhen as one of the mages Geralt recruits. Her presence requires that she was successfully helped in Novigrad — if she left and Geralt made no effort to contact her afterward, she may not appear.

At Kaer Morhen she works alongside Yennefer (tense co-existing), Keira Metz, and the witchers. She contributes her magic to the defense and the planning phase. The scene of the witchers and sorceresses at the strategy table together is one of the game's better ensemble moments.

After Vesemir's death, she is part of the grieving group that needs to regroup and plan. Her Temerian loyalties make her invested in the fight — the Wild Hunt represents a threat that affects everyone, and she has made clear she will not stand aside from it. [game_w3]

## Endings and Resolution

**The romance ending (if "Now or Never" led to Triss staying and Geralt pursued only her):**
Triss and Geralt end up together. If Blood and Wine is installed, she appears at Corvo Bianco in the epilogue — in the vineyard, planting a garden or working with the estate. The domestic detail is meaningful: a sorceress who spent her career moving through courts and wars, finally in one place. It is quietly earned.

**The "nobody" ending:**
If Geralt pursued both Triss and Yennefer, he ends up with neither — both women confront him about playing both sides, and that's that. He is alone. This is not unfair.

**The Yennefer ending:**
Triss is not present at Corvo Bianco. She is presumably in Kovir, continuing the work of rebuilding mage political influence in the North after the war.

**Political outcome:**
If Geralt helped with Radovid's assassination and the Temerian resistance (Vernon Roche, Ves, and Thaler) succeeded, Temeria gets autonomous status as a Nilfgaardian vassal. This matters to Triss. She cares about Temeria's survival. [game_w3]

## Netflix Divergence
[canon_source: netflix]

Netflix Triss (Anna Shaffer) has red hair matching the games, though the specific shade differs. In the books, Triss's hair is auburn/chestnut. The Netflix version plays Triss as a younger and more uncertain character in the early seasons, with her arc in later seasons expanding her role. The book/game complexity of her relationship with Geralt — specifically the ethics of the amnesia romance — is handled differently across the adaptations.
