# Vernon Roche

## Overview

Vernon Roche is the former Commander of the Blue Stripes — Temeria's special forces unit under King Foltest — and the current leader of the Temerian partisan resistance operating out of the forests of Velen. He is a Temerian loyalist in the most precise sense: not because Temeria is particularly good, but because it is his, because Foltest was his king, because the people who live there are his people, and because Nilfgaard's occupation is a specific ongoing insult to a specific place he will not stop caring about. He is practical, military-minded, and loyal to a degree that other people find either admirable or exhausting depending on context. He has been fighting an impossible war from the woods of Velen since Temeria fell, and he intends to keep fighting it.

## Key Facts

- **Former title:** Commander of the Blue Stripes (Temerian special forces) [game_w1w2] [game_w3]
- **Current role:** Leader of the Temerian partisan resistance in Velen [game_w3]
- **Affiliation:** Temeria — dead kingdom, surviving identity [game_w3]
- **Personality:** Military discipline, Temerian loyalty, not given to sentiment but capable of it under specific conditions [game_w3]
- **Appearance:** Compact, military bearing, carries himself like someone who expects combat and has made peace with that expectation. [game_w3]
- **Lieutenant:** Ves, a capable Blue Stripes veteran who maintained her loyalty through the fall of Temeria. [game_w3]
- **The Blue Stripes:** Temeria's elite intelligence and special operations unit, known for brutal effectiveness. They were Foltest's instrument for the kind of work that couldn't be acknowledged publicly. [game_w1w2]
- **King Foltest:** Roche's king, who was assassinated in Witcher 2 (beginning that game's plot). Roche spent Witcher 2 hunting the assassin. The grief and anger of losing Foltest drives Roche's continued Temerian resistance even after the kingdom has nominally fallen. [game_w1w2]

## Relationships

- **Geralt:** They have significant shared history from Witcher 2 — they worked together to investigate Foltest's assassination, and Roche provided significant help to Geralt during that game depending on choices. Their relationship is mutual professional respect and cautious trust. Not friends in the Dandelion sense; allies in the Roche sense, which means something. [game_w1w2] [game_w3]
- **Ves:** His lieutenant and arguably his most trusted person. She has stayed with him through the fall of Temeria, through the partisan camp, through everything. [game_w3]
- **Thaler:** A Temerian intelligence operative operating in Novigrad. Part of the network that eventually plans Radovid's assassination. Roche knows him through their mutual service to Temeria. [game_w3]
- **Dijkstra:** Antagonism. They are on opposite sides of the Northern conflict's aftermath — Roche is Temerian, Dijkstra is Redanian, and their interests diverge sharply on what the post-Radovid power structure should look like. [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

Roche's partisan camp is hidden in the Temerian-adjacent forests of Velen — the area that was Temerian before Nilfgaard's conquest. He runs raids and operations from there, harassing Nilfgaardian supply lines and maintaining the fiction (which is also a reality) that Temeria isn't entirely gone.

**"An Eye for an Eye" and related quests:** Geralt can help Roche with resistance operations — locating an informant who has been selling partisan positions to Nilfgaard, conducting raids on Nilfgaardian checkpoints, assisting with specific operations that matter to the resistance. These quests establish Roche's current situation and his priorities.

Roche is not trying to liberate Temeria through force — he knows that's impossible with the numbers he has. He is trying to maintain a presence, keep Temerian identity alive, make the occupation costly enough that Nilfgaard finds value in negotiation. He is playing a long game from a weak position, and he knows it, and he plays it anyway. [game_w3]

## Skellige and Beyond

Roche does not travel to Skellige. He stays in Velen, maintaining the partisan operation. News from Skellige and Novigrad reaches him; he monitors the larger political situation because his long game requires understanding the board.

[spoiler_tier: kaer_morhen]
## Kaer Morhen Events

Roche does not participate in the Battle of Kaer Morhen. His operations are in Velen and Novigrad's political vicinity; the Wild Hunt battle is above the scale his resistance forces can engage.

His contribution to the larger war comes in the political arc — specifically the Radovid assassination — rather than the Wild Hunt arc.

## Endings and Resolution

**"Reason of State" — Roche's critical role:**

The conspiracy to assassinate Radovid involves Roche, Ves, and Thaler working together with Geralt's assistance to identify and exploit an opportunity. Radovid is lured to a specific location — the Novigrad docks or similar — and killed. The specifics of the assassination (Roche or one of his people delivers the kill) are straightforward once the setup is complete.

The immediate aftermath is the problem: Dijkstra moves to eliminate Roche and Ves (seeing Temerian loyalists as an obstacle to his Redanian power consolidation). Geralt must choose whether to intervene.

**If Geralt helps Roche:**
Roche and Ves survive. Dijkstra is defeated (killed or driven off). The Temerian resistance has leverage in the post-Radovid chaos. Through negotiations they cannot fully control, Temeria gets a deal: autonomous status as a vassal kingdom under Nilfgaard, with internal self-governance and Temerian identity preserved. Nilfgaard technically wins the war but Temeria exists as something.

This is what Roche has been fighting for — not liberation (impossible) but survival with identity intact. He gets it. It is not clean. It is also real. [game_w3]

**If Geralt doesn't help Roche:**
Dijkstra kills Roche and Ves. The Temerian resistance ends. Whatever Temeria was, it is now definitively gone. [game_w3]
