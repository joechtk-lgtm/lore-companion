# Eskel

## Overview

Eskel is Geralt's oldest friend among the Wolf School witchers — they grew up at Kaer Morhen together, trained under the same instructors, and have known each other for the better part of a century. He is steady, competent, and quietly warm in the particular understated way of someone who doesn't need recognition to know what he's worth. He has a facial scar from a kikimora attack when he was young — one of those early contract near-misses that leaves a mark. He is less famous than Geralt, which suits him; fame brings attention, and attention is mostly a complication for professional monster hunters.

## Key Facts

- **School:** Wolf School (Kaer Morhen) [game_w3]
- **Age:** Approximately contemporary with Geralt — decades of witchery behind him [game_w3]
- **Scar:** A significant facial scar from a kikimora, received early in his career [game_w3]
- **Personality:** Steady, dry humor, thoughtful before speaking, reliable [game_w3]
- **Craft skills:** Eskel has maintained practical skills at Kaer Morhen beyond pure witchery — he works the Witcher's Forge, maintains equipment. [game_w3]
- **Contract work:** Takes contracts across a wide region, winters at Kaer Morhen. [game_w3]

## Relationships

- **Geralt:** Oldest friend. The relationship is comfortable in the way of people who have been through the same things and don't need to explain them. They don't always agree but they don't need to. [game_w3]
- **Lambert:** Witcher-brother with more patience for Lambert's manner than most people can sustain. Eskel has the steadiness to weather Lambert's sharp edges without it becoming a fight every time. [game_w3]
- **Vesemir:** Deep respect. Vesemir trained him. He takes the senior witcher's judgment seriously in a way Lambert does and Geralt does-but-argues-about. [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

Eskel is at Kaer Morhen during the Velen and Novigrad arcs. He and Lambert are keeping the keep functional between contracts and working on equipment maintenance.

When Geralt brings Uma — the cursed, monstrous figure that turns out to be Avallac'h — to Kaer Morhen, Eskel helps evaluate and prepare for the restoration ritual. He has practical knowledge of what Kaer Morhen's alchemical infrastructure can support and what's available. He's not a mage, but a century of working alongside magical tools and preparations gives him practical familiarity. [game_w3]

[spoiler_tier: kaer_morhen]
## Kaer Morhen Events

Eskel coordinates elements of the Battle of Kaer Morhen defense — specifically the wall positions and the coordination between the defenders. He is a steady tactical presence, not given to panicking, which matters when the Wild Hunt is attacking from multiple directions.

He survives the battle. When the Hunt retreats after Ciri's power eruption, Eskel is among those who gathers the survivors. Vesemir's death hits him — they were trained by the same man. He processes it like he processes most things: by being present and doing what needs to be done.

**The snowball fight:** The brief moment before the battle where Geralt, Ciri, Eskel, Lambert, and Vesemir have a snowball fight in Kaer Morhen's courtyard. Eskel participates. It is the right note — the last good moment before everything gets hard. [game_w3]

## Endings and Resolution

Eskel survives the events of Witcher 3. He continues his work.

He is not among the characters with major ending variations — his story does not depend on Geralt's choices the way Ciri's or Lambert's (with Keira) does. He is simply still there, still steady, still doing the work. This is its own kind of resolution for a character who never needed more than that. [game_w3]
