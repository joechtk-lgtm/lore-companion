# Philippa Eilhart

## Overview

Philippa Eilhart is one of the most politically dangerous women in the Northern Kingdoms and one of the most powerful sorceresses alive. She served as the principal intelligence adviser to two kings of Redania — Vizimir II and, effectively, Radovid V before his final break with the Lodge — and was the primary architect of the Lodge of Sorceresses. She is brilliant, ruthless, and entirely self-aware about what she is: a political operator who happens to also be a sorceress rather than the reverse. She has murdered, manipulated, and maneuvered her way through decades of Northern politics. She is also the person who, when the mages of the world most needed an organization, built one. The two things coexist.

## Key Facts

- **Full name:** Philippa Eilhart [books] [game_w3]
- **Profession:** Sorceress, political adviser, intelligence operative, co-founder of the Lodge of Sorceresses [books] [game_w3]
- **Affiliation:** Formerly: Brotherhood of Sorcerers, Royal Intelligence Service of Redania (de facto head). Currently: Lodge of Sorceresses (co-founder). [books] [game_w3]
- **Age:** Over a century old. Appears middle-aged due to magical rejuvenation. [books]
- **Appearance:** Dark hair, typically dressed for political occasions — she presents herself as a woman of authority in every room she enters. Her owl form is a golden owl. [books] [game_w3]
- **Power:** Extremely high. One of the most capable mages of the era. Her combat ability is matched by her intelligence work. [books] [game_w3]
- **Polymorph ability:** Philippa can transform into a golden owl at will — a rare ability. This is not a curse imposed by Radovid; she had this ability. Radovid's curse is specifically that she is *trapped* in owl form, unable to change back without magical intervention. [game_w3]
- **Politics:** Redanian — she served Redania above all, but always with her own agenda running parallel. She does not serve; she allies. [books] [game_w3]
- **The Lodge:** She co-founded the Lodge with Margarita Laux-Antille after the Thanedd Coup. Her vision for the Lodge was always more political than Margarita's — she wanted it to be a power capable of directing Northern policy, not merely surviving it. [books] [game_w3]

## Relationships

- **Sigismund Dijkstra:** Former counterpart and rival in Redanian intelligence. They are both very good at the same things. They respect each other professionally. They do not trust each other personally. After Radovid's increasing instability made Redanian service untenable, both went their separate ways. [books] [game_w3]
- **Yennefer:** Mutual professional respect with an undercurrent of competition. Philippa sees Yennefer as an equal — one of very few people she credits with full intelligence — and therefore watches her carefully. [books] [game_w3]
- **Radovid V:** Former ally turned mortal enemy. Philippa served Radovid's interests until his paranoia led him to turn on the Lodge specifically. He had her eyes put out and transformed her. She wants him dead with the controlled, specific fury of someone who does not waste anger. [game_w3]
- **King Vizimir II:** The previous Redanian king, whom Philippa advised for years. Vizimir was assassinated — and Philippa was implicated, though the full truth is complicated. The question of whether she arranged or permitted his death to shape what came next is one the books address obliquely. [books]
- **Margarita Laux-Antille:** Lodge co-founder. Margarita is the more idealistic of the two founders — a headmistress, invested in Aretuza, genuinely committed to mage education as a value. Philippa is the operator. They complement each other and compete on institutional vision. [books] [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

By the time Geralt arrives in Novigrad, Philippa is trapped in owl form — Radovid had her eyes gouged out and her polymorphing ability warped so she cannot reassume human form on her own. She is hiding in Novigrad's slums, surviving.

**Finding Philippa:** The quest chain to locate and restore Philippa is part of the secondary Lodge questline. Geralt can work with Dijkstra and others to track her down. She is not easy to find — being trapped as an owl in a city hostile to mages, with witch hunters actively searching for her, means she has gone very deep into hiding.

**Restoring her form:** Returning Philippa to human form requires magical components and the assistance of at least one other qualified mage. If Margarita Laux-Antille has been freed from Oxenfurt (a separate quest), she can assist in the ritual. The restoration is possible and worth doing if Geralt wants the most capable possible alliance for Kaer Morhen.

**After restoration:** Philippa is pragmatically grateful — she acknowledges what Geralt did and will cooperate for the Battle of Kaer Morhen. She does not become friendly. She is not the type. She also immediately begins planning what comes after the battle, which is entirely in character. [game_w3]

**The Novigrad political situation:** Philippa was, before her capture, one of the architects of the anti-Radovid political maneuvering that the Lodge attempted. Her capture and transformation was Radovid's response to the Lodge's influence. The witch hunter purges are partly aimed at the Lodge specifically — at erasing its members as a political network. Philippa's restoration is not merely rescue; it is rebuilding the Lodge's capacity. [game_w3]

## Skellige and Beyond

Philippa does not travel to Skellige. After her restoration in Novigrad, she focuses on the preparation for whatever comes next — the Kaer Morhen alliance and the post-battle political landscape. Her presence on Skellige would be counterproductive; she is still hunted by Radovid's agents, and Skellige is too close to Northern Kingdom politics to be comfortable. [game_w3]

[spoiler_tier: kaer_morhen]
## Kaer Morhen Events

If Geralt successfully restored Philippa, she participates in the Battle of Kaer Morhen as one of the mages. Her contribution is substantial — she is extremely powerful and has extensive combat experience from decades of serving intelligence operations that occasionally required direct action.

At the strategy table, she is one of the more decisive voices. She assesses situations, cuts through sentiment, and arrives at conclusions efficiently. The other mages may find this difficult. It is effective. [game_w3]

After Vesemir's death, Philippa's response is professional — she pivots immediately to what comes next. This is not callousness; it is how she handles everything. Grief is something she does privately and efficiently.

## Endings and Resolution

**If Radovid is assassinated:**
Philippa is likely involved in the planning and execution of the assassination. The quest "Reason of State" involves Roche, Thaler, and others, with Philippa's role depending on what Geralt has done. If she is restored and has been working toward this for the whole game, she is part of the faction that orchestrates Radovid's death.

**After Radovid's death:**
Dijkstra attempts to seize power for himself in the aftermath. If he succeeds (because Geralt does not intervene to protect Roche and Ves), Philippa may or may not survive Dijkstra's consolidation — they were colleagues but he would not hesitate to eliminate a rival. If the Temerian resistance succeeds (Geralt helps Roche), the political landscape is different.

**Philippa's likely future:**
If she survives, she will continue doing what she does: operating, influencing, building something. Whether it is a reformed Lodge, a new position in the post-war political landscape, or something else is left open. She is too capable to simply retire. [game_w3]

## DLC Content

Philippa does not appear in Hearts of Stone or Blood and Wine. The DLC conflicts — O'Dimm's supernatural deal-making, Toussaint's vampire crisis — are outside her domain of interest and expertise. She operates in politics. Those are different kinds of problems.
