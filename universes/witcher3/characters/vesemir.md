# Vesemir

## Overview

Vesemir is the oldest surviving witcher, the senior instructor of the Wolf School, and the closest thing to a father that Geralt has ever had. He has lived through the massacre of Kaer Morhen's witchers a generation ago — an attack by mobs stirred up by anti-witcher sentiment that killed most of the school's members and destroyed the formulas for creating new witchers. He survived by being away from the keep that night. He has spent decades since maintaining Kaer Morhen as a winter refuge for the remaining Wolf School witchers, training the handful of young men who still came to the school (including Geralt, Lambert, and Eskel), and doing what he has always done: enduring. He is old — ancient by human standards, though witchers do not show age like humans — and he is tough and practical and carries several lifetimes of grief without much outward sign. He is also, in ways that matter more than he lets on, deeply committed to the people in his care.

## Key Facts

- **School:** Wolf School (founder-adjacent; the most senior remaining witcher of the school) [game_w3] [books]
- **Age:** Oldest living witcher. Exact age unknown — centuries old, having undergone witcher mutations long before the massacre of Kaer Morhen's training staff. [game_w3]
- **Appearance:** White hair and beard, deeply lined face, yellow witcher eyes. Looks old and moves like something that has survived everything. [game_w3]
- **Role:** Instructor, administrator of Kaer Morhen, the witchers' de facto patriarch. [game_w3]
- **Combat:** Still highly capable. Decades of experience and refinement have made his fighting style economical and extremely effective despite age. [game_w3]
- **The Massacre:** A generation before Witcher 3, organized mobs attacked Kaer Morhen, killing most of the school's witchers and students, destroying the equipment and knowledge needed to create new witchers. Vesemir was not at the keep when it happened. He returned to find the dead. He has kept the keep functioning since. [game_w3] [books]
- **The Trial of the Grasses:** Vesemir administered the Trial of the Grasses — the dangerous mutagen process that makes a witcher — to the last generation of Wolf School witchers, including Geralt. He takes this responsibility seriously. [books]
- **Known students:** Geralt of Rivia, Lambert, Eskel, and by extension Ciri (trained in combat, not mutations). [game_w3]

## Relationships

- **Geralt:** Mentor and father-figure. The relationship is not soft — Vesemir is not a soft man — but it is genuine. Geralt trusts Vesemir's judgment more than almost anyone. [game_w3] [books]
- **Ciri:** He made her a wolf medallion at Kaer Morhen. He treats her as belonging to the school — to the family. He is proud of her in the way he is proud of any student who turns out to be genuinely extraordinary. [game_w3]
- **Lambert:** A difficult student who grew into a capable witcher. Their relationship has friction but Vesemir invested in Lambert's formation regardless. [game_w3]
- **Eskel:** The quieter, steadier student. Vesemir trusts Eskel's practicality. [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

Vesemir is at Kaer Morhen during the Velen and Novigrad portions of the game — maintaining the keep, waiting, and receiving witchers who have wintered there over the years. He participates in the early portions of the plot when Geralt brings Uma (the cursed Avallac'h) to Kaer Morhen for the restoration ritual.

His practical knowledge of Kaer Morhen's resources, including the keep's alchemical equipment, is essential to restoring Uma. He understands what Geralt is building toward and supports it without needing explanation. [game_w3]

[spoiler_tier: skellige]
## Skellige and Beyond

Vesemir remains at Kaer Morhen. He is organizing and preparing for what he understands is coming. He knows the Wild Hunt is real and that it will come for Ciri — has always known, in the way experienced people understand threats before they arrive.

[spoiler_tier: kaer_morhen]
## Kaer Morhen Events

The Battle of Kaer Morhen is Vesemir's final chapter.

When the Wild Hunt attacks the keep, Vesemir fights. He is extremely effective — a century of witcher experience and a lifetime of fighting anything that came through those gates. But the Wild Hunt is not a monster hunt. They are organized, numerous, and led by Imlerith, one of Eredin's generals.

The moment: Imlerith gets past the defenders and reaches Ciri. She is cornered. Vesemir physically grabs Imlerith — closes with him, holds him — to give Ciri the seconds she needs to run.

Imlerith kills him. Breaks his neck.

The wolf medallion Vesemir had given Ciri drops from her. Ciri's grief erupts as Elder Blood power — the explosion that drives the Wild Hunt back is fueled by what losing Vesemir does to her.

**The funeral:**
Vesemir is burned on a pyre in the Kaer Morhen courtyard. Geralt, Ciri, Yennefer, and the others are present. The scene is quiet and properly weighted. For long-time Witcher readers and players who have spent time with Vesemir across multiple games, this is a significant loss. [game_w3]

[spoiler_tier: main_complete]
## Endings and Resolution

Vesemir does not survive the main game. His death is fixed. What varies is how well-attended his funeral is based on how many allies Geralt successfully recruited for the battle.

His influence continues: the medallion he gave Ciri is what she wears when she faces the White Frost. The sword techniques he taught Geralt are present in every fight. The keep he maintained is where the final alliance was forged. He built something that outlasted him.

[spoiler_tier: everything]
## DLC Content

Vesemir does not appear in Hearts of Stone or Blood and Wine (set after his death). His absence is felt most in Blood and Wine, where Geralt's vineyard retirement contrasts with the loss of the only true home he ever had at Kaer Morhen — which Vesemir kept for him for decades.

In "The Witcher: Nightmare of the Wolf" (Netflix animated film), a version of Vesemir's earlier life and the massacre at Kaer Morhen is depicted. Canon status relative to the games is unclear; treat as a parallel account. [canon_source: netflix]
