# Eredin Bréacc Glas

## Overview

Eredin Bréacc Glas is the King of the Wild Hunt, commander of the Aen Elle's spectral cavalry, and the primary antagonist of The Witcher 3. He is an Aen Elle elf — one of the elves of Tir ná Lia, the parallel world the Aen Elle inhabit — and formerly a general in the service of King Auberon Muircetach, the Aen Elle king. He is tactical, brutal, and utterly focused on a single objective: securing Ciri and her Elder Blood power to save his people from the White Frost. Everything he does follows from this with cold, precise logic. He is not a monster who kills for pleasure; he is a conqueror who kills because it is the most efficient means to an end, and because everything is a means to an end.

## Key Facts

- **Full name:** Eredin Bréacc Glas [game_w3] [books]
- **Title:** King of the Wild Hunt, Commander of the Dearg Ruadhri (Red Riders) [game_w3] [books]
- **Race:** Aen Elle (elves of the parallel world Tir ná Lia) [books] [game_w3]
- **Appearance:** Red armor, skull-like helmet or face-covering, ethereal and terrifying in appearance. The "spectral" quality of the Wild Hunt comes partly from how they appear in this world — alien and wrong-looking, riding through weather they conjure. [game_w3]
- **The Wild Hunt's composition:** The Aen Elle's elite cavalry, disciplined soldiers rather than mindless spirits. The spectral terror is partly deliberate intimidation tactic. [books] [game_w3]
- **Origin of the Wild Hunt myth:** In the Northern Kingdoms, the Wild Hunt is a folk horror — a procession of spectral riders who appear before disasters and abduct the living. The actual Aen Elle cavalry is the source of this myth, interpreted through fear by people who could not understand what they were seeing. [books] [game_w3]
- **How he became King:** Eredin poisoned King Auberon Muircetach to take the throne. Avallac'h knows this. Eredin knows Avallac'h knows this. Their mutual distrust and shared interests make for a complicated relationship. [game_w3]
- **Generals:** Imlerith, Caranthir, and Ge'els are his primary subordinates. [game_w3]
  - **Imlerith** — brutal, physically powerful, leads from the front, killed by Geralt at Bald Mountain
  - **Caranthir** — the navigator, manipulates the magical pathways between worlds, killed by Ciri (or Geralt)
  - **Ge'els** — more philosophical; can be turned against Eredin through Avallac'h's plan

## Relationships

- **Ciri:** His objective, not a person to him. He needs her Elder Blood power to save his world. He wants her alive and controllable, not dead. [game_w3]
- **Avallac'h:** Mutual distrust, forced cooperation. Avallac'h served the previous king (Auberon) and knows Eredin killed him. Eredin needs Avallac'h's knowledge of Elder Blood and world-traversal. Both parties are aware the alliance is provisional. [game_w3]
- **Auberon Muircetach (deceased):** The previous Aen Elle king, who Eredin poisoned. Auberon was obsessed with Ciri during her captivity in Tir ná Lia — an obsession that blended the political need for an Elder Blood heir with something more personal, more tortured. Eredin eliminated him when the obsession seemed to be preventing practical action. [game_w3] [books]
- **Ge'els:** Eredin's advisor and a man with doubts. Ge'els can be reached through Avallac'h and shown the truth of how Auberon died — which undermines his loyalty to Eredin and weakens the Hunt's leadership cohesion. [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

The Wild Hunt's presence in the Northern Kingdoms is background horror during the Velen and Novigrad arcs. The Hunt has been pursuing Ciri across worlds for years. By the time Witcher 3 begins, they have come close but not caught her. Their raids — the abductions of people, the spectral cavalry crossing the sky — are the myth that Northern peasants know.

Eredin does not appear directly in the Velen/Novigrad sections. His agents and soldiers do. The sense of being hunted — the reason Ciri kept moving — is established throughout these chapters as the threat that shaped everything before the game's start. [game_w3]

[spoiler_tier: skellige]
## Skellige and Beyond

Eredin leads the Wild Hunt's attack on Kaer Trolde when Ciri is sheltered there with Crach an Craite's family. The assault is direct and devastating — the Hunt tears through Skellige's defenses to reach Ciri. She escapes with Avallac'h's help.

Eredin is present during the Skellige events but does not directly confront Geralt at this stage. The confrontation is still building. He is patient because he has been patient for years, and patience has historically worked: Ciri cannot run forever. [game_w3]

[spoiler_tier: kaer_morhen]
## Kaer Morhen Events

Eredin does not lead the Kaer Morhen attack personally — he sends Imlerith. This is tactically sound: the keep is well-defended and Eredin does not risk himself unnecessarily. Imlerith is powerful enough for the task and Eredin has other preparations to make.

When Imlerith is killed by Geralt on Bald Mountain, Eredin loses one of his three generals. This matters. The Wild Hunt is not infinitely resourceful; losing Imlerith changes the math for the final confrontation. [game_w3]

**Ge'els:**
Through Avallac'h's plan, Geralt can reach Ge'els in Tir ná Lia — Eredin's philosophical advisor — and show him evidence of how Eredin took the throne. Ge'els's loyalty to Eredin cracks. He does not actively betray Eredin but removes his active support. This weakens the Hunt's unified command structure. [game_w3]

## Endings and Resolution

**The final confrontation:**
Eredin faces Geralt after the alliance storms through Tir ná Lia and the Aen Elle's defenses are broken. The fight is Geralt's hardest in the game — Eredin uses Caranthir's death to steal enough energy to fight effectively even wounded. He uses a portaling ability during the fight, requiring Geralt to track and anticipate his movements rather than simply staying in melee range.

Eredin dies. His last words (if Geralt engages him rather than simply finishing him) are revealing: he accuses Avallac'h of using both of them — using the Wild Hunt's pursuit of Ciri as a piece in Avallac'h's own longer plan. He may not be wrong. Whether Avallac'h genuinely worked for Ciri's benefit or manipulated everything from the beginning is left ambiguous. Eredin's perspective on this is the last testimony before it closes.

**Aftermath:**
With Eredin dead and his generals dead or removed, the Wild Hunt dissolves as a coherent force. The Aen Elle's threat to the Continent is ended. Whether they still face the White Frost in their own world is not addressed. Ge'els was the most humane among them and the most likely to try a different approach. [game_w3]

## DLC Content

Eredin does not appear in Hearts of Stone or Blood and Wine. The threat of the Wild Hunt is resolved in the main game.

The DLC antagonists — Gaunter O'Dimm and the vampires of Toussaint — represent entirely different categories of supernatural danger, both of which are in some respects more purely threatening than Eredin, whose motivations were ultimately comprehensible even if his methods were not.
