# Sigismund Dijkstra

## Overview

Sigismund Dijkstra is the former head of Redanian intelligence — the most effective spymaster in the Northern Kingdoms, possibly on the Continent — and one of the game's most morally complex secondary figures. He is large, physically imposing, deliberately non-threatening in presentation (rumpled, cheerful, apparently casual), and one of the most dangerous people Geralt will meet in Novigrad. He appears as Sigi Reuven, a crime boss, when Geralt first encounters him — a cover so thoroughly maintained that it functions as an identity. He and Geralt have history: Geralt broke both his legs in a confrontation before the game's events (the Thanedd incident, which Geralt was involved in). Dijkstra has not forgotten this. He is also a professional who separates what he wants from what is useful, most of the time.

## Key Facts

- **Full name:** Sigismund Dijkstra [books] [game_w3]
- **Cover identity:** Sigi Reuven, Novigrad crime boss [game_w3]
- **Former role:** Head of Redanian intelligence, serving King Vizimir II and technically Radovid V [books] [game_w3]
- **Current role:** Independent operator, maintaining influence through the Novigrad criminal network [game_w3]
- **Appearance:** Very tall and heavyset — physically imposing in a way that he contrasts with his genial manner. Looks like a prosperous merchant. [game_w3] [books]
- **Intelligence:** Exceptional. One of the few characters in the game who is genuinely always a step ahead of most people in a room. Geralt is one of a small number of people he treats as a genuine equal in terms of practical capability. [books] [game_w3]
- **The broken legs:** Dijkstra's confrontation with Geralt during the Thanedd Coup left him with broken legs. He recovered fully. He brings it up. [game_w3] [books]
- **Politics:** Redanian nationalist first. He does not love Radovid — he has no illusions about Radovid's cruelty and increasing instability — but he believes in Redanian supremacy in the North. His goal is not to serve Radovid but to position Redania to emerge from the war as the dominant Northern power. [books] [game_w3]
- **The bathhouse:** Dijkstra operates his crime network out of a bathhouse — the Passiflora or its equivalent in Novigrad. The bathhouse as a meeting location is both cover and practical (people come and go, weapons are difficult to hide, conversations can be private). [game_w3]

## Relationships

- **Geralt:** Complicated mutual professional respect. Dijkstra uses Geralt when useful and would eliminate him if Geralt became an obstacle. He also respects Geralt's capability in a way he respects very few people. The broken-leg incident is a recurring note in their dynamic — Dijkstra doesn't let it become a vendetta because that would be unprofessional, but he doesn't let it go either. [game_w3] [books]
- **Philippa Eilhart:** Former colleagues in Redanian intelligence. They worked together, they competed, they respected each other. Dijkstra's decision to let Radovid destroy Philippa rather than protect her was purely pragmatic: she had become too expensive to defend. She knows this. [books] [game_w3]
- **Vernon Roche:** Temerian resistance leader. Mutual antagonism — Roche is Temerian and Dijkstra is Redanian, which puts them on opposite sides of the war's aftermath. Dijkstra would sacrifice Roche without a moment's hesitation if it served Redanian interests. [game_w3]
- **Radovid V:** Served Radovid's father (Vizimir) loyally. Radovid V has become too unstable to work with and too dangerous to oppose openly. Dijkstra's position is: get useful distance, wait for the right moment, plan for after. [books] [game_w3]
- **Thaler:** A lower-level Temerian spy who works in Novigrad. One of the people in the "Reason of State" conspiracy. Dijkstra's relationship with him is professional utility. [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

Geralt encounters Dijkstra in Novigrad as Sigi Reuven — one of the four crime bosses controlling Novigrad's underworld. The encounter is necessary: Dijkstra has information about Dandelion and about what happened to Ciri in the city.

**The bathhouse meeting:** When Geralt first properly meets Dijkstra in the game, Dijkstra is in his bathhouse, surrounded by his network and apparently relaxed. He knows Geralt immediately. The dynamic of the scene — two capable people who have history, speaking carefully around what they actually want — is one of the better character interactions in the Novigrad arc.

**The Dandelion investigation:** Dijkstra is a key source of information for tracking down Dandelion. He has intelligence (literally — he is an intelligence officer operating a criminal network) on what happened in the city. His cooperation is not free and not unconditional. He has his own agenda and will share information when it serves him to do so.

**The crime boss coalition:** Dijkstra, Francis Bedlam (the King of Beggars), Whoreson Junior, and Count Reuven's associates make up the Novigrad power structure Geralt must navigate. Whoreson Junior is the immediate problem; he held Ciri and held Dandelion and is the most violently unhinged of the four. Dijkstra is the most politically dangerous. Bedlam is the most humane. [game_w3]

**His intelligence work:** Even operating as a crime boss, Dijkstra is running intelligence operations. He knows what happens in Novigrad — who comes and goes, what witch hunters are planning, what Eternal Fire's operatives are doing. This makes him valuable and dangerous simultaneously. [game_w3]

## Skellige and Beyond

Dijkstra does not travel to Skellige. He operates from Novigrad's infrastructure and his intelligence network. News from Skellige reaches him — he monitors the succession crisis from a distance — but his personal presence is in the city.

[spoiler_tier: kaer_morhen]
## Kaer Morhen Events

Dijkstra does not participate in the Battle of Kaer Morhen. He is not fighting for Geralt's cause; he is operating on his own timeline toward his own goals. The Wild Hunt's threat to Ciri is real but it does not directly intersect with what Dijkstra cares about: post-war Redanian political dominance.

## Endings and Resolution

**"Reason of State" quest — The critical Dijkstra moment:**

This is the quest where Dijkstra's true intentions emerge. The setup: a conspiracy to assassinate Radovid, organized by Roche, Ves, and Thaler, with Geralt's involvement. Dijkstra is part of the conspiracy — he wants Radovid dead because Radovid is a liability and an increasingly dangerous one.

The assassination succeeds. Radovid is killed.

Immediately after, Dijkstra reveals his actual next step: he intends to kill Roche, Ves, and Thaler. Not because they are threats to him personally but because they represent a power structure (the Temerian resistance) that would complicate Redanian dominance. With Radovid gone and the Temerian loyalists also gone, Dijkstra can position himself to control what Redania becomes.

**The choice for Geralt:**
- Let Dijkstra proceed: Roche and Ves die. Dijkstra takes power. Nilfgaard wins the war (the Temerian compromise deal falls through without the resistance to pressure it). Dijkstra rules. The North doesn't fall entirely — Dijkstra is capable — but it's a worse outcome for the people Geralt cares about and a worse political outcome overall.
- Intervene: Fight Dijkstra and his men. Save Roche and Ves. Dijkstra is defeated — killed or driven off depending on choices. The Temerian resistance survives and negotiates the autonomy deal with Nilfgaard.

**If Dijkstra succeeds:**
He does what he said he would — consolidates Redanian power and positions himself as the dominant force in the post-war North. He is competent and Redania benefits. It is not a catastrophe. It is just not what Geralt's allies would have chosen.

**If Dijkstra is stopped:**
He is either killed or he escapes and disappears. His intelligence network in Novigrad presumably persists or dissolves. He is too capable to simply vanish, but without institutional backing, his power is reduced. [game_w3]
