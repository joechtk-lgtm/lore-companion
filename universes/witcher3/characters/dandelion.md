# Dandelion (Julian Alfred Pankratz, Viscount de Lettenhove)

## Overview

Dandelion — born Julian Alfred Pankratz, holding the title Viscount de Lettenhove though he rarely uses it — is a poet, bard, lute-player, and the most famous traveling entertainer in the Northern Kingdoms. He is also Geralt of Rivia's best friend, which is a relationship that says something interesting about both of them. He has been documenting Geralt's adventures in verse and ballad for over thirty years, embellishing freely, taking liberties with facts, and producing work that is simultaneously popular, inaccurate, and occasionally quite good. He is vain, lecherous, easily frightened, and one of the most loyal people Geralt has ever met — though Geralt would phrase this differently if asked.

## Key Facts

- **Full name:** Julian Alfred Pankratz, Viscount de Lettenhove [books] [game_w3]
- **Known as:** Dandelion in the games and English translations; Jaskier (Polish: "buttercup") in the original Polish and the Netflix series. [books] [game_w3]
- **Profession:** Bard, poet, lute player, sometime spy, reluctant adventurer. [books] [game_w3]
- **Education:** Oxenfurt Academy — the most prestigious educational institution in the Northern Kingdoms. He studied there legitimately and was talented enough to be considered genuinely skilled, not merely famous. [books] [game_w3]
- **Appearance:** Well-dressed at all times (or attempting to be). Keeps his clothes finer than his circumstances usually warrant. Brown hair, unremarkable features that he presents with maximum confidence. [books] [game_w3]
- **Skills:** Composing and performing music, poetry in multiple Northern Kingdom languages, navigation of courtly and underworld social environments, surviving proximity to Geralt without dying (this is harder than it sounds). Speaks fluent Elven. [books] [game_w3]
- **The Ballads:** Dandelion's songs about Geralt have been heard across the Continent. They are largely responsible for Geralt's popular reputation — good and bad. "Toss a Coin to Your Witcher" is not one of the books' ballads but is the Netflix version. Book ballads include "The Song of the Lark" and other compositions that Geralt finds embarrassing. [books]
- **Chronicle:** Dandelion wrote a chronicle of his adventures with Geralt titled "A Half Century of Poetry" — his memoirs, essentially. They inform much of the backstory the games draw on. [books]

## Relationships

- **Geralt of Rivia:** Best friend. Has been for thirty-plus years. Geralt saves him from his own bad decisions constantly. Dandelion documents Geralt's life in verse with enthusiastic creative license. The friendship is genuine, undemonstrative on Geralt's end, and probably the most stable long-term relationship either of them has had. [books] [game_w3]
- **Yennefer:** Mutual exasperation, deep down a grudging respect. Yennefer finds him frivolous; he finds her terrifying; both of them would act to protect the other because of Geralt. [books] [game_w3]
- **Priscilla (Dudu):** In the Novigrad portion of Witcher 3, Dandelion is romantically involved with Priscilla, a talented young singer at the Rosemary and Thyme. Priscilla is also a friend of Triss. [game_w3]
- **Dudu (Doregray of Gueste):** A doppler — a shapeshifter — who has been one of Dandelion's most reliable associates in Novigrad. Dudu can become anyone and has used this to protect both Dandelion and Ciri. [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

In Witcher 3, Dandelion is the linchpin of the Novigrad investigation. By the time Geralt arrives in the city, Dandelion has gone missing — and finding him is one of the main threads of the Novigrad arc.

Dandelion had been operating the Rosemary and Thyme, a cabaret and entertainment venue in Novigrad, as a cover that allowed him to help people move through the city — including Ciri. He was also involved with Priscilla and connected to the city's underground networks through his relationship with Dudu the doppler and acquaintances like Zoltan Chivay.

The reason for his disappearance: he was taken by Whoreson Junior's men during the process of Whoreson Junior trying to capture Ciri. Dandelion was collateral — a useful piece to hold. He ends up in Whoreson Junior's arena, not the most comfortable situation for a bard.

**The quest "Get Junior":** Geralt investigates the Novigrad underworld to find both what happened to Ciri and where Dandelion is. The trail goes through the King of Beggars (Francis Bedlam), Sigi Reuven (Dijkstra), Zoltan, and eventually into Whoreson Junior's operations directly. Geralt finds Dandelion in Junior's fighting pit. He is unharmed but very glad to see Geralt.

Dandelion is also essential to understanding Ciri's movements in Novigrad: he is one of the few people who saw her and helped her directly before she left. His testimony fills in critical pieces of the puzzle.

After being rescued, Dandelion reestablishes the Rosemary and Thyme as a functioning cabaret. He stages productions, one of which (for Priscilla) becomes a love ballad that causes a small crisis when Priscilla is attacked — the "Carnal Sins" secondary quest branching from the main Novigrad investigations.

## Skellige and Beyond

Dandelion does not travel to Skellige. He remains in Novigrad running the Rosemary and Thyme, surviving. His role in the Skellige and later arcs is indirect — he is a fixed point Geralt can return to in Novigrad for information, context, and the occasional drink.

[spoiler_tier: kaer_morhen]
## Kaer Morhen Events

Dandelion does not participate in the Battle of Kaer Morhen. He is not a fighter. He is wisely in Novigrad during this period.

[spoiler_tier: main_complete]
## Endings and Resolution

Dandelion survives the events of Witcher 3 regardless of choices. He continues performing, writing, and existing in his particular way. If Ciri survives and becomes a witcher, he is presumably writing ballads about her as well.

In the epilogue framing device (if Geralt's ending involves Toussaint), Dandelion is the narrator of the game's story — his chronicle of Geralt's life is the implied framing for much of what the player experienced. The "A Half Century of Poetry" framing device gives Dandelion a meaningful structural role even when he is not present in a scene. [game_w3]

## Netflix Divergence
[canon_source: netflix]

In the Netflix series, the character is named Jaskier (the original Polish name; Dandelion is an English translation used in the games). Joey Batey's performance was critically well-received — energetic, funny, and occasionally genuinely sympathetic. The song "Toss a Coin to Your Witcher" became the series' cultural moment. In terms of the books and games, Jaskier/Dandelion's character is broadly recognizable — vain, loyal, the bard who documents a witcher — though specific events and his timeline with Geralt differ between the adaptations.
