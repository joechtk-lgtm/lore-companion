# Avallac'h (Crevan Espane aep Caomhan Macha)

## Overview

Avallac'h is an Aen Elle elf — one of the elves of the parallel world Tir ná Lia — and a Sage of extraordinary knowledge and age. He is the most sophisticated person in Witcher 3 and possibly the most dangerous, not in the combat sense but in the sense that he has been planning for centuries and has already accounted for most of the other players' responses. He was Lara Dorren's betrothed — the Aen Elle elf she left when she chose the human mage Cregennan of Lod. He has spent the intervening centuries studying Elder Blood, the White Frost, and the dimensional mathematics that connect them, while nursing something that is not quite grief and not quite obsession about what Lara chose and what her descendants became. His interest in Ciri is deeply ambiguous: he genuinely works to protect her survival and simultaneously treats her as a subject in his research and an instrument for ends she did not consent to. He is not evil. He is something more uncomfortable than that.

## Relationships

- **Ciri:** He is her protector, research subject, surrogate for Lara Dorren's memory, and the person who knows most precisely what she can do. These roles are not compatible and he does not resolve them cleanly. He genuinely wants her to survive. He also designed a situation in which her choices are constrained toward an outcome he determined. [game_w3] [books]
- **Lara Dorren (deceased):** The elven Sage whose choices made everything that followed possible. Avallac'h's relationship with her memory is the core of who he is. [books]
- **Eredin Bréacc Glas:** Former colleague, now mutual distrust. Eredin knows Avallac'h operates independently of the Wild Hunt's interests. Avallac'h knows Eredin killed Auberon. Their forced cooperation during the events of Witcher 3 is conditional on shared immediate interests. [game_w3]
- **King Auberon Muircetach (deceased):** The Aen Elle king Eredin poisoned. Auberon had held Ciri captive in Tir ná Lia and developed an obsessive interest in her. Avallac'h served Auberon and was present during Ciri's captivity. [books] [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

The Uma connection: Uma is found at Crow's Perch in Velen, in the care of (or proximity to) the Nilfgaardian commander there. Uma is grotesque, half-mad, and wearing a crown-like deformity. Geralt, following Ciri's trail, identifies Uma as connected to her.

The reveal that Uma is Avallac'h, cursed by parties who wanted him out of the way, comes after Geralt brings Uma to Kaer Morhen for the restoration ritual. The ritual — researched by Yennefer using notes from Avallac'h's own laboratory — undoes the curse.

Avallac'h's laboratory on Skellige contains extensive research into Elder Blood, dimensional traversal mechanics, and the White Frost. Geralt and Yennefer investigate it during the Skellige arc and learn the full scope of what Avallac'h knows — which is considerably more than anyone else. [game_w3]

## Skellige and Beyond

Avallac'h has been protecting Ciri on Skellige after the Wild Hunt attack on Kaer Trolde. He has her hidden in his care by the time Geralt tracks them down. The meeting between Geralt and Avallac'h at this stage is tense: Avallac'h has information Geralt needs, has been protecting Ciri, and is also clearly operating from his own agenda rather than pure altruism.

Avallac'h explains the White Frost — its reality, its movement through the dimensional sphere-multiverse, its threat. He explains what Ciri's power is capable of. He explains that he has been working toward the moment when Ciri's power is sufficient to enter the Frost and act against it. He frames this as saving multiple worlds. He does not fully account for what this means for Ciri as a person rather than an instrument.

Geralt's distrust of Avallac'h is constant throughout their forced cooperation. This is appropriate. [game_w3]

## Kaer Morhen Events

Avallac'h, once restored from Uma's form, joins the planning for the final confrontation with the Wild Hunt. He is not a fighter — his value is knowledge and specific capabilities. He knows Tir ná Lia, knows the Hunt's structure, knows the dimensional mechanics needed to open a gate to their world.

He designs the plan: use the Sunstone to summon the Hunt to a battlefield in Skellige, engage and weaken them there, then open a gate to Tir ná Lia and take the fight to them directly. It is a militarily sound plan for the situation. It also requires Ciri to ultimately enter the White Frost alone, which was always his intention.

His interactions with the assembled allies at Kaer Morhen are cool and correct. He answers questions precisely. He does not offer more than asked. He makes the others uneasy, which he does not appear to mind. [game_w3]

## Endings and Resolution

**The assault on Tir ná Lia:**
Avallac'h opens the gate to his world. He participates in the approach. When the moment comes — when Ciri goes into the White Frost — he is present.

**Eredin's dying accusation:**
Eredin, as he dies, tells Geralt that Avallac'h used them all — manipulated the Wild Hunt's pursuit of Ciri, manipulated Geralt and the quest to find Ciri, orchestrated everything toward Ciri entering the White Frost. Whether this is true, or true in the sense Eredin means, or partially true — the game leaves this open. Avallac'h does not deny it in terms that fully settle the question.

**After the Frost:**
Avallac'h escapes consequences. This is consistent with who he is. He is too knowledgeable, too prepared, and too patient to be cornered by someone else's anger. He may return. He may not. The Aen Elle world's future — with Eredin dead, Ge'els potentially in charge, the White Frost potentially stopped — is left for other stories. [game_w3]

## DLC Content

Avallac'h does not appear in Hearts of Stone or Blood and Wine. The DLC antagonists (O'Dimm, Dettlaff) operate in entirely different registers. O'Dimm is the only entity in the game's DLC scope that is potentially in Avallac'h's category of ancient, prepared, and operating on a timescale most mortals can't perceive. They do not interact.
