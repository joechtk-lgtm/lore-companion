# Geralt of Rivia

## Overview

Geralt of Rivia is a witcher — a mutant professional monster hunter — and one of the last practitioners of his trade. The name "of Rivia" is an affectation; he is not from Rivia. His actual birthplace is unknown even to him. He was given to the witchers of Kaer Morhen by his mother Visenna, a druid and healer, as a child. He survived the Trial of the Grasses and then survived additional experimental mutations that most candidates did not — mutations that turned his hair white and his eyes the vertical-pupiled yellow of a cat. By the time of Witcher 3, he is over ninety years old, looks roughly forty, and is widely regarded as the finest witcher alive, which has done very little to improve his general outlook.

## Key Facts

- **Born:** Unknown location. Mother is Visenna, a druid-healer. Father unknown or disputed. [books]
- **School:** Wolf School, trained and based at Kaer Morhen, the fortified keep in the Blue Mountains. [books] [game_w3]
- **Age:** Over 90 years due to witcher slow-aging from mutations. Appears mid-forties. [books]
- **Signs used in combat:** Aard (telekinetic force), Igni (fire projection), Yrden (magical trap/slowing field), Quen (shield), Axii (mind influence, can coerce NPCs into cooperation or combat confusion). [game_w3] [books]
- **Swords:** Steel blade for humans and natural creatures; silver blade for monsters and the supernatural. Both worn on his back, cross-draw. [game_w3] [books]
- **Mutations beyond standard:** White hair and yellow eyes are not standard witcher traits — they result from additional experimental mutations beyond the Trial of the Grasses. Most who underwent them died. Geralt did not. The additional mutations also enhanced his reflexes and senses beyond the witcher baseline. [books]
- **Known epithets:** The Butcher of Blaviken (infamy), White Wolf (common), Gwynbleidd (Elder Speech: "White Wolf," used by elves), the White One. [books] [game_w3]
- **Titles refused:** Knight. He is not one, will not be one, has turned down the offer. [books]
- **Combat style:** Fast, precise, efficient. Combines swordsmanship with Sign use and alchemical preparations (potions, oils, bombs). Fights with minimal wasted motion. Dislikes theatrical dueling conventions. [game_w3] [books]

## Relationships

- **Ciri:** Daughter in everything but blood, claimed through the Law of Surprise. Trained her at Kaer Morhen. She is his most important person. He would burn the world for her and be unhappy about it. [books] [game_w3]
- **Yennefer of Vengerberg:** Lover, on-again-off-again across decades. Bound by a djinn wish (Geralt's). The relationship is complicated by whether the bond is magical compulsion or genuine love — probably both, and probably the distinction stopped mattering some time ago. [books] [game_w3]
- **Vesemir:** Mentor and the closest thing to a father Geralt has known. Respected, trusted, and deeply cared for, even if Geralt would phrase it less sentimentally. [books] [game_w3]
- **Dandelion (Jaskier):** Best friend. A bard and poet who has documented Geralt's adventures with wild creative license for over thirty years. Geralt complains about this constantly and has rescued him from his own poor decisions more times than either cares to count. [books] [game_w3]
- **Triss Merigold:** Friend and, during Geralt's amnesia period, a former lover. She helped raise Ciri and cares for Geralt genuinely. The dynamic after his memory returned is complicated by what happened during his amnesia. [game_w1w2] [game_w3]
- **Lambert and Eskel:** Fellow Wolf School witchers. Not as close as Vesemir but genuine brothers-in-arms with shared history at Kaer Morhen. [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

Geralt begins Witcher 3 in White Orchard following a lead on Yennefer, who has been located in Vizima by an Imperial escort sent by Emhyr var Emreis. The Emperor of Nilfgaard tasks Geralt with finding Ciri — his daughter — before the Wild Hunt does. Geralt accepts, for his own reasons.

The trail leads to Velen, a region of swamp and ruin caught between Nilfgaard and Redania, occupied by neither effectively. Here Geralt seeks the Ladies of the Wood — the Crones of Crookback Bog. The Crones are ancient entities who command the region through fear and genuine supernatural power. They had contact with Ciri when she passed through. The quest "Ladies of the Wood" leads through the village of Downwarren, the Crones' bog, and the ugliness underneath the folk-religion veneer. They give information in exchange for a task. They are not allies; they are a transaction.

In Novigrad, Geralt searches for Triss Merigold and pieces together Ciri's movements through the city. Novigrad is under the influence of the Church of the Eternal Fire, whose witch hunters are actively persecuting mages, elves, and dwarves. The city's underworld is controlled by a council of crime bosses: Francis Bedlam (the King of Beggars), Sigi Reuven (Dijkstra in disguise), Whoreson Junior (Caesar Bilzen), and Count Reuven's associate Cleaver.

Key quest chain: "Novigrad Dreaming" / "Get Junior" — Geralt tracks Dandelion through Whoreson Junior's operations, discovers Junior held Ciri, eventually retrieves Dandelion from the Rosemary and Thyme cabaret. The Novigrad arc ends with understanding where Ciri went next: Skellige.

## Skellige and Beyond

Geralt sails to Skellige to continue the trail. He participates in the Skellige succession crisis (who replaces King Bran after his death from old age on a longship, per Norse-inspired tradition), typically helping Cerys an Craite or Hjalmar an Craite, children of Crach an Craite. Crach is an old ally of Geralt and Ciri's.

Yennefer is already in Skellige investigating the magical explosion at Undvik — a massive burst of Elder Blood energy that left a trace detectable by skilled mages. She and Geralt investigate Avallac'h's abandoned laboratory on Skellige, which reveals the scope of what Avallac'h knows about Ciri and the White Frost.

The investigation ends with Ciri found — she has been hidden with Avallac'h's help. She is alive. She is exhausted. They are briefly, carefully, together again.

## Kaer Morhen Events

Geralt brings Ciri to Kaer Morhen and spends time assembling allies for the inevitable confrontation with the Wild Hunt. This involves individual quests to recruit: Keira Metz (send her to Kaer Morhen or let her go to Redania — different consequences), Triss (help her with the mage evacuation from Novigrad), Yennefer (ongoing), Ermion the druid, Philippa Eilhart, and optionally Letho of Gulet (carried over from Witcher 2 choices).

The Wild Hunt attacks Kaer Morhen in force. Imlerith — one of Eredin's generals — leads the assault. Geralt fights during the battle but cannot be everywhere. Vesemir grabs Imlerith physically to buy Ciri time to escape and is killed for it — neck snapped.

Ciri's grief erupts as Elder Blood power. The blast drives the Hunt back. Geralt kills Imlerith later on Bald Mountain, during the Crones' sabbath. Ciri kills the Crones on the same mountain (in the best version of that quest, she gets to do it herself).

## Endings and Resolution

**The Battle of Kaer Morhen, continued:**
After assembling the Sunstone (used to lure the Hunt to a chosen battlefield in Skellige), Geralt leads the assault. Avallac'h opens a gate to Tir ná Lia, the Aen Elle world. Geralt fights through the Hunt's forces and faces Eredin Bréacc Glas directly. He kills Eredin. Avallac'h's personal history — his role in Lara Dorren's story, his manipulation of both the Hunt and Ciri — is partially revealed. He escapes consequences.

Ciri enters the White Frost alone.

**Geralt's fate:**
- If Ciri survives and becomes a witcher: Geralt receives a sword forged for her by Yennefer. He gives it to her. Both are free.
- If Ciri becomes Empress: Geralt watches her ride away in a royal carriage. Bittersweet. She chose the responsible path. He is proud and sad simultaneously.
- If Ciri dies: Geralt takes her medallion from the snow. He goes after her alone. The implication is he does not intend to come back.

**Political resolution:** Whether Radovid lives or dies (and whether Dijkstra or the Temerian resistance takes advantage of the resulting chaos) affects the fate of the North but not Geralt's personal ending directly.

## DLC Content

**Hearts of Stone:**
Geralt accepts a contract from Olgierd von Everec — a man who cannot die, made a deal with Gaunter O'Dimm (Master Mirror, also called the Man of Glass, the Evil Incarnate, the Merchant of Mirrors) for immortality and had his heart turned to stone in exchange. The contract requires Geralt to complete three tasks for Olgierd: get the house of Maximilian Borsodi, get the caress of the toad prince's princess, get a starry night sky taken from the tower. These are fairy-tale impossible tasks that Geralt has to make literal.

O'Dimm is the antagonist and arguably one of the most dangerous entities in the entire Witcher universe. Polite, precise, and genuinely supernatural in a way that exceeds ordinary monsters. At the contract's end, Geralt must either let O'Dimm take Olgierd's soul (as payment for the original deal) or find a way to free Olgierd using a riddle O'Dimm cannot answer. The riddle answer — "your own shadow" or a variant depending on phrasing — requires clever interpretation of what O'Dimm cannot see. Freeing Olgierd is possible; O'Dimm is not destroyed, merely outwitted and forced to concede. He leaves, promising nothing good.

Olgierd himself is complex — a man who made a terrible bargain for selfish reasons, lost everything he actually cared about in the process (his wife Iris, his humanity), and spent his immortality becoming progressively worse. He may or may not deserve saving. The game lets Geralt decide. [game_w3]

**Blood and Wine:**
Geralt is hired by Duchess Anna Henrietta of Toussaint (Beauclair) to investigate a series of murders following a chivalric code — bodies left with clues referencing virtues of knighthood. The investigation leads to Dettlaff van der Eretein, a Higher Vampire manipulated by the Duchess's estranged sister Syanna into targeting specific men who wronged her long ago.

Regis appears — Emiel Regis Rohellec Terzieff-Godefroy, the Higher Vampire who traveled with Geralt in the books during the Baptism of Fire and died at Stygga Castle, staked through with an iron bar and his body burned. Regis regenerated over decades. He is whole. His return is the emotional core of Blood and Wine for anyone who read the books.

The ending options involve whether Syanna lives or dies, whether Dettlaff is killed or escapes, and whether the Duchess is reconciled with her sister. The good ending is genuinely difficult to achieve.

Corvo Bianco, a vineyard estate Geralt receives as payment, becomes his retirement home if he survived the main game. The final scene — in the best ending — shows Geralt at his estate with a beehive, a horse, and whoever he ended up with (Yennefer, or Triss, or alone). For longtime players and readers, the image of Geralt with a home is quietly moving. [game_w3]
