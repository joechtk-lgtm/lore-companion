# Lambert

## Overview

Lambert is the youngest of the Wolf School's surviving core witchers and the most vocally cynical of the lot. He has a sharp tongue, a low opinion of most people, and a habit of saying the true thing in the worst possible way. Underneath this, he is one of the most loyal people at Kaer Morhen, genuinely committed to the witchers who are what remains of his world, and capable of grief and affection that he works very hard to keep invisible. He was sold to the witchers by his father — not out of poverty or necessity, but for money. He has never quite digested this. He has made something of himself regardless, which doesn't make the swallowed thing hurt less.

## Key Facts

- **School:** Wolf School (Kaer Morhen) [game_w3]
- **Age:** Appears the youngest of the core Wolf School survivors; exact age unknown [game_w3]
- **Personality:** Sardonic, abrasive, genuinely funny when the situation allows, deeply loyal [game_w3]
- **Backstory:** Given to the witchers by his father, who sold him for coin rather than for survival necessity. Lambert knows this and has never forgiven it. [game_w3]
- **Combat:** Standard Wolf School style. Less refined than Eskel or Geralt in technique, compensates with aggression and the fact that being underestimated is tactically useful. [game_w3]
- **The traps:** Lambert rigs traps and defensive mechanisms at Kaer Morhen; he's thought carefully about the keep's defense. [game_w3]

## Relationships

- **Geralt:** Witcher-brother. Older than Lambert, often the adult in the room when Lambert is starting something. Lambert gives Geralt more credit than he'd admit aloud. [game_w3]
- **Eskel:** His closest peer. They argue constantly and would die for each other. [game_w3]
- **Vesemir:** Complicated. Vesemir trained him, is the closest thing to a father he has, and Lambert has the specific bitterness of someone who was abandoned by a real father and is therefore perpetually skeptical of the substitute. He also respects Vesemir enormously. Both things. [game_w3]
- **Keira Metz:** A relationship develops at Kaer Morhen if Geralt sends Keira there. This catches both of them off guard. It's good for Lambert in ways he's not comfortable articulating. [game_w3]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

Lambert is at Kaer Morhen during the Velen and Novigrad arcs, on winter break from contract work. He participates in the preliminary work around Uma — helping Vesemir and Eskel prepare the keep and contributing to the equipment needed for the ritual restoration.

**"Following the Thread":** Lambert's personal questline in Witcher 3. He's been investigating the death of a fellow witcher — a man who was set up and killed through a chain of treachery involving a corrupt nobleman. Lambert follows this chain from the witcher's death backward to the people responsible, trying to get justice or at least satisfaction. Geralt can join him for the investigation and its conclusion.

The quest is character-revealing: Lambert's anger at betrayal, at people who use witchers and then discard them, is personal. The Kaer Morhen massacre, his father selling him, the general pattern of humans treating witchers as tools — "Following the Thread" is him working that out on a small, specific case. He's not looking for a philosophical resolution. He wants the chain of responsible parties located and dealt with. [game_w3]

## Kaer Morhen Events

Lambert fights in the Battle of Kaer Morhen — his trap preparation is part of why the defense holds as long as it does. The Wild Hunt runs into Lambert's rigged positions before they reach the main defenders.

He survives. Vesemir does not.

Lambert's response to Vesemir's death is not described at length — he's not someone who describes his responses at length — but his presence at the pyre says what needs to be said. He is not okay. He deals with things by moving forward because stopping is worse.

**The Keira complication:** If Geralt sent Keira to Kaer Morhen, she is present through the battle. Her survival and the beginning of whatever she and Lambert are to each other is one of the few good notes in a chapter that costs the school its patriarch. [game_w3]

## Endings and Resolution

Lambert survives the events of Witcher 3. He continues witcher contract work.

If Keira survived (Geralt sent her to Kaer Morhen rather than letting her go to Radovid), she and Lambert are together. This is the best possible outcome for Lambert personally — a sorceress who is as pragmatically self-interested as he is sardonic, and who finds him interesting rather than insufferable. Neither of them would say they're happy about it. Both are. [game_w3]
