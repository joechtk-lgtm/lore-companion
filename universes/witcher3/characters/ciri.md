# Cirilla Fiona Elen Riannon (Ciri)

## Overview

Cirilla Fiona Elen Riannon — called Ciri by everyone who matters — is the Princess of Cintra, granddaughter of Queen Calanthe (the Lioness of Cintra), and daughter of Emperor Emhyr var Emreis of Nilfgaard and Princess Pavetta of Cintra, though her parentage was concealed from her for years. She is also Geralt's adopted daughter through the Law of Surprise — the most important relationship in both their lives. She carries Elder Blood (Hen Ichaer), a rare genetic inheritance from the elven Sage Lara Dorren aep Shiadhal, which gives her wild, innate power over space and time that has no parallel in trained magic. She is hunted by the Wild Hunt, sought by Nilfgaard, manipulated by Avallac'h, and loved by two people who would die for her. She is approximately twenty-one years old and has survived more than most people manage in a full lifetime.

## Key Facts

- **Full name:** Cirilla Fiona Elen Riannon [books]
- **Titles:** Princess of Cintra, Lady of Space and Time, Zireael (Elder Speech: "Swallow"), Child of Elder Blood, Child of Destiny, Child of Surprise [books] [game_w3]
- **Appearance:** Straight ashen-white hair (not curly — see Netflix note), green eyes, a prominent scar on her left cheek from witcher training at Kaer Morhen. Lean, fast, compact build. [books] [game_w3]
- **Age:** Born circa 1251-1253; approximately 21-22 during Witcher 3 (set ~1272). [books]
- **Magical ability:** Wild and untrained in the conventional sense. Elder Blood power manifests as spatial portals, temporal distortion, involuntary explosions of force when emotional. Cannot be fully controlled by traditional means. Has learned to suppress rather than direct it. [books] [game_w3]
- **Combat training:** Received witcher fighting techniques at Kaer Morhen from Geralt, Lambert, Eskel, and Vesemir — though she never underwent the witcher mutations. Also trained in a variant of the Cat School's agile style. Fast and unpredictable. [books] [game_w3]
- **Elder Blood lineage:** Ciri inherits through: Lara Dorren aep Shiadhal (elven Sage) → unnamed daughter (raised by humans) → generations of concealed descent → Calanthe → Pavetta → Ciri. The lineage was deliberately obscured. [books]
- **The Rats:** During her years wandering between escaping Cintra and being found by Geralt, Ciri fell in with a gang of young outlaws. She called herself Falka (the name of a historical tyrant-princess). She killed people during this period. She has not fully forgiven herself. [books]
- **Mistle:** A member of the Rats, Ciri's lover during that wandering period. Mistle was killed. Ciri's grief over this was significant. Not referenced in the games directly but part of the book backstory that shapes her. [books]

## Relationships

- **Geralt of Rivia:** Father in everything but blood. He claimed her through the Law of Surprise before she was born, found her in the forest after Cintra's fall, trained her, loves her fiercely. She calls him "Father" in the books. [books] [game_w3]
- **Yennefer of Vengerberg:** Mother in everything but blood. Trained Ciri in the theory of magic and helped her understand and partially control her Elder Blood power. Their relationship is prickly, intense, and deeply genuine. Yennefer would not describe herself as maternal but she is, with Ciri. [books] [game_w3]
- **Avallac'h (Crevan Espane aep Caomhan Macha):** An Aen Elle elf, a Sage of immense knowledge, and a profound complication. He was Lara Dorren's betrothed — the man she left for a human. He has spent centuries trying to recover what he sees as Lara's lost legacy. His relationship to Ciri is simultaneously protective and manipulative; he genuinely wants her to survive and also intends to use her. [books] [game_w3]
- **Queen Calanthe:** Grandmother and primary caregiver after Pavetta's death. The Lioness of Cintra was fierce, politically sharp, and loved Ciri intensely while also trying to avoid the destiny attached to her. She died when Cintra fell rather than surrender. [books]
- **Crach an Craite:** Jarl of Skellige, old friend of Calanthe and Ciri's. Reliable. Shelters Ciri on Skellige. [game_w3] [books]

## Velen and Novigrad Context

By the time Geralt begins searching for Ciri, she has been moving through the Northern Kingdoms for months, always one step ahead of the Wild Hunt. Geralt traces her backwards through locations she left.

**In Velen:** Ciri came to the Ladies of the Wood — the three Crones of Crookback Bog. She struck a bargain with them for safety and shelter. The terms are never fully specified but the Crones do not give anything freely; she paid some price for their protection. She stayed in and around the village of Downwarren under their authority. When the situation became untenable, she fled — destroying the Crones' forest behind her as she went. The fire and destruction she left is visible evidence of both her desperation and the scale her power can reach when she stops containing it.

**In Novigrad:** Ciri connected with Dandelion and the doppler Dudu (who could impersonate her acquaintances). She was being hunted by witch hunters and agents of the Eternal Fire as a mage and political target. Whoreson Junior — one of the city's crime bosses — captured her briefly, holding her for what he expected would be a profitable handover. She escaped. The full story of how she moved through the city emerges from the quest "Get Junior" and related investigations: Geralt pieces together that she was scared, improvising, and eventually got out by a combination of luck, her power, and the help of people like Triss.

## Skellige and Beyond

Ciri sailed to Skellige following an instinct or a vision. She experienced a terrifying vision of the White Frost on the islands — an apocalyptic magical phenomenon consuming world after world, moving inevitably toward the Continent. The vision broke her concentration and she lost control of her power, causing a massive Elder Blood explosion near Undvik that Yennefer later tracks.

She nearly died in the frozen wastes of Ard Skellig. Rescued by warriors of Clan Drummond, she was brought to Lofoten. Crach an Craite's family — including Cerys and Hjalmar — sheltered her at Kaer Trolde. The Wild Hunt attacked Kaer Trolde to reach her. She escaped into Avallac'h's protection.

**The Wild Hunt's objective:** Eredin Bréacc Glas needs Ciri not merely for her power but specifically for her ability to open stable gates between worlds. The Aen Elle — the elves of Tir ná Lia — are facing extinction as the White Frost consumes their world. Eredin intends to use Ciri to lead his people through to the Continent, displacing or eliminating its current inhabitants. Avallac'h's plan is different: use Ciri's power to stop the White Frost at its source.

**The White Frost (Blossom of Ice / Tedd Deireadh):** An ancient magical catastrophe that moves through the multiverse, freezing and extinguishing worlds. Ciri is the only being with power sufficient to enter the White Frost and survive long enough to act against it. This is the existential core of the entire game's plot. [books] [game_w3]

## Kaer Morhen Events

Ciri is brought to Kaer Morhen after Geralt finds her with Avallac'h. There is a period of genuine peace — a snowball fight in the courtyard, conversations by the fire, the feeling of being home. Vesemir crafts a wolf medallion for her. It is brief and it is earned.

The Wild Hunt attacks in force. Imlerith — one of Eredin's three generals — leads. Vesemir grabs Imlerith bodily when Imlerith has Ciri cornered, buying her the seconds she needs. Imlerith kills Vesemir by breaking his neck.

Ciri's Elder Blood power erupts in full. The wave of force that emanates from her grief is unlike anything seen in the game — it scatters the Wild Hunt and damages Imlerith. The Hunt withdraws.

Vesemir's body is burned on a pyre. Ciri is present. She is quiet with a kind of grief that has been compressed into something very hard and very purposeful.

The question of what to do about the White Frost is now urgent. Avallac'h's plan requires Ciri to enter the Frost. Ciri, having seen it in her vision, understands what she is agreeing to.

## Endings and Resolution

Three primary endings determined by the cumulative weight of choices Geralt makes throughout the game — all of them concerning Ciri's emotional wellbeing, autonomy, and confidence.

**Choices that affect the outcome (positive direction):**
- Going with Ciri to retrieve Uma in Velen (you accompany her, she doesn't face it alone)
- The snowball fight at Kaer Morhen — not skipping it
- Helping her commemorate Skjall's grave on Skellige
- Allowing her to kill the Crones at Bald Mountain herself
- Not pressuring her when she expresses doubt

**Ciri becomes a Witcher:**
She faces the White Frost, returns, and chooses to remain herself — to travel, to hunt monsters, to live freely. Geralt gives her a witcher sword Yennefer had made for her. The scene is quiet, warm, and one of the most satisfying endings in the game. Both of them are free. [game_w3]

**Ciri becomes Empress of Nilfgaard:**
She meets Emhyr var Emreis — her father, a cold and calculating man who genuinely wants her as his heir — and chooses to accept the responsibility of the throne. She will try to change the Empire from within. This ending requires Geralt to have taken her to meet the Emperor during the game. Whether it is a good outcome is genuinely ambiguous: she will have power to do enormous good, and she will be constrained in ways that are not her nature. The final scene shows her in Imperial dress, and Geralt watching her go. [game_w3]

**Ciri dies:**
She enters the White Frost and does not return. This ending results from a pattern where Geralt consistently failed to reinforce her confidence and sense of self-worth. The final scene shows Geralt alone in the snow with her medallion. He goes after her. He does not intend to come back. [game_w3]

## DLC Content

**Blood and Wine:**
If Ciri survived and became a witcher, she visits Geralt at Corvo Bianco — his vineyard estate in Toussaint. The scene is brief and warm: she rides up, they exchange a few lines, she is doing well and is clearly glad he has a home. It is the right ending note for both of them. [game_w3]

**Hearts of Stone:**
Ciri does not appear directly, but the themes of impossible bargains and the cost of power are thematically connected to her arc. O'Dimm's deal-making echoes the Crones' transaction with Ciri in Velen. [game_w3]

## Netflix Divergence
[canon_source: netflix]

Netflix Ciri (Freya Allan) has curly brown hair, which differs from the straight ashen hair of the books and games. The Netflix series significantly expands Ciri's solo storyline and considerably restructures her timeline and relationships. Her bond with Geralt takes much longer to develop on screen. The core identity — princess with Elder Blood, running from her destiny — is recognizable; the specific events and character beats differ substantially from the source material.
