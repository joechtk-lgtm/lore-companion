# Canon Rules: The Witcher Universe

## Hierarchy of Canon

1. **Books (Andrzej Sapkowski)** — Primary canon. Short stories and the main saga. What happened in the books is what happened. Period.
2. **Games (CD Projekt Red)** — Faithful sequels. Geralt's amnesia in Witcher 1 bridges the gap. The games treat the books as gospel and build from there. Game events are canon within the game timeline.
3. **Netflix Series** — Adaptation. Significant deviations from the source material. Ciri's arc, timelines, and character histories differ substantially. Never treat Netflix-only details as game or book canon.

## Mixing Sources

- When answering from multiple sources, note which source each claim comes from.
- Book and game canon can usually coexist. Note discrepancies if they exist.
- Netflix canon must always be labeled. Example: "In the Netflix adaptation, [X] — this differs from the books/games where [Y]."
- Never blend Netflix details silently into a book or game answer.

## Specific Known Divergences (Netflix vs Books/Games)

- **Ciri's appearance**: Netflix Ciri has curly hair. Book/game Ciri has straight ash-blond hair and a scar.
- **Nilfgaard armor**: Netflix season 1 Nilfgaard armor is widely mocked and not canon to games.
- **Timeline**: Netflix interleaves Geralt's past and present in a non-linear way that does not match book chronology.
- **Renfri**: Netflix portrays Renfri with expanded backstory. Core events align roughly with the short story "A Grain of Truth" — wait, that's Beauty and the Beast. Renfri appears in "The Lesser Evil" short story.
- **Yennefer's origin**: Netflix expands Yennefer's backstory significantly. Broad strokes (ugly girl, Aretuza, transformation) align; specifics diverge.

## Spoiler Handling

Each lore file tags content by spoiler tier. Do not reveal higher-tier content to users at a lower tier. When content would be a spoiler, say so without revealing it:

"That gets into what happens later in the story. Set your spoiler tier higher if you want to know."

## Uncertainty

If something is genuinely ambiguous in canon, say so. "The books leave this unclear" or "The games contradict the books here" are valid answers. Do not invent canon to fill gaps.
