# The Purple Wedding

## Overview

The Purple Wedding is the name given to the wedding feast of King Joffrey Baratheon and Margaery Tyrell, at which Joffrey died — poisoned in front of the assembled nobility of the realm. "Purple" from the color of the poison's effect on his face. The event is significant for multiple reasons: it removed the story's most visibly cruel antagonist, it framed Tyrion for the murder, it revealed layers of conspiracy operating simultaneously, and it triggered the chain of events that sent Tyrion to trial and then to flight.

## Background

Joffrey Baratheon had been king since his father's death, which he was nine years old at the time of. He was seventeen at the Purple Wedding. In those eight years, he had been consistently cruel — to Sansa, to petitioners, to those he had power over. He had ordered Ned Stark's execution against the advice of his own council. He had had Ros killed with crossbow bolts. He was, by any measure, both personally dangerous and politically destabilizing. Multiple people at his wedding had reasons to want him dead. ## The Conspiracy

**Olenna Tyrell:** The Queen of Thorns had arranged Margaery's betrothal to Joffrey as a political move — the Tyrell-Lannister alliance was good for both houses. But Olenna was clear-eyed about what Joffrey was. He was dangerous to Margaery, specifically. Olenna removed him. She took the strangler crystal from Sansa's hairnet during the feast, dropped it into Joffrey's wine cup when she adjusted his cloak. She never admitted it in the show until she told Jaime, dying, that she wanted Cersei to know it was her. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8]

**Petyr Baelish:** He provided the strangler poison and had it sewn into Sansa's hairnet, carried unknowingly by her to the feast. He had his own reasons for wanting Joffrey dead — the specifics are not entirely clear, but removing Joffrey destabilized the Lannister control of King's Landing in ways Littlefinger could exploit. He arranged Sansa's escape from King's Landing through Dontos Hollard simultaneously with the poison — he knew the wedding would end in Joffrey's death and had the escape planned in advance. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

The two conspiracies were parallel but independent: Olenna took the crystal; Littlefinger provided it; neither was aware of the full picture the other knew.

## The Death

Joffrey began choking during the feast, during a humiliating staged event in which a troupe of dwarfs performed a mock re-enactment of the War of the Five Kings, which Joffrey found hilarious and Tyrion found personally offensive. He forced Tyrion to serve as his cup-bearer. He poured wine over Tyrion's head. He drank his wine.

The poison worked quickly — the strangler constricts the throat. His face turned purple. He pointed at Tyrion while dying. Cersei screamed. He died in Cersei's arms, his eyes beginning to bleed. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

## The Aftermath

**Tyrion's arrest:** He was the last person to handle the cup. He was arrested. In a world that already viewed him as threatening and illegitimate, the circumstantial case was overwhelming. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

**Sansa's escape:** Ser Dontos led Sansa through the chaos to a rowboat and out to a ship. Littlefinger was waiting. Dontos was killed (an arrow, paid off). Sansa learned she had carried the murder weapon without knowing it. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

**Cersei's grief:** Genuine, regardless of everything. He was her son. She had protected him and enabled him for seventeen years and she watched him die and immediately identified the closest target for blame. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

**Tommen becomes king:** Joffrey's younger brother, far gentler, far more susceptible to influence. From Cersei's perspective, perhaps easier to control. From the story's perspective, more vulnerable to the machinations that followed.
