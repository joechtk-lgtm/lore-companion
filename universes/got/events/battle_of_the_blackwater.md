# The Battle of the Blackwater

## Overview

The Battle of the Blackwater was the decisive engagement of the War of the Five Kings in the south — Stannis Baratheon's assault on King's Landing by sea and land, and Tyrion Lannister's defense of the city using wildfire, chains, and a sortie he led personally. It is the battle that kept the Lannisters on the throne. It is also one of the story's clearest illustrations that wars are won and lost not by the most righteous combatant but by whoever is better prepared.

## The Setup

Stannis had assembled a fleet and an army after the failure of his political campaign (his letters to the lords of the realm about Joffrey's illegitimacy had been largely ignored). He moved on King's Landing by sea, the only viable approach given his position at Dragonstone. His fleet outnumbered the city's naval defense significantly. His army was capable. His timing was calculated.

Tyrion, as acting Hand, had been preparing for this. He commissioned an enormous quantity of wildfire from the city's alchemists — thousands of jars of the volatile green substance. He had them loaded onto a single ship and positioned it at the mouth of the Blackwater Rush, with a chain ready to seal the river behind Stannis's fleet once they entered. ## The Battle

**The wildfire:** Stannis's fleet followed the fleeing city ships into the Blackwater Rush. The chain was raised behind them. A burning arrow set the wildfire ship alight. The explosion was catastrophic — a chain reaction through the stored jars, a wall of green fire across the river, burning ships, burning men, burning water. The sound was heard for miles. The color was visible from the city walls. Hundreds of ships were destroyed or set ablaze in the initial explosion. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

**The sortie:** Joffrey fled the walls at Cersei's insistence before the battle was decided. Tyrion led a sortie through the tunnels along the Blackwater's shore — a small force attacking Stannis's men from an unexpected angle. He rallied them with a speech: "Those are brave men. Let's go kill them." He fought in the melee personally. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

**Tyrion's wound:** He was nearly killed — by Ser Mandon Moore, who cut his face with a blade. In the books, it is strongly implied Cersei arranged this assassination. The show is less specific. Tyrion lost half his nose in the books; he received a facial scar in the show. Podrick Payne killed Ser Mandon Moore and dragged Tyrion to safety. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

**The Tyrell relief force:** Tywin Lannister, marching from the Westerlands, had negotiated with House Tyrell after Renly's death — Mace Tyrell and his forces joined Tywin's. They arrived through the western gate as the battle was tipping against the city. The combined force broke Stannis's assault. Stannis retreated to his ships and then to Dragonstone. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## Aftermath

Joffrey took credit for the victory and rewarded those who fought conspicuously, which did not include Tyrion, who was injured and in bed. Tywin was named Hand of the King. Tyrion was moved to a small room to recover and was not visited by his father or his sister. The victory that saved the city was used by everyone except the man who arranged it.

The Tyrell alliance was cemented by Joffrey's betrothal to Margaery. The price of the relief force was a marriage that Cersei would spend the next two years trying to undermine. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

Stannis lost his real chance at the throne on the Blackwater. He had the right by law. He had the numbers on approach. He did not have the wildfire or the relief force, and he lost. He would spend the rest of his story fighting diminishing campaigns from an increasingly weak position.
