# The Dance of the Dragons

## Overview

The Dance of the Dragons was a Targaryen civil war fought roughly 170 years before the events of the main story — a conflict over succession between two claimants to the Iron Throne, both with legitimate claims, both with dragons. It is one of the bloodiest events in Westerosi history. Its most significant consequence was the near-extinction of the dragons: at the war's start, the Targaryens had seventeen living dragons. At its end, four. Within a generation, none.

The books on the Dance are presented as historical record from in-world sources, compiled in Fire & Blood by Grand Maester Munkun (with the story framed as a collection of unreliable sources — a deliberate narrative choice by Martin). [book_fire_blood]

## The Cause

King Viserys I Targaryen (not the same Viserys as Daenerys's brother — different generation) had a daughter named Rhaenyra by his first wife, Aemma Arryn. He named Rhaenyra his heir explicitly and publicly, requiring the lords of the realm to swear fealty to her. He then married Alicent Hightower and had sons: Aegon, Aemond, Daeron. When Viserys I died, the question was immediate: did his named heir (Rhaenyra, female, first marriage) outrank his eldest male heir (Aegon II, second marriage)?

The realm divided. [book_fire_blood]

## The Factions

**The Blacks:** Rhaenyra Targaryen's faction — named for their black and gold colors. Supported by the older lords who had sworn the oath, and by those who supported Rhaenyra personally. Her husband Daemon Targaryen (her uncle — Targaryen incestuous marriage tradition) was her most formidable military asset. Her sons from her first marriage (Jacaerys, Lucerys, Joffrey Velaryon) and her sons from Daemon provided the riders for her dragons. [book_fire_blood]

**The Greens:** Aegon II's faction — named for the green Hightower colors, the colors worn by Alicent Hightower. Supported by those who believed male primogeniture was the correct reading of succession law, and by those who had interests in Alicent's Hightower family connections. Aegon II rode Sunfyre. His brother Aemond rode Vhagar — the oldest and largest living dragon, the mount of Visenya herself during the Conquest. [book_fire_blood]

## Key Events

**Lucerys and Aemond:** Aemond Targaryen (Aegon II's brother) had lost an eye as a child to Lucerys Velaryon (Rhaenyra's son) in a fight over the dragon Vhagar. He claimed Vhagar himself eventually. During the war, he encountered Lucerys in the storm above Shipbreaker Bay. He killed him — both dragon and rider, though Aemond claimed it was not his intention. This act destroyed any hope of negotiation. [book_fire_blood]

**The Blood and Cheese:** After Lucerys's death, Daemon Targaryen hired two assassins to kill Aemond. They could not reach him — so instead, they killed Aemond's nephew Jaehaerys (Aegon II's young son) in his own room, in front of his mother. "A son for a son." The war became absolute. [book_fire_blood]

**The Fall of King's Landing:** Rhaenyra eventually took King's Landing, though the city rose against her and she was forced to flee. She was eventually captured and fed to Aegon II's dragon Sunfyre, dying before the war ended. [book_fire_blood]

**The End:** Aegon II died shortly after, poisoned by his own supporters who wanted the war to end. Rhaenyra's surviving son Aegon III took the throne. Both claimants were dead. The realm had burned for years and the dragons were nearly all gone. [book_fire_blood]

## Significance to the Main Story

**The parallel:** The Dance of the Dragons is explicitly echoed in the main story — Daenerys as Rhaenyra (female claimant with dragons, rightful by some interpretations) against Cersei (the occupying power). In the show, both die. The Iron Throne is melted. The Dance's lesson — that two dragon-riding claimants fighting each other destroys the dragons and the realm — is not learned until after it is lived again. [show_s5_8]

**The dragon extinction:** The Dance directly caused the end of Targaryen dragon power. The surviving dragons were few, small, and increasingly sickly. Within decades, the last dragon died. Daenerys's three are the first in over a century, which is why their existence is so destabilizing — the world had adapted to a world without dragons, and now it must adapt back. [book_fire_blood]

**Fire & Blood as source:** Martin's book Fire & Blood (2018) covers the Dance of the Dragons in detail. It is presented as in-world history — unreliable narrators, conflicting accounts, the propaganda of both sides. This is deliberate: the book is about how history is written, not just what happened. The TV series House of the Dragon adapts this story directly. [book_fire_blood]
