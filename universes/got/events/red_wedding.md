# The Red Wedding

## Overview

The Red Wedding is the single most consequential event in A Song of Ice and Fire — not the most dramatic or the most violent in the history of Westeros, but the one that broke a narrative assumption and restructured the story. Until this event, the reader/viewer could reasonably assume that the "good" faction would survive, that Robb Stark's war had a chance, that the story would eventually converge on a conventional resolution. The Red Wedding ended that assumption. The north's army was destroyed in one evening by a violation of guest right, and nothing was ever the same.

## Background: The Broken Pact

Robb Stark had agreed to marry one of Walder Frey's daughters or granddaughters — payment for the use of the Twins, the only crossing of the Green Fork in the region, at the beginning of his campaign. He married Jeyne Westerling (books) or Talisa Maegyr (show) instead. This was a catastrophic political error — Walder Frey had many daughters and grandchildren, had been known as "the Late Lord Frey" for arriving late at the Trident during Robert's Rebellion, and had spent decades nursing grievances about disrespect. The broken marriage pact gave him something he could act on. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

## The Planning

Tywin Lannister coordinated the arrangement with Walder Frey and Roose Bolton. The Freys provided the location and the seeming reconciliation; Roose Bolton provided military support and insider access to Robb's army; Tywin provided the political guarantees — Roose would be named Warden of the North, the Freys would receive appropriate honors. The plan required Robb to attend a wedding at the Twins, inside Frey walls, trusting in guest right. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

Guest right — the ancient custom of the north and most of Westeros — holds that a host cannot harm a guest who has eaten their bread and salt under their roof, and vice versa. Walder Frey deliberately extended guest right before the wedding began. He knew what he was doing. [book_asoiaf]

## The Execution

The wedding was for Robb's uncle Edmure Tully to one of Frey's granddaughters — a genuine match to repair the alliance, proposed by Robb as compensation. The feast was held inside the Twins. Robb's army camped outside; the northern lords and officers were inside.

The signal: "The Rains of Castamere," the Lannister song written about Tywin's destruction of House Reyne when he was young, played by the musicians. The doors were bolted. Crossbowmen behind the tapestries began firing. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

- **Robb Stark** was killed by Roose Bolton personally — "The Lannisters send their regards" — shot repeatedly with crossbow bolts first, then stabbed through the heart. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]
- **Talisa/Jeyne** was killed first in the show; in the books Jeyne was not present (Robb sent her to safety). Show presentation compressed the timeline. [book_asoiaf vs show_s1_4]
- **Grey Wind**, Robb's direwolf, was shot in his pen outside. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]
- **Catelyn Stark** watched Robb die, held Walder Frey's young wife hostage and had her throat cut when Walder refused to honor Robb's life. Catelyn's own throat was cut afterward. Her body was thrown naked into the river. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]
- The northern lords and knights inside the Twins were killed. The army outside was attacked and largely destroyed. [book_asoiaf]

## Arya's Witness

Arya Stark and the Hound arrived at the Twins during the massacre. They were outside. Arya heard and saw the aftermath — the Grey Wind's body, the chaos, the burning. She killed her first man. The Hound knocked her out to prevent her from doing something fatal. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

## Consequences

- Robb Stark's kingdom was extinguished in one evening.
- Roose Bolton became Warden of the North.
- The Frey-Lannister-Bolton alliance controlled the north.
- The war in the south effectively ended.
- The north was left headless and occupied.

"The north remembers." The phrase that followed. The violation of guest right — the most sacred of northern customs — was not forgotten. [book_asoiaf]

## Retribution

In the show, Arya returned to the Twins, baked Walder Frey's sons into a pie, served it to him, and cut his throat while wearing his face. This is show canon [show_s5_8]. In the books, Arya had not yet reached the Freys at the end of published material. The Freys' eventual fate in the books is not yet shown.

Lady Stoneheart — Catelyn Stark's resurrection by Thoros of Myr in the books, her body recovered from the river three days later — is absent from the show entirely [book_asoiaf only]. She returns as a cold, nearly mindless version of herself focused entirely on vengeance against Freys and Lannisters. She is one of the most significant book-only characters in the series.
