# Robert's Rebellion (The War of the Usurper)

## Overview

Robert's Rebellion was the war that ended the Targaryen dynasty and placed Robert Baratheon on the Iron Throne — roughly seventeen years before the main story begins. It is the event that produced the world of Game of Thrones: the Lannister alliance through Cersei's marriage, Ned Stark as Robert's most trusted friend, Jon Snow's existence and his false identity, the bitterness of those who lost (Targaryen loyalists, Dornish whose princess died). Everything flows from it.

The story Robert tells himself about the Rebellion is the version most of the realm accepts. The truth is considerably more complicated.

## The Official Story

Prince Rhaegar Targaryen abducted Lyanna Stark — Ned's sister, Robert's betrothed — at the Tournament of Harrenhal, where he had crowned her Queen of Love and Beauty over his own wife, scandalizing the court. He took her to Dorne. When Lord Rickard Stark and his son Brandon demanded her return, the Mad King Aerys burned them alive — Brandon strangled himself trying to escape his bonds while watching his father burned in his armor in the throne room. Jon Arryn, warden of the Vale who had fostered both Ned and Robert, refused the demand that he execute his wards. The three of them raised their banners. The realm joined them. [book_asoiaf]

## The War

- **The Battle of Summerhall:** Robert won three battles in one day. His hammer was a genuine military asset; he was an exceptional commander and fighter.
- **The Battle of Ashford:** Robert lost, was forced to retreat.
- **The Battle of the Bells at Stoney Sept:** Robert was hiding in the town. Ned, Jon Arryn, and Hoster Tully arrived with their forces; Rhaegar arrived too late with the royalist army. Robert won.
- **The Battle of the Trident:** The decisive battle. Robert killed Rhaegar Targaryen with his warhammer in the river. Rhaegar's rubies scattered across the water; the ford is called the Ruby Ford ever since. [book_asoiaf]
- **The Sack of King's Landing:** Tywin Lannister opened the gates to Robert's forces, having ostensibly joined the rebellion. His troops killed the remaining Targaryen princes — Rhaegar's children — in the Red Keep. Ser Jaime killed Aerys. Robert arrived to find Ned Stark standing over the bodies of Rhaegar's children in Elia Martell's arms. [book_asoiaf]
- **The Tower of Joy:** Ned traveled to Dorne with six companions to find Lyanna, guarded by three remaining Kingsguard knights — including Ser Arthur Dayne, the Sword of the Morning, possibly the greatest swordsman of his generation. All three knights died. Most of Ned's companions died. Ned found Lyanna inside. [book_asoiaf]

## The Truth

Lyanna Stark was not abducted. She ran away with Rhaegar. She loved him. They were secretly married at the tower of joy — the show confirms this at the end of Season 6 [show_s5_8]; the books have accumulated this evidence strongly enough that "R+L=J" is the most accepted fan theory in the series' history. [book_asoiaf, widely theorized and confirmed by show_s5_8]

Rhaegar's wife Elia Martell did not object to a plural marriage — Valyrian tradition and some Westerosi precedent (Aegon's two wives) made it legally arguable. The marriage was likely legal under Westerosi law if valid.

Lyanna was pregnant. She died giving birth to Rhaegar's son at the Tower of Joy. She made Ned promise to protect the child from Robert, who would have killed any Targaryen infant. The three Kingsguard knights who died defending the tower were not defending Lyanna — they were defending the last Targaryen heir, doing their sworn duty to protect the royal bloodline. Ned understood this. He took the child, named him Jon, claimed him as his own bastard, and carried the weight of that lie for the rest of his life.

Robert's war was fought over a kidnapping that never happened. Robert's grief for Lyanna was real. His story was not. The realm was built on a false premise, and the true heir was hidden in plain sight in Winterfell for seventeen years. [book_asoiaf; confirmed by show_s5_8]
