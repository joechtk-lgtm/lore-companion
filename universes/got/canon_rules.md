# Canon Rules: Game of Thrones / A Song of Ice and Fire

## Primary Canon
George R.R. Martin's A Song of Ice and Fire novels:
- A Game of Thrones (1996)
- A Clash of Kings (1998)
- A Storm of Swords (2000)
- A Feast for Crows (2005)
- A Dance with Dragons (2011)

These are the definitive primary sources. When books and show conflict, the books are canon.

## Extended Book Canon
- Fire & Blood (2018): History of House Targaryen, the Dance of the Dragons. Canon.
- The World of Ice and Fire (2014): Illustrated history, geography, and deep lore. Canon.
- The Dunk and Egg novellas (The Hedge Knight, The Sworn Sword, The Mystery Knight): Set ~90 years before the main story. Canon.

## Show Canon: Seasons 1–4 [show_s1_4]
Largely faithful to the books with compression and some changes. Characters, events, and dialogue sourced from seasons 1–4 should be labeled [show_s1_4] and are generally consistent with book canon. Note any significant divergences.

## Show Canon: Seasons 5–8 [show_s5_8]
Diverges significantly from the direction of the books. Always label [show_s5_8] when referencing show-specific material from these seasons. Never present show endings as book canon. Where books are expected to differ, say so explicitly.

## The Unfinished Saga
The Winds of Winter and A Dream of Spring are unpublished as of 2026. When discussing future plot developments for characters whose arcs are unresolved in the books (Jon's resurrection, Arya in Braavos, Stannis at Winterfell, Daenerys in Meereen, etc.), present book evidence and strong theories but acknowledge the books remain unfinished.

## Disputed Canon
Show-only characters, events, and plotlines from seasons 5–8 that have no book equivalent: always label and note they may not reflect Martin's intended direction.
