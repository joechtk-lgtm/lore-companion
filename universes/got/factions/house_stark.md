# House Stark of Winterfell

## Overview

House Stark is one of the oldest noble houses in Westeros — older than the Targaryens, older than the Andal invasion, rooted in the First Men who settled the continent eight thousand years ago. They ruled the north as Kings of Winter for thousands of years before Aegon's Conquest, and their seat at Winterfell is both a fortress and a monument to what the north is. Their words, "Winter Is Coming," are not a boast. They are a statement of fact, a warning about the nature of the world, a philosophy held against the forgetting that prosperity encourages. The north remembers what the south tends to dismiss.

## Key Facts

- **Words:** "Winter Is Coming" — unique among the great houses in being neither boast nor prayer. It is a preparation. [book_asoiaf]
- **Sigil:** Grey direwolf on a white field. [book_asoiaf]
- **Seat:** Winterfell, an enormous castle built over natural hot springs that heat the walls year-round. Ancient. Built, legend says, by Bran the Builder. [book_asoiaf]
- **Religion:** The old gods, not the Seven. The godswood at Winterfell contains a heart tree — a weirwood with a face carved into it, red leaves, white bark. The Starks pray there alone, without priests, without ceremony. [book_asoiaf]
- **The crypts:** Beneath Winterfell lie the crypts of the Kings of Winter — ancient lords and kings, their effigies seated in stone with iron swords across their laps to keep their spirits from walking. The crypts go deeper than the castle, older than memory. Something is in those crypts that the narrative has been gesturing toward. [book_asoiaf]
- **The direwolves:** Each Stark child found a direwolf pup on the road from Winterfell — one for each trueborn Stark child, and one white runt for Jon Snow. The direwolves mirror their bond partners. This is not coincidence. [book_asoiaf]

## The Pack Survives

House Stark's internal ethic is pack loyalty. They function as a unit. Their dispersal — Ned dead, Robb dead, Catelyn dead, Sansa in King's Landing, Arya fleeing, Bran and Rickon believed dead — is the story's central loss, and their gradual reassembly (or failure to reassemble) is its emotional spine. The phrase "when the snows fall and the white winds blow, the lone wolf dies but the pack survives" is Ned's, from Arya's memory. It is what the story is about. [book_asoiaf]

## The Northern Culture

The north is different from the south. It is larger, colder, more sparsely populated. The northern lords have a more direct relationship with their liege lord — not because they are simpler, but because isolation creates directness. The northern value on honor is genuine rather than performative. They remember debts and slights across generations. "The north remembers" is both a cultural fact and a warning. [book_asoiaf]

## Robb's War

After Ned's execution, Robb Stark raised his banners and marched south. He was seventeen. He won every battle he fought, including capturing Jaime Lannister at the Whispering Wood. The lords of the north and the Riverlands declared him King in the North, the first King in the North since Torrhen Stark knelt to Aegon three hundred years before.

He could win battles. He could not navigate court politics. His decision to marry Jeyne Westerling (books) / Talisa Maegyr (show) broke his marriage pact with the Freys and gave Tywin Lannister the opening he needed. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## The Red Wedding

Robb Stark was murdered at the Twins by Walder Frey's men, having attended a wedding to repair the broken alliance. His mother Catelyn Tully was murdered alongside him. His direwolf Grey Wind was killed in his pen. The northern army was slaughtered.

The north was left headless. The Starks were believed either dead or scattered. Roose Bolton was named Warden of the North. The great House Stark had been extinguished, or so it appeared. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

"The north remembers." The phrase became a rallying point for those who had not forgotten what was done to the Starks.

## Reclamation

In the show, Jon and Sansa retook Winterfell from the Boltons with Wildling forces and the Knights of the Vale. The surviving Stark children — Jon, Sansa, Arya, Bran — were briefly reunited at Winterfell. After Daenerys's war and the destruction of King's Landing, Sansa refused to have the north as part of the new kingdom and was named Queen in the North. [show_got_s5_8]

The direwolf flies again over Winterfell.
