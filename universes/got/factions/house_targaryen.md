# House Targaryen

## Overview

House Targaryen came from Valyria — the great dragon-riding civilization of the eastern continent, a magical empire that dominated Essos for centuries. When Valyria was destroyed in the Doom (a volcanic catastrophe of uncertain but enormous scale, possibly with magical involvement), the Targaryens had already left — Aenar Targaryen took his family and their five dragons to Dragonstone, a small island off the coast of Westeros, twelve years before the Doom. It is widely noted that his daughter Daenys had dreamed the Doom before it happened. They survived. No one else from the Freehold did.

They ruled Westeros for nearly three hundred years. Then Robert Baratheon's hammer ended the dynasty, or nearly ended it.

## Key Facts

- **Words:** "Fire and Blood." Both description and instruction. [book_asoiaf]
- **Sigil:** Three-headed dragon, black on red. The three heads represent Aegon and his two sister-wives: Visenya and Rhaenys, who rode with him during the Conquest. [book_asoiaf] [book_fire_blood]
- **Seat:** Dragonstone (ancestral) and the Red Keep in King's Landing (after the Conquest). [book_asoiaf]
- **Hair and eyes:** Valyrian features — silver-gold hair, purple or violet eyes — which distinguished them from the general Westerosi population. Not all carriers are obvious; bastard lines can lose the coloring. [book_asoiaf]
- **The incest:** Targaryens practiced sibling marriage to keep the bloodline pure. This had two effects: it maintained the Valyrian bloodline (and the ability to ride dragons), and it concentrated the genetic instability of the family. The common phrase is that "when a Targaryen is born, the gods flip a coin." [book_asoiaf]
- **Magic resistance:** Targaryens have some resistance to fire, but this is not reliable or absolute. The books are more nuanced than the show on this point. [book_asoiaf]

## The Conquest

Aegon I Targaryen landed at the mouth of the Blackwater Rush with his two sisters, three dragons (Balerion the Black Dread, Meraxes, and Vhagar), and a small force. He did not have a large army. He had dragons. Within two years he had the submission of all seven kingdoms except Dorne, which resisted through guerrilla tactics and eventually through assassinating Meraxes. The Targaryens and Dorne were at war for decades. [book_asoiaf] [book_fire_blood]

King's Landing was built around the site of Aegon's landing. The Red Keep was built later, incorporating Maegor the Cruel's dungeons. Dragonstone remained the traditional seat of the Targaryen heir. [book_asoiaf]

## The Last Dragons

Daenerys Targaryen hatched three dragon eggs at Drogo's funeral pyre. Dragons had been absent from the world for over a century — the last Targaryen dragons were small, sickly things by the end, casualties of the Dance and the years after. Her dragons were the first in the world in living memory. They were also growing fast. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## The Dance of the Dragons

The civil war within House Targaryen, roughly 170 years before the main story. King Viserys I died and left disputed succession: his daughter Rhaenyra (named heir but female) against his son Aegon II (male, from a second wife). The realm split. The Blacks (Rhaenyra's faction) against the Greens (Aegon II's). Dragons fought dragons above Westeros. The dragon population was devastated. After the Dance ended, the last dragons lived and died small. The species' numbers were never recovered. [book_fire_blood]

Key figures: Daemon Targaryen (Rhaenyra's husband and uncle, the consummate warrior), Aemond One-Eye (lost an eye to Lucerys Velaryon as a child, claimed Vhagar), Rhaenys Targaryen "the Queen Who Never Was," and the eventual end of both claimants. The Dance is important not merely as history but as the direct cause of why, three hundred years later, Daenerys's three dragons are the only dragons in the world. [book_fire_blood]
