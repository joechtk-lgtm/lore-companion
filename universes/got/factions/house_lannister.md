# House Lannister of Casterly Rock

## Overview

House Lannister is the wealthiest house in Westeros, the dominant political power for most of the story's main timeline, and a family whose strengths and failures are all produced by the same source: the belief that Lannister power is inherent and self-sustaining. Tywin built the reputation on genuine competence. His children inherited the reputation without inheriting his instinct for when to use it and when to hold back. The story watches the Lannister political dominance built over decades collapse in a few years.

## Key Facts

- **Official words:** "Hear Me Roar" — rarely used, not self-explanatory as a slogan. [book_asoiaf]
- **Unofficial motto:** "A Lannister always pays his debts" — not a boast, originally. It meant their financial obligations were met, their alliances honored, their enemies punished. It became associated with revenge as much as honor. [book_asoiaf]
- **Sigil:** Golden lion on crimson. [book_asoiaf]
- **Seat:** Casterly Rock — built into an enormous rock formation on the western coast. Virtually impregnable. Contains the gold mines that made the Lannisters the wealthiest house in the realm. [book_asoiaf]
- **The mines:** In the books, Casterly Rock's gold veins are running dry by the time of the main story. This is not widely known. It means the Lannister financial power is more fragile than it appears. [book_asoiaf]
- **The Iron Bank debt:** The crown is deeply in debt to the Iron Bank of Braavos. The Lannisters, having placed their family on the throne, are responsible for it. [book_asoiaf]

## Family Structure

**Tywin Lannister** — patriarch, builder of Lannister power, dead on a privy.
**Cersei** — eldest, queen regent, intelligent in limited directions, increasingly erratic without Tywin.
**Jaime** — twin of Cersei, Kingsguard, Kingslayer, no inheritance by his vow.
**Tyrion** — youngest, dwarf, disinherited in all but law, the most politically capable of the three.
**Kevan Lannister** — Tywin's brother, the steady competent administrator who could have held things together after Tywin's death. Varys killed him. [book_asoiaf]
**Lancel Lannister** — Cersei's cousin and former lover who joined the Sparrows. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

## The Dominance

The Lannisters controlled the Iron Throne through Joffrey's reign. Tywin as Hand, Cersei as queen regent, the Tyrell alliance through Margaery's betrothal to Joffrey — they had overwhelming financial and military resources. The war against Robb Stark was expensive but sustainable. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

[spoiler_tier: red_wedding]
## After the Red Wedding

The Red Wedding ended the northern war. Tywin's plan — using Frey and Bolton betrayal to destroy Robb without a battle — was successful. The Lannisters stood uncontested. Then Joffrey died. Then Tywin died. Then Tyrion fled. In weeks, the family went from having a patriarch, the most feared Hand in a generation, and Joffrey on the throne to having Cersei ruling alone with a child-king (Tommen) and no Tywin to catch her mistakes.

[spoiler_tier: dance_of_dragons]
## The Hollowing

In the books, the Lannister power begins to hollow. The gold mines are running dry. Cersei's governance is erratic. Kevan tries to stabilize and is killed by Varys. The Tyrell alliance is strained. The Faith Militant is empowered. The great power of the realm is bleeding out from within. [book_asoiaf]

[spoiler_tier: show_complete]
## The End of the Dynasty

The Lannister line effectively ends in the show with Cersei and Jaime's deaths in the Red Keep. Tyrion survives as Hand of the new king. He is the last Lannister of any consequence, serving a Stark-Targaryen king. The Lannister legacy — Tywin's obsession — does not survive its architect by long. [show_s5_8]
