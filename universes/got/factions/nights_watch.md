# The Night's Watch

## Overview

The Night's Watch is an ancient order sworn to defend the Wall and the realm from whatever lies north of it. At its founding — thousands of years ago, after the Long Night and the construction of the Wall — it was a great military order, nine thousand strong, with nineteen castles along the Wall's length. By the time of the main story it has fewer than a thousand men, three manned castles, and a reputation composed mostly of jokes. It recruits criminals, poachers, and the dispossessed. The lords of the realm send them their problems and their embarrassments.

The Watch knows what is actually beyond the Wall. No one else wants to believe them.

## Key Facts

- **The vow:** "Night gathers, and now my watch begins. It shall not end until my death. I shall take no wife, hold no lands, father no children. I shall wear no crowns and win no glory. I shall live and die at my post. I am the sword in the darkness. I am the watcher on the walls. I am the shield that guards the realms of men." [book_asoiaf]
- **The three orders:** Rangers (the fighters, who range beyond the Wall), Builders (who maintain the Wall and its castles), Stewards (who manage logistics, cooking, horses — and who are sometimes quietly being prepared for command). [book_asoiaf]
- **No political allegiance:** The Watch swears no loyalty to any lord or king. They are outside the feudal system entirely. This is why men who take the black escape their previous crimes — they have died to the world and been reborn as the Watch. [book_asoiaf]
- **Castle Black:** The main garrison, halfway along the Wall. The Lord Commander's seat. Three hundred men at the time of the story, when nine thousand is what it requires to man the Wall properly. [book_asoiaf]
- **Eastwatch-by-the-Sea and the Shadow Tower:** The other two manned castles, one at each end of the Wall. [book_asoiaf]

## What They Guard Against

The Watch's founding purpose is defense against the Others — the White Walkers, the creatures of cold and death that ended the Long Night. But the Watch has been at its post for eight thousand years. The Others have not appeared in living memory. The Watch has drifted into treating the Wildlings as the primary threat, forgetting what the Wildlings themselves are fleeing from. [book_asoiaf]

## The Ranging Beyond the Wall

Lord Commander Jeor Mormont led a ranging expedition beyond the Wall to investigate reports of Wildling mobilization and disappearances. What they found at the Fist of the First Men was wights — the dead raised by the Others — attacking in force. The ranging was nearly destroyed. Mormont and the survivors retreated to Craster's Keep, where Craster was killed and Mormont was murdered in the mutiny that followed. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

The Watch returned to Castle Black with the news that the Others were real and active. No one outside the Wall wanted to hear it.

[spoiler_tier: red_wedding]
## Battle of Castle Black

Mance Rayder, King-Beyond-the-Wall, mobilized hundreds of thousands of Wildlings in a coordinated assault on the Wall — attacking simultaneously from beyond the Wall and from south of it (through the tunnel). The Watch, desperately undermanned, held Castle Black through a night's fighting. Jon Snow led the defense. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

Stannis Baratheon's army arrived and broke the assault.

Jon was elected Lord Commander. He opened the gates and let the surviving Wildlings through — the most divisive decision in the Watch's history within the order.

[spoiler_tier: dance_of_dragons]
## The Assassination

Jon's decision to bring the Wildlings through the Wall — combined with his plan to march on Winterfell to challenge Ramsay Bolton for Arya's sake — was the final rupture with the traditionalist Watch brothers. "For the Watch." Jon was stabbed repeatedly in the courtyard of Castle Black. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8 for specifics]

In the show, Melisandre resurrected him. He executed the conspirators and declared his watch ended. The Night's Watch survived as an institution but was fundamentally broken — its purpose, its unity, its numbers all compromised. [show_s5_8]
