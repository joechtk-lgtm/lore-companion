# The White Walkers (The Others)

## Overview

In A Song of Ice and Fire, they are called "the Others." In the HBO show, they are called "White Walkers." Both terms are now in common use. They are the existential threat that the Wall was built to stop and the Night's Watch was created to warn against, and they have not been seen in living memory until the story begins. The prologue of A Game of Thrones opens with them. The Watch members who die in that prologue become the first wights of the story. The Others are real, they are moving, and the realm has spent centuries forgetting why the Wall exists.

## Key Facts

- **Appearance:** Tall, pale, thin — almost human in shape but inhuman in presence. Cold emanates from them. Their eyes in the books are described as blue, crystalline, inhuman. Their armor and weapons seem formed of ice or something similar. [book_asoiaf]
- **Their weapons:** Ice blades (or something harder than steel) that shatter Valyrian bronze on contact. Ordinary steel shatters. Valyrian steel is one of two known weapons that can harm or kill them. Dragonglass (obsidian) is the other. [book_asoiaf]
- **Wights:** The dead raised and animated in the Others' service. They do not feel pain, do not need air, function as long as their bodies do. Fire kills them. They can be stopped by destroying their head or severing their connection to the Others — in the show, killing the Walker who raised them destroys its wights. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8 for the "kill the Walker, kill the wights" mechanic which may differ from books]
- **Dragonglass:** Obsidian, mined in significant quantities at Dragonstone. Sam Tarly killed an Other with a dragonglass blade at the Fist of the First Men. This was the first known killing of an Other in thousands of years. [book_asoiaf]
- **Valyrian steel:** Also lethal to Others. Extremely rare — the knowledge of how to make it was lost with Valyria. [book_asoiaf]

## The Night King

The Night King is a show creation [show_got_s5_8]. He does not exist in the books as a named individual character. In the show, he is the leader of the White Walkers, created by the Children of the Forest at a weirwood tree — a man captured and transformed by obsidian driven into his chest. His history is revealed in show Season 6 [show_got_s5_8].

In the books, "the Night's King" is a historical figure from the age of heroes — the thirteenth Lord Commander of the Night's Watch, who fell in love with a woman with blue eyes (possibly an Other), declared himself king, and was eventually destroyed by the combined forces of Stark and Wildling. This figure may or may not be connected to the Others in the books; the connection is implied but not confirmed. [book_asoiaf]

The show's Night King as a character — a single ruling intelligence with the mark-of-Cain power to raise wights — is show canon only. The books present the Others as a collective threat of uncertain political/social structure. [book_asoiaf vs show_s5_8]

## What the Others Want — The Books

The books are deliberately ambiguous about the Others' motivation. This is a choice. Martin has said they are not simply "kill everything" villains. The Wildlings know more about them than the Watch does — the Wildlings have been living in the same land for thousands of years, observing. Craster's arrangement (giving male infant children to the Others) suggests they have something they want besides destruction, and that they are capable of making deals. [book_asoiaf]

Theories: they may be connected to the long winter cycles of the world, to the imbalance between the old magic and the new. The children of the forest made them, possibly as weapons against the First Men, and then could not stop them. The relationship between the children, the weirwood network, and the Others may be deeper than the story has yet revealed. [book_asoiaf — speculative/theory territory]

## The Threat Made Real

The surviving rangers returning from the Fist of the First Men brought word of what they had seen. Sam's killing of an Other with dragonglass was the first evidence in thousands of years that they could be killed at all. Jon Snow saw the army of the dead beyond the Wall — the sheer scale of it, the numbers of wights, the Walkers themselves. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## The Battle of Winterfell

In the show, Arya Stark killed the Night King at the Battle of Winterfell with a Valyrian steel dagger, destroying him and all the wights he had raised. The threat ended in the episode. [show_got_s5_8]

Whether the book version of the Others ends this way, or whether their arc is longer and more complex, is unknown. The Winds of Winter and A Dream of Spring are unpublished.
