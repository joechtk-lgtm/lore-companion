# Sansa Stark

## Overview

Sansa Stark arrived in King's Landing believing in knights and songs and the chivalric code that courtly culture advertised. She was thirteen. Everything she had been taught — that nobility was real, that honor was rewarded, that the right behavior would produce the right outcome — was systematically dismantled in King's Landing, in the Red Keep, in the company of Joffrey and Cersei and Littlefinger. Her arc is the story of a girl who received a thorough, brutal education in how power actually works and became, in time, one of the most politically careful people in the story. She does not become cold. She becomes precise.

## Key Facts

- **Direwolf:** Lady — killed on Joffrey's order early in the first season/book, as punishment for Nymeria biting Joffrey. Lady's death was Sansa's first cost for loving the wrong things. [book_asoiaf]
- **Her education:** King's Landing was her first school. Margaery Tyrell, watching the game rather than playing it openly, was her second education. Littlefinger, using her as a prop while teaching her the game's mechanics, was her third. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]
- **Her value as a political piece:** She is the key to the North — as Ned Stark's daughter, she is the heir to Winterfell (with Robb, Bran, Rickon dead or missing). Everyone in the game recognizes this. Her own choices have been constrained by it. [book_asoiaf]
- **The names she uses:** In the Vale, under Littlefinger's protection, she is "Alayne Stone," his bastard daughter. The alias is practical and psychologically complicated. [book_asoiaf]

## Relationships

- **Joffrey:** Her betrothed for a period. His cruelty toward her was creative and deliberate. She survived it by learning to hide her face. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]
- **Tyrion:** Her reluctant husband by Tywin's decree — a political match to secure Sansa's claim to Winterfell. Tyrion did not touch her; she was not grateful so much as functionally protected. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]
- **Littlefinger:** He orchestrated her escape from King's Landing. He was in love with her mother and transferred that obsession to her. She is his project, his investment, and his danger — she eventually understands him clearly enough to use that understanding against him. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]
- **The Hound:** He offered to take her away from King's Landing during the Battle of the Blackwater. She refused, in the books. In the show this scene is handled differently. The moment stayed with both of them. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

## Captivity and Survival

After Ned's execution, Sansa was kept in King's Landing as a hostage — technically a guest, practically imprisoned. Joffrey used her to demonstrate power: making her look at her father's head on the walls, having her beaten. She learned to control her face. She learned to say exactly what was required. She learned that Cersei, for all her cruelty, occasionally protected her from the worst of Joffrey's impulses because Sansa's value as a hostage required her intact. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

Tyrion's betrothal to her — by Tywin's decree — replaced Joffrey's. He did not beat her. He did not touch her. He was kind in a way that cost him nothing and meant something to her nevertheless. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

## The Purple Wedding and Escape

The Purple Wedding killed Joffrey during his wedding to Margaery Tyrell. Sansa, wearing a hairnet threaded with strangler crystals (Littlefinger's poison, unknowingly carried by her), escaped in the chaos. Ser Dontos Hollard — a fool who had warned her months before — led her to a ship. Littlefinger was waiting. He killed Dontos. He took her to the Vale. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

In the Vale, she was "Alayne Stone," Littlefinger's bastard daughter. Lysa Arryn — her aunt, Littlefinger's new wife — was obsessively jealous of his attention toward Sansa. Littlefinger kissed Sansa in front of Lysa. Lysa threatened to push her through the Moon Door. Littlefinger arrived, soothed Lysa, and then pushed Lysa through the Moon Door himself. He then told the lords of the Vale that Lysa had jumped. Sansa confirmed the lie. She had watched him do it. She chose to protect herself by protecting him. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

This was the lesson: the game does not reward the honest. She chose.

## The Vale and Beyond

In the books, Sansa is still in the Vale at the end of ADwD, managing Sweetrobin (Robert Arryn, the sickly Lord of the Vale) and Littlefinger's political games — the tourney, the lords of the Vale, the long game of positioning. Her arc is building toward something, but the published books have not yet shown what. [book_asoiaf]

In the show, Sansa's Vale arc is compressed significantly. She is then sent to Winterfell as "Arya Stark" to marry Ramsay Bolton — a show invention [show_s5_8]. In the books, this plot involves a different character entirely (Jeyne Poole, pretending to be Arya). Sansa does not go to Ramsay in the books. [book_asoiaf]

[spoiler_tier: show_complete]
## Queen in the North

Show Sansa survived Ramsay Bolton's abuse, escaped with Theon's help, reunited with Jon, fought at the Battle of the Bastards, and secured the Vale's intervention through Littlefinger. She had Littlefinger tried and executed with Arya as the blade. She was present at the Great Council and refused the independent North's membership in the new kingdom. She became Queen in the North. [show_s5_8]

This is the show's resolution. The book arc is unresolved.
