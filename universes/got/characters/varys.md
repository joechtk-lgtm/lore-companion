# Varys

## Overview

Varys is the Master of Whisperers on the Small Council, the man who knows everything and is owed by no one. He was born a slave, was sold to a sorcerer who castrated him in a magical ritual, heard a voice speak from his severed parts, and decided that the world was deeply interested in causing him harm and he would have to be cleverer than it. He built a network of informants — "little birds," orphan children trained to gather information — and made himself indispensable to a succession of kings. His stated purpose is always the realm: he serves the realm, not the king, not the great houses, not himself. Whether this is true is the question that defines him.

## Key Facts

- **Title:** Lord Varys, Master of Whisperers. [book_asoiaf]
- **Background:** Born in Essos, a slave. Sold to a sorcerer-entertainer. Castrated in a magical ritual; the voice that spoke from the flames during the ritual was what set Varys on his path — he has a genuine terror of magic, and a genuine hatred of those who use it. [book_asoiaf]
- **His birds:** Children, usually orphans from Flea Bottom or equivalent places in Essos — trained from young enough that they don't read or write, only whisper to Varys what they have heard. He has birds in every court. [book_asoiaf]
- **His wigs:** He wears different wigs and identities, maintaining the fat, bald eunuch persona as a kind of armor. When he chooses to present otherwise, no one recognizes him. [book_asoiaf]
- **His stated allegiance:** The realm. The small folk. The millions of people who suffer when kings play the game badly. He is genuinely revolted by gratuitous cruelty. This does not mean he is good. [book_asoiaf]

## Relationships

- **Illyrio Mopatis:** His partner since their days in Essos — a merchant prince of Pentos who sheltered Daenerys and Viserys. They have been working together for decades. [book_asoiaf]
- **Littlefinger:** His counterpart. They dislike each other with the precision of men who understand exactly what the other is. [book_asoiaf]
- **Tyrion:** He protected Tyrion, arranged his escape, accompanied him to Essos. The protection was partially affection and partially utility. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]
- **Ned Stark:** He tried to warn him. He was the one who advised Ned to confess, to take the black, to live. He understood what would happen. Ned did not take the advice in time. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## The Spider's Work

Varys served Aerys II, then Robert Baratheon, then Joffrey/Cersei. He appeared to serve each king while actually maintaining the information advantage that allowed him to survive any transition. He told Ned that he served the realm. He intervened to prevent various cruelties when he could do so without exposing himself. He did not prevent Ned's execution, which he claims he could not have stopped — and which may be true, given Joffrey's defiance of his own advisors. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## The True Allegiance

The books reveal Varys's true allegiance more explicitly than the show at this point. At the very end of A Dance with Dragons, Varys appears to kill Kevan Lannister — Tywin's brother, who had been effectively stabilizing Cersei's chaotic governance. His little birds kill Pycelle too. Varys explains: he has been working all along to restore the Targaryen dynasty. Not Daenerys — in the books, he has been positioning a different candidate: Aegon Targaryen, supposedly Rhaegar's son, who was believed killed as an infant but (according to Illyrio and Varys) was spirited away and replaced with another baby. [book_asoiaf]

This "Young Griff" / fAegon plot is book-only. The show does not include this character. Whether "Aegon" is genuine or a Blackfyre pretender is one of the great unresolved mysteries of the book series. [book_asoiaf]

## In the Show

In the show, Varys's allegiance is to Daenerys after he accompanies Tyrion to Essos. He is her advisor. He eventually begins to doubt her after the burning of King's Landing — he is seen writing letters, apparently to warn lords of Jon's Targaryen claim as an alternative. Daenerys executes him for treason by dragonfire before the assault on King's Landing. [show_got_s5_8]

The show's version of Varys's arc is significantly compressed and his "fAegon" plot is absent. His death is earlier and more decisive than the books suggest for his equivalent moment.

## What He Was

Varys was the character who most explicitly asked the question the series circles: what is power? Power is where men believe it resides. He told Tyrion this with a riddle: a king, a high priest, and a rich man each order a sellsword to kill the other two. Who lives? The sellsword decides. Power resides where men believe it resides. A shadow on the wall, yet shadows can kill. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

He was not wrong. He was also not as in control of his shadows as he believed.
