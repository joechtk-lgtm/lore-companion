# Rhaenyra Targaryen

## Overview

Rhaenyra Targaryen was her father's named heir, the first woman in Westerosi history publicly designated to inherit the Iron Throne — and then she was not. She is remembered by different names depending on who is doing the remembering: "The Realm's Delight" for the early years when she was her father's celebrated princess; "The Half-Year Queen" for her brief reign in King's Landing; the crueler epithet "Maegor with teats" from her enemies during the war. She rode Syrax — a she-dragon, yellow-gold, bound to her since girlhood. She fought a civil war for a throne she had been sworn to her entire life. She lost it, won it back, and lost it again. She was executed by her half-brother Aegon II, fed to his dragon in the dragonpit. She was thirty-three. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]

## Key Facts

- **House:** Targaryen. Daughter of King Viserys I and Queen Aemma Arryn. [book_fire_blood]
- **Dragon:** Syrax — a yellow female dragon she had ridden since childhood. Syrax died during the fall of King's Landing, released into the city and killed in the chaos. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **First marriage:** Laenor Velaryon — a political match engineered to secure the Velaryon fleet. Laenor preferred men; the marriage was unconsummated in any meaningful sense. Her three sons from this marriage — Jacaerys, Lucerys, Joffrey — were widely rumored to be bastards fathered by Ser Harwin Strong. She never confirmed or denied it. [book_fire_blood]
- **Second marriage:** Daemon Targaryen, her uncle. After Laenor's death (under circumstances that remain murky), she married Daemon. Their sons: Aegon the Younger and Viserys. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Her faction:** The Blacks — named for Targaryen black and gold. Her seat was Dragonstone. [book_fire_blood]

## Relationships

- **Viserys I:** Her father, who loved her and named her heir and left her without the political groundwork to survive what came after. His death triggered the war she had spent years trying to prevent through diplomacy. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Alicent Hightower:** In the show, they were once close friends before circumstances and ideology separated them entirely. Their conflict is personal as well as political — two women maneuvering within a system that would grant formal power to neither, acting through their children. In the books, the friendship is less developed. [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Daemon Targaryen:** Her uncle, her ally, eventually her husband. He was her most formidable military asset and also a source of constant volatility. He loved her after his fashion. He died at the Gods Eye lake fighting Aemond Targaryen and Vhagar. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Jacaerys Velaryon:** Her eldest son and heir. He died at the Battle of the Gullet. The losses accumulated until there were no more sons left to lose. [book_fire_blood]

## The War

Rhaenyra learned of her father's death and her half-brother's coronation at the same time. She was at Dragonstone. She convened her council and prepared for war.

The early years of the Dance favored the Blacks in certain respects — they had more dragons and more dragonriders, and Rhaenyra had secured important alliances. But losses mounted faster than replacements could be found. Lucerys Velaryon was killed by Aemond above Shipbreaker Bay. Jacaerys was killed at the Battle of the Gullet. Her son Joffrey, trying to claim an unbroken dragon, was thrown and died. Blood and Cheese killed Aegon II's son in the Red Keep. The war ground on.

Rhaenyra eventually took King's Landing. She sat the Iron Throne for half a year. The city rose against her — food shortages, chaos, the smallfolk turning violent. Her dragon Syrax was killed. She was forced to flee, betrayed by allies, reduced from queen to fugitive. [book_fire_blood]

## Death

She was captured and brought before Aegon II. She was fed to Sunfyre, Aegon's maimed and barely-alive dragon, in the dragonpit. It took some time. Aegon II made her surviving son Aegon the Younger watch.

The war continued after her death. Aegon II himself died shortly afterward — poisoned, most historians believe, by his own supporters who simply wanted it to end. The throne passed to Aegon III, Rhaenyra's son, who ruled for decades. The Blacks had lost the queen they were fighting for. They won the succession anyway. [book_fire_blood]
