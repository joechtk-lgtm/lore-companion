# Theon Greyjoy

## Overview

Theon Greyjoy is a man who never belonged anywhere. He was taken from the Iron Islands as a ward — a hostage — after the Greyjoy Rebellion, when his father Balon rose against Robert and was crushed, and Theon was handed over as insurance against future rebellion. He grew up in Winterfell among Starks, never fully Stark, never trusted by them entirely, never fully trusted by himself. When he returned to the Iron Islands to prove himself, he found he was not fully Ironborn either. He made catastrophic decisions in the middle ground between identities, and those decisions led him somewhere far worse than either.

## Key Facts

- **House:** Greyjoy of Pyke. Words: "We Do Not Sow." Sigil: Golden kraken on black. [book_asoiaf]
- **Position in story:** Ward (hostage) of Winterfell from age nine. Grew up among the Starks, closest to Robb. [book_asoiaf]
- **His father:** Balon Greyjoy — old, bitter, who views Theon's years among the Starks as contamination. "We are ironborn. We take what is ours." [book_asoiaf]
- **Ironborn culture:** "The Old Way" — raiders, reavers, those who take rather than trade. Balon's obsession. The Iron Islands are poor; the Old Way was how they had always survived. [book_asoiaf]
- **Reek:** The name Ramsay Bolton imposed on him after his capture. The name was a former flayed servant of Ramsay's. Taking Theon's name was part of a systematic process of identity destruction. [book_asoiaf]

## Relationships

- **Robb Stark:** His friend, or close enough to it. The closest relationship he had before the Rebellion. His betrayal of Robb is the central moral event of his arc. [book_asoiaf]
- **Yara/Asha Greyjoy:** His sister. (Asha in the books; renamed Yara in the show to avoid confusion with Arya.) She comes to rescue him from the Dreadfort and he refuses — "Reek" cannot accept that he is still Theon. She leaves. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8 for specific scenes]
- **Ramsay Bolton:** His captor and tormentor. Ramsay systematically destroyed Theon's identity — physical torture, psychological breaking, the renaming. Theon became Reek. [book_asoiaf]

## The Capture of Winterfell

Theon returned to Pyke to propose an alliance between Robb Stark's rebellion and the Iron Islands, hoping to use his connections to win Ironborn ships for the cause. Balon refused and declared his own independence. He gave Theon's command to Yara and sent Theon to take small fishing villages on the coast. Theon, desperate to prove himself, pivoted. He attacked Winterfell — lightly defended — with a small force and took it.

He held it for a time. Maester Luwin counseled surrender. Theon knew he couldn't hold it but couldn't admit he'd made a mistake. He faked the execution of two boys (actually miller's sons) as Bran and Rickon. He was betrayed by his own men to Ramsay Bolton's forces, who had arrived to take the castle. Ramsay burned Winterfell and took Theon captive. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## Reek

Ramsay Bolton tortured Theon systematically — not for information but for the process itself. He was renamed Reek. The Theon Greyjoy who captured Winterfell and made catastrophically bad decisions was dismantled piece by piece. He became genuinely Reek — unable to remember that he was Theon, unable to respond to the name, conditioned to serve. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8 for show's depiction]

[spoiler_tier: dance_of_dragons]
## The Breaking of Reek

In the books, Theon was used by Ramsay to legitimize his claim through the "Arya" marriage — he stood at the wedding (it was Jeyne Poole pretending to be Arya, not the real Arya [book_asoiaf]). He eventually began to reassemble himself when Bran's old friend Mance Rayder arrived (disguised) and helped rescue "Arya." Theon jumped from the walls of Winterfell with her, into the snow. He began to be Theon again. [book_asoiaf]

In the show, the equivalent arc involved the real Sansa (not Jeyne Poole) and ended with Theon and Sansa jumping from the walls together. The show condensed and modified the details considerably. [show_got_s5_8]

He returned to the Iron Islands. Balon was dead (killed by Euron Greyjoy, his brother). He supported Yara's claim in the kingsmoot and lost to Euron. He and Yara fled with the best ships and allied with Daenerys. [show_got_s5_8]

[spoiler_tier: show_complete]
## Death at Winterfell

Theon escorted Bran to the godswood during the Battle of Winterfell as a decoy for the Night King. He charged the Night King alone when all hope seemed lost. The Night King caught his spear. Arya killed the Night King before Theon died. He died in the godswood, Bran thanking him, a Stark and not quite a Stark to the end. [show_got_s5_8]

The book arc for Theon after his escape from Winterfell is unresolved.
