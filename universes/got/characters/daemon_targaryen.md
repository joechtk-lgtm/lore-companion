# Daemon Targaryen

## Overview

Daemon Targaryen is the younger brother of King Viserys I — a man who seems genuinely most alive when something is burning. He is brilliant, vicious, charismatic, and almost impossible to manage. He held the City Watch of King's Landing into something effective through methods that made the smallfolk afraid of him and the lords more afraid still. He carved a kingdom from the Stepstones through sheer martial force when the realm would not give him a proper command. He is also the only person who loved Rhaenyra without reservation and without calculation, which in a story full of people using other people is notable. He rode Caraxes, the Blood Wyrm — a lean, serpentine red dragon that looked hungry even at rest. He died at the Gods Eye, dragging Aemond Targaryen into the lake with his own hands. ## Key Facts

- **House:** Targaryen. Prince. Viserys I's brother. [book_fire_blood]
- **Dragon:** Caraxes — the Blood Wyrm. A red, serpentine dragon, unusually long and lean. Ferocious in combat. He died at the Gods Eye fighting Vhagar, killing her in the process. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **The City Watch:** He commanded the City Watch of King's Landing and transformed it into an effective force — by means including public brutality that scandalized the small council. He armed them in gold cloaks. They are still called the Gold Cloaks centuries later. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **The Stepstones:** He led a campaign against the Triarchy (a confederation of Essos free cities) who had seized the Stepstones, the island chain between Westeros and Essos. He won. He briefly styled himself King of the Narrow Sea, to Viserys's irritation. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Marriages:** First to Rhea Royce of the Vale — an arranged marriage both of them despised. She died during his tenure at the Stepstones under circumstances that remain suspicious. Then to Laena Velaryon (who rode Vhagar), who died giving birth. Then to Rhaenyra, his niece. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]

## Relationships

- **Viserys I:** His brother, whom he loved and resented in equal measure. Viserys had what Daemon wanted — the throne — and never fully understood what to do with it. They forgave each other repeatedly, and Daemon served him anyway, not out of duty but out of something more complicated. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Rhaenyra:** His niece, and eventually his wife. In the show, their relationship begins in complicated territory — she admires him, he sees her clearly, both of them recognize something in each other that the rest of the court does not understand. Their marriage was genuine, which in the Targaryen dynasty is unusual. [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Aemond Targaryen:** His eventual killer and the man he killed. Aemond rode Vhagar, the largest dragon alive. At the Gods Eye lake, Daemon dismounted in midair — jumped from Caraxes onto Vhagar's neck — and drove his Valyrian steel sword Dark Sister through Aemond's eye. Both men went into the lake. Caraxes survived long enough to crawl to shore before dying. Vhagar sank with Aemond. Daemon's body was never found. [book_fire_blood]

## The Dance

When the war began and Rhaenyra needed a general, Daemon was the most effective military commander in Westeros. He took Harrenhal — the massive, cursed, decaying castle that served no one well — through psychological pressure rather than assault, forcing the castle's garrison to simply abandon it. He coordinated with the river lords. He fought wherever the fighting was.

His most famous act in the war, and his darkest, was the Blood and Cheese affair: after Aemond killed Lucerys Velaryon (Rhaenyra's son), Daemon hired two assassins to kill Aemond. They could not reach Aemond — so they killed Aegon II's young son Jaehaerys instead, in the nursery, in front of his mother Helaena. "A son for a son." The act was Daemon's. The war became something that could no longer be negotiated after that. [book_fire_blood]

## Death at the Gods Eye

Daemon flew Caraxes to the Gods Eye — the massive sacred lake in the Riverlands, where the Isle of Faces sits in the center. He flew alone. He sent word that Aemond would meet him there.

Aemond came on Vhagar, the largest living dragon, far larger than Caraxes. What happened above the lake was witnessed from the shore. The two dragons fought. Caraxes seized Vhagar by the throat. Daemon, somehow, leaped from Caraxes onto Vhagar's neck while both dragons were falling. He drove Dark Sister through Aemond's eye. Both riders and both dragons went into the lake.

Caraxes crawled to shore and died there.

Vhagar and Aemond sank.

Daemon's body was never recovered. There are songs about what might have happened — that he survived, that he went somewhere — but there is no evidence of this. He was gone. [book_fire_blood]
