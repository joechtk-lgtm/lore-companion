# Daenerys Targaryen

## Overview

Daenerys Targaryen is the last surviving child of Aerys II, born in a storm at Dragonstone as her family fled the realm. She spent her childhood in exile in the Free Cities, controlled by her brother Viserys, who treated her as a means to reclaim the throne. She was sold to Khal Drogo. She survived Drogo's death and the loss of everything, and she walked into a funeral pyre and emerged with three dragons. What she became afterward — conqueror, liberator, ruler — is the story's most contested arc, because it is the story of someone who wanted to do good and discovered that power does not care about your intentions.

She is not simply a hero. She is not simply a villain. The question the story asks about her is whether the two are the same person, or whether one destroyed the other.

## Key Facts

- **House:** Targaryen. Words: "Fire and Blood." Sigil: Three-headed dragon, black on red. [book_asoiaf]
- **Titles accumulated:** Khaleesi of the Great Grass Sea, Breaker of Chains, Mother of Dragons, Queen of the Andals and the Rhoynar and the First Men, Mhysa, Daenerys Stormborn. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]
- **Her brother:** Viserys Targaryen — "the Beggar King," who sold her to Drogo for an army. Drogo killed him by pouring molten gold on his head. Daenerys did not grieve. [book_asoiaf]
- **Her dragons:** Drogon (black, named for Khal Drogo, the largest), Rhaegal (green, named for her brother Rhaegar), Viserion (cream and gold, named for Viserys). Hatched at Drogo's funeral pyre. [book_asoiaf]
- **Fire immunity:** The show implies she is fire-immune as a Targaryen trait. The books are more nuanced — her hands were burned at Drogo's pyre (she was not unscathed), and fire immunity may not be a reliable Targaryen characteristic. Martin has said it was a one-time magical event in the novels. [book_asoiaf vs show_s1_4]
- **Her capacity for violence:** Present from early on, but framed as liberation. She crucified 163 masters in Meereen (they had crucified 163 slave children on the road as a message). The tension between liberation and cruelty, between justified force and its uses, is the moral engine of her arc. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## Relationships

- **Khal Drogo:** Her husband by purchase who became genuinely loved. His death — from a wound gone septic due to a khaleen's blood magic, a magic Daenerys herself chose to use — was the rupture that ended one version of her life. [book_asoiaf]
- **Missandei:** Her translator, confidante, and one of her few genuine friendships. Freed from slavery in Astapor. [show_got_s1_4]
- **Ser Jorah Mormont:** Her first trusted advisor, who was initially spying on her for Robert Baratheon. His betrayal, when she discovered it, broke something between them that took a long time to partially rebuild. He loved her. She valued him. The distinction mattered. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]
- **Daario Naharis:** Her lover in Meereen. Useful. Not complicated by deep feeling on her part. [show_got_s1_4]

## The Hatching and the Path East

At Drogo's funeral pyre, with her husband dead and the khalasar dissolved and her unborn son killed by Mirri Maz Duur's magic, Daenerys walked into the fire with the three petrified dragon eggs she had carried since her wedding. She emerged with three dragons on her shoulders. No one had seen a living dragon in over a century. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

She crossed the Red Waste with the remaining Dothraki. She arrived in Qarth. She moved through the east: Astapor, where she bought the Unsullied with the promise of a dragon and then refused to pay and killed the masters; Yunkai, taken; Meereen, taken. Each city conquered and each city presenting a new problem — freedom requires governance, and governance requires compromises that complicate the clean narrative of liberation. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## Qarth and the House of the Undying

In Qarth, Daenerys encountered the warlocks of the House of the Undying, who stole her dragons. She retrieved them. The Undying showed her visions — in the books, complex and prophetic; in the show, simplified. The dragons burned the warlocks. Qarth offered her ships, briefly, before she had to take what she needed by other means. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

Her dragons were small at this point — the size of large cats. They grew.

## Meereen and the Problem of Ruling

She conquered Meereen and chose to stay — to learn to rule before attempting to rule Westeros. The Sons of the Harpy, a resistance movement of the old slaver class, began killing her Unsullied and Freedmen. She opened the fighting pits (a compromise that disgusted her). She was in a city that hated what she represented, struggling to hold order, when Drogon — now full-grown, enormous, uncontrolled — appeared at the fighting pit. She rode him for the first time. He flew her away. She was gone from Meereen for an extended period. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

In the books, her Meereen chapters in A Dance with Dragons end with her in the Dothraki Sea, having ridden Drogon far from the city. The situation in Meereen behind her is deteriorating. This is where the published books end.

[spoiler_tier: dance_of_dragons]
## Crossing to Westeros

In the show, Daenerys returned to Meereen, resolved the Sons of the Harpy situation, gathered her fleet (Yara and Theon's Iron Fleet plus the Tyrell and Martell fleets), and sailed for Westeros with her three dragons, the Unsullied, and the Dothraki. She arrived at Dragonstone — her ancestral seat. [show_got_s5_8]

In the books, as of A Dance with Dragons, she has not crossed to Westeros. She is in the Dothraki Sea, reunited with a new khalasar. The crossing has not yet happened in published material. [book_asoiaf]

## The Burning of King's Landing and Her Death

In the show, Daenerys won the war but lost what she was fighting for. Missandei was executed by Cersei; Rhaegal was killed; her Hand Jorah was dead. She burned the city after its bells rang surrender. She burned it anyway. Thousands of civilians died. [show_got_s5_8]

Jon Snow, who loved her and was also her nephew (a fact both knew by this point), killed her in the throne room before she could extend the burning to other cities. Drogon melted the Iron Throne and flew her body east. [show_got_s5_8]

This ending is the show's resolution. Martin has not published the equivalent material in the books. Whether Daenerys's arc ends here, or differently, or for different reasons, is unknown. The "mad queen" trajectory was present in the show's DNA for years; whether the books are heading there, or toward something else, is actively debated.
