# Aegon II Targaryen

## Overview

Aegon II Targaryen did not particularly want to be king. He was dissolute, indulgent, and possessed of enough intelligence to understand that he was not suited for what his mother and grandfather were pushing him toward. He accepted the crown anyway — and then defended it with a viciousness that surprised even his supporters. He rode Sunfyre, a golden dragon of unusual beauty who became as broken and maimed as his rider by the war's end. He won the Dance of the Dragons in the technical sense: he killed his half-sister Rhaenyra, fed her to Sunfyre in the dragonpit, and held the Iron Throne. He died approximately six months later, poisoned by his own supporters who had decided the war needed to end and that Aegon II's continuation was an obstacle to ending it. ## Key Facts

- **House:** Targaryen. Son of King Viserys I and Queen Alicent Hightower. [book_fire_blood]
- **Dragon:** Sunfyre — a golden dragon, uniquely beautiful, described as the most magnificent dragon since Balerion. He was severely burned by Rhaenyra's faction during the war. By the time he killed Rhaenyra, he could barely fly, sustained burns across much of his body. He died shortly after Aegon II. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **His faction:** The Greens — Hightower colors. He was the figurehead; his mother Alicent and grandfather Otto were the political operators behind the coronation. [book_fire_blood]
- **His character:** In the show, he is depicted as genuinely reluctant to be king — a man who understood the crown being placed on his head was being used against his will as much as for his benefit. He was not cruel by instinct. The war made him cruel. [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **His injuries:** He was severely injured during a battle at Rook's Rest — Sunfyre fought Rhaenyra's dragon Meleys, the Red Queen. Meleys was killed but Sunfyre was badly burned. Aegon himself was burned and broken by the fall. He spent much of the rest of the war recovering. [book_fire_blood]

## Relationships

- **Alicent Hightower:** His mother, who arranged his coronation and spent years managing the political machinery of the war. Her conviction in his cause was stronger than his own. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Aemond Targaryen:** His brother, who became the war's most effective military commander. Aemond rode Vhagar and held the Greens' offensive together while Aegon II recovered from his injuries. They respected each other as siblings without being close. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Rhaenyra:** His half-sister and rival claimant. He had her executed — fed to Sunfyre in the dragonpit, making her son Aegon (the Younger) watch. It was his most definitive act as king, and also his last meaningful one. [book_fire_blood]
- **Helaena:** His wife and sister (Targaryen tradition). The show suggests their relationship was uncomfortable — an arrangement neither chose. She was broken by the war; he may have cared for her more than he showed. [show_hotd_s1_2]

## The War

Aegon II's tenure as king was largely shaped by others — his mother, his grandfather, his brother Aemond — because his injuries kept him incapacitated for extended periods. He was not a passive king out of laziness but out of necessity. When he recovered enough to act, his decisions tended toward the brutal and the personal.

He refused to negotiate peace terms even when the strategic situation worsened. He had Rhaenyra's supporters executed with particular thoroughness. He demanded Rhaenyra's death as a precondition for any settlement long after it would have been strategically wise to accept different terms.

## Death

Aegon II was poisoned. The method, the specific person who administered it, and exactly when it happened are debated by the in-world historians of Fire & Blood — Martin's framing of the book as compiled from unreliable sources makes the truth uncertain by design.

What is clear: Aegon II died before he could name a successor other than Aegon the Younger (Rhaenyra's son, whom he was holding captive). His supporters, exhausted by years of war and recognizing that the realm needed resolution, arranged his death. The Dance of the Dragons ended with both original claimants dead and the throne passing to the child of the woman Aegon II had killed.

His reign lasted approximately two and a half years. His reign is not remembered fondly. [book_fire_blood]
