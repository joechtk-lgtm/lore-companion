# Melisandre of Asshai

## Overview

Melisandre is a Red Priestess of R'hllor, the Lord of Light — a faith built on fire and certainty and the war between light and eternal darkness. She is very old. Centuries old, possibly. The ruby at her throat is a glamour that hides what she actually looks like without it: when Davos takes it from her in the show, she collapses into an ancient crone. She does not know her own birth name. She was born a slave and knows nothing of her origins. What she does know is the Lord of Light, his fires, and his purpose — and she has been wrong about the specifics more than once, which she finds genuinely destabilizing.

## Key Facts

- **Origin:** Asshai, the city at the edge of the known world — a place no one from the west has seen clearly described. Dark, magical, strange. She does not talk about it. [book_asoiaf]
- **Her powers:** Genuine. She can see visions in fire. She can birth shadows from her own body — the "shadowbabies" require a man of king's blood as a partner. She can create glamours (false appearances). She can, apparently, raise the dead — though she does not fully understand how she did it with Jon. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8]
- **The ruby:** Her glamour. It conceals her true age. In the books, this is implied throughout; in the show, it is made explicit. [show_s5_8]
- **Her failures:** She believed Stannis Baratheon was Azor Ahai reborn — the prince that was promised. She was wrong. She persuaded him to burn his daughter Shireen for military victory at Winterfell. He lost anyway. Her certainty cost Shireen her life. [show_s5_8 for Shireen's burning; books have this happening differently/later]

## Relationships

- **Stannis Baratheon:** She believed in him completely and was wrong. Her faith did not waver in R'hllor; her identification of Stannis as the champion was the mistake. After his death, she began to see Jon in the flames. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8]
- **Davos Seaworth:** The one person around Stannis who consistently distrusted her. He saw her use as clearly as anyone and resented it. After Shireen's death, he hated her. He had her banished after Jon's resurrection. [book_asoiaf for dynamic, show_s5_8 for specific events]
- **Jon Snow:** She resurrected him. She had begun to see him in the flames before his assassination — she was at Castle Black partly because her visions told her to be. After the resurrection, she told him she didn't fully understand how she did it. [show_s5_8]

## Renly's Death and the Blackwater

She birthed a shadow using Stannis's king's blood — a creature shaped like Stannis that slipped into Renly's tent and killed him before his army could join the war against King's Landing. This removed Stannis's most popular rival and delivered Renly's fleet and part of his army to Stannis's cause. It did not save the Blackwater assault. She was not present at the Blackwater — Davos convinced Stannis to leave her behind, fearing her influence. The assault failed anyway. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

[spoiler_tier: red_wedding]
## After the Blackwater

She continued to burn sacrifices for R'hllor, to read the flames, to advise Stannis. Her influence over him deepened as his options narrowed. In the books, Edric Storm (Robert's bastard) is at Dragonstone and she wants his blood for a blood magic ritual; Davos engineers his escape. The show uses Gendry for this role. [book_asoiaf vs show_s1_4]

[spoiler_tier: dance_of_dragons]
## Castle Black

She accompanied Stannis to the Wall. When Stannis marched on Winterfell and burned Shireen, she was present, advising, certain. His army was destroyed in the snow. Brienne killed Stannis [show_s5_8] (in the books his fate at the end of ADwD is technically unknown, though the implication is his defeat).

At Castle Black, she saw Jon Snow in the flames. She attempted to resurrect him. Nothing happened for a long time. Then it did.

[spoiler_tier: show_complete]
## The End

After Jon's resurrection and the Battle of the Bastards, Davos discovered proof that she had burned Shireen. He confronted her and Jon and demanded her execution. Jon banished her instead — she could not return on pain of death. She walked south into the dawn after the Battle of Winterfell [show_s5_8], removing her ruby. She died of age, finally, after Arya killed the Night King. [show_s5_8]

The book arc for Melisandre is unresolved. A Winds of Winter sample chapter from her perspective has been released, showing her at Castle Black — it runs parallel to the Jon stabbing from her point of view. Her future in the books is unknown.
