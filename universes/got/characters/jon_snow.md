# Jon Snow

## Overview

Jon Snow believes he is the bastard son of Eddard Stark — born of an unnamed woman during Robert's Rebellion, a secret his father carried with visible discomfort and never explained. He grew up in Winterfell as half-Stark, loved by his father, resented by his stepmother Catelyn, excluded from family ceremonies. He joined the Night's Watch to find purpose and found it: first as a steward, then a ranger, then Lord Commander. He is the man who brought Wildlings through the Wall, was assassinated by his own brothers, was resurrected, and led the retaking of Winterfell. He is straightforward, honest, occasionally dense, and possessed of a conviction about doing the right thing that gets him killed — at least once.

## Key Facts

- **Birth name (unknown to himself until late):** Aegon Targaryen, son of Rhaegar Targaryen and Lyanna Stark. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8 for confirmation]
- **Raised as:** Jon Snow, bastard of Eddard Stark. [book_asoiaf]
- **Direwolf:** Ghost — a white direwolf, the odd one of the litter (as Jon is the odd one of the Stark children). [book_asoiaf]
- **Sword:** Longclaw, Valyrian steel, given to him by Jeor Mormont after Jon saved his life from a wight. A bear's head pommel, traditionally; Jon replaced it with a wolf's head. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]
- **His natural authority:** Something about him draws men to follow him. He does not try to be charming or commanding. People follow him anyway. This is either a Stark quality or a Targaryen one. Possibly both. [book_asoiaf]
- **His warging:** In the books, Jon has skinchanger tendencies — he slips into Ghost's consciousness. The show does not develop this. [book_asoiaf]

## Relationships

- **Ned Stark:** His father — not his biological father, but the man who raised him and loved him and died protecting his secret. [book_asoiaf]
- **Arya:** The sibling he was closest to. Both were the odd ones of Winterfell. He gave her Needle before she left for King's Landing. [book_asoiaf]
- **Ygritte:** The Wildling woman who became his partner when he infiltrated Mance Rayder's army. She loved him. He broke her heart by returning to the Watch. She died in the battle for Castle Black, killed by someone else's arrow in the show. [show_s1_4]
- **Sam Tarly:** His closest friend at the Wall. Cowardly but brilliant, loyal beyond reason. Jon protected him; Sam eventually repaid it. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]
- **Melisandre:** She misidentified Stannis as the prince that was promised, then began to see Jon in the flames. She resurrected him. He banished her for burning Shireen. [show_s5_8]

## The Night's Watch and Beyond the Wall

Jon was assigned to the stewards rather than the rangers — which his pride initially took as insult, until Sam pointed out that Jeor Mormont, the Lord Commander, was being given a personal steward, which meant he was being groomed for command. He ranged beyond the Wall with the Lord Commander's expedition. He encountered wights — the dead raised by the Others. He fought one in Castle Black itself, fire as the only reliable solution. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

He infiltrated Mance Rayder's Free Folk army on orders, spent months among the Wildlings, crossed the Wall with them. The experience changed him — he understood the Wildlings were not savages fleeing south for easy pickings. They were fleeing the Others. They understood the threat the Watch had not yet taken seriously.

## Lord Commander

After the Battle of Castle Black — the Wildling assault on the Wall, Mance's army attacking from both sides — Stannis's arrival broke the siege. Jon helped negotiate with Mance. He was elected Lord Commander by the Watch, defeating Ser Alliser Thorne.

He made the most controversial decision in the Watch's history: he opened the gates and let the surviving Wildlings through the Wall, settling them south of it. The Watch viewed this as betrayal. He viewed it as the only rational response to the coming threat — every Wildling who died beyond the Wall would rise again for the Others. The Wall's strength was already limited; adding thousands of corpses to the enemy's army made no sense.

The Watch did not agree. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

## Assassination and Resurrection

"For the Watch." Jon was stabbed repeatedly by his brothers — Ser Alliser Thorne and others, including Olly in the show (show_s5_8) or unnamed conspirators in the books [book_asoiaf] — and left for dead in the snow. The last ADwD chapter containing Jon ends with his stabbing; whether he died or survived is technically ambiguous in the text, though his resurrection was not surprising to readers.

Melisandre, still at Castle Black after Stannis's defeat, performed the ritual. Nothing happened immediately. Then he gasped. [show_s5_8 for the immediate mechanics; the book ritual is implied but not yet shown]

He came back. He was colder than he had been. He hanged the men who killed him, including Olly. He said his watch had ended — he had technically fulfilled his vow by dying — and he handed his command to Edd Tollett. He walked out of Castle Black. [show_s5_8]

## The Battle of the Bastards and Winterfell

Jon raised an army from the north with Sansa's help — Wildlings, a few houses. They faced Ramsay Bolton's larger and better-equipped force at Winterfell. The battle nearly destroyed them: Jon's forces were surrounded, crushing inward, almost finished. The Knights of the Vale arrived on Sansa's secret arrangement with Littlefinger. Ramsay was defeated. Jon beat him with his fists in the courtyard. Sansa fed him to his own dogs. [show_s5_8]

## True Identity and the End

Jon's true parentage was revealed first to Sam by Bran, then to Jon himself. His name is Aegon Targaryen. His claim to the Iron Throne is stronger than Daenerys's. He told her. She asked him not to tell anyone else. He told Sansa and Arya anyway. It reached Cersei through Varys. [show_s5_8]

He killed Daenerys in the throne room after the burning of King's Landing, choosing the realm over his love and his family. He was imprisoned. The Great Council chose Bran as king. Jon was sent back to the Wall — and beyond it, with the Free Folk, because the Watch still existed as a formality and his exile beyond the Wall was the political compromise. He walked north, into the trees, with Ghost and Tormund. [show_s5_8]

In the books, Jon's arc has not reached this point. He is dead (or not quite dead) at the end of ADwD. His parentage is known to readers (R+L=J is the most widely accepted fan theory, later confirmed by the show) but has not been revealed to any character in the published text.
