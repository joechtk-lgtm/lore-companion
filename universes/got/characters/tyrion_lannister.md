# Tyrion Lannister

## Overview

Tyrion Lannister is the youngest child of Tywin Lannister — a dwarf, a survivor, and one of the sharpest political minds in Westeros. His family treats him as a disgrace to be managed. He has spent his life compensating with intelligence, wine, reading, and a black humor that functions as both armor and genuine personality. He loves his family in spite of their contempt and despite knowing exactly what they think of him. He is not a villain. He is not a hero. He is a man who wants to be loved and has never quite been able to stop wanting it from the people least likely to offer it.

## Key Facts

- **House:** Lannister of Casterly Rock. [book_asoiaf]
- **Physical:** A dwarf — mismatched eyes (one green, one black in books; both green in the show). Blonde Lannister hair. The show (Peter Dinklage) diverges somewhat from the book description. [book_asoiaf]
- **His mother:** Joanna Lannister, who died giving birth to him. Tywin never forgave him for it. [book_asoiaf]
- **First wife:** Tysha — a girl Tyrion married for love as a teenager. Tywin forced him to watch the guards rape her, then told Tyrion she was a whore hired by Jaime to make him feel better. This was a lie. This lie defines Tyrion's relationship with his family. [book_asoiaf]
- **Known for:** Wine, books, whores. His own description of himself. Also for political acumen, tactical ingenuity, and a genuine capacity for loyalty when someone treats him decently.
- **His style:** He uses information, money, and alliances where others use force. He cannot fight, so he thinks. [book_asoiaf]

## Relationships

- **Tywin:** His father, who respects his intelligence and despises his person. Tywin used him when useful and sidelined him when not. Tyrion spent his life trying to earn acknowledgment and never received it. [book_asoiaf]
- **Cersei:** His sister, who blames him for their mother's death with a hatred that predates reason. Their relationship is mutual hostility punctuated by rare and grudging acknowledgment. [book_asoiaf]
- **Jaime:** His brother, the one person in his family who treats him as a person. Their relationship is the most complex Lannister bond — genuine affection, genuine guilt on Jaime's part about Tysha, and the moment when it all breaks. [book_asoiaf]
- **Shae:** His mistress in King's Landing, forbidden by Tywin (who tolerated whores but not love). What she represented to Tyrion, and what she did to him at his trial, is the key to understanding why Tywin died the way he did. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]
- **Pod:** Podrick Payne, his squire — saved his life at the Blackwater. One of Tyrion's few uncomplicated loyalties. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]
- **Bronn:** The sellsword who serves him for money and occasional genuine affection. Honest about both. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## Hand of the King

When Tyrion arrived in King's Landing as acting Hand (Tywin remained in the field), he found chaos: Cersei managing an unmanageable king, a treasury emptied, allies alienated. He began to clean house. He manipulated Cersei's allies against each other, removed the corrupt City Watch commander Janos Slynt, and began preparing the city's defenses against Stannis Baratheon's inevitable assault. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

He was better at the job than anyone acknowledged.

## The Battle of the Blackwater

When Stannis attacked King's Landing by sea, Tyrion's preparation was what saved the city. He commissioned a massive store of wildfire — the alchemist's volatile green substance, Greek fire by another name — and had it deployed in the Blackwater Rush. A chain sealed the river mouth; the wildfire detonated among the packed fleet. The harbor became fire.

Joffrey fled the battlements. Tyrion led a sortie through the tunnels, fighting in the field himself, rallying the men. He was nearly killed — by Ser Mandon Moore, who was bribed to do it (in the books, it is implied Cersei arranged this; the show is less specific). Podrick Payne saved his life. Tyrion was dragged away with his face slashed — half his nose removed in the books; a scar across his face in the show.

Tywin arrived with Tyrell reinforcements and broke the siege. Joffrey thanked him. Tyrion was moved to a lesser room to recover, stripped of his temporary Hand's power, and received not one word of gratitude. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## Trial, Betrayal, and Flight

Joffrey died at his wedding to Margaery Tyrell — poisoned. Tyrion held the cup. He was arrested. The trial that followed was Cersei's triumph and Tyrion's destruction: witness after witness, coached, testified to his crimes. Shae appeared and accused him. In the books she claims he forced her; in the show she delivers prepared testimony. Either way, it was the end.

Tyrion demanded a trial by combat. His champion Oberyn Martell lost against Cersei's champion, the Mountain. He was sentenced to death.

Jaime freed him from the cells. They parted. Tyrion did not leave cleanly: he went to Tywin's chambers, found Shae in his father's bed, strangled her with the Hand's chain, and then found Tywin on the privy. He picked up the crossbow Janos Slynt once used. He told his father a few things. He shot him twice.

"Wherever whores go" were Tywin's last words to him. They were also Jaime's answer when Tyrion asked what had happened to Tysha — the truth Jaime finally told him. That she had not been a whore. That she had loved him. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4 for broad strokes, detail varies]

He fled Westeros in a shipping crate, smuggled by Varys.

## Essos and the Road to Daenerys

Tyrion spent his time in Essos drunk, self-pitying, and increasingly purposeful. He encountered (and was kidnapped by) Jorah Mormont. He traveled through the ruins of Valyria. He was briefly enslaved. He demonstrated his worth to a Meereenese fighting pit. He reached Daenerys and became her advisor. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8 — show compresses significantly]

His value to her: he understands Westeros as an insider, understands how the great houses think, and has a political instinct she lacks. His limitation: he is still a Lannister, and Westeros will not forget it.

## Show Ending

Tyrion is captured after Daenerys burns King's Landing, imprisoned for freeing Jaime. In his cell he brokers the Great Council outcome: Bran Stark as king. He is appointed Hand of the King again. [show_got_s5_8]

This is the show's resolution. The book arc for Tyrion is unresolved. The Winds of Winter is unpublished. His trajectory in the books — still in Essos, still en route to Daenerys — has not yet reached this point.
