# Eddard "Ned" Stark

## Overview

Eddard Stark, Lord of Winterfell and Warden of the North, is the man the story uses to establish its rules — and then break them. He is honorable in a world that treats honor as a weapon to be turned against those who wield it. He is the closest thing the story has to a traditional hero, and his death in the first book/season is the signal that this story does not work the way traditional stories work. He arrived in King's Landing as Hand of the King believing that the truth, once discovered, would be sufficient. It was not.

## Key Facts

- **House:** Stark of Winterfell. Words: "Winter Is Coming." Sigil: Grey direwolf on white. [book_asoiaf]
- **Title:** Lord of Winterfell, Lord Paramount of the North, Warden of the North, Hand of the King (briefly). [book_asoiaf]
- **Siblings:** Lyanna (deceased), Benjen (Night's Watch). [book_asoiaf]
- **Wife:** Catelyn Tully, from Riverrun. A political match that became genuine. [book_asoiaf]
- **Children:** Robb, Sansa, Arya, Bran, Rickon — and, ostensibly, Jon Snow (bastard). [book_asoiaf]
- **His sword:** Ice, a Valyrian steel greatsword passed down through House Stark for generations. It was melted down by the Lannisters after his execution and reforged into two swords: Widow's Wail (Joffrey's) and Oathkeeper (given to Jaime, who gave it to Brienne). [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]
- **His religion:** The old gods of the forest. He prays alone in the godswood, not in a sept. [book_asoiaf]
- **His philosophy:** A man who cannot face death shouldn't pass the sentence. He executed criminals himself. [book_asoiaf]
- **His reputation:** Widely considered one of the most honorable men in the realm. His enemies did not disagree — they simply used it against him. [book_asoiaf]

## Relationships

- **Robert Baratheon:** His closest friend since boyhood, fostered together under Jon Arryn in the Vale. Robert trusted him above all others. Ned loved him and was disappointed in what he became. [book_asoiaf]
- **Catelyn:** A marriage arranged after Lyanna's death (Ned was originally meant to marry her sister Lysa, before his brother Brandon was killed). They grew genuinely close. Catelyn was his counsel and his constant. [book_asoiaf]
- **Jon Snow:** He raised Jon as his bastard, protecting his true identity at enormous personal cost to his own reputation. The lie followed him to his grave. [book_asoiaf]
- **Littlefinger:** Petyr Baelish was in love with Catelyn. He helped Ned in King's Landing while working against him from the first moment. Ned was warned; he did not listen carefully enough. [book_asoiaf]

## Arriving at King's Landing

Robert Baratheon rides to Winterfell to ask Ned to serve as Hand — Jon Arryn, the previous Hand, has died under suspicious circumstances. Robert needs someone he trusts in the position. Ned accepts reluctantly. He brings his daughters south. His sons remain in Winterfell. What he discovers in King's Landing is the thread Jon Arryn was pulling when he died: the royal children are not Robert's. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

The investigation leads him to the conclusion that Cersei's children are fathered by Jaime Lannister. The succession law is clear: Joffrey has no claim. Stannis Baratheon is the rightful heir.

Ned, being Ned, tells Cersei what he has found before acting on it — giving her a chance to flee with her children. She uses the time to strike first. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## Arrest, Confession, and Execution

When Robert died from a hunting wound (Cersei had the wine strengthened; Robert's squire Lancel kept refilling his cup [book_asoiaf]), Ned attempted to use the knowledge of Joffrey's illegitimacy to prevent his coronation. Littlefinger betrayed him at the critical moment — the gold cloaks Ned thought he had bought turned against him. He was arrested on charges of treason.

In the dungeons, he was offered a deal: confess to treason, take the black, and his daughters would be kept safe. He refused at first. Then Sansa's safety was placed before him. He confessed.

Joffrey had agreed to spare him. He did not spare him. On the steps of the Sept of Baelor, in front of the city and his daughters, Eddard Stark's head was removed from his body by his own sword, Ice, wielded by Ser Ilyn Payne. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

This was the moment the story announced its terms. The protagonist died in the first book. His death set the War of the Five Kings in motion — Robb called his banners, declared himself King in the North, and marched south.

## Legacy and the North's Grief

Ned's execution did not end the story his children now live inside. His death defined each of them:

- Robb went to war for him and died at the Red Wedding without achieving justice.
- Sansa was trapped in King's Landing for years, Ned's execution used to remind her of her position.
- Arya watched him die from the crowd and began her list.
- Bran and Rickon fled Winterfell when it was taken; Rickon eventually died at Ramsay Bolton's hand.
- Jon Snow carried his name and lived under his protection without knowing what that protection cost.

"The north remembers" is not simply a phrase. It refers to what was done to Ned Stark, to Robb Stark, to the honor of the north — and to the debt the realm owes for it. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## The Secret He Kept: R+L=J

The bastard Jon Snow was not Ned's son. He was the son of Lyanna Stark and Rhaegar Targaryen — born at the Tower of Joy in Dorne, where Ned and the last three knights of the Kingsguard fought their final battle. Ned won. Inside the tower he found Lyanna dying in a bed of blood, having given birth.

She made him promise. He took the child, rode home, and told Catelyn — and the world — that Jon was his bastard. He accepted the damage to his own honor, the questions about his faithfulness, the years of Catelyn's cold resentment, rather than let the truth be known. Because Robert would have killed the child. Robert built his entire claim on Targaryen guilt, on his love of Lyanna, on the story that the Targaryens took her. The truth — that she went willingly, that she loved Rhaegar, that her son lived — would have ended everything. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8 for confirmation]

Jon's true name is Aegon Targaryen. He is the son of a secret marriage between Rhaegar and Lyanna. He is, by law, the true heir to the Iron Throne.

Ned Stark carried this secret for seventeen years. He never told anyone. He died without telling anyone. The promise to Lyanna mattered more to him than his own reputation, than justice, than anything.

That is who Eddard Stark was.
