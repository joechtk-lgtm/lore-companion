# Stannis Baratheon

## Overview

Stannis Baratheon is the rightful King of the Seven Kingdoms. This is not a matter of opinion. Robert's children are illegitimate. By the laws of succession, the throne passed to the eldest surviving Baratheon brother when Robert died. That is Stannis. He has almost no personality in the conventional story-sense — no warmth, no charisma, no gift for inspiring love. He has duty, rigid honor, and a complete inability to bend. He knows he is right and he expects the world to acknowledge it, which is not how the world works.

George R.R. Martin has said Stannis is the character the show got most right — the most accurate translation from page to screen in the series. This is worth noting.

## Key Facts

- **House:** Baratheon of Dragonstone. Words: "Ours is the Fury." Sigil: Golden stag. [book_asoiaf]
- **His seat:** Dragonstone — given to him by Robert, who also denied him Storm's End (which went to Renly, the youngest, who had no legal right to it above Stannis). Stannis has never forgiven either assignment. [book_asoiaf]
- **His achievement during the Rebellion:** He held Storm's End through a year-long siege, starving, eating cats and shoe leather, refusing to surrender even as his men died around him. He did not receive recognition for this. Robert gave Storm's End to Renly. [book_asoiaf]
- **His relationship with his claim:** He does not want the throne. He knows this is irrelevant. It is his by law and he will claim it or die. Duty does not bend for want. [book_asoiaf]
- **His wife:** Selyse Florent, deeply religious (a fervent R'hllor convert), whom he does not love. Their daughter Shireen has greyscale on her face — it was stopped in time, leaving her scarred but alive. [book_asoiaf]
- **His daughter:** Shireen Baratheon — one of the story's most beloved minor characters. She taught Davos to read. She was burned alive at Melisandre's urging, for king's blood, to buy military victory at Winterfell. He lost anyway. [show_s5_8 for the burning; books have this differently/unresolved]

## Relationships

- **Davos Seaworth:** His Hand and the one person who will tell him the truth. Davos is a former smuggler Stannis had mutilated (removed the fingers he used to smuggle with) after the siege of Storm's End was broken by Davos's food run — and then knighted for the same act. Stannis values honesty too much to ignore the man who offers it, even when he dislikes hearing it. [book_asoiaf]
- **Melisandre:** He converted to R'hllor partly through her influence. He uses her powers tactically. Whether he believes in her faith is genuinely ambiguous — he believes in victory. [book_asoiaf]
- **Renly:** His younger brother, who claimed the throne with Tyrell support and no legitimate right. Stannis had him killed with shadow magic. He does not express regret about it. He expresses that it was necessary. The distinction matters. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

## The Five Kings

When Joffrey's illegitimacy was established (Ned's discovery, Stannis's own independent knowledge), Stannis wrote letters to every lord in the realm declaring the truth and his claim. He received almost no support. The lords chose the convenient fiction of Joffrey's legitimacy over the inconvenient reality of Stannis's. He attacked King's Landing by sea. He lost. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

His defeat at the Blackwater — the wildfire, the Tyrell arrival — broke his immediate military power. He retreated to Dragonstone.

[spoiler_tier: red_wedding]
## At Dragonstone

He sat at Dragonstone and contemplated options. His fleet was destroyed. His army was depleted. Jon Snow's ravens arrived, asking for help against the Wildlings. Davos eventually convinced him that the real war was in the north, and that the Iron Bank would fund him. He sailed north. He arrived at Castle Black and broke Mance Rayder's assault on the Wall in time to save the Watch. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

[spoiler_tier: dance_of_dragons]
## The March on Winterfell and the End

Stannis decided to march on Winterfell to destroy the Boltons and reclaim the north. In the show, Melisandre advised burning Shireen for the victory. He agreed. Shireen burned. His army was destroyed by the Boltons; the show implies many deserted after the burning. Brienne found him wounded and killed him in the snow after he acknowledged who he was. [show_s5_8]

In the books, the situation is more ambiguous. The Winds of Winter chapters released by Martin (as "sample chapters") show the battle still in progress, with the outcome uncertain. A manipulated Pink Letter was sent to Jon claiming Stannis was dead — but the Pink Letter's authenticity is actively disputed in the book. Stannis may yet be alive in the books at the point the story currently stands. [book_asoiaf]

The burning of Shireen in the books: she is at Castle Black, not with the army. Whether this burning happens in the books, when, or by whose hand is unknown.
