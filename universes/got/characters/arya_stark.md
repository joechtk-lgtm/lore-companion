# Arya Stark

## Overview

Arya Stark is the second daughter of Eddard and Catelyn Stark, the one who refused every mold available to her. She wanted to fight, not sew. She was nine when she watched her father die in front of her, standing in the crowd with a man's face pressed around her ears to muffle her protests. Everything since has been survival, movement, and the list of names she recites before sleeping. The list is both practical and psychological: it organizes the chaos of her world into something she can control. She will cross the names off, one by one, or she will die trying.

## Key Facts

- **Sword:** Needle — a thin sword made for her by Mikken of Winterfell, given to her by Jon Snow before she left for King's Landing. Light enough for her, quick enough for her style. She has lost it and found it. She refuses to give it up. [book_asoiaf]
- **Her list:** Cersei, Walder Frey, Meryn Trant, the Hound (removed), Tywin (crossed off when Tyrion killed him), Polliver, Rorge, Biter, the Mountain, Ilyn Payne, Joffrey (crossed off), and others. Names are added and removed as circumstances change. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]
- **Her teacher:** Syrio Forel, First Sword of Braavos — "What do we say to the god of death? Not today." He died (presumably) allowing Arya to escape Lannister guards. The specifics of his death are off-page in both book and show, which has fed considerable speculation. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]
- **Warging:** In the books, Arya is a skinchanger with a strong bond to Nymeria, her direwolf, which she wargs in her sleep while in Braavos. The show does not develop this. [book_asoiaf]

## Relationships

- **Jon Snow:** The sibling she was closest to. He gave her Needle and understood her in a way no one else in the family quite did. Their reunion in the show is earned. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s5_8]
- **The Hound:** Sandor Clegane held her hostage for a portion of the journey. It became something more complicated — mutual utility that shaded into grudging respect. She left him to die after his fight with Brienne. He was on the list. She could not kill him in the end. [show_got_s1_4]
- **Jaqen H'ghar:** The Faceless Man who first appeared in her story as a prisoner being transported north. He gave her three deaths, and then the iron coin and the phrase "Valar Morghulis." The coin was her passage to Braavos. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]
- **Nymeria:** Her direwolf, sent away when a Lannister demanded her death. In the books, Nymeria leads a massive wolf pack in the Riverlands and Arya wargs her. They are eventually reunited. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8 for brief reunion scene]

## Disguise and Harrenhal

After Ned's execution, Arya was cut from the crowd by Yoren of the Night's Watch, who was recruiting. She traveled north disguised as a boy, "Arry." The Night's Watch recruits were attacked; she escaped. She was captured eventually and taken to Harrenhal, the enormous ruined castle in the Riverlands, as a servant.

At Harrenhal she encountered Jaqen H'ghar again — one of three prisoners she had freed from a burning wagon during the attack. He owed her three deaths. She named: Chiswyck (a cruel guard), Weese (her master), and when she could not use the third effectively, she named Jaqen himself, forcing him to help her free prisoners from the dungeon. He helped. Then he changed his face in front of her, gave her the iron coin, and told her: "Valar Morghulis." [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

She escaped Harrenhal with Gendry and Hot Pie. The Brotherhood Without Banners found them. Melisandre arrived and purchased Gendry from the Brotherhood. Arya ran, was captured by the Hound, and began her strange journey with him toward Riverrun and the Red Wedding. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## The Red Wedding's Edge

Arya and the Hound arrived at the Twins during the Red Wedding. She heard the sounds from outside the walls. She did not enter. She saw the Grey Wind's body being paraded. She killed a man in the ensuing chaos — her first direct kill of a person, not a monster. The Hound knocked her out to prevent her from doing something fatal.

She recited her list. Robb Stark was on it now, but crossed off already, because some names go off the list in the worst possible way. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

## Braavos and the House of Black and White

She arrived in Braavos with the iron coin. She found the House of Black and White, the Faceless Men's temple. The man she had known as Jaqen was there, wearing another face. He gave her the terms: she must give up everything she is. She must be no one.

She could not fully do it. She still answered to "Arya." She still kept Needle hidden. She was given the gift of sight in the books (her eyes are removed as part of her training; she develops enhanced smell and touch, and eventually sees through cats, and through the eyes of the weirwood in her warging dreams). The show simplified her training into something more conventional. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s5_8]

She killed her first target for the Many-Faced God. She killed a second. She killed Meryn Trant in the show [show_got_s5_8] / Raff the Sweetling in the books [book_asoiaf] — a name from her list, not one the Faceless Men assigned her. She was blinded as punishment. She eventually was allowed to see again, left the House of Black and White, and reclaimed her identity. The books have not fully resolved where this leads. [book_asoiaf]

## Revenge and the End

In the show, Arya returned to Westeros and killed Walder Frey — baking his sons into a pie, then cutting his throat, wearing a face. She walked into Winterfell. She was reunited with Sansa and Bran. She killed Petyr Baelish on Sansa's command. [show_got_s5_8]

She killed the Night King at the Battle of Winterfell — leaping from ambush, deflecting his hand, delivering the blade to his throat. This is purely show canon [show_got_s5_8]; the Night King is a show creation and does not exist in the books in this form.

She sailed west — beyond the known maps of Westeros, into what lies beyond the edge of the world. [show_got_s5_8]

The book arc for Arya remains unresolved. The Winds of Winter is unpublished.
