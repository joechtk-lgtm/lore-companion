# Alicent Hightower

## Overview

Alicent Hightower is the daughter of Otto Hightower, Hand of the King — a woman who was brought to court as a companion for Princess Rhaenyra and became queen instead. She married King Viserys I, bore him four children, and spent her queenship maneuvering within a court where her father's faction and Rhaenyra's faction were pulling in opposite directions. She loved Viserys, in her way. She also believed, genuinely and with real conviction, that her son Aegon should be king rather than Rhaenyra — not only because it served her interests but because she believed in the tradition of male succession and because she thought Rhaenyra's character made her unfit to rule. Whether she was right about any of this is a question the Dance of the Dragons answered in the worst possible way for everyone. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]

## Key Facts

- **House:** Hightower of Oldtown. Daughter of Ser Otto Hightower. [book_fire_blood]
- **Marriage:** Queen consort to Viserys I Targaryen. Their marriage was arranged — her father's doing — but she was fond of Viserys, and he valued her company. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Children:** Aegon (II), Helaena, Aemond, Daeron — the Greens' claimants to the throne. Her eldest son Aegon became king. Her daughter Helaena married Aegon. Aemond became the war's most feared dragonrider. Daeron was lost to the war as well. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **The Greens:** Her faction, named for the green Hightower colors she wore. The political movement to crown Aegon II rather than honor Viserys's designation of Rhaenyra as heir. [book_fire_blood]
- **Her piety:** In the show, she is depicted as genuinely devout — a follower of the Seven with real faith, in contrast to the more pragmatic Targaryen approach to the gods. Her piety informs her conviction. [show_hotd_s1_2]

## Relationships

- **Rhaenyra:** In the show, they were friends as girls. Alicent was brought to keep Rhaenyra company; they read together, confided in each other, were close in the way that girls at court sometimes are before the court's pressures separate them. The friendship broke over politics, secrets, and the fundamental incompatibility of their interests. Neither fully forgave the other. [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Otto Hightower:** Her father, who arranged her marriage to the king and managed her positioning at court as a deliberate political project. She eventually came to understand how fully she had been maneuvered, and her relationship with him grew complicated. He was reinstated as Hand after Viserys dismissed him earlier. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Aegon II:** Her eldest son and her champion, though not the champion she might have wanted. Aegon was dissolute, reluctant, not particularly suited to kingship. She spent years pushing him toward a throne he did not much want. When he had it, he held it badly. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Helaena:** Her daughter, who married Aegon and became queen. The show portrays Helaena as gentle, dreaming, faintly prophetic — a woman broken by the war and its demands. Alicent loved her and could not protect her. [show_hotd_s1_2]

## The Coronation and the War

When Viserys I died, Alicent acted within hours. She and her father convened the small council and arranged the coronation of Aegon II before Rhaenyra could be informed. Whether Viserys's dying words were a final wish for Aegon's succession or an old man's confusion — mistaking his queen for his daughter — has never been resolved. Alicent believed he meant it. She said so. The act that began the Dance was this: one woman's interpretation of a dying man's mumbled words.

The war that followed was not what she had imagined. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]

## What the War Cost Her

By the war's end, Alicent had lost Aemond — killed at the Gods Eye. She lost Daeron. Her daughter Helaena died, likely suicide, after Blood and Cheese killed Helaena's son in front of her. Her son Aegon II was maimed, his dragon Sunfyre burned and broken.

Alicent herself survived. When Rhaenyra briefly held King's Landing, Alicent remained in the city and was imprisoned. When the Greens reclaimed it, she was freed. When Aegon II was eventually poisoned and Aegon III (Rhaenyra's son) took the throne, Alicent was kept alive.

She died in her chambers in the Red Keep, years later, when a plague swept through the city. She had outlived her husband, her sons, her daughter, and the cause she had fought for. The throne was held by the son of the woman she had spent her life opposing. [book_fire_blood]
