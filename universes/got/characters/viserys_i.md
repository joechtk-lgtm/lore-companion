# Viserys I Targaryen

## Overview

Viserys I Targaryen is the fifth king of the Targaryen dynasty — a man remembered as gentle, beloved, and catastrophically ill-suited for the one decision his reign demanded of him. He was not cruel. He was not stupid. He simply could not bear conflict and could not accept that love alone would not resolve the contradiction at the center of his family. He named Rhaenyra his heir. He married Alicent Hightower and had sons with her. He never reconciled these two facts. He kept promising himself there was still time. He was wrong.

He spent the last years of his life visibly decaying — a slow deterioration of flesh that consumed him piece by piece. He held the realm together through will and through the sheer force of everyone's reluctance to fight while the old king still sat the throne. When he died, that reluctance ended. ## Key Facts

- **Reign:** 103 AC to 129 AC — one of the longest Targaryen reigns, largely peaceful. [book_fire_blood]
- **Dragon:** Balerion the Black Dread's last rider. Balerion died during Viserys's reign; Viserys never claimed another. He said there would never be another Balerion. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **First wife:** Aemma Arryn — whom he loved genuinely. She died in childbirth delivering a son who also died, after years of miscarriages. Her death broke something in him that never fully healed. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **His heir:** Rhaenyra — named publicly, after Aemma's death, with lords summoned to swear oaths. He meant it. He never retracted it. He apparently believed, until his death, that the realm would honor it. [book_fire_blood]
- **His second wife:** Alicent Hightower — daughter of Otto Hightower, his Hand. Their marriage was political and personal at once. He had four children with her: Aegon, Helaena, Aemond, Daeron. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]

## Relationships

- **Rhaenyra:** His daughter and named heir. He adored her. He brought her to council, trained her in governance, shared with her the lore of their house including the secret of Aegon's prophecy about the Long Night. His failure was his refusal to confront the inevitable — that his sons' existence would be used against her claim. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Alicent Hightower:** His queen. He was fond of her. He did not see, or refused to see, that she was maneuvering for their sons. In his final years, barely conscious, he apparently mistook her for Rhaenyra and murmured that his wishes for succession still stood — a moment Alicent interpreted as an instruction to crown Aegon. Whether this was genuine confusion or not is contested. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Daemon:** His brother — brilliant, dangerous, impossible to manage. Viserys briefly named Daemon heir before settling on Rhaenyra. He banished Daemon multiple times. He always forgave him. They had a complicated love neither of them articulated well. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]
- **Otto Hightower:** His first Hand, Alicent's father. Otto warned him about Daemon repeatedly. Otto also arranged the introduction to Alicent, which Viserys came to resent when he understood it as manipulation. He eventually dismissed Otto as Hand. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]

## Death and Aftermath

Viserys I died in 129 AC after a long illness that had been consuming him for years — missing fingers, an infected arm, a body failing faster than his will. He died in his bed, in the Red Keep, likely believing that his wishes for succession were known and would be honored.

They were not honored for long. Alicent and the small council, led by her father Otto Hightower who had been reinstated, crowned Aegon II the same day. Rhaenyra, at Dragonstone, was not informed. By the time she learned her father was dead, her half-brother was already king.

The war that Viserys spent his entire reign trying to prevent began immediately after his death. [book_fire_blood] [show_hotd_s1_2]

## The Prophecy He Carried

According to the show, Viserys carried a secret first revealed to Aegon the Conqueror: a prophecy known as "The Song of Ice and Fire" — a vision of the Long Night returning, and the belief that a Targaryen must sit the Iron Throne when it comes, to unite the realm against the darkness. This was the reason Aegon Conquered Westeros. Viserys passed this to Rhaenyra alone.

In the books, this is not explicitly stated in the same way — Fire & Blood does not contain this framing. It is a show invention that frames the entire Targaryen dynasty as motivated by prophetic obligation. [show_hotd_s1_2]
