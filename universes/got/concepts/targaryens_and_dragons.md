# Targaryens and Dragons

## Overview

For three hundred years, the Targaryens ruled Westeros because they had dragons and no one else did. The relationship between House Targaryen and their dragons was not merely ownership — it was a bond, a partnership, something between rider and mount that changed both. When the dragons died, so did something essential about the Targaryens. When Daenerys hatched three new dragons from eggs everyone thought were dead stone, something that had been absent from the world for over a century returned. The dragons are not weapons. They are not pets. They are participants in the story.

## Key Facts

- **Why Targaryens can ride dragons:** Valyrian blood — the Targaryens come from the Freehold of Valyria, where dragonlord families practiced magic and bred dragons for centuries. The specific nature of this connection (magical, genetic, both) is not mechanically explained in the books, but it is consistent: Valyrian blood is what matters. Bastards with Valyrian blood can potentially ride dragons. [book_asoiaf]
- **The bond:** A dragon chooses a rider. Once bonded, the dragon responds to that rider's voice, carries them willingly, and will not accept another rider while the bond holds. The bond is life-long. A dragon's death can shatter the rider psychologically; riders have been known to die with their dragons. [book_asoiaf] [book_fire_blood]
- **Dragon intelligence:** Dragons understand their names and the voice of their rider. They understand commands. They can be trained to some extent. They are not animals. They are also not humans. Their intelligence is real and present and alien. [book_asoiaf]
- **Fire immunity:** Targaryens have some degree of fire resistance. This is not absolute, not reliable as a trait, and not an inherent species characteristic in the books — Martin has said Daenerys's survival of Drogo's pyre was a unique magical event, not evidence of general Targaryen fire immunity. The show presents it differently [show_s1_4 onwards], showing her handling fire and hot metal repeatedly. Book canon is more cautious. [book_asoiaf]

## Daenerys's Three Dragons

- **Drogon:** The largest, black with red markings, named for Khal Drogo. Daenerys's primary bond. Instinctively connected to her in a way the others are not — he is described as "the black beast" in the books, wild and large and difficult to control. When he appears in Meereen's fighting pit, something in him responds to her distress. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]
- **Rhaegal:** Green with bronze highlights, named for Rhaegar Targaryen. Bonded primarily to Jon Snow in the show [show_got_s5_8]. Killed by Euron Greyjoy's scorpion bolts in the show [show_got_s5_8]. Book status: still alive in published material. [book_asoiaf]
- **Viserion:** Cream and gold, named for Viserys. Killed by the Night King beyond the Wall in the show [show_got_s5_8], reanimated as an ice dragon. Used to destroy the Wall. Book status: alive in published material. [book_asoiaf]

## The History of Valyrian Dragonriding

The Valyrian Freehold had many dragonlord families — the Targaryens were not unique among Valyrians in their dragon bonds, merely unique in surviving the Doom. Aegon had three dragons for his Conquest. His sisters rode with him. The number of dragons fluctuated through the Targaryen dynasty: new eggs hatched, young dragons died, relationships formed between riders and mounts across generations. [book_asoiaf] [book_fire_blood]

The Dance of the Dragons killed most of them. Dragon fought dragon. The survivors were small, sickly, increasingly inbred. The last Targaryen dragon died small in the reign of the last Targaryen kings before Robert's Rebellion. The knowledge of how to train and bond with dragons was not entirely lost, but the animals themselves were gone. [book_fire_blood]

Daenerys's three were the first in over a century. They were also growing fast — by the show's later seasons, they are city-destroying weapons. By the end of A Dance with Dragons (the last published book), they are already enormous. [book_asoiaf]

## The Three-Headed Dragon

The Targaryen sigil is a three-headed dragon — Aegon and his two sisters. In the books, there is a prophecy referenced by characters like Rhaegar and the House of the Undying: "The dragon has three heads." Three riders for three dragons. The identity of the three heads in the current story is one of the great book mysteries. Daenerys is one. Jon Snow (a Targaryen by blood) is likely another. The third has been theorized but not confirmed. [book_asoiaf]
