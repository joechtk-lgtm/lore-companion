# Braavos

## Overview

Braavos is one of the nine Free Cities of Essos — the most powerful, in many respects, because it controls the Iron Bank, which finances the operations of kings, wars, and trade routes across the known world. It is also the only city that was founded in secret. Braavosi history says their ancestors were slaves who escaped from Valyrian ships and settled the lagoon at the northern tip of Essos, hiding their city behind fogs and islands for a century before revealing themselves to the world. Their hatred of slavery is foundational to their identity. When Daenerys came to them as the Breaker of Chains, the connection had resonance the show does not fully articulate.

## Key Facts

- **Location:** A lagoon on the north coast of Essos, connected to the sea by a channel guarded by the Titan. [book_asoiaf]
- **The Titan of Braavos:** An enormous statue — a warrior standing astride the channel entrance, through whose legs ships must pass to enter. It is many times the height of any tower in Westeros. It roars in fog or storm, a sound heard for miles. [book_asoiaf]
- **Canal city:** Built on hundreds of islands connected by canals and bridges. No horses — transport is by gondola, foot, and boat. Cats are everywhere; the city is famous for them. They keep the rats manageable. [book_asoiaf]
- **No slavery:** Braavos was founded by escaped slaves and has never permitted the institution. This makes it unusual among the Free Cities and fundamentally at odds with the Slaver's Bay cities to the east. [book_asoiaf]
- **"Valar Morghulis" / "Valar Dohaeris":** High Valyrian phrases ubiquitous in Braavos. "All men must die" / "All men must serve." The reciprocal greeting of the city. Both an acknowledgment of mortality and, in the context of the Faceless Men, a statement of professional purpose. [book_asoiaf]

## The Iron Bank

The Iron Bank of Braavos is one of the most powerful institutions in the world. Its vaults hold gold that dwarfs what most kings control. It lends money to governments, merchant consortiums, and individuals on a scale that makes the Lannisters look like a local lender. Its ledgers are its holy texts.

The Bank does not forgive debts. It does not forget them. The phrase attributed to it: when kings default, the Iron Bank funds their enemies. The Baratheon crown was deeply in debt to the Iron Bank. Stannis Baratheon borrowed from it to fund his northern campaign. [book_asoiaf] [show_s1_4]

## The House of Black and White

The Faceless Men's temple in Braavos, open to anyone who comes to it. Located on an island accessible by boat. The building is half black stone, half white — two sides of the same structure. Inside: a great hall with alcoves containing images of different gods (all the same god), candles, and the pool of the Many-Faced God where the dead are brought. Below: the hall of faces, and the places where the Faceless Men work. [book_asoiaf]

Anyone may enter and ask for the gift. The price is always extracted. The poor get nothing, because they have nothing to give. The very poor sometimes receive the gift for free, as genuine mercy for those who are suffering beyond help. The Faceless Men make this distinction quietly. [book_asoiaf]

Arya's time in Braavos was centered on the House of Black and White. See Arya Stark and Faceless Men entries for detail. [book_asoiaf] [show_s5_8]

## The Mummers and the Theaters

Braavos has a theatrical tradition — the Mummers, performers who put on plays, including historical dramas about the events of the wider world. In A Feast for Crows, Arya encounters a troupe performing a play about Joffrey's reign — from the perspective of Joffrey as heroic king and Tyrion as murderous villain. The play's version of history is instructive about how stories become propaganda, and how quickly. [book_asoiaf]
