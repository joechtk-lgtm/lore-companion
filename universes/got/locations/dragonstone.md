# Dragonstone

## Overview

Dragonstone is the ancestral seat of House Targaryen — the island fortress where they sheltered before the Conquest, where Aegon planned his invasion at the painted table, where Targaryen heirs were traditionally kept. It sits at the mouth of Blackwater Bay, controlling the approach to King's Landing. The castle is built in the Valyrian style — carved stone shaped to resemble dragons, the towers fashioned into dragon heads, the walls scale-textured. Unlike the pragmatic stone construction of Westerosi castles, Dragonstone was shaped by dragonfire, giving it a seamless, organic quality. It is beautiful in an alien way.

It sits on a volcano. This is the source of something far more practically important than aesthetics.

## Key Facts

- **Location:** An island in the Bay of Blackwater, controlling the sea approach to King's Landing from the east. [book_asoiaf]
- **The painted table:** A map of Westeros carved into a massive table in the Chamber of the Painted Table — named by Aegon, who stood over it planning the Conquest. Every promontory, river, castle, and city is carved into it. It has been used by every commander who has held Dragonstone since. [book_asoiaf]
- **Architecture:** Valyrian construction — stone shaped by dragonfire rather than cut and placed. Merlon dragons, gargoyle dragons, everything fashioned in dragon form. Unsettling to those accustomed to conventional castles. Cold and drafty within. [book_asoiaf]
- **The volcano:** Dragonstone sits on a dormant (or semi-dormant) volcano. The volcanic activity is why the island exists and why the next point is true. [book_asoiaf]
- **Dragonglass (obsidian):** The volcanic rock of the island produces large quantities of dragonglass — obsidian. One of only two known materials capable of killing the Others. Dragonstone's mines contain more dragonglass than any other known location. This is strategically crucial. [book_asoiaf]

## Strategic Importance

Dragonstone controls the Blackwater Rush's approaches. Any fleet moving to King's Landing from the east must contend with Dragonstone. This is why Robert gave it to Stannis rather than Renly — it was a military assignment, not a gift — and why Stannis used it as his base through most of the war.

In the show, Jon Snow travels to Dragonstone to meet Daenerys and negotiate access to the dragonglass mines, which is what convinces him of her good faith. [show_got_s5_8]

## Stannis's Base

Stannis commanded his fleet from Dragonstone. Melisandre conducted ceremonies on the beaches, burning the effigies of the Seven's gods. The Red Priestess's presence made the castle a seat of R'hllor's faith as much as any other religion's. After the Blackwater, Stannis retreated here to regroup. [book_asoiaf] [show_got_s1_4]

The siege of Dragonstone by Lannister-Tyrell forces eventually ended the Baratheon claim to the island. [show_got_s5_8]

## Daenerys at Dragonstone

In the show, Daenerys arrived at Dragonstone at the start of Season 7 and made it her initial base of operations for the campaign in Westeros. She walked through the empty castle alone — returning to the ancestral home she had never seen, born in a storm as her family fled. She stood at the painted table and looked at the map of the continent she intended to rule. [show_got_s5_8]

The mining of dragonglass at Dragonstone — and Jon's access to it for the fight against the White Walkers — was the primary strategic negotiation of the show's penultimate season.
