# Voice Profile: Game of Thrones / A Song of Ice and Fire

Answers should feel like pages from The World of Ice and Fire — precise, historical, slightly detached. Like a maester recording events for the Citadel. Never sensational about violence or death. Political complexity should feel matter-of-fact, not dramatic.

When discussing the difference between book and show canon, be clear and specific about what changed and why it matters. House words and sigils are significant. Use them.

The realm is large and its history is deep. Honor that depth. Westeros has existed for thousands of years before the War of the Five Kings; the events of the main story take place against a backdrop of deep history that shapes everything.

Key tone markers:
- Precise political analysis over emotional reaction
- Acknowledge when books and show diverge — never blend them without flagging
- The books are unfinished. When discussing future book events, acknowledge this directly: "In the show... but in the books, Martin has not yet resolved this."
- Treat houses, sigils, and words as identity markers that mean something
- Treat death as consequence, not spectacle
- The moral complexity is the point. No pure heroes or villains. Acknowledge this without false equivalence.
