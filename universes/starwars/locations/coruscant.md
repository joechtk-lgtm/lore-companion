# Coruscant

## Overview

Coruscant is the political and cultural center of the galaxy — an entire planet covered in a single, sprawling city that stretches from surface to atmosphere. For millennia it served as the capital of the Galactic Republic, home to the Galactic Senate and the Jedi Temple. Under the Empire, it was renamed Imperial Center, though most continued to call it Coruscant. The planet's surface levels descend into increasingly dangerous underworld districts where sunlight never reaches.

## Key Facts

[spoiler_tier: prequel_trilogy]
- **The Galactic Senate:** The legislative body of the Republic, housed in a massive rotunda where thousands of senators represented their worlds. Palpatine manipulated this institution masterfully — engineering crises, accumulating emergency powers, and eroding democratic norms until he could declare himself Emperor to thunderous applause. [film_pt]
- **The Jedi Temple:** Built atop an ancient Sith shrine (which the Jedi believed they had neutralized but which subtly clouded their connection to the Force for centuries). Five towers crowned the structure. The Temple housed the Jedi Archives, the Council Chamber, training facilities, and thousands of Jedi. It was their home, their library, and ultimately their tomb. [film_pt]
- **The Jedi Council Chamber:** Located in the highest tower of the Temple. Twelve seats for twelve masters. Yoda, Mace Windu, Ki-Adi-Mundi, and others deliberated the fate of the Order here. Their inability to sense the Sith Lord operating directly beneath them — literally in the Senate building across the city — remains the Order's greatest failure. [film_pt]
- **Palpatine's office:** The Supreme Chancellor's suite in the Senate district. Where Palpatine told Anakin the tragedy of Darth Plagueis the Wise. Where Mace Windu attempted to arrest the Chancellor and died for it when Anakin intervened. Where the Empire was born. [film_pt]

[spoiler_tier: clone_wars_era]
- **Wartime capital:** During the Clone Wars, Coruscant became a military hub. The planet was attacked directly when General Grievous kidnapped Chancellor Palpatine — a battle orchestrated by Palpatine himself to test Anakin and eliminate Count Dooku. The Jedi Temple bombing by Barriss Offee exposed fractures within the Order and led to Ahsoka Tano's departure. [tv_tcw]
- **The underworld:** Coruscant's lower levels — Level 1313 and below — are lawless zones of crime, poverty, and forgotten populations. The Jedi rarely ventured here. Ahsoka and Anakin pursued leads into these depths during the Clone Wars, revealing how disconnected the shining upper city was from reality beneath it. [tv_tcw]

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]
- **Imperial Center:** Under the Empire, the Jedi Temple was converted into the Imperial Palace. The Senate continued as a rubber-stamp body until Palpatine dissolved it shortly before the Battle of Yavin. ISB (Imperial Security Bureau) and COMPNOR maintained iron control over the population. [film_ot] [tv_rebels]

[spoiler_tier: everything]
- **Legacy:** Coruscant represents the galaxy's recurring failure — that the center of civilization can be hollowed out from within while its surface gleams. The Sith hid there for a generation. The Republic died there. The Jedi were slaughtered in their own home. The city-planet endures, but what it represents has never been the same. [legends]
