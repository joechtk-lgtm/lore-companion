# Mandalore

## Overview

Mandalore is the ancestral homeworld of the Mandalorian people, located in the Outer Rim. Once a lush world, centuries of warfare — both internal and external — scarred its surface into white desert wastes. The planet's history is defined by a tension between its warrior heritage and attempts at peaceful civilization, a conflict that ultimately led to its near-total destruction.

## Key Facts

[spoiler_tier: clone_wars_era]
- **The New Mandalorians:** By the Clone Wars era, Mandalore was governed by Duchess Satine Kryze, who led a pacifist movement that renounced the warrior ways. The capital city of Sundari was built inside a massive bio-dome on the ravaged surface. Satine maintained neutrality in the Clone Wars despite pressure from both the Republic and Separatists. [tv_tcw]
- **Death Watch:** A militant faction led by Pre Vizsla that sought to restore Mandalore's warrior traditions. They wielded the Darksaber — an ancient black-bladed lightsaber created by the first Mandalorian Jedi, Tarre Vizsla. Death Watch allied with Darth Maul's Shadow Collective to overthrow Satine. [tv_tcw]
- **Maul's takeover:** Darth Maul killed Pre Vizsla in single combat, claimed the Darksaber, and installed a puppet government. Satine was murdered by Maul to torment Obi-Wan Kenobi, who had loved her. Mandalore fell into chaos under Maul's rule — a criminal warlord on the throne of a civilization that had tried to leave war behind. [tv_tcw]
- **The Siege of Mandalore:** In the final days of the Clone Wars, Ahsoka Tano and Clone Commander Rex led a portion of the 501st Legion to liberate Mandalore from Maul. Bo-Katan Kryze (Satine's sister, a former Death Watch member who turned against Maul) fought alongside them. Maul was captured, but Order 66 struck during the aftermath. The Republic forces became Imperial forces overnight. [tv_tcw]

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]
- **Imperial occupation:** The Empire occupied Mandalore, exploiting its beskar resources and suppressing Mandalorian identity. Sabine Wren of Clan Wren — a Mandalorian and member of the rebel crew of the Ghost — recovered the Darksaber and eventually passed it to Bo-Katan Kryze to unite the clans against the Empire. [tv_rebels]

[spoiler_tier: new_republic_era]
- **The Great Purge:** The Empire enacted a devastating orbital bombardment and ground assault on Mandalore, killing or scattering the vast majority of the Mandalorian people. Survivors became refugees and wanderers. The surface was reduced to glass in places. This genocide is the defining trauma for the Mandalorian diaspora. [tv_mando]
- **The Children of the Watch:** Din Djarin belongs to an orthodox sect — the Children of the Watch — that follows strict adherence to the old creed, including never removing one's helmet in front of another living being. Most mainstream Mandalorians consider this an extreme interpretation. The Armorer leads this covert. [tv_mando]
- **Bo-Katan's quest:** Bo-Katan Kryze seeks to reclaim the Darksaber and reunite the scattered Mandalorian people. The Darksaber's rule states it must be won in combat — a complication when Din Djarin won it from Moff Gideon without intending to claim leadership. [tv_mando]

[spoiler_tier: everything]
- **Legacy:** Mandalore's story is a cycle: warriors who tried peace, peace that was destroyed by war, and survivors who carry the identity of a dead world. Whether the planet can ever truly be reclaimed — or whether Mandalore is now an idea rather than a place — remains unresolved. [tv_mando]
