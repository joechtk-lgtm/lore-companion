# Mustafar

## Overview

Mustafar is a small volcanic world in the Outer Rim, its surface a hellscape of lava rivers, erupting volcanoes, and toxic atmosphere. Mining operations extract valuable minerals from the molten rock, but the planet's true significance lies in what happened there: the final confrontation between Obi-Wan Kenobi and Anakin Skywalker, the moment the prequel tragedy reached its climax.

## Key Facts

[spoiler_tier: prequel_trilogy]
- **Separatist hideout:** In the final stage of the Clone Wars, Darth Sidious sent the newly christened Darth Vader to Mustafar to eliminate the Separatist Council — Nute Gunray, Wat Tambor, Poggle the Lesser, and the others — who had served their purpose. Vader slaughtered them all in the command center of a mining facility while lava flowed outside the windows. The Clone Wars ended not with a treaty but with a massacre. [film_pt]
- **The duel:** Obi-Wan Kenobi tracked Anakin to Mustafar. Padme Amidala came too, hoping to reach her husband. When Anakin saw Obi-Wan emerge from Padme's ship, he Force-choked Padme in rage, believing she had betrayed him. Then master and apprentice fought. [film_pt]
- **"You were my brother, Anakin.":** The duel ranged across the mining facility — on catwalks, over lava rivers, on floating platforms in the molten currents. It ended when Obi-Wan gained the high ground. Anakin leapt anyway. Obi-Wan severed his legs and his remaining organic arm. Anakin slid down the volcanic bank toward the lava, his body igniting. Obi-Wan stood above him, weeping, and said: "You were my brother, Anakin. I loved you." Then he took Anakin's lightsaber and walked away, leaving him burning. [film_pt]
- **Vader's birth:** Palpatine arrived and recovered the ruined body. The medical droids on Coruscant rebuilt Anakin into the black-armored life-support suit that would define Darth Vader for the next two decades. His first question was about Padme. Palpatine told him he had killed her in his anger. The scream that followed — and the Force energy that crushed the medical equipment around him — marked the complete death of Anakin Skywalker. Or so it seemed. [film_pt]

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]
- **Vader's fortress:** Vader later built a towering castle on Mustafar — Fortress Vader — on the site of his greatest defeat and rebirth. He chose the planet deliberately. The pain of the place fed his connection to the dark side. The fortress was built over an ancient Sith cave and contained a bacta tank where Vader could briefly remove his suit and exist in his ruined body. Director Krennic visited the fortress to discuss the Death Star project. [film_anthology]

[spoiler_tier: sequel_trilogy]
- **Kylo Ren's quest:** Kylo Ren traveled to Mustafar to obtain a Sith wayfinder that would lead to the hidden world of Exegol and the resurrected Emperor Palpatine. He carved through Vader's cultists in the forests that had partially regrown on the planet's surface. The grandson followed the grandfather's path through the same world of fire. [film_st]

[spoiler_tier: everything]
- **Significance:** Mustafar is where the saga's central tragedy crystallized into physical form. A planet of fire and pain, chosen by Vader as his home precisely because it was where everything ended. Every scene set there carries the weight of what happened on those lava banks — a brother who could not be saved, and a master who could not save him. [film_pt]
