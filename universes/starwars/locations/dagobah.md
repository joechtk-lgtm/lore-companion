# Dagobah

## Overview

Dagobah is a remote swamp planet in the Outer Rim, teeming with life but devoid of civilization. Dense fog, massive gnarled trees, and bogs that can swallow a starship whole make it one of the most inhospitable habitable worlds in the galaxy. It has no settlements, no strategic value, and no reason for anyone to visit — which is precisely why Yoda chose it.

## Key Facts

[spoiler_tier: original_trilogy]
- **Yoda's exile:** After the fall of the Republic and the destruction of the Jedi Order, Yoda fled to Dagobah and lived there alone for nearly twenty years. He built a small mud hut and survived on the swamp's meager offerings, meditating and communing with the Force while waiting for the next generation's hope to emerge. [film_ot]
- **Strong in the Force:** Dagobah is saturated with Force energy — both light and dark. A massive dark side nexus exists in a cave beneath a great tree, likely the remnant of a fallen dark side user or an ancient vergence. This ambient Force energy helped mask Yoda's powerful presence from the Emperor and Vader. [film_ot]
- **Luke's training:** Luke Skywalker arrived on Dagobah following the Force ghost of Obi-Wan Kenobi's instruction. He initially dismissed the small, eccentric green creature who found him, not realizing he was standing before the greatest Jedi Master alive. Yoda trained Luke in the ways of the Force — lifting rocks, running through the swamp, handstands with the X-wing balanced overhead. The training was physical, mental, and philosophical. [film_ot]
- **The dark side cave:** Yoda sent Luke into the cave strong with the dark side. Inside, Luke confronted a vision of Darth Vader, struck him down, and saw his own face beneath the shattered mask. The lesson: the dark side is not something external to fight — it lives within. Luke did not understand this at the time. [film_ot]
- **"Do or do not. There is no try.":** Yoda's teaching on Dagobah. When Luke's X-wing sank into the swamp and Luke said he would "try" to lift it, Yoda lifted the entire starfighter with the Force alone, effortlessly. "That is why you fail" — because Luke did not believe. [film_ot]
- **Luke's departure:** Luke left Dagobah early, against Yoda's wishes, after sensing Han and Leia in danger on Cloud City. Yoda and Obi-Wan's ghost warned him he was not ready. They were correct — Luke lost his hand and nearly his life to Vader. [film_ot]
- **Yoda's death:** Luke returned to Dagobah in Return of the Jedi. Yoda, now 900 years old, was dying. He confirmed that Vader was Luke's father and revealed that "there is another" Skywalker — Leia. Then he became one with the Force, his body vanishing. The last of the old Jedi Masters was gone. [film_ot]

[spoiler_tier: clone_wars_era]
- **Yoda's vision quest:** During the Clone Wars, Yoda was guided by the voice of Qui-Gon Jinn's Force ghost to Dagobah, where he confronted visions of the dark side and his own shadow self. This journey taught Yoda the path to preserving consciousness after death — knowledge he later shared with Obi-Wan. It was also his first encounter with the planet he would one day call home. [tv_tcw]

[spoiler_tier: everything]
- **Significance:** Dagobah represents the Jedi path stripped to its essence — no Temple, no politics, no army. Just the Force, a teacher, and a student in the mud. Everything the prequel-era Jedi Order lost sight of, Yoda rediscovered alone on a swamp planet at the edge of nowhere. [film_ot]
