# The Great Purge

## Overview

The Great Purge of Mandalore was the Empire's systematic destruction of Mandalorian civilization — an orbital bombardment and ground assault that reduced the planet's surface to glass, scattered its people across the galaxy, and turned one of the galaxy's proudest warrior cultures into refugees and hidden survivors. It is the defining trauma of the Mandalorian diaspora and the event that shapes everything in Din Djarin's world.

## Key Events

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]
- **Imperial occupation:** Following the end of the Clone Wars, the Empire occupied Mandalore and installed puppet leaders. Mandalorian beskar was seized and smelted for Imperial use. The Empire exploited Mandalorian weapons technology while systematically suppressing Mandalorian identity and autonomy. Resistance movements formed but were fragmented. [tv_rebels]
- **The buildup:** Bo-Katan Kryze accepted the Darksaber from Sabine Wren and attempted to unite the clans against the Empire. The Empire viewed this unification as an existential threat — a united Mandalore had historically been one of the few forces capable of challenging galactic powers. The decision was made to eliminate the threat permanently. [tv_rebels]

[spoiler_tier: new_republic_era]
- **Night of a Thousand Tears:** The Empire's assault on Mandalore included massive orbital bombardment, deployment of KX-series security droids and other ground forces in extermination operations, and the systematic destruction of Mandalorian cities and strongholds. The capital city of Sundari and other dome cities were breached and devastated. The surface was bombarded until sections turned to fused glass. The name the survivors gave it — the Night of a Thousand Tears — reflects the scale of loss. [tv_mando]
- **The scattering:** Surviving Mandalorians fled to the far corners of the galaxy. Some, like Bo-Katan and her loyalists, became nomadic warriors seeking a way to retake their homeworld. Others, like Din Djarin's sect (the Children of the Watch), went deep underground, living in hidden coverts on remote worlds, never removing their helmets, preserving the creed in its strictest form as a response to near-extinction. [tv_mando]
- **Beskar hoarding:** The Empire stockpiled Mandalorian beskar. After the Empire's fall, Imperial remnants like Moff Gideon retained significant beskar reserves, using them to fund and equip private military forces. Gideon had beskar armor forged for himself — a deliberate insult to Mandalorian culture, wearing their sacred metal as a conqueror's trophy. [tv_mando]
- **The Living Waters:** Beneath the mines of Mandalore lie the Living Waters, sacred to Mandalorian faith. Bathing in them can redeem a Mandalorian who has broken the creed. Din Djarin traveled to the ruined surface of Mandalore and found the Living Waters still intact beneath the destruction — proof that Mandalore was wounded but not dead. The Mythosaur, an ancient creature of Mandalorian legend, was glimpsed in the depths. [tv_mando]
- **Din Djarin's origin:** Din Djarin was not born on Mandalore — he was a foundling, rescued during a Separatist attack on his homeworld during the Clone Wars by a Mandalorian of the Death Watch. He was raised in the Children of the Watch and became Mandalorian by creed. The Great Purge destroyed his adopted culture's homeworld just as war had destroyed his biological one. [tv_mando]
- **Retaking Mandalore:** Bo-Katan, Din Djarin, and allied Mandalorians eventually returned to Mandalore's surface, confronted Moff Gideon's forces operating from a hidden base there, and began the process of reclamation. The planet's surface, while devastated, was not lifeless — a fragile ecosystem persisted. The possibility of rebuilding became real. [tv_mando]

[spoiler_tier: everything]
- **Significance:** The Great Purge mirrors the Jedi Purge in its scope and intent — a galactic power destroying a culture it could not control. But where the Jedi were scattered as individuals, the Mandalorians were scattered as a people. Their survival is communal: the creed, the armor, the foundlings, the shared phrase "This is the Way." The Empire destroyed their world. It could not destroy what made them Mandalorian. [tv_mando]
