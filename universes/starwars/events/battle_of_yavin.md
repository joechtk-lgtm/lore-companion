# Battle of Yavin

## Overview

The Battle of Yavin was the Rebel Alliance's first decisive military victory against the Galactic Empire — the destruction of the Death Star, a moon-sized battle station capable of annihilating entire planets. It proved the Empire was not invincible, transformed a scattered insurgency into a legitimate military threat, and made a farm boy from Tatooine into the symbol of hope for a galaxy under tyranny.

## Key Events

[spoiler_tier: original_trilogy]
- **The Death Star:** The Empire's ultimate weapon: a space station with a superlaser powered by kyber crystals, capable of destroying an entire planet with a single shot. Conceived during the Clone Wars, constructed in secret over two decades, and commanded by Grand Moff Wilhuff Tarkin. It was designed to rule through fear — the "Tarkin Doctrine" made manifest. [film_ot]
- **The destruction of Alderaan:** To demonstrate the Death Star's power and extract information from Princess Leia Organa, Tarkin ordered the destruction of her homeworld, Alderaan — a peaceful, unarmed planet of two billion people. The planet was obliterated in seconds. Obi-Wan Kenobi felt the disturbance from across the galaxy: "I felt a great disturbance in the Force, as if millions of voices suddenly cried out in terror and were suddenly silenced." [film_ot]
- **The stolen plans:** Rebel spies obtained the technical readouts of the Death Star, revealing an exhaust port — a thermal exhaust port, two meters wide, that led directly to the main reactor. A precise hit would trigger a chain reaction destroying the station. The plans were transmitted to Princess Leia's ship, hidden in the astromech droid R2-D2, and eventually delivered to the Rebel base on Yavin 4. [film_ot]
- **The assault:** The Rebel Alliance launched a desperate attack with approximately thirty starfighters — X-wings and Y-wings — against a battle station crewed by over a million personnel and defended by turbolaser batteries and TIE fighter squadrons. The attack plan required flying down a narrow trench on the station's surface and firing proton torpedoes into the exhaust port at the end. Most pilots who attempted the trench run were killed. [film_ot]
- **Luke's shot:** Luke Skywalker, flying an X-wing with R2-D2 as his astromech, made the final trench run. Darth Vader, flying personally in his TIE Advanced, was seconds from shooting Luke down when Han Solo arrived in the Millennium Falcon and scattered Vader's wingmen. Obi-Wan's voice told Luke to trust the Force. Luke switched off his targeting computer and fired blind. The torpedoes went in. The Death Star exploded. [film_ot]
- **Casualties:** The Rebel Alliance lost most of its attacking fighters. The Empire lost the Death Star, Grand Moff Tarkin, and the entire crew — over a million Imperial personnel. Darth Vader survived, spinning away in his damaged fighter. The balance of power did not shift overnight, but the myth of Imperial invincibility shattered. [film_ot]

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]
- **Rogue One:** The mission to steal the Death Star plans was carried out by Jyn Erso, Cassian Andor, and a small team of Rebel operatives on Scarif. The exhaust port vulnerability was deliberately designed by Galen Erso, Jyn's father, who had been forced to work on the project and built in a flaw the Empire could not detect. Every member of the Rogue One team died transmitting the plans. Their sacrifice made the Battle of Yavin possible. [film_anthology]

[spoiler_tier: everything]
- **Calendar significance:** The Battle of Yavin became the standard date marker for galactic history — events are recorded as BBY (Before the Battle of Yavin) or ABY (After the Battle of Yavin). A farm boy trusting the Force over a targeting computer became the moment the galaxy measures time from. [film_ot]
