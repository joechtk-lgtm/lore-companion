# Battle of Endor

## Overview

The Battle of Endor was the decisive engagement of the Galactic Civil War — the battle where the Emperor died, the second Death Star was destroyed, and Darth Vader returned to the light. It was fought on three fronts simultaneously: in space above the forest moon, on the moon's surface to destroy the shield generator, and in the Emperor's throne room where the fate of the Skywalker family — and the galaxy — was decided by a son who refused to kill his father.

## Key Events

[spoiler_tier: original_trilogy]
- **The Emperor's trap:** The second Death Star, still under construction above the forest moon of Endor, was bait. The Emperor deliberately leaked its location and the fact that it was operational, knowing the Rebel Alliance would commit its entire fleet to destroy it. The shield generator on the moon protected the station, and an Imperial fleet waited behind Endor to spring the trap. Palpatine intended to crush the Rebellion in a single engagement. [film_ot]
- **The ground assault:** Han Solo led a strike team — including Leia, Chewbacca, Luke, R2-D2, and C-3PO — to the forest moon to destroy the shield generator protecting the Death Star. They were aided by the Ewoks, the moon's indigenous species. The Ewoks, using primitive weapons and knowledge of the terrain, turned the battle against the Imperial garrison. The shield generator was destroyed, exposing the Death Star. [film_ot]
- **The space battle:** Admiral Ackbar commanded the Rebel fleet. When they arrived, the Death Star's superlaser was operational — "It's a trap!" The Rebel fleet was caught between the Imperial Star Destroyer fleet and the Death Star's superlaser, which picked off capital ships one at a time. Lando Calrissian, piloting the Millennium Falcon, led the fighter assault on the Death Star's interior once the shields fell. [film_ot]
- **Luke confronts the Emperor:** Luke surrendered to Vader on Endor, believing he could turn his father back to the light. Vader brought him before the Emperor. Palpatine goaded Luke — showing him the Rebel fleet being destroyed, taunting him about his friends' imminent deaths — trying to provoke Luke into striking in anger and falling to the dark side. [film_ot]
- **The duel:** Luke fought Vader. When Vader sensed Luke's thoughts about Leia and threatened to turn her instead, Luke attacked in fury and overwhelmed Vader, severing his mechanical hand. The Emperor urged Luke to finish it and take Vader's place. Luke looked at Vader's severed hand, then at his own mechanical hand, and understood. He threw his lightsaber away. "I am a Jedi, like my father before me." [film_ot]
- **Vader's redemption:** The Emperor attacked Luke with Force lightning, slowly killing him. Luke cried out for his father. Vader — Anakin — stood between the two, watching his son die and his master laugh. He chose. He grabbed the Emperor, lifted him overhead, and hurled him into the reactor shaft. The lightning ravaged Vader's life-support systems. The Emperor fell, screaming, and died. [film_ot]
- **Anakin's death:** Luke dragged his father to a shuttle. Vader asked Luke to remove his mask so he could see his son with his own eyes. Luke saw a scarred, pale face that smiled at him. "You were right about me," Anakin said. "Tell your sister... you were right." He died. Luke cremated his father's armor on a funeral pyre on Endor. That night, Anakin's Force ghost appeared alongside Obi-Wan and Yoda — young, whole, and at peace. [film_ot]
- **The aftermath:** The Death Star was destroyed when Lando piloted the Falcon into its superstructure and hit the main reactor. The Imperial fleet scattered. Across the galaxy, news of the Emperor's death sparked celebrations and uprisings. The war was not over — the Empire did not collapse instantly — but its center was gone. [film_ot]

[spoiler_tier: everything]
- **Significance:** The Battle of Endor is where every thread of the original saga converges. The Rebellion wins through alliance (the Ewoks, the fleet, the ground team). The Sith are destroyed by their own Rule of Two — the apprentice kills the master, but for love instead of power. And a son saves his father not through victory in combat but through the refusal to fight. Endor proved that the Force is not about power. It is about choice. [film_ot]
