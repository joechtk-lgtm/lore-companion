# Fall of the Republic

## Overview

The fall of the Galactic Republic was not a sudden collapse but a carefully orchestrated demolition carried out over more than a decade by a single man: Sheev Palpatine, Senator from Naboo, Supreme Chancellor of the Republic, and secret Sith Lord Darth Sidious. He engineered a galactic war, played both sides, corrupted the Republic's institutions from within, turned its greatest hero to darkness, and transformed democracy into dictatorship — all while being applauded for it.

## Key Events

[spoiler_tier: prequel_trilogy]
- **The Naboo crisis:** Palpatine orchestrated the Trade Federation's blockade and invasion of his own homeworld, Naboo, to create a sympathy vote that made him Supreme Chancellor. His predecessor, Valorum, was voted out for inaction. Palpatine's rise began with manufactured suffering. [film_pt]
- **The clone army:** A secret army of clone troopers was commissioned on Kamino, ostensibly by Jedi Master Sifo-Dyas (who foresaw the coming war), but co-opted by the Sith through Count Dooku and Darth Tyranus. The army was designed from inception to serve the Sith's endgame — each clone implanted with an inhibitor chip that would force obedience to Order 66. [film_pt]
- **The Separatist crisis:** Palpatine, through his apprentice Count Dooku, organized thousands of star systems into the Confederacy of Independent Systems, threatening to split the Republic. This manufactured threat gave Palpatine the justification to demand emergency powers and the authority to deploy the clone army. The Senate voted to grant these powers. Jar Jar Binks made the motion. [film_pt]
- **Emergency powers:** Once granted, never returned. Each year of the Clone Wars brought new justifications for expanded authority. Palpatine accumulated control over the military, the courts, and the Senate itself. The Republic's democratic institutions were hollowed out while their facades remained standing. [film_pt]
- **Turning Anakin:** Palpatine cultivated Anakin Skywalker for over a decade — a father figure, mentor, and confidant who offered what the Jedi Council withheld: trust, praise, and the promise that the rules could be bent. When Anakin's visions of Padme's death made him desperate, Palpatine revealed the dark side could prevent death. Anakin's fall was the final piece. [film_pt]
- **Order 66 and the declaration of Empire:** With Anakin turned and the Jedi branded traitors, Palpatine executed Order 66 and addressed the Senate: "The Republic will be reorganized into the first Galactic Empire, for a safe and secure society." The Senate applauded. Padme Amidala watched and said: "So this is how liberty dies... with thunderous applause." [film_pt]

[spoiler_tier: clone_wars_era]
- **The war's true purpose:** The Clone Wars were never about the Republic winning or losing — they were about exhausting the galaxy, discrediting the Jedi, and creating the conditions where autocracy felt like relief. The Jedi became generals, compromising their role as peacekeepers. The Republic became militarized, normalizing authoritarianism. Every battle served Palpatine regardless of which side won it. [tv_tcw]
- **Ahsoka's departure:** When Ahsoka Tano was framed for bombing the Jedi Temple, the Council expelled her and handed her to a military tribunal without sufficient evidence. When her innocence was proven, they offered to take her back as if nothing happened. She refused. Her departure symbolized the Order's moral decay — they had become so entangled in the war that they could not even protect their own. [tv_tcw]
- **The Fives conspiracy:** ARC trooper Fives discovered the inhibitor chips and tried to warn the Republic. Palpatine personally ensured Fives was discredited and killed. The Jedi dismissed the warning. The last chance to prevent Order 66 died on a loading dock on Coruscant. [tv_tcw]

[spoiler_tier: everything]
- **Legacy:** The fall of the Republic is a story about how democracies die — not from external conquest but from internal rot, manufactured fear, and a population willing to trade freedom for security. Palpatine did not seize power; it was handed to him, legally, by institutions too compromised to resist. The Jedi failed not because they were weak but because they were complicit — they fought a war that should not have existed, for a Republic that had already ceased to function, and did not realize until the clones turned their guns. [film_pt] [tv_tcw]
