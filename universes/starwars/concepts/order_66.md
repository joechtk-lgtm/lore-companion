# Order 66

## Overview

Order 66 was the contingency command that transformed the clone army of the Republic into the executioners of the Jedi Order. Embedded in every clone trooper, it was Palpatine's final stroke — a single phrase that turned soldiers against their generals simultaneously across the entire galaxy. In minutes, the Jedi Order that had stood for a thousand generations was nearly annihilated by the very army they had led into war.

## Key Facts

[spoiler_tier: prequel_trilogy]
- **Execution:** Chancellor Palpatine — now revealed as Darth Sidious — transmitted Order 66 to clone commanders across the galaxy. "Execute Order 66." The clones turned on their Jedi without hesitation, without warning. On Utapau, Commander Cody ordered fire on Obi-Wan Kenobi moments after returning his lightsaber. On Kashyyyk, Yoda sensed the betrayal and killed his would-be assassins. On Felucia, Mygeeto, Cato Neimoidia, and dozens of other worlds, Jedi were shot in the back by soldiers they trusted. [film_pt]
- **The Jedi Temple assault:** Anakin Skywalker, newly christened Darth Vader, led the 501st Legion into the Jedi Temple on Coruscant. They killed everyone — Jedi Knights, Padawans, younglings. A group of children asked Anakin for help. He ignited his lightsaber. The security recordings later showed Obi-Wan what his former apprentice had become. [film_pt]
- **Survivors:** Very few Jedi survived. Obi-Wan Kenobi and Yoda escaped and went into hiding. Others scattered to remote worlds, abandoned the Force, or disguised themselves among the general population. The Empire spent the following years hunting down survivors through the Inquisitorius — former Jedi turned to the dark side and tasked with eliminating their own kind. [film_pt]
- **The trap:** The clone army was commissioned by Sifo-Dyas (a Jedi who foresaw the coming war) and secretly co-opted by the Sith. The Jedi accepted an army they did not create, did not fully understand, and could not have refused without losing the war. Palpatine designed the Clone Wars so the Jedi would become dependent on the very weapon that would destroy them. [film_pt]

[spoiler_tier: clone_wars_era]
- **Inhibitor chips:** The Clone Wars series revealed that Order 66 was enforced through organic inhibitor chips implanted in every clone's brain during development on Kamino. The chips suppressed individuality and free will when activated, overriding any personal loyalty to their Jedi commanders. [tv_tcw]
- **Tup and Fives:** Clone trooper Tup's chip malfunctioned prematurely, causing him to kill Jedi General Tiplar. ARC trooper Fives investigated, discovered the conspiracy, and tried to warn the Jedi and Chancellor Palpatine. Palpatine framed Fives as mentally unstable. Fives was shot and killed before he could expose the truth. He died saying "the mission... the nightmares... they're finally over." The Jedi dismissed the incident. [tv_tcw]
- **Rex's resistance:** Captain Rex, who had served alongside Ahsoka Tano for years, had his inhibitor chip partially removed after Fives's warning. When Order 66 was activated during the Siege of Mandalore, Rex fought the programming long enough to tell Ahsoka to find Fives's report. Ahsoka removed his chip, and together they escaped the other clones — but could save no one else. [tv_tcw]

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]
- **The long shadow:** Surviving Jedi lived in constant fear. Kanan Jarrus (Caleb Dume) hid his identity for years, suppressing his connection to the Force. Cal Kestis survived as a scrapper on Bracca. Ahsoka Tano operated in the shadows as "Fulcrum," aiding the nascent Rebellion. Every survivor carried the trauma of that day — the moment their entire world ended and the people they trusted became their killers. [tv_rebels]

[spoiler_tier: everything]
- **Legacy:** Order 66 was not just a military operation; it was the culmination of a thousand-year Sith plan. The Jedi were not merely killed — they were betrayed by the Republic they served, the army they led, and the student they trusted most. The galaxy largely accepted the Emperor's story that the Jedi had attempted a coup. History is written by the survivors, and the Sith made certain very few Jedi survived. [film_pt] [tv_tcw]
