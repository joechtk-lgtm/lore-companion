# Lightsabers

## Overview

A lightsaber is the signature weapon of the Jedi and Sith — an elegant blade of plasma energy contained by a magnetic field, powered by a kyber crystal at its core. Every lightsaber is unique, built by its wielder as a rite of passage. The weapon is simultaneously a tool, a symbol, and a test of character. "This weapon is your life," Obi-Wan told Anakin. He was not exaggerating.

## Key Facts

[spoiler_tier: original_trilogy]
- **Construction:** A lightsaber consists of a hilt containing a power cell, focusing lens, and emitter matrix, all built around a kyber crystal. The crystal determines the blade's properties. Jedi traditionally construct their own lightsabers as part of their training, guided by the Force. [film_ot]
- **Anakin's lightsaber:** The blue-bladed lightsaber Anakin Skywalker built during the Clone Wars, taken by Obi-Wan on Mustafar, given to Luke on Tatooine, lost along with Luke's hand on Cloud City. It called to Rey in Maz Kanata's castle decades later. The most consequential single weapon in the galaxy. [film_ot]
- **Luke's green blade:** After losing his father's lightsaber, Luke constructed a new one with a green blade, following instructions found in Obi-Wan's hut on Tatooine. He wielded it at Jabba's Palace and during the final confrontation with Vader and the Emperor. [film_ot]

[spoiler_tier: prequel_trilogy]
- **Blade colors and meaning:** Blue kyber crystals are traditionally associated with Jedi Guardians (combat-focused), green with Jedi Consulars (diplomacy and Force study). These are tendencies, not rules — the crystal chooses its Jedi as much as the Jedi chooses the crystal. [film_pt]
- **Mace Windu's purple blade:** Unique among the Jedi of his era. Purple suggests a wielder who walks close to the edge between light and dark — Windu developed Vaapad, a fighting form that channels the user's enjoyment of combat, dangerously close to the dark side. He mastered it without falling. Almost no one else could. [film_pt]
- **Red blades — bleeding:** Sith lightsabers are red because the dark side user forces the kyber crystal to submit to their will through a process called "bleeding." The crystal, a living thing attuned to the Force, resists and cries out. The corruption turns it crimson. Sith do not find red crystals; they create them through domination. [film_pt]
- **Dooku's curved hilt:** Count Dooku's lightsaber featured a curved hilt designed for precision dueling in Form II (Makashi). An elegant weapon for an elegant fighter — and a man whose sophistication masked his betrayal of everything the Jedi stood for. [film_pt]

[spoiler_tier: clone_wars_era]
- **Kyber crystal gathering:** Jedi younglings traveled to the ice planet Ilum to find their kyber crystals in the Crystal Caves. The caves tested each youngling, presenting challenges tailored to their fears and weaknesses. The crystal that called to you through the Force was yours. [tv_tcw]
- **The Darksaber:** Created by Tarre Vizsla, the first Mandalorian inducted into the Jedi Order. A flat, black blade edged with white energy, shorter than a standard lightsaber. After Tarre's death, House Vizsla reclaimed it from the Jedi Temple. It became the symbol of Mandalorian leadership — whoever wields it can unite the clans, but only if won in combat. [tv_tcw]

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]
- **White blades — purification:** Ahsoka Tano's twin white lightsabers were created from red kyber crystals taken from a defeated Inquisitor. She purified the bled crystals through the Force, healing them. White blades represent a Force user who is neither Jedi nor Sith — independent, but aligned with the light. [tv_rebels]

[spoiler_tier: sequel_trilogy]
- **Kylo Ren's crossguard saber:** A red, unstable blade with two lateral vents forming a crossguard. The instability came from a cracked kyber crystal, which Ren bled but could not fully control. The lateral vents were necessary to prevent the weapon from destroying itself. A weapon as conflicted as its wielder. [film_st]
- **Rey's golden blade:** After the events of the sequel trilogy, Rey constructed her own lightsaber with a yellow blade, built from her quarterstaff. Yellow traditionally signifies a Jedi Sentinel — a balance between combat and scholarship, a guardian of knowledge. [film_st]

[spoiler_tier: everything]
- **Fighting forms:** Seven classical forms exist: Form I Shii-Cho (basic), Form II Makashi (dueling), Form III Soresu (defense — Obi-Wan's specialty), Form IV Ataru (acrobatic — Yoda's form), Form V Shien/Djem So (counterattacking — Anakin's form), Form VI Niman (balanced), Form VII Juyo/Vaapad (aggressive — Mace Windu's mastery). Each reflects a philosophy of combat. [legends] [film_pt]
