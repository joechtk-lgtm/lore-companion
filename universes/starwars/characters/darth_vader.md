# Darth Vader

[spoiler_tier: original_trilogy]
## Overview
Darth Vader is the dark lord of the Sith, the Emperor's chief enforcer, and the most feared figure in the Galactic Empire. [film_ot] Encased in black armor and a life-support suit, he commands through terror and the dark side of the Force. He is a master of telekinesis, lightsaber combat, and Force choke. His mechanical breathing announces his presence before he is seen.

## The Emperor's Fist
Vader personally leads Imperial forces in the hunt for the Rebellion. [film_ot] He destroyed the remnants of the Jedi Order, commands the Super Star Destroyer Executor, and answers only to the Emperor. He killed Obi-Wan Kenobi aboard the first Death Star. He pursued the Rebel fleet relentlessly after the Battle of Yavin, obsessed with the pilot who destroyed the Death Star.

## The Revelation
On Cloud City, Vader reveals to Luke Skywalker that he is Luke's father. [film_ot] "No — I am your father." This is the defining revelation of the saga. Vader offers Luke the chance to join him and overthrow the Emperor — to rule the galaxy as father and son. Luke refuses and falls. Vader is not simply a villain; he is a father consumed by the dark side, still reaching for his son.

## Redemption
Aboard the second Death Star, Luke surrenders to Vader and appeals to the good still in him. [film_ot] When the Emperor tortures Luke with Force lightning, Vader turns. He seizes the Emperor and throws him into the reactor shaft, mortally wounding himself in the process. He asks Luke to remove his mask — revealing the scarred, pale face of a broken man. He dies as Anakin Skywalker, not as Vader. His body is cremated on Endor.

[spoiler_tier: prequel_trilogy]
## The Man Behind the Mask
Darth Vader was once Anakin Skywalker — the Chosen One of Jedi prophecy. [film_pt] His fall to the dark side, his sealing in the suit after defeat on Mustafar, and his transformation from hero to monster are chronicled under Anakin Skywalker's entry. The suit is not merely armor — it is a prison. It causes constant pain. Vader lives in suffering, and that suffering fuels his connection to the dark side.

[spoiler_tier: clone_wars_era]
## Shadow of the Clone Wars
Vader's command style retains echoes of Anakin's Clone Wars generalship — aggressive, tactical, and personally involved. [tv_tcw] But where Anakin fought alongside his troops with loyalty, Vader commands through fear. Officers who fail him are killed without hesitation. The contrast between who he was and who he became is the tragedy at the heart of the saga.

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]
## The Inquisitors
Vader oversaw the Inquisitorius — Force-sensitive agents tasked with hunting surviving Jedi. [tv_rebels] He personally hunted Ahsoka Tano, his former Padawan, confronting her on Malachor. Their duel ended with Ahsoka slashing his mask open, revealing Anakin's eye beneath. She called his name. He replied: "Anakin Skywalker was weak. I destroyed him." He meant it — and he did not.

[spoiler_tier: everything]
## Cultural Impact
Darth Vader is often discussed as both a separate entity and the same person as Anakin Skywalker. [film_ot] [film_pt] Within the lore, the distinction matters: Vader is what Palpatine created, but Anakin is what Luke redeemed. The suit, the breathing, the voice — these defined villainy for a generation, making the man beneath them all the more tragic.
