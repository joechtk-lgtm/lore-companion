# Rey

[spoiler_tier: sequel_trilogy]
## Overview
Rey is a scavenger from the desert planet Jakku who becomes the central figure of the galaxy's struggle against the First Order and the resurgence of the Sith. [film_st] She was abandoned on Jakku as a child, surviving alone by scavenging Imperial wreckage for portions of food. She waited years for her family to return. Despite having no formal training, she is extraordinarily strong in the Force — a raw, instinctive power that manifests when she needs it most.

## The Force Awakens
Rey's life changed when she encountered the droid BB-8, carrying a map to Luke Skywalker. [film_st] She fled Jakku with the defecting stormtrooper Finn aboard the Millennium Falcon. She discovered her Force abilities when Kylo Ren attempted to interrogate her — she turned his own mind probe back against him. She fought Kylo Ren with Anakin Skywalker's lightsaber in the forests of Starkiller Base. She traveled to Ahch-To and found Luke Skywalker.

## Training and the Dyad
Luke reluctantly trained Rey on Ahch-To, but their relationship was complicated by her connection to the dark side — she went straight to it during meditation, without fear. [film_st] She shared a Force bond with Kylo Ren, a connection neither of them chose. Through this bond, she saw Ben Solo's conflict and believed she could turn him. She went to Snoke's flagship to save him. Kylo killed Snoke, but chose power over redemption, asking Rey to rule with him. She refused.

## Identity
Rey believed her parents were nobody — junk traders who sold her for drinking money. [film_st] The truth was worse: she is the granddaughter of Emperor Palpatine. Her father was Palpatine's son, who fled Exegol and hid Rey on Jakku to protect her from the Emperor. This revelation shook her to the core, forcing her to confront the fear that her power came from darkness.

## The Battle of Exegol
Palpatine, resurrected on Exegol, demanded that Rey strike him down so his spirit could transfer into her. [film_st] She refused. Calling upon the strength of all the Jedi who came before — their voices rising in chorus — she turned Palpatine's own lightning back against him, destroying him and the Sith Eternal. The effort killed her. Ben Solo, having turned back to the light, used Force healing to revive her at the cost of his own life. Rey survived. Ben did not.

## Rey Skywalker
Rey traveled to the Lars homestead on Tatooine, where she buried Luke and Leia's lightsabers in the sand. [film_st] When a local asked her name, she saw the Force ghosts of Luke and Leia watching over her. "Rey Skywalker," she answered. She chose her own lineage — not the bloodline of Palpatine, but the legacy of the family that saved the galaxy.

[spoiler_tier: everything]
## Significance
Rey's story is about identity forged by choice rather than inheritance. [film_st] She was born of darkness and chose light. The Force dyad she shared with Ben Solo was described as a power unseen for generations — two that are one. Her adoption of the Skywalker name closes the saga by affirming that legacy is not blood.
