# Luke Skywalker

[spoiler_tier: original_trilogy]
## Overview
Luke Skywalker is a farm boy from Tatooine who became the last Jedi Knight of the original era. [film_ot] Raised by his uncle Owen and aunt Beru Lars, he believed his father was a navigator on a spice freighter. His life changed when he acquired two droids — R2-D2 and C-3PO — carrying a message from Princess Leia Organa. He joined Obi-Wan Kenobi on a mission to rescue her, and in doing so, became a central figure in the Rebel Alliance.

## Training and the Force
Obi-Wan Kenobi gave Luke his father's lightsaber and began his instruction in the Force. [film_ot] After Obi-Wan's death aboard the Death Star, Luke destroyed the battle station using the Force to guide his proton torpedo shot — a feat that made him a legend of the Rebellion. He later trained under Yoda on Dagobah, where he learned discipline, patience, and the deeper nature of the Force.

## The Truth About His Father
On Cloud City, Darth Vader revealed himself as Luke's father — Anakin Skywalker. [film_ot] This shattered Luke's understanding of his own identity. Rather than join the dark side, Luke chose to fall. He later confronted Vader and the Emperor aboard the second Death Star at Endor. He refused to kill his father, declaring himself a Jedi. His compassion redeemed Anakin Skywalker, who destroyed the Emperor.

[spoiler_tier: prequel_trilogy]
## Birth and Separation
Luke and his twin sister Leia were born on Polis Massa moments after their mother Padme Amidala died. [film_pt] Obi-Wan and Yoda separated the twins to hide them from the Empire. Luke was placed with Owen and Beru Lars on Tatooine; Leia was adopted by Bail Organa of Alderaan. Neither knew the other existed.

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]
## The New Jedi
After the Battle of Endor, Luke became the last living Jedi and the galaxy's best hope for restoring the Order. [film_ot] He carried the knowledge passed down by Obi-Wan and Yoda, and Anakin's redemption proved his belief that no one was beyond saving.

[spoiler_tier: new_republic_era]
## Luke's Jedi Academy
Luke established a new Jedi training temple during the New Republic era. [film_st] He took on students, including his nephew Ben Solo, son of Han and Leia. He sought to rebuild what was lost, studying Jedi texts and artifacts across the galaxy. Grogu, the Force-sensitive youngling rescued by Din Djarin, was briefly offered training by Luke on this path. [tv_mando]

[spoiler_tier: sequel_trilogy]
## Exile and the Last Jedi
Ben Solo's turn to the dark side destroyed Luke's temple and killed his students. [film_st] Haunted by a moment of weakness — he had briefly considered striking Ben down after sensing overwhelming darkness — Luke exiled himself to Ahch-To, the site of the first Jedi Temple. He cut himself off from the Force. Rey found him there and convinced him to train her. Yoda's Force ghost appeared to Luke, burning the ancient Jedi texts and reminding him that failure is the greatest teacher.

## The Battle of Crait
Luke projected himself across the galaxy to face Kylo Ren on Crait, buying the Resistance time to escape. [film_st] It was not a physical presence but a Force projection of extraordinary power. After the effort, Luke became one with the Force on Ahch-To, at peace, watching the twin suns set. His sacrifice became a legend that inspired hope across the galaxy.

[spoiler_tier: everything]
## Legacy
Luke Skywalker's voice was among the Jedi who spoke to Rey during the Battle of Exegol. [film_st] His legacy proved greater as a symbol than as an institution-builder. The Jedi he trained mostly perished, but the hope he represented endured. He and Leia appeared as Force ghosts on Tatooine, watching over the place where his story began.
