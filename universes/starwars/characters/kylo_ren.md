# Kylo Ren

[spoiler_tier: sequel_trilogy]
## Overview
Kylo Ren is the dark side persona of Ben Solo — son of Han Solo and Leia Organa, nephew of Luke Skywalker, and grandson of Darth Vader. [film_st] He is the master of the Knights of Ren and serves as the primary enforcer of Supreme Leader Snoke and the First Order. He wields a crossguard lightsaber with an unstable, cracked kyber crystal that vents excess energy through lateral exhausts. His power in the Force is immense — he can freeze blaster bolts in midair and probe minds with terrifying precision.

## The Fall of Ben Solo
Ben Solo was sent to train at Luke Skywalker's Jedi academy. [film_st] Snoke — secretly a puppet of Palpatine — had been whispering to Ben through the Force since childhood, feeding his darkness. When Luke sensed the depth of that darkness and momentarily ignited his lightsaber over his sleeping nephew, Ben saw only betrayal. He destroyed the temple, killed several students, and fled to Snoke. He took the name Kylo Ren and committed himself to the dark side — but never completely. The pull to the light never stopped.

## Patricide
On Starkiller Base, Kylo Ren killed his father, Han Solo. [film_st] Han had come to bring his son home. Kylo asked for help, took Han's hand — and ignited his saber through his father's chest. He believed this act would silence his conflict. It did not. Snoke later mocked him for it, sensing that the act had split his spirit further rather than strengthening his resolve.

## Supreme Leader
Kylo killed Snoke by turning Anakin Skywalker's lightsaber through him, saving Rey. [film_st] He offered Rey a new order — neither Sith nor Jedi, neither First Order nor Resistance. She refused. He declared himself Supreme Leader. He was a tyrant without conviction, ruling through rage and isolation. He sought to destroy every connection to his past: "Let the past die. Kill it, if you have to."

## The Force Dyad
Kylo and Rey shared a Force bond — a dyad in the Force, two halves of one power. [film_st] Through this bond they communicated across the galaxy, fought, and even physically passed objects. Their connection was deeper than either understood. It was not chosen — it simply was. Palpatine later revealed he had bridged their minds initially, but the dyad itself was a phenomenon beyond his control.

## Redemption
On the wreckage of the second Death Star, Rey stabbed Kylo Ren — then immediately healed him with the Force. [film_st] Leia reached out to her son one final time, spending her remaining life force to call to him. Alone, Ben Solo saw a vision of his father. "I know what I have to do, but I don't know if I have the strength to do it," he said again — the same words, a different choice. He threw away his crossguard saber and became Ben Solo once more. He traveled to Exegol, helped Rey defeat Palpatine, and used the last of his life force to resurrect her. He died in her arms and became one with the Force.

## Legacy
Ben Solo is the last of the Skywalker bloodline. [film_st] His life recapitulates his grandfather's arc — fall, suffering, and redemption through love — but compressed into a single generation. Where Vader was redeemed in his final moments after decades of evil, Ben was redeemed while still young, having never fully committed to the darkness he professed. His tragedy is not that he fell, but that he returned too late to live.
