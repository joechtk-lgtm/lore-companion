# Han Solo

[spoiler_tier: original_trilogy]
## Overview
Han Solo is a Corellian smuggler, captain of the Millennium Falcon, and partner of the Wookiee Chewbacca. [film_ot] He was hired by Obi-Wan Kenobi and Luke Skywalker in the Mos Eisley cantina to transport them to Alderaan. What began as a job for money became the defining commitment of his life. Han is cynical, quick-witted, and deeply reluctant to believe in anything larger than himself — until he does.

## The Smuggler and the Rebellion
Han rescued Princess Leia from the Death Star, returned to destroy the battle station at Yavin, and became a key figure in the Rebel Alliance. [film_ot] He was captured on Bespin by Darth Vader and frozen in carbonite, delivered to Jabba the Hutt as a trophy. Leia, Luke, Chewbacca, and Lando Calrissian rescued him from Jabba's palace. He led the ground assault team on Endor that destroyed the Death Star's shield generator. His relationship with Leia defined both of them — "I know" was his answer to her declaration of love.

## The Millennium Falcon
The Falcon is a modified YT-1300 Corellian freighter — the fastest hunk of junk in the galaxy. [film_ot] Han won it from Lando Calrissian in a card game. It made the Kessel Run in less than twelve parsecs. The ship is as much a character as its captain: unreliable, surprising, and impossible to kill.

[spoiler_tier: prequel_trilogy]
## Background
Han Solo's early life is largely unchronicled in the prequel era. [film_anthology] He grew up on Corellia, escaped a criminal gang, briefly served in the Imperial infantry, and met Chewbacca in an Imperial labor camp. He won the Millennium Falcon from Lando in a game of sabacc on Vandor. His early adventures established his reputation as a skilled pilot and unreliable partner — a reputation he spent the rest of his life alternately confirming and contradicting.

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]
## General Solo
After the Battle of Endor, Han held the rank of General in the Rebel Alliance. [film_ot] He never fully embraced military structure — he was a leader by instinct, not by protocol. His bond with Chewbacca remained the most constant relationship in his life, predating and outlasting everything else.

[spoiler_tier: new_republic_era]
## After the War
Han and Leia married and had a son, Ben Solo. [film_st] As Ben grew and his darkness became apparent, the family fractured. Han returned to what he knew — smuggling, the Falcon, Chewbacca. He and Leia separated, though their love never ended. He lost the Falcon and spent years trying to recover it.

[spoiler_tier: sequel_trilogy]
## Death on Starkiller Base
Han encountered Rey and Finn aboard the recovered Millennium Falcon and was drawn back into the fight against the First Order. [film_st] On Starkiller Base, he confronted his son — now Kylo Ren. He called him by his real name. Ben ignited his lightsaber through Han's chest. Han touched his son's face as he fell. His death was the act of a father who walked toward his child knowing the cost. It was not enough to turn Ben — not yet. But it planted the seed.

[spoiler_tier: everything]
## Legacy
Han appeared to Ben Solo as a memory — not a Force ghost, but a vision born of Ben's own conscience — during the Battle of Exegol. [film_st] Their exchange mirrored the scene on Starkiller Base, but this time Ben chose differently. Han Solo never believed in the Force, but the Force believed in him. He was a scoundrel who became a hero, and a father whose love outlasted his death.
