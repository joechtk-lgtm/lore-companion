# Din Djarin

[spoiler_tier: new_republic_era]
## Overview
Din Djarin, known as "The Mandalorian" or simply "Mando," is a Mandalorian bounty hunter operating in the Outer Rim during the New Republic era. [tv_mando] He is a foundling — orphaned as a child during a Separatist attack and rescued by Mandalorians of the Children of the Watch, a traditionalist sect. He follows the Way of the Mandalore strictly: he never removes his helmet in the presence of another living being. His armor is beskar — Mandalorian iron, among the rarest and most durable materials in the galaxy.

## The Child
Din accepted a bounty from an Imperial remnant client for an asset on Arvala-7. [tv_mando] The asset was a child — a fifty-year-old infant of the same species as Yoda, later known as Grogu. Din delivered the child as contracted, then broke his own code and every professional instinct to go back and rescue him. This act made him a fugitive from the Bounty Hunters' Guild and set his life on a new course.

## The Bond
Din and Grogu traveled together across the galaxy, pursued by Moff Gideon and Imperial remnants who wanted Grogu for his blood — rich in midi-chlorians. [tv_mando] Din sought out Jedi who could train the child, encountering Ahsoka Tano on Corvus and eventually reaching Luke Skywalker. He removed his helmet to say goodbye to Grogu — breaking the Way for the first time, for love.

## The Darksaber
Din won the Darksaber in combat from Moff Gideon. [tv_mando] By Mandalorian tradition, the wielder of the Darksaber has the right to rule Mandalore. Din did not want this burden. The blade resisted him — it felt heavy, fighting his movements. Bo-Katan Kryze, who sought to reunite the Mandalorian people, could not accept the saber as a gift; it had to be won in combat.

## Reunion
Grogu chose Din over Jedi training with Luke Skywalker, returning to his father in all but blood. [tv_mando] Din was expelled from the Children of the Watch for removing his helmet. He sought redemption in the Living Waters beneath the mines of Mandalore, and in doing so, helped begin the reclamation of the Mandalorian homeworld. He adopted Grogu as his son — Din Grogu, officially a Mandalorian foundling.

## Legacy
Din Djarin represents the Mandalorian way at its most essential: not a bloodline but a creed, not a homeworld but a culture carried in the heart. [tv_mando] He is a quiet man in a galaxy of loud causes, defined not by what he fights for but by who he protects.
