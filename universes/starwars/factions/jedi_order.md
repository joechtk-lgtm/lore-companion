# Jedi Order

[spoiler_tier: original_trilogy]

The Jedi Order is an ancient monastic organization of Force-sensitive individuals dedicated to the light side of the Force. For over a thousand generations, the Jedi served as guardians of peace and justice in the Galactic Republic. [film_ot] By the time of the Galactic Civil War, the Jedi are all but extinct, hunted to near-annihilation by the Empire. Obi-Wan Kenobi and Yoda survive in hiding, placing their hope in Luke Skywalker as the last of the Jedi. [film_ot] Luke trains briefly under both masters, learning the ways of the Force, and ultimately redeems his father Darth Vader rather than destroying him — embodying the Jedi ideal of compassion over aggression. [film_ot]

[spoiler_tier: prequel_trilogy]

At the height of the Republic, the Jedi Order numbered roughly ten thousand Knights operating from the Jedi Temple on Coruscant. [film_pt] The Jedi Council, led by senior masters including Yoda and Mace Windu, governed the Order and advised the Republic Senate. [film_pt] Jedi philosophy centered on selflessness, emotional detachment, and harmony with the Force. Two interpretive traditions existed: the Living Force, championed by Qui-Gon Jinn, which emphasized present awareness and intuition; and the Unifying Force, favored by the Council majority, which stressed prophecy, destiny, and the cosmic will. [film_pt] The Order's rigid code forbade personal attachment, a rule that directly contributed to Anakin Skywalker's secret marriage and eventual fall to the dark side. [film_pt] Critically, the Jedi grew complacent and politically entangled, failing to detect the Sith Lord Darth Sidious operating as Chancellor Palpatine in plain sight. Their ability to use the Force diminished as the dark side clouded their vision. [film_pt] This blindness culminated in Order 66: Palpatine activated a contingency protocol in the clone troopers, who turned on their Jedi commanders across the galaxy, massacring virtually the entire Order in a single coordinated strike. [film_pt]

[spoiler_tier: clone_wars_era]

During the Clone Wars, the Jedi assumed the role of military generals commanding the Grand Army of the Republic — a departure from their traditional peacekeeping mandate that eroded their principles. [tv_tcw] Ahsoka Tano, Anakin Skywalker's Padawan, was falsely accused of sedition by the Council, and though eventually exonerated, she left the Order disillusioned — a sign of the institution's decay. [tv_tcw] Jedi such as Pong Krell and Barriss Offee fell to the dark side or betrayed the Order during the war, exposing the strain the conflict placed on Jedi ideals. [tv_tcw] Master Yoda undertook a spiritual journey guided by Qui-Gon Jinn's Force spirit, learning the secret of consciousness after death — knowledge he later passed to Obi-Wan. [tv_tcw]

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]

After Order 66, scattered Jedi survivors went into hiding. Kanan Jarrus trained Ezra Bridger in secret while fighting alongside the early rebel cell known as the Spectres. [tv_rebels] Former Padawan Ahsoka Tano operated as the intelligence agent "Fulcrum," aiding the Rebellion while continuing her own path outside the formal Jedi structure. [tv_rebels] The Inquisitorius, dark-side agents trained by Darth Vader, actively hunted surviving Jedi and Force-sensitive children throughout the Imperial era. [tv_rebels]

[spoiler_tier: sequel_trilogy]

After the fall of the Empire, Luke Skywalker established a new Jedi training temple to rebuild the Order. [film_st] His nephew Ben Solo, corrupted by Snoke's influence, destroyed the temple and slaughtered most of Luke's students, becoming Kylo Ren. [film_st] Devastated by his failure and a moment of weakness in which he had briefly considered striking down Ben, Luke exiled himself to the ancient Jedi temple on Ahch-To. [film_st] Rey sought him out and convinced him to pass on what he had learned. Luke ultimately sacrificed himself through a massive Force projection to save the Resistance, becoming one with the Force. [film_st] Rey, trained by both Luke and Leia Organa, carried forward the Jedi legacy and claimed the Skywalker name, signaling the Order's renewal. [film_st]

[spoiler_tier: everything]

The Jedi Order's full history stretches back over twenty-five thousand years, with ancient temples on worlds like Jedha, Ilum, and Tython. [legends] The earliest Jedi studied both light and dark aspects of the Force before a schism led dark-side practitioners to become the first Sith. [legends] Throughout galactic history, the Jedi endured multiple near-extinctions and reformations, each time adapting their code and philosophy to the era. [legends]
