# Galactic Empire

[spoiler_tier: prequel_trilogy]

The Galactic Empire was born from the Galactic Republic through the machinations of Chancellor Palpatine, who engineered the Clone Wars to consolidate emergency powers. [film_pt] After declaring the Jedi enemies of the state and executing Order 66, Palpatine reorganized the Republic into the first Galactic Empire, proclaiming himself Emperor to thunderous applause from the Senate. [film_pt] The transition was framed as a necessary measure to ensure security and stability, with most galactic citizens initially welcoming strong centralized rule after years of devastating war. [film_pt]

[spoiler_tier: original_trilogy]

The Empire ruled the galaxy through military supremacy and fear. Its doctrine, codified in the Tarkin Doctrine named after Grand Moff Wilhuff Tarkin, held that overwhelming force and the threat of annihilation would keep star systems in line. [film_ot] The Imperial military comprised the Imperial Navy with its iconic Star Destroyer fleet, the Stormtrooper Corps, and specialized forces including the Imperial Royal Guard and the ISB (Imperial Security Bureau). [film_ot] The Empire's ultimate weapon was the Death Star, a moon-sized battle station capable of destroying entire planets. The first Death Star obliterated Alderaan as a demonstration of power before being destroyed by Luke Skywalker at the Battle of Yavin. [film_ot] A second, larger Death Star was constructed over the forest moon of Endor. Emperor Palpatine intended it as a trap for the Rebel Alliance, but the Rebels destroyed the station's shield generator on the surface, and the Death Star was destroyed — killing Palpatine and crippling the Empire. [film_ot]

[spoiler_tier: clone_wars_era]

The military infrastructure of the Empire was seeded during the Clone Wars. The Republic's clone army, Grand Army logistics, and Venator-class Star Destroyers were all precursors to Imperial military doctrine. [tv_tcw] Palpatine used the war to install loyalists in key positions and marginalize the Jedi's political influence, ensuring the transition to Empire would face minimal institutional resistance. [tv_tcw]

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]

Under Imperial rule, non-human species faced systemic persecution and slavery. Worlds that resisted were subjected to orbital bombardment or occupation. [tv_rebels] The Empire's military-industrial complex produced weapons at a staggering scale, strip-mining planets like Lothal and Jedha for resources. [tv_rebels] Director Orson Krennic oversaw the Death Star project for two decades, with scientist Galen Erso coerced into designing its superlaser — though Erso secretly built in a fatal vulnerability. [film_anthology] The Battle of Scarif, where Rebel operatives stole the Death Star plans at enormous cost, marked the beginning of the Empire's eventual downfall. [film_anthology]

[spoiler_tier: new_republic_era]

After the Emperor's death at Endor, the Empire fragmented. Competing Moffs and admirals carved out their own territories while the Rebel Alliance transitioned into the New Republic. [tv_mando] The decisive Battle of Jakku, roughly a year after Endor, shattered the Empire's remaining coordinated fleet. The Empire signed the Galactic Concordance, a formal surrender that imposed strict disarmament terms. [film_st] However, not all Imperials accepted defeat. Loyalist officers and remnant forces retreated to the Outer Rim and Unknown Regions, operating in secret. [tv_mando] Moff Gideon commanded one such remnant faction that conducted illegal cloning and weapons research, including pursuit of Force-sensitive subjects. [tv_mando]

[spoiler_tier: sequel_trilogy]

Imperial remnants who fled to the Unknown Regions gradually coalesced into the First Order under the guidance of the mysterious Snoke, himself a puppet of the resurrected Emperor Palpatine. [film_st] The Empire's legacy of authoritarian ideology and militarism thus survived in a new form, producing Starkiller Base — a weapon that dwarfed both Death Stars — and eventually the Final Order fleet on Exegol. [film_st] The cycle of Imperial tyranny was only truly broken with the defeat of Palpatine's Sith Eternal forces at the Battle of Exegol. [film_st]
