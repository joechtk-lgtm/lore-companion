# Mandalorians

[spoiler_tier: clone_wars_era]

The Mandalorians are a culture of warriors defined not by species but by creed. Originating on the planet Mandalore, their society revolved around combat prowess, clan loyalty, and beskar — a nearly indestructible metal forged into their iconic armor. [tv_tcw] By the Clone Wars era, Mandalore was ruled by Duchess Satine Kryze, a pacifist reformer who had transformed the traditionally martial society into a neutral, peaceful world. [tv_tcw] This pacifism was violently opposed by Death Watch, a splinter faction led by Pre Vizsla who wielded the Darksaber — an ancient Mandalorian lightsaber created by Tarre Vizsla, the first Mandalorian Jedi. The Darksaber carried enormous cultural weight: whoever won it in combat held a legitimate claim to rule all Mandalore. [tv_tcw] Darth Maul exploited Death Watch's ambitions, using them to seize control of Mandalore. He killed Pre Vizsla in single combat, claiming the Darksaber, and murdered Duchess Satine to torment Obi-Wan Kenobi. [tv_tcw] Bo-Katan Kryze, Satine's sister and a Death Watch lieutenant, refused to serve an outsider and led a resistance against Maul's regime. The Republic eventually laid siege to Mandalore in the final days of the Clone Wars, with Ahsoka Tano leading clone forces alongside Bo-Katan to topple Maul's government. [tv_tcw]

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]

Under Imperial occupation, Mandalore suffered greatly. The Empire installed a puppet government and exploited Mandalorian resources and warriors. [tv_rebels] Sabine Wren, a Mandalorian of Clan Wren and member of the rebel crew of the Ghost, recovered the Darksaber and trained in its use under Kanan Jarrus. She ultimately passed the blade to Bo-Katan Kryze, rallying Mandalorian clans to resist the Empire. [tv_rebels] The Empire responded with the Great Purge of Mandalore — a devastating military campaign that bombarded the planet's surface, scattered the Mandalorian people, and seized vast quantities of beskar. [tv_mando] Surviving Mandalorians dispersed into hidden enclaves across the galaxy. The Children of the Watch, a fundamentalist sect, adhered to the ancient Way of the Mandalore: never removing one's helmet in the presence of others, a practice most mainstream Mandalorians had abandoned generations earlier. [tv_mando]

[spoiler_tier: new_republic_era]

In the years following the Empire's defeat, Din Djarin, a foundling raised by the Children of the Watch, became an unlikely unifying figure for the Mandalorian people. [tv_mando] Djarin came into possession of the Darksaber after defeating Moff Gideon, though he initially struggled with the weapon's weight — the blade responds to the mental state of its wielder, growing heavier with internal conflict. [tv_mando] Bo-Katan Kryze, who had previously refused to accept the Darksaber without winning it in combat, eventually came to work alongside Djarin. The two represented the tension between orthodox and progressive Mandalorian traditions. [tv_mando] Djarin's relationship with the Force-sensitive foundling Grogu (known colloquially as "the Child") challenged traditional Mandalorian attitudes, as his paternal bond with a Jedi-trained being bridged two historically opposed cultures. [tv_mando] The scattered Mandalorian clans began reconvening with the goal of reclaiming and rebuilding their devastated homeworld, with the retaking of Mandalore from Imperial remnant forces marking a pivotal step toward reunification. [tv_mando]

[spoiler_tier: everything]

Ancient Mandalorian history includes galaxy-spanning wars against the Jedi Order, most notably the Mandalorian-Jedi War, which devastated Mandalore's surface and left much of the planet a white desert of sand and cube-shaped ruins. [legends] The Mandalorian Neo-Crusaders under Mandalore the Ultimate waged a devastating war against the Republic that drew the Jedi Revan and Malak into conflict — and ultimately to the dark side. [legends] Mandalorian culture has always valued adoption of outsiders regardless of species, with the concept of foundlings and the saying "this is the Way" reflecting a creed open to all who prove themselves worthy. [tv_mando]
