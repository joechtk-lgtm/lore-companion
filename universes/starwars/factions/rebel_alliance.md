# Rebel Alliance

[spoiler_tier: prequel_trilogy]

The seeds of rebellion were planted during the final days of the Republic. Senators Bail Organa of Alderaan and Mon Mothma of Chandrila were among the first to recognize Palpatine's authoritarian trajectory. [film_pt] Along with Padme Amidala and a group of like-minded senators, they drafted the Petition of the Two Thousand — a formal protest against the Chancellor's emergency powers — though it was suppressed after the Empire's formation. [film_pt]

[spoiler_tier: original_trilogy]

The Alliance to Restore the Republic, commonly known as the Rebel Alliance, was a coalition of resistance fighters, dissident senators, and freedom cells unified against Imperial rule. [film_ot] Led by Mon Mothma as Chief of State and supported by military commanders like Admiral Ackbar and General Jan Dodonna, the Alliance operated from hidden bases including Yavin 4 and later Hoth. [film_ot] The Rebellion's first major victory was the destruction of the Death Star at the Battle of Yavin, achieved by Luke Skywalker's proton torpedo shot guided by the Force. [film_ot] The Empire struck back devastatingly at the Battle of Hoth, overrunning the Rebel base and scattering Alliance forces. [film_ot] The Alliance regrouped and launched its decisive operation at the Battle of Endor, where a combined fleet assault and a ground commando team led by Han Solo and Leia Organa destroyed the second Death Star and killed the Emperor. [film_ot] Princess Leia Organa served as one of the Rebellion's most important leaders — a diplomat, strategist, and field operative who personally carried the stolen Death Star plans that made the first victory possible. [film_ot]

[spoiler_tier: rebellion_era]

Before the Alliance formally unified, scattered rebel cells operated independently across the galaxy. Bail Organa coordinated many of these cells through intermediaries, using the codename "Fulcrum" for intelligence operatives including Ahsoka Tano. [tv_rebels] The crew of the Ghost — Hera Syndulla, Kanan Jarrus, Ezra Bridger, Sabine Wren, Zeb Orrelios, and the droid Chopper — was one such cell on Lothal that grew into Phoenix Squadron, a key component of the early Rebellion. [tv_rebels] Hera Syndulla rose to become one of the Alliance's most decorated generals, while Sabine Wren's Mandalorian heritage brought crucial allies and knowledge to the cause. [tv_rebels] The intelligence operative Cassian Andor exemplified the Rebellion's moral complexity — a spy willing to do terrible things in service of the cause, who ultimately gave his life alongside Jyn Erso and Rogue One to transmit the Death Star plans from Scarif. [film_anthology] The Battle of Scarif was the Rebellion's first major engagement against the Empire and came at an enormous cost, with the entire Rogue One ground team killed in action. [film_anthology]

[spoiler_tier: new_republic_era]

After the victory at Endor, the Rebel Alliance formally reorganized into the New Republic, establishing a democratic government with a rotating capital system to prevent the concentration of power that had doomed the old Republic. [film_st] The New Republic demilitarized significantly after the Battle of Jakku and the signing of the Galactic Concordance, a decision that left it vulnerable to the rising First Order threat. [film_st]

[spoiler_tier: sequel_trilogy]

When the New Republic Senate refused to acknowledge the First Order as a serious threat, Leia Organa founded the Resistance — a privately funded paramilitary organization that carried forward the Rebel Alliance's spirit. [film_st] The Resistance operated with a fraction of the resources the old Alliance had commanded, relying on veteran Rebel fighters and a new generation of volunteers. After the First Order destroyed the Hosnian system, the Resistance became the galaxy's last line of defense, ultimately rallying a citizens' fleet at the Battle of Exegol to defeat the Sith Eternal. [film_st]
