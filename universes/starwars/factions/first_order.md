# First Order

[spoiler_tier: sequel_trilogy]

The First Order arose from the ashes of the Galactic Empire. After the Battle of Jakku and the signing of the Galactic Concordance, loyalist Imperial officers, nobles, and military commanders refused to accept surrender. [film_st] Under contingency plans devised by Emperor Palpatine himself — the so-called Contingency, overseen by Counselor Gallius Rax — select Imperial forces retreated into the Unknown Regions, beyond the reach of the New Republic. [film_st]

Over the following decades, these remnants rebuilt in secret, constructing a war machine that combined Imperial military doctrine with fanatical ideological indoctrination. [film_st] Unlike the Empire, which conscripted and recruited soldiers, the First Order abducted children from remote worlds and raised them from birth as stormtroopers, stripping them of names and assigning designations. FN-2187, later known as Finn, was one such child soldier who defected after witnessing a civilian massacre on Jakku. [film_st]

The First Order was led by Supreme Leader Snoke, a powerful dark-side wielder who commanded from the massive Supremacy mega-Star Destroyer. [film_st] Snoke was secretly an artificial being created by the resurrected Emperor Palpatine on the Sith world of Exegol, designed to manipulate galactic events and corrupt Ben Solo to the dark side. [film_st] Under Snoke, the First Order's military was commanded by General Armitage Hux, a zealot who oversaw the construction of Starkiller Base — a planet converted into a hyperspace weapon capable of destroying entire star systems. Starkiller Base annihilated the Hosnian system, seat of the New Republic government, in a single devastating strike before being destroyed by a Resistance assault. [film_st]

The First Order's command structure was riven by internal rivalry. Hux and Kylo Ren, Snoke's dark-side apprentice, competed for authority and influence. [film_st] After Kylo Ren killed Snoke and seized control as Supreme Leader, the First Order's direction shifted from calculated conquest to Ren's personal obsession with destroying the past and eliminating the Resistance. [film_st] Hux, resentful of Ren's dominance, secretly became a Resistance spy — not out of ideology, but purely to undermine Ren. He was discovered and executed by Allegiant General Pryde, a Palpatine loyalist. [film_st]

The First Order's military assets included Resurgent-class Star Destroyers (larger and more powerful than Imperial-class vessels), TIE/fo fighters, AT-M6 walkers, and the Mandator IV-class Siege Dreadnought. [film_st] Their forces occupied and subjugated numerous worlds across the galaxy after the destruction of the Hosnian system, exploiting the power vacuum left by the New Republic's collapse. [film_st]

The First Order's reign proved short-lived. When Palpatine revealed himself and the Sith Eternal fleet — the Final Order — on Exegol, the First Order was effectively subsumed into his larger plan. [film_st] Following Palpatine's final destruction by Rey and the defeat of the Sith Eternal fleet at the Battle of Exegol, both the First Order and the Final Order collapsed. Uprisings erupted across the galaxy as subjugated worlds overthrew their First Order occupiers. [film_st]

[spoiler_tier: everything]

The Unknown Regions into which the Imperial remnants fled had been partially mapped by the Chiss Ascendancy, and Grand Admiral Thrawn's earlier explorations of the region provided navigational data that made the retreat possible. [legends] The First Order's rise also benefited from sympathetic elements within the New Republic Senate — centrist politicians who admired the Empire's order and secretly funneled resources to the militarist faction in the Unknown Regions. [legends]
