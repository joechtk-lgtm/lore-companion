# Ranni the Witch

[spoiler_tier: limgrave]
## Overview
Ranni is a witch and demigod — daughter of Radagon and Rennala, making her born of both Elden Lords. [game_eldenring] She is an Empyrean: marked by the Two Fingers as a candidate to become the next god of the Lands Between. She refused this fate. She inhabits a small doll-like puppet body. Her true body is dead.

She can be found at Ranni's Rise in Three Sisters, north of Liurnia. She offers a questline.

[spoiler_tier: liurnia]
## Her Questline
Ranni tasks the Tarnished with recovering a hidden treasure from Nokron, Eternal City — accessible after Radahn's defeat. [game_eldenring] Her quest leads through underground cities, Ainsel River, and Lake of Rot. She seeks something she deliberately does not explain.

[spoiler_tier: mountaintops]
## The Night of Black Knives
On the Night of Black Knives, Ranni orchestrated the assassination of Godwyn the Golden using blades imbued with a fragment of the Rune of Death — stolen from Maliketh. [game_eldenring] Simultaneously, she used a sliver of that same rune to kill her own flesh body, separating her soul from it. This let her escape the Greater Will's control: an Empyrean with no body cannot be used as a vessel. She has since operated outside the sight of the Two Fingers. Ranni killed her own body so she could not be enslaved.

[spoiler_tier: main_complete]
## The Age of Stars
Ranni's ending: she becomes a god of her own design — a god of the moon, outside the Greater Will's order. [game_eldenring] The Age of Stars begins: a long journey of stars, cold and dark, guided by no outer god. The Tarnished becomes her consort and travels with her into the dark of the cosmos. It is the ending furthest from the Golden Order's design.
