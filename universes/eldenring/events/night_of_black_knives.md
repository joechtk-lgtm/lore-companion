# The Night of Black Knives

[spoiler_tier: limgrave]
## Overview
The Night of Black Knives preceded the Shattering. [game_eldenring] Assassins struck across the Lands Between in a single night using blades imbued with a fragment of the Rune of Death — stolen from Maliketh, the Black Blade. Their primary target was Godwyn the Golden — Marika's first son, beloved of all in the Lands Between. He was killed.

[spoiler_tier: liurnia]
## Godwyn's Fate
Godwyn's soul was killed, but not his body. [game_eldenring] This was deliberate — the assassins killed only one half of him. His body remains beneath Leyndell, in the Erdtree's roots, deathless and soulless. He is called the Prince of Death. His corruption spreads through the roots of the Erdtree and into the ground above — Deathroot, which the Beast Clergyman hunts and consumes.

[spoiler_tier: mountaintops]
## Ranni's Role
The Night of Black Knives was Ranni's design. [game_eldenring] She arranged the assassination of Godwyn to steal the moment — at the exact instant Godwyn's soul was killed, she used a sliver of the stolen Rune of Death to kill her own body, separating her soul from her flesh. This let her appear dead to the Two Fingers while remaining alive as a soul inhabiting a puppet body. She killed her flesh to escape the Greater Will's plans for her as an Empyrean. The assassins served her purpose while believing they served another.

The Night of Black Knives broke Marika. It was the event that preceded her shattering of the Elden Ring. Whether the two events were connected by Ranni's design or coincidence is not confirmed.
