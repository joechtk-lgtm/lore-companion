# The Fellowship of the Ring

## Overview

The Fellowship of the Ring is the company of nine formed at the Council of Elrond to accompany the Ring-bearer to Mordor — nine against the Nine Ringwraiths, as Elrond frames it, though this symmetry is more poetic than strategic. The Fellowship represents the free peoples of Middle-earth united in a single purpose: Elves, Men, Dwarves, Hobbits, and the wizard who set it all in motion.

## The Nine Walkers

- **Frodo Baggins** — Ring-bearer, Hobbit of the Shire [book_lotr]
- **Samwise Gamgee** — His gardener and companion [book_lotr]
- **Meriadoc Brandybuck (Merry)** — Hobbit, Frodo's cousin [book_lotr]
- **Peregrin Took (Pippin)** — Hobbit, Frodo's youngest companion [book_lotr]
- **Gandalf the Grey** — Wizard, leader in the field [book_lotr]
- **Aragorn son of Arathorn** — Ranger, heir of Isildur [book_lotr]
- **Legolas** — Elf of Mirkwood [book_lotr]
- **Gimli son of Glóin** — Dwarf of Erebor [book_lotr]
- **Boromir son of Denethor** — Man of Gondor [book_lotr]

## Purpose

The Fellowship's mission is to accompany Frodo to Mordor and assist in the destruction of the One Ring. They are not tasked with fighting Sauron's armies — they are nine people who must travel in secret. Their size is calculated to be small enough to pass unnoticed where a great host could not, large enough to defend against ordinary dangers. [book_lotr]

## The Breaking

The Fellowship holds together through Moria and Lothlórien. On the shores of Nen Hithoel, it breaks. Boromir's assault on Frodo forces Frodo to decide: he takes the Ring and goes alone into Mordor. The Fellowship was never sworn to accompany him all the way — only as far as necessary. It has dissolved because it has served its purpose: getting Frodo to the point where he could go on without it.

The remaining eight scatter into the roles the wider war requires: Merry and Pippin into Fangorn and then Rohan and Gondor; Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli to Rohan and the Paths of the Dead; Sam with Frodo into Mordor; Gandalf returning as the White. The Fellowship breaks, and because it breaks, the War of the Ring can be fought on all its fronts simultaneously. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]
