# The Rohirrim (The Horse-lords)

## Overview

The Rohirrim are the horse-lords of Rohan — the Riddermark — a people of the north who settled the grassy plains between the White Mountains and Fangorn Forest roughly 500 years before the War of the Ring. They are long-descended from the Northmen of Rhovanion, related by blood to the ancestors of the Dúnedain of Gondor though the connection is distant. They are not Númenorean; their tradition is closer to the heroic Germanic cultures Tolkien drew from for their language and culture (Old English). Their horses are famous throughout Middle-earth; their relationship with horses is cultural and near-sacred.

## Key Facts

- **Origin:** They came to Rohan at the invitation of Gondor, to hold the province against enemies from the north. The arrangement has been maintained for five centuries through mutual respect and alliance. [book_lotr]
- **Language:** Tolkien represented Rohirric with Old English — their personal names and place names are Old English, giving them a distinct cultural flavor from the Latinate-derived Númenorean traditions of Gondor. [book_lotr]
- **Mead-hall culture:** Their architecture and social structure is the mead-hall — Meduseld in Edoras is a great golden hall, and much of Rohirric life centers on it. This directly parallels the Anglo-Saxon Beowulf tradition Tolkien was expert in. [book_lotr] [film_two_towers]
- **The King's Line:** The Kings of Rohan trace descent from Eorl the Young, who brought the people south. The Oath of Eorl — sworn to Gondor — is honored at the War of the Ring. [book_lotr]

## Rohan Under Threat

In the years before the War of the Ring, Rohan is weakened from multiple directions: Saruman's growing power at Isengard, incursions from Dunlanders (enemies of Rohan stirred up by Saruman), the paralysis of Théoden under Wormtongue's influence. When Gandalf breaks Wormtongue's hold and Théoden is restored, the Rohirrim respond immediately — the muster, the ride to Helm's Deep, and then the ride to Gondor's aid are all expressions of a people that was suppressed and is now free to act as it was always willing to act. ## The Ride of the Rohirrim

The Rohirrim come to the Pelennor Fields at dawn. Théoden's speech before the charge — "Arise, arise, Riders of Théoden" — and the sound of the horns of the Rohirrim are among the most emotionally charged moments in the text. They strike the besieging army from the rear at its moment of apparent victory. Théoden is killed by the Witch-king's fell beast. Éowyn kills the Witch-king. Éomer takes command and drives the battle to its turn. [book_lotr] [film_return_king]
