# The Valar

## Overview

The Valar are the fourteen Ainur — the greatest of the angelic beings created by Ilúvatar before the world — who descended into Arda at its creation to shape it and guide its history. They are not gods in the theological sense Tolkien reserved for Ilúvatar alone; they are more like the Powers of the World, immensely great spirits who took on physical forms and inhabit the Undying Lands. They are responsible for the shaping of Arda, the ordering of sea and sky, the making of the Sun and Moon, and the protection of Valinor.

They are also, critically, limited in what they can do in Middle-earth. They acted directly against Morgoth in the First Age and the resulting wars left Middle-earth broken and the sea reshaped. After that experience, they were more cautious about direct intervention — more likely to send emissaries (the Istari) than to act themselves. This restraint is part of what makes Tolkien's world work: the Powers exist but they are not the solution, because the free peoples must be the ones to act.

## The Major Valar

- **Manwë** — King of the Valar. Lord of winds and air. He is the most devoted to Ilúvatar's will and the least able to understand evil, because it is wholly foreign to him. [book_silmarillion]
- **Varda** — Queen of the Valar, Lady of the Stars (Elbereth, Gilthoniel). She kindled the stars of Arda. Her name is the Elves' most immediate call for protection — more so than any other Vala. [book_silmarillion]
- **Ulmo** — Lord of Waters. He never took a fixed dwelling; he moves through all the seas and rivers of Arda. He was most sympathetic to the peoples of Middle-earth and most willing to act within Middle-earth. [book_silmarillion]
- **Aulë** — Lord of the Earth. Smith and craftsman. He made the Dwarves, impatient for people to come and work the earth with. He repented when Ilúvatar revealed what he had done and offered to destroy them; Ilúvatar gave them independent life. Sauron and Saruman were both of his order of Maiar — his gift of craft turned toward domination. [book_silmarillion]
- **Yavanna** — Lady of Living Things. She made the plants and trees. Her most magnificent creation was Telperion and Laurelin, the Two Trees of Valinor, whose light preceded the Sun and Moon. She asked for defenders of the forests; the Ents were the result. [book_silmarillion]
- **Mandos** — Lord of the Dead. He keeps the halls where the spirits of the dead in Arda wait. He pronounces the fates of the world. [book_silmarillion]
- **Morgoth (Melkor)** — The first and greatest of the Ainur, who turned to evil before the world was made. He is the first Dark Lord, of whom Sauron is only a servant. He was cast out of the world after the War of Wrath. [book_silmarillion]

## Their Limits and Their Choices

The Valar's deliberate restraint after the War of Wrath shaped the entire history of the Third Age. They sent the Istari rather than acting directly. They permitted the history of Middle-earth to unfold according to the choices of its peoples. This is not indifference — it is a principled reluctance to determine outcomes by their own power that echoes Ilúvatar's gift of free will.

The Eagles are sometimes understood as the Valar's instruments in the world — they intervene at moments of extremity but cannot take the Ring, cannot destroy it, cannot solve the problem directly. They can carry people from impossible places after the impossible thing has been done. [book_silmarillion] [book_lotr]
