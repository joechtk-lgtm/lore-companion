# Gondor

## Overview

Gondor is the South Kingdom — the great realm of the Dúnedain established by Isildur and Anárion, sons of Elendil, after the Downfall of Númenor. At its height it was the greatest kingdom of Men in Middle-earth, its power extending from the Sea to the borders of Mordor and beyond. By the time of the War of the Ring, it has been declining for centuries — the line of kings ended long ago and it is ruled by the Stewards, caretaker rulers who hold the kingdom in trust for a king who never comes.

## Key Facts

- **Founded:** At the end of the Second Age, after the Downfall of Númenor, when Elendil and his sons led the faithful Númenoreans to Middle-earth. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]
- **The Stewards:** The line of kings failed when King Eärnur rode to the Witch-king's challenge and was never seen again. The Stewards of the House of Húrin have ruled since — always with the caveat "until the King returns." Denethor is the twenty-sixth and last Steward. [book_lotr]
- **Minas Tirith:** The White City, formerly Minas Anor — the citadel of Gondor. Seven concentric circles of white stone built into the side of Mount Mindolluin. The White Tree of Gondor stands dead in its courtyard at the time of the War of the Ring. [book_lotr]
- **The Decline:** Gondor has been losing territory for centuries — to the Corsairs of Umbar in the south, to Haradrim and Easterlings, to the growing shadow of Mordor. The population has declined. The blood of Númenor is thinning. Denethor himself has a touch of the old greatness, but his despair grows as Sauron's power grows. [book_lotr]
- **Military:** The armies of Gondor are reduced from their peak but still formidable — the fortresses of Minas Tirith and the garrison of Osgiliath have held Gondor's eastern frontier for centuries against steady pressure. [book_lotr]

## The Restoration

When Aragorn takes the throne as King Elessar, the Reunited Kingdom is restored — North and South kingdoms, Arnor and Gondor, joined again. The White Tree blooms again (Gandalf finds a sapling on the slopes of Mindolluin). The age of the Stewards ends and the age of the Restored Kingdom begins. Gondor, in its restoration, is already different from what it was — leaner, more consciously held, the last house of a tradition rather than a thriving empire. It will endure for hundreds of years under the line of Elessar. [book_lotr]
