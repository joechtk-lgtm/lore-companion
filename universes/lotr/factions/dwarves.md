# The Dwarves

## Overview

The Dwarves were made by Aulë the Smith — he was impatient for children to come and shape the earth, and he fashioned the Seven Fathers of the Dwarves in secret before Ilúvatar had awakened the Elves. Ilúvatar revealed what Aulë had done; Aulë repented and offered to destroy them. Ilúvatar gave them independent life instead, but decreed that they must sleep until after the Elves had woken. They are, therefore, not Ilúvatar's Children in the same sense as Elves and Men, but they are not without his blessing. Their stubbornness, their love of craft, and their secretiveness all come from being a people made in secret by a craftsman who was reprimanded.

## Key Facts

- **The Seven Fathers:** Aulë made seven, who became the ancestors of the seven Dwarven clans. The greatest was Durin the Deathless, ancestor of the Longbeards — the most important of the Dwarven clans for the purposes of the Third Age. [book_silmarillion]
- **Erebor (the Lonely Mountain):** The greatest of the Dwarven kingdoms of the Third Age, in the north beneath the Lonely Mountain. Reclaimed by Thorin Oakenshield and Bilbo Baggins in the events of The Hobbit. Gimli's father Glóin was among those Dwarves. [book_lotr] [book_hobbit]
- **Khazad-dûm (Moria):** The greatest of all Dwarven works — the mines beneath the Misty Mountains, delved by Durin's people over thousands of years. They delved too deep and woke the Balrog. [book_lotr]
- **The Ring of Durin:** One of the Seven Dwarf-rings was given to Durin's line. It multiplied gold and stirred avarice — but could not wholly corrupt the Dwarves in the way the rings did to Men, because Dwarves are harder to dominate. Sauron eventually retrieved most of the Seven, or the dragons destroyed them. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]
- **Language:** Khuzdul, the secret language of the Dwarves, is never taught to outsiders and rarely spoken in their presence. [book_lotr]
