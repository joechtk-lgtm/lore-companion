# The Rings of Power

## Overview

The Rings of Power — the great rings made in Eregion in the Second Age — are the central device of Sauron's plan to dominate Middle-earth. Nineteen rings were made: three for the Elves, seven for the Dwarf lords, nine for mortal Men. All were made with Sauron's guidance and touched by his craft. The One Ring was made in secret to rule them all. The rings given to Men did what Sauron intended — their bearers became the Nazgûl, the Ringwraiths, his most terrible servants. The rings given to Dwarves failed; Dwarves were too stubborn to be dominated, and the rings merely inflamed their greed.

## The Three Elven Rings

- **Narya (Ring of Fire):** The red ring, borne by Gandalf (given to him by Círdan, who received it when the Istari arrived). It kindles courage and the hearts of those who falter. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]
- **Nenya (Ring of Water/Adamant):** Borne by Galadriel. It preserves and protects — the source of Lothlórien's timeless quality. [book_lotr]
- **Vilya (Ring of Air):** The greatest of the Three, a sapphire ring. Borne by Elrond. It sustains Rivendell as a place of peace and healing. [book_lotr]

The Three were never touched by Sauron directly — they were made by Celebrimbor alone after he grew suspicious. This is why the Elves who bore them could use them without submitting to Sauron. But they are still linked to the One: when the One Ring is destroyed, their power fades. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]

## The Nine and the Nazgûl

The nine rings given to mortal Men made their bearers powerful kings, sorcerers, and lords — and then unmade them. They became invisible to the living world, sustained only by the Ring's power, utterly enslaved to Sauron's will. They are the Nazgûl — Ringwraiths — and their leader is the Witch-king of Angmar. They can see in darkness, smell the Ring-bearer, and inspire dread that paralyzes the will. [book_lotr]

## The Seven Dwarf Rings

Sauron intended the seven rings given to Dwarves to dominate their lords as the nine dominated Men. Dwarves proved resistant — their hard, stubborn natures made full domination impossible. The rings instead magnified their love of gold and treasures, breeding the great Dwarf hoards that sometimes attracted dragons. Three of the seven were consumed by dragon-fire; Sauron recovered the other four. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]

## After the One Ring

When the One Ring is destroyed, the power of all the rings fades. The Three lose their virtue — the preservation of Lothlórien and Rivendell departs. The age of the Elves and their rings ends. The Fourth Age begins, the age of Men, who have no such rings and no such power. This is the inevitable cost of victory. [book_lotr]
