# The One Ring

## Overview

The One Ring — the Ruling Ring, the Master Ring — is the central object of the entire story. Sauron forged it in the fires of Mount Doom in the Second Age, pouring into it a great part of his own power, his will, his malice, his very essence, so that through it he could dominate the wills of all who bore the lesser rings. It is not merely a weapon; it is a part of Sauron. As long as it exists, Sauron cannot be destroyed. As long as it exists, it will tend toward its maker, and whoever carries it will be slowly remade in Sauron's image.

## Key Facts

- **Its Making:** Sauron forged the One Ring in the volcanic fires of Orodruin (Mount Doom) around the year 1600 of the Second Age, having spent centuries in friendship with the Elvish smiths of Eregion learning their craft. As he put it on, the Elves who bore the Three became aware of him and took them off immediately — but the trap had been sprung for the others. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]
- **Its Inscription:** In Black Speech, on the Ring's surface and readable only in fire: "One Ring to rule them all, One Ring to find them / One Ring to bring them all and in the darkness bind them." The verse in full describes the rings' hierarchy. [book_lotr]
- **The Corruption:** The Ring does not merely grant power — it amplifies what is already in the bearer and twists it. The desire to use the Ring for good ends is itself corrupting, because using it means imposing one's will on others in the way Sauron did. Frodo is corrupted slowly; Boromir sees his desire for it clearly enough to be ashamed. Galadriel refuses it because she understands that if she took it she would become a dark queen, beautiful and terrible. [book_lotr]
- **Why Frodo:** The Ring tempts everyone. It tempts Frodo less badly than it would tempt the powerful, because he has less ambition and less power to magnify. Sam, for similar reasons, can carry it briefly and then voluntarily lay it down — something essentially no one else in the story is capable of. [book_lotr]
- **The Eye of Sauron:** When someone puts on the Ring, they become partly in Sauron's world — invisible to normal eyes but visible to him. The Eye is always looking. [book_lotr]
- **Gollum:** Sméagol possessed the Ring for nearly five hundred years. It extended his life grotesquely while hollowing him out. His obsession with "the Precious" is what the Ring does on a long timescale. [book_lotr]

## The Destruction

The Ring can only be destroyed in the fires of Mount Doom where it was made. Every other fire merely melts it temporarily; it would cool and reform. At the Crack of Doom, Frodo — overtaken at the last moment by the Ring's power — claims it for himself rather than destroying it. Gollum bites off his finger and falls with it into the fire. The Ring is destroyed. Sauron's power collapses with it. The destruction of the Ring is not willed but accidental — the consequence of Gollum's obsession and Frodo's mercy in sparing Gollum's life years earlier. [book_lotr]
