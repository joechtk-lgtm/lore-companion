# Elvish Immortality (The Gift of Elves, the Gift of Men)

## Overview

The question of immortality and mortality — the Gift of the Elves and the Gift of Men — is one of the central philosophical concerns of Tolkien's world. It is not simply that Elves live forever and Men die. The truth is more complicated, more tragic, and more interesting than that: Elves are bound to the world while it lasts; Men are given the freedom to leave it. Neither gift is simply better. Both are given; neither is taken away without cost.

## The Elves

- **Bound to Arda:** Elves do not die of age or disease. They can be slain in battle or by grief. If slain, their spirits go to the Halls of Mandos in Valinor and may in time be rehoused in new bodies. They are essentially immortal within the life of the world (Arda) — but they are of the world, woven into it, and when the world ends they end. [book_silmarillion]
- **Fading:** As the world ages and Men become dominant, Elves diminish. The power they once had in the world fades. Those who remain in Middle-earth into the Fourth Age will dwindle — not die, but lose their presence and their force in the world. The Elves of the Third Age are already far less than the Elves of the First Age. [book_lotr]
- **The Sea-longing:** After Elves have seen the sea or heard a gull's cry, many develop an insatiable longing for Valinor — the Undying Lands. This is the "sea-longing" that Legolas develops after hearing gulls at Pelargir, and that will eventually draw him to sail West even after the War. [book_lotr]
- **The Choice of Half-elven:** Those of mixed Elven and Mortal blood — Eärendil, Elrond, Elros, Arwen, the sons of Elrond — are given a choice between the two kindreds and their respective fates. Elros chose mortal; he became the first King of Númenor and died after five hundred years. Elrond chose Elvish immortality. Arwen, at the time of her marriage to Aragorn, chose mortality. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]

## Men and the Gift of Death

- **Estel:** In Tolkien's theology, death is called the Gift of Men (or the Gift of Ilúvatar to Men) — freedom from the world, a destiny beyond the circles of Arda. Where Elves are bound to the world's fate, Men go somewhere else. What that somewhere is, not even the Valar know. [book_silmarillion]
- **The Shadow:** Morgoth corrupted Men's perception of their gift early in their history, so they learned to fear death rather than accept it. This is the Shadow — not death itself, but the dread of it. The Númenóreans' rebellion against the Ban of the Valar, driven by Sauron, was ultimately the Shadow made manifest in political form. [book_silmarillion]

## Arwen's Choice

When Arwen chooses to wed Aragorn, she gives up her immortality. She becomes mortal, subject to age and death. She will not sail West with Elrond and her kin. This choice is presented not as a tragedy exactly, but as a final gift: she chooses love and the life of the world over safety. After Aragorn's death in old age, Arwen goes to Lothlórien alone and dies there. The last of the great Elvish presences in Middle-earth. [book_lotr]
