# Faramir

## Overview

Faramir is the second son of Denethor, Steward of Gondor, and the Captain of the Rangers of Ithilien. He is the younger brother of Boromir and Tolkien's explicit corrective to the failure Boromir represents — not because Faramir is simply better but because he faces the same test and passes it without struggle. He encounters Frodo and Sam in Ithilien, learns what Frodo carries, and lets them go. He would not lift the Ring even if he found it lying on the road. He is Tolkien's portrait of the Northern virtue refined through Gondorian civilization: the scholar-soldier who reads Elvish and quotes from the lore of Númenor and also commands troops in a hopeless war with quiet competence.

## Key Facts

- **Position:** Captain of the Rangers of Ithilien — the guerrilla force harrying Sauron's supply lines in the province closest to Mordor. [book_lotr]
- **Scholarly temperament:** Unlike Boromir, Faramir is described by Tolkien as a lover of lore and music, of history and learning. He can speak Quenya. He is the son Denethor least understood and least valued. [book_lotr]
- **The test:** He encounters Frodo and Sam in Ithilien, learns they carry the One Ring, and refuses to take it to Gondor despite the military advantage it might represent. In the book, this refusal costs him nothing — he simply lets them go. In the film, his character is temporarily bent toward taking them to Gondor before relenting; Tolkien considered this a misrepresentation of Faramir's nature. [book_lotr] [film_two_towers]
- **Denethor:** His father favored Boromir. He treated Faramir with something approaching contempt for the qualities Tolkien most admires. When Boromir dies, Denethor's grief is genuine but it also sours further against Faramir, who survived when Boromir did not. [book_lotr]

## The Siege

Faramir leads the defense of Osgiliath against a force he cannot hold. He retreats under orders — and is shot by an arrow, returning to Minas Tirith barely alive. Denethor, convinced the battle is lost and his son is dying, attempts to burn them both alive on a funeral pyre in Rath Dínen. Pippin brings Gandalf. Faramir is rescued. Denethor burns alone. [book_lotr] [film_return_king]

Faramir heals in the Houses of Healing under Aragorn's hands. He meets Éowyn there. Their courtship, conducted from adjacent windows of the Houses of Healing while the armies go to the Black Gate, is one of the quieter moments of the ending. He becomes Steward of Gondor under King Elessar and Prince of Ithilien. [book_lotr]
