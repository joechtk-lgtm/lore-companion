# Éowyn

## Overview

Éowyn is a shield-maiden of Rohan, niece of King Théoden, sister of Éomer. She is the person who kills the Witch-king of Angmar at the Battle of the Pelennor Fields — fulfilling a prophecy that "no living man" can kill him, since she is no man. Her arc is one of Tolkien's most complete character stories: a person trapped by her circumstances, seeking death in battle as the only honor available to her, finding instead the very thing that her desperate reach for glory could not have predicted.

## Key Facts

- **Position:** Shield-maiden of Rohan. She is trained in arms and combat — this is within Rohirrim culture. [book_lotr]
- **The cage:** Her situation before the War of the Ring is one of enforced inaction. She is required to remain behind, to manage the household, to tend her uncle while Wormtongue's influence corrupts him. She is capable of leading armies and has no army to lead. [book_lotr]
- **Dernhelm:** She disguises herself as a male Rider of Rohan, calls herself Dernhelm, and rides to the Pelennor Fields with Merry concealed in her gear. She cannot be left behind because she cannot accept the life being left behind implies. [book_lotr] [film_return_king]
- **The prophecy:** The ancient saying that "no living man" could slay the Witch-king was uttered by Glorfindel and appeared in the lore of Gondor. Tolkien did not intend it as a loophole cleverly exploited — he intended it as fate working itself out. Éowyn was not present because of the prophecy; the prophecy was present because of Éowyn. [book_lotr]
- **Faramir:** In the Houses of Healing, while recovering from the Black Breath, she meets Faramir. Their relationship is one of the quieter threads of the ending — two people who came through darkness to find something unexpected on the other side. [book_lotr]

## The Witch-king

On the Pelennor Fields, the Witch-king kills Théoden's horse and Théoden is pinned. He approaches Théoden to deliver the killing blow. Éowyn interposes herself. The Witch-king's boast — "No living man am I" — is met with her response: "But no living man am I." She kills his fell beast and then, when he rises, her blade shatters on his helmet but Merry stabs him from behind with a blade of the Barrow-downs forged specifically against him. She drives her sword through his faceless head.

She is left by the Black Breath — a spiritual death-cold of the Nazgûl — and must be healed by Aragorn in the Houses of Healing. [book_lotr] [film_return_king]
