# Aragorn

## Overview

Aragorn son of Arathorn is the heir of Isildur, the last descendant of the Kings of Númenor, and the rightful claimant to the throne of Gondor and the reunited kingdoms. He has been living in the wilderness as a Ranger of the North — known as Strider to the folk of Bree, unknown to Gondor — for decades, his heritage concealed for his own protection from his youth. He is approximately 87 years old at the time of the War of the Ring, carrying the longevity of his Númenorean blood.

He is a man of exceptional capability — the finest tracker, swordsman, and captain of his generation — who has spent his life in deliberate obscurity, neither claiming the throne nor abandoning the duties that come with his lineage. His arc is the long patience of a man who knows what he is and waits until the world is ready to know it too.

## Key Facts

- **Lineage:** Descended directly from Isildur, from Elendil who came from Númenor, from the half-elven line through the oldest lines of men. He carries in his blood the longevity and vitality of Númenor — he will live for 210 years and retain his strength until he chooses to die. [book_lotr]
- **Upbringing:** Raised in Rivendell by Elrond after his father's death. Known there as Estel (Hope) — his name and heritage concealed from him until he came of age. Elrond revealed his heritage when he was twenty. [book_lotr]
- **The Sword that was Broken:** Anduril, the Flame of the West, reforged from the shards of Narsil — the sword with which Isildur cut the Ring from Sauron's hand and that broke beneath him in the Gladden Fields. Reforging it is a political as much as practical act: it announces the return of the king. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship] [film_return_king]
- **The Dúnedain:** He is the Chieftain of the Rangers of the North — the remnant Dúnedain who maintained watch over the Shire and the north without acknowledgment or payment for generations. [book_lotr]
- **Arwen:** He loves Arwen Undómiel, Elrond's daughter, and has loved her since he first saw her as a young man. Their marriage requires him to claim the throne — Elrond will not give his daughter to a wandering Ranger, however noble his blood, and Arwen's choice carries enormous cost. [book_lotr]
- **The Paths of the Dead:** He calls the Dead of Dunharrow — the oathbreakers cursed by Isildur for their failure — to fulfill their ancient oath and aid the fight for Gondor. He is the only one of Isildur's line who can summon them. [book_lotr] [film_return_king]

## Strider

In Bree, he is Strider — rough-looking, pipe-smoking, suspicious. The hobbits' first impression is uncertainty. Frodo has been warned he might encounter a friend named Aragorn, but Aragorn's appearance does not inspire immediate confidence. He earns it over the journey to Weathertop and beyond. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]

## The Council and the Fellowship

At the Council of Elrond, Aragorn reveals himself — not with ceremony but through the moment requiring it. Boromir questions whether the sword that was broken can be trusted. Aragorn draws the shards of Narsil. It is not a declaration; it is a demonstration. He joins the Fellowship as its captain in the field. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]

[spoiler_tier: rohan]
## The Three Hunters

After the Fellowship's breaking, Aragorn leads Legolas and Gimli on the pursuit of Merry and Pippin's captors across Rohan — the Three Hunters. The pursuit reveals his tracking ability, his endurance, and the respect he commands even from those who know him only by legend. In Rohan he helps break Saruman's hold on Théoden. He takes the Palantír from Saruman's tower and uses it — revealing himself to Sauron deliberately, drawing Sauron's gaze away from Frodo. [book_lotr] [film_two_towers]

[spoiler_tier: gondor]
## The Return of the King

Aragorn comes to Minas Tirith not from the main road but through the Paths of the Dead, with the Dead fulfilling their oath at Pelargir. He arrives by river as the siege reaches its worst. He fights. He heals — the hands of the King are the hands of a healer; this is how the people of Gondor will know he is the true king. He treats the wounded in the Houses of Healing before the armies march to the Black Gate.

At the Black Gate, the final confrontation — outnumbered, the distraction being offered to give Frodo the last chance he needs — Aragorn leads. The Ring is destroyed. He is crowned by Gandalf in Minas Tirith as Elessar Telcontar, King of the Reunited Kingdom. He marries Arwen. He rules for 120 years. [book_lotr] [film_return_king]
