# Treebeard (Fangorn)

## Overview

Treebeard — Fangorn in his own tongue — is the oldest of the Ents, the tree-herders of Fangorn Forest. He is among the oldest living things in Middle-earth, having walked the forests since before the Elves woke. The Ents were made by Ilúvatar in response to Yavanna's plea — she had made the trees and the plants, and feared they would be hewn without defense while Aulë was making Dwarves with axes. Manwë interceded with Ilúvatar, and the Ents were the response: guardians of the forests, slow to anger, long of memory, the shepherds of the trees.

## Key Facts

- **Age:** One of the oldest creatures in Middle-earth. He remembers the world before the Elves named the mountains. [book_lotr]
- **Voice:** Ents speak slowly and at great length — they do not say anything in their own language that is not worth taking a very long time over. When Treebeard speaks Quenya (the full name of a thing, in Ent-speech) it takes days. This is not a flaw; it reflects their nature. [book_lotr]
- **The Entwives:** The Ents lost the Entwives long ago — the female Ents who loved the ordered gardens and fields rather than the wild forests. They went east to cultivate. When Sauron's wars destroyed those lands, the Entwives were lost. The Ents have not found them. There are no more Entings. The Ents are fading. [book_lotr]
- **Treebeard and Saruman:** He had known Saruman — there was a time when Saruman walked in Fangorn and they spoke. He trusted him. Saruman's Orcs have been cutting the trees of Fangorn. Treebeard calls him a liar woven of many different lies. [book_lotr] [film_two_towers]
- **Hasty:** Treebeard says hobbits are not Orcs, and they are not exactly Elves or Men, but they seem to be on the right side. He also says he is not altogether on anyone's side, because no one is altogether on his side. He decides to march on Isengard because he is angry. [book_lotr]

## The March on Isengard

Merry and Pippin's encounter with Treebeard and the subsequent Entmoot — the great council of Ents, which takes three days — leads to the decision to march on Isengard. The Ents are deliberate. When they decide, they do not waver. They come to Isengard like a forest walking, break the dams, flood the grounds, tear down the walls. Saruman retreats to Orthanc. The Ents stand around the tower and wait. [book_lotr] [film_two_towers]

Tolkien's note: the Ents' attack on Isengard is a direct response to Saruman's industrialism. The machines and fire of Isengard are the specific desecration of what Treebeard was made to protect. Their response is not political; it is natural.
