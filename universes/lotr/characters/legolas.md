# Legolas

## Overview

Legolas is a Wood-elf of Mirkwood, son of King Thranduil, and the Elven representative in the Fellowship of the Ring. He brings the gifts of Elvish perception and woodcraft — sight beyond mortal range, hearing that catches what ears of Men and Hobbits cannot, an archer's precision that exceeds any human capability. He is ancient by any reckoning though he appears ageless; the exact count of his years is not given, but he predates most of the events of the Third Age. His friendship with Gimli the Dwarf is one of the most significant relationships in the trilogy — a reconciliation across a centuries-long enmity between their peoples.

## Key Facts

- **Race:** Sindarin Elf, the Wood-elves of Mirkwood. [book_lotr]
- **Father:** King Thranduil of the Woodland Realm in Mirkwood — the same king who imprisoned Thorin Oakenshield's company in The Hobbit. [book_lotr] [book_hobbit]
- **Perceptions:** Elvish sight allows him to see across distances that defeat mortal eyes in daylight. In Rohan, he counts the Riders of Rohan at a distance Aragorn cannot see. He sees things in Moria before others are aware of them. [book_lotr]
- **Bow:** He fights primarily with a bow — described as rarely missing, moving and shooting with a fluidity that reads as supernatural but is simply Elvish capability. [book_lotr]
- **The Sea-longing:** In Lothlórien he hears the cry of seagulls — awakening the Sea-longing, the desire for the Undying Lands that is kindled in Elves by the sound of the sea. It haunts him thereafter. [book_lotr]
- **Friendship with Gimli:** They begin the Fellowship with mutual suspicion rooted in the historic tension between Elves and Dwarves. Over the course of the quest they become the closest of friends. Gimli is permitted to enter Lothlórien and begs for a strand of Galadriel's hair. Legolas eventually sails West — and takes Gimli with him, the only Dwarf ever to reach the Undying Lands. [book_lotr]
