# Galadriel

## Overview

Galadriel is the Lady of Lothlórien, co-ruler of the Golden Wood with her husband Celeborn, and one of the most powerful Elves remaining in Middle-earth at the time of the War of the Ring. She is also among the oldest — she was born in Valinor before the First Age, participated in the rebellion of the Noldor and the crossing of the Helcaraxë, fought through the wars of the First Age, and has lived in Middle-earth ever since. She is the only person in the Fellowship's journey who can look directly at the One Ring and understand its full power, because she herself bears one of the Three Elven Rings — Nenya, the Ring of Water — and has the wisdom of many thousands of years.

She is the most explicit test the Fellowship encounters: a person of immense power, of genuine goodness, who is offered the Ring and refuses it.

## Key Facts

- **Age:** Born in Valinor in the Years of the Trees, before the creation of the Sun and Moon. She is among the oldest and most powerful of the Noldor remaining in Middle-earth. [book_silmarillion]
- **Nenya:** The Ring of Water, one of the Three Elven Rings forged by Celebrimbor. It has helped maintain Lothlórien as a place outside of time's normal passage. When the One Ring is destroyed, Nenya's power will fade and Lothlórien will diminish. [book_lotr]
- **The test:** When Frodo offers her the Ring, she experiences its full temptation — she would use it for good, she says, and become a great queen, and all would love her and despair. She refuses. She will diminish and go into the West and remain Galadriel. This is one of the great moments of the text. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]
- **Her Mirror:** The Mirror of Galadriel shows things that were, are, and may be. What it shows is not prophecy but possibility. Looking into it is not without danger. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]
- **Her history:** In the First Age she was among the Noldorin Elves who followed Fëanor's rebellion against the Valar and came to Middle-earth. She did not swear the Oath of Fëanor and was not guilty of the Kinslaying at Alqualondë, but she came east regardless. She has been seeking permission to return to the West for an Age of the world. [book_silmarillion]

## Lothlórien

The Fellowship passes through Lothlórien after Moria. It is the first safe harbor. Galadriel reads the minds of the Fellowship individually — she looks in and does not speak of what she sees, but her gaze is unmistakable. She gives gifts to each member of the Fellowship when they depart: lembas, Elven cloaks, the phial of light for Frodo, the box of earth from her garden for Sam. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]

## Departure

When the Ring is destroyed, Nenya's power fades. Lothlórien will diminish. It is no longer sustained. Galadriel goes to the Grey Havens and sails West — she is at last permitted to return to Valinor, her long exile over. The Ring-bearers sail together. She, who refused the Ring and remained herself, goes home. [book_lotr]

## The First Age

In the Silmarillion's account, Galadriel is among the mightiest of the Noldor — she is explicitly described as the greatest of the Elven women, equal to Fëanor in power if not in the specific gifts of craft. She came to Middle-earth partly because she desired a land to rule as her own, not in submission to the authority of the Valar. Her refusal of the Ring is therefore a refusal not just of power but of the particular desire for dominion that she herself once possessed. She spent several thousand years growing past what she was. [book_silmarillion]
