# Gimli

## Overview

Gimli son of Glóin is the Dwarven representative in the Fellowship of the Ring. He is the son of one of Thorin's companions from the Quest of Erebor — meaning the events of The Hobbit are his father's story, and he grew up with them as living memory. He comes to Rivendell as part of his father's delegation. He joins the Fellowship out of Dwarven stubbornness and ancestral obligation, and becomes one of its most capable fighters and most steadfast members.

## Key Facts

- **Lineage:** Son of Glóin, one of the thirteen Dwarves who traveled with Bilbo Baggins and Gandalf to Erebor. [book_lotr] [book_hobbit]
- **Axes:** He fights with axes. His killing count at Helm's Deep becomes one of the running competitions with Legolas. [book_lotr]
- **Moria:** The Fellowship passes through Moria partly because of Gimli's desire to see the ancient Dwarven kingdom — and what they find is ruin. The record of the last days is carved in stone: "We cannot get out. The end comes." He grieves. He fights. He continues. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]
- **Lothlórien:** He enters Lothlórien — remarkable in itself, given the Elves' suspicion of Dwarves — and is moved to something approaching reverence by Galadriel. His request: a single strand of her hair (Fëanor himself had once asked for three). She gives him three. [book_lotr]
- **The Glittering Caves:** After the battle of Helm's Deep, Gimli describes the Glittering Caves of Aglarond to Legolas with a reverence that surprises even himself. He will later become Lord of the Glittering Caves, founding a new Dwarven stronghold there. [book_lotr]
- **The Undying Lands:** Legolas eventually takes him to the Undying Lands when he sails West. This is unprecedented — no Dwarf had ever made that passage. [book_lotr]
