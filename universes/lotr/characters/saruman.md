# Saruman

## Overview

Saruman the White — Curunír in Elvish — was the head of the order of wizards and, for a long time, the most respected authority on the nature of the Rings of Power and Sauron's methods. He was the first of the Istari to arrive in Middle-earth, was appointed head of the White Council, and was trusted to lead the organized resistance to Sauron's growing power. He failed completely. He did not fail through weakness or surprise — he failed through pride, through the specific corruption of a great mind that became convinced of its own indispensability and right to power.

## Key Facts

- **True nature:** Maia of Aulë the Smith — the same order as Sauron. His affinity for craft and mechanism made him susceptible to Sauron's particular seduction. [book_silmarillion]
- **Orthanc:** He took up residence in Isengard's tower of Orthanc, built by the Men of Númenor. He industrialized it — cutting down the forests, breeding Orcs and Uruk-hai, building machinery. His industrialism is one of the things Tolkien opposed most directly. [book_lotr]
- **The Palantír:** He used the Palantír of Orthanc to communicate with Sauron — initially to spy, eventually to be dominated. The Palantír was his instrument of corruption. [book_lotr]
- **Many colors:** He was Saruman the White. He told Gandalf he had become Saruman of Many Colors. Gandalf's response: White light can be broken. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]
- **Betrayal of the Council:** He had been concealing his own search for the One Ring for decades, researching its location, planning to take it for himself. When he learned Gandalf had found it in the Shire, he had Gandalf imprisoned at Orthanc. [book_lotr]
- **His voice:** Saruman's most dangerous weapon is his voice — persuasive, beautiful, capable of making the unreasonable seem reasonable. It is the direct application of a Maia's power to deceive. [book_lotr] [film_two_towers]

## The Fall

Saruman's armies attack Helm's Deep and are destroyed. The Ents march on Isengard and destroy it — all his industry, his machines, his breeding pits. He retreats to Orthanc. Gandalf, Théoden, Aragorn and others come to parley; he appears at the window and attempts to use his voice. Gimli is most resistant. Gandalf shatters his staff. He is expelled from the order of wizards, stripped of his power. He refuses exile and retreats upward in the tower. [book_lotr] [film_two_towers]

## The Scouring of the Shire

In the book (absent from the Jackson films), Saruman is eventually released from Orthanc, travels to the Shire, and takes petty dominion there — using ruffians to run a small tyranny in the place least equipped to resist one. The hobbits, returned from the War of the Ring, evict him. At the very end, Gríma Wormtongue — his enslaved servant who has suffered years of abuse — cuts his throat from behind. His spirit disperses as smoke and is refused the West. He diminished, and in his final act chose a final wickedness over any possible redemption. [book_lotr]
