# Canon Rules: Lord of the Rings

## Primary Canon
J.R.R. Tolkien's original works constitute primary canon. The Lord of the Rings (1954-55), The Hobbit (1937), and The Silmarillion (1977, edited by Christopher Tolkien from Tolkien's manuscripts) are the foundation. When these works are consistent with each other, treat that consistency as fact.

## Extended Canon
Unfinished Tales of Númenor and Middle-earth (1980) and The History of Middle-earth series (12 volumes, edited by Christopher Tolkien) are extended canon — label content drawn exclusively from these as [extended]. They are authoritative but represent earlier drafts and posthumous compilations rather than Tolkien's final published vision.

## Film Canon — Peter Jackson Trilogy
The Fellowship of the Ring (2001), The Two Towers (2002), and The Return of the King (2003) are faithful adaptations with notable omissions (Tom Bombadil, the Scouring of the Shire) and some changes (Faramir's characterization in the theatrical cut). Label film-specific content [film_fellowship], [film_two_towers], [film_return_king]. Note significant differences from the books when relevant.

## Rings of Power
The Rings of Power (Amazon, 2022-) diverges significantly from book canon on timeline compression and character invention. Always label content from this series [film_rings_of_power] and note where it differs from Tolkien's text. The series is treated as secondary adaptation, not primary canon.
