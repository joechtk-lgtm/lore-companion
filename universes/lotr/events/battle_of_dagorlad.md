# The Battle of Dagorlad and the Last Alliance

## Overview

The Battle of Dagorlad — fought on the plain before the Black Gate of Mordor at the end of the Second Age (year 3434 SA) — was the culminating battle of the Last Alliance of Elves and Men, and the greatest military engagement of the Second Age. The Last Alliance was formed by Gil-galad (High King of the Noldor in Middle-earth) and Elendil (King of Arnor and Gondor, leader of the Númenórean exiles) to bring Sauron down once and for all after his escape from Númenor and his return to Mordor.

## The Last Alliance

After the Downfall of Númenor, Sauron — no longer able to take fair form — returned to Mordor and rebuilt his power. Gil-galad and Elendil, who had witnessed the corruption of Númenor and knew Sauron's nature directly, allied. They gathered the armies of the free peoples: Noldor Elves, Sindar Elves, the Númenórean exiles of Gondor and Arnor, Men of various peoples. It was, until the War of the Ring, the greatest coalition ever assembled in Middle-earth. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]

## The Battle

The army gathered at Rivendell and marched through Rhovanion to the plain of Dagorlad outside the Morannon (Black Gate). The battle was long and terrible. Sauron's forces were defeated in the open field; the allies crossed into Mordor and besieged Barad-dûr for seven years. [book_lotr]

## The Siege and its End

After seven years, Sauron himself came out of Barad-dûr to fight. He killed both Gil-galad and Elendil — the two greatest leaders of the Alliance — with his bare hands. Isildur, Elendil's son, took the broken hilt-shard of Narsil and cut the One Ring from Sauron's finger. Sauron's body was destroyed; his spirit fled. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]

## Isildur's Failure

The One Ring was taken by Isildur. Elrond and Círdan counseled him to destroy it in the fires of Mount Doom while they stood there on the slopes of Orodruin. Isildur refused. He claimed it as weregild for the death of his father and brother. He marched north with the Ring. In the Gladden Fields, his small company was ambushed; Isildur tried to flee by swimming the Anduin; the Ring slipped from his finger in the water; the Orcs saw him and killed him. The Ring was lost in the river, and Sauron's defeat was not final. The Third Age began. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]

## Why It Matters for the Main Story

Every event in the Lord of the Rings traces to this moment of failure. The Ring was lost but not destroyed. Sauron eventually recovered enough to return. The Third Age is the age defined by Isildur's decision at Mount Doom. The prologue to the film explicitly shows it; the books recount it in the Council of Elrond. Elrond was there. He tried to stop it. He carries that failure for three thousand years. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]
