# The Council of Elrond

## Overview

The Council of Elrond takes place in Rivendell in the autumn of Year 3018 of the Third Age — a gathering of representatives of the free peoples, convened by Elrond, to determine what should be done with the One Ring that Frodo has brought there. It is the scene in which the full history of the Ring is laid out, the factions of Middle-earth are shown in miniature, and the decision is made to destroy the Ring by carrying it to Mount Doom.

## The Gathering

The Council assembles by what seems like chance but is acknowledged to be more: Boromir came seeking an answer to a prophetic dream; the Dwarves (Glóin and Gimli) came to report that Sauron had sent messengers to the Lonely Mountain seeking Bilbo and the Ring; Legolas came with news of Gollum's escape from the Wood-elves; Frodo and Gandalf were already there. Aragorn arrived with Frodo. Elrond convened the Council because the Ring was in Rivendell and a decision had to be made. [book_lotr]

## What Is Revealed

- Gandalf's account of discovering the Ring's identity through Gollum and the fire test.
- Gandalf's capture by Saruman (and his rescue by Gwaihir the Eagle) — revealing Saruman's treachery.
- The full history of the Ring: its making, the War of the Elves and Sauron, the Last Alliance, Isildur's failure, the Ring's loss and long concealment, Bilbo's finding of it.
- Gollum's history and the chain of possession from Gollum to Bilbo to Frodo.
- Glóin's account of Sauron's messengers — revealing that Sauron already knows the Ring is somewhere in the North.
- Boromir's account of Gondor's situation and his prophetic dream. [book_lotr]

## The Decision

The Council debates: can the Ring be used against Sauron? No — it would corrupt anyone who used it, and if a new Dark Lord wielded it, Sauron's return would be preferable. Can it be hidden? No — Sauron will find it eventually. Can it be sent to the Undying Lands? No — that would bring the danger there and it would not destroy the Ring. Can it be given to Tom Bombadil, who seems immune? He would lose it or forget it; the Ring would remain.

Only one option remains: the Ring must be destroyed in the fires of Mount Doom. And it cannot be sent alone. [book_lotr]

## The Fellowship

Frodo volunteers to carry the Ring — or rather, says he will take it, not knowing where to begin. Elrond accepts. Gandalf, Aragorn, Boromir, Legolas, Gimli, Merry, Pippin, and Sam complete the Fellowship of the Ring — nine walkers, to oppose the nine Ringwraiths. [book_lotr]

The Council of Elrond is the scene that most fully explains the nature of the Ring and the stakes of the Quest. It is long in the book and detailed; the films compress it significantly. The crucial difference: in the book, no one at the Council argues to use the Ring for long — the corrupting logic is disposed of quickly, and the debate is about alternatives to destroying it. The decision to destroy it is reached by elimination of all other options. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]
