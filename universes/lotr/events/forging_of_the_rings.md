# The Forging of the Rings of Power

## Overview

The Rings of Power were made in Eregion in the Second Age — a collaboration between Sauron (then calling himself Annatar, "Lord of Gifts") and the Elvish smiths of Eregion, chief among them Celebrimbor, the greatest craftsman of the age. It was one of history's most consequential betrayals: Sauron's disguise was designed from the beginning to enable the forging of the Rings, and the Rings were designed from the beginning to be controlled by the One.

## The Deception

Around the year 1200 of the Second Age, a fair-seeming stranger calling himself Annatar came to the Elvish realms bearing gifts and offering wisdom. Gil-galad and Galadriel refused him — they had seen Sauron in Valinor and were not deceived. The smiths of Eregion, led by Celebrimbor (grandson of Fëanor), welcomed him. His knowledge of craft and ring-lore was genuine; he taught them much that was true. The teaching was the trap. [book_silmarillion]

## The Making

Working with Celebrimbor, Sauron guided the creation of the Rings of Power — rings that could preserve what was beautiful, enhance power, prevent decay. The Three highest rings (Narya, Nenya, Vilya) were made by Celebrimbor alone, in secret, after he began to suspect Sauron's motives. The Seven and the Nine were made with Sauron's direct involvement. [book_silmarillion] [book_lotr]

Then, in secret in Orodruin, Sauron forged the One Ring — pouring into it his will, his power, his malice — and put it on. The Elves who bore the rings immediately felt it and took them off. The trap was sprung.

## The War of the Elves and Sauron

Sauron demanded the rings back. The Elves refused. Sauron went to war. Eregion was destroyed, Celebrimbor was captured and tortured for the location of the rings, and killed. The Three, which he would not reveal under torture, were distributed to Gil-galad (Narya and Vilya, later passed on), and Galadriel (Nenya). Sauron recovered many of the remaining rings. Eregion was left in ruin. [book_silmarillion]

## Why It Matters

The entire arc of the Third Age — Sauron's return, the quest to destroy the Ring, the fading of the Elves — is the consequence of this event. The Three kept Lothlórien and Rivendell alive. The Nine made the Nazgûl. The One's existence meant Sauron could never be permanently destroyed as long as it existed and could never fully reclaim his power while it was lost.
