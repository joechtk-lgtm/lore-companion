# The Shire

## Overview

The Shire is the home of the Hobbits — a region in the northwest of Middle-earth, in the old lands of the North Kingdom of Arnor, granted to the hobbits by King Argeleb II around 1,600 years before the War of the Ring. It is a land of rolling green hills, farms, inns, and carefully kept smials (hobbit-holes). It is, by design and by instinct, a place that does not participate in the wider world. No large armies have marched through it. No great battles have been fought on its soil, not within living hobbit memory. It is one of the most ordinary places in Middle-earth, and its ordinariness is the point.

## Key Facts

- **Size:** Roughly 120 miles east to west, 150 miles north to south. The Brandywine River forms its eastern border. [book_lotr]
- **Hobbiton:** The central settlement. Bag End, on the Hill, is among the most respected hobbit-holes — property of the Baggins family, one of the wealthiest and most established Hobbit families. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]
- **The Green Dragon:** The local inn. Where Frodo, Sam, Merry, and Pippin drink and are oblivious to everything before the adventure begins. [book_lotr]
- **Pipe-weed:** The Shire's most famous export. Grown in Longbottom and elsewhere. Taken up by Men and Wizards as well as Hobbits. A hobby Tolkien found appropriate for a people who like comfort and small pleasures. [book_lotr]
- **The Shirriffs:** The Shire's only law enforcement — a small police force of about twelve hobbits who deal with stray animals. They have no martial function. The Shire has no army. [book_lotr]

## What the Shire Represents

Tolkien was explicit that the Shire was modeled on the English countryside he loved — the rural Midlands before industrialization transformed them. It is an ideal of the small, the ordinary, and the good: people who care about their gardens, their meals, their families, their neighbors, and very little else. Its contrast with Mordor is not accidental.

The hobbits who leave it and come back are changed by what they have seen. The Shire itself is largely unchanged — except in the books, where Saruman has briefly taken it over in pettiness. [book_lotr]

## The Scouring of the Shire (Book only)

In the book, Saruman retreats to the Shire and runs a small tyranny there — cutting down trees, building mills, using ruffians to bully the hobbits. The returned hobbits — Frodo, Sam, Merry, Pippin — organize the Shire's resistance and drive them out. Merry and Pippin are now soldiers of Rohan and Gondor; they handle the organizing with an ease that alarms the hobbits who didn't leave. Gríma kills Saruman; Sam uses Galadriel's gift to replant the trees. This chapter is absent from the Jackson films. Tolkien considered it essential — the heroes cannot be protected from the world they went out to protect. [book_lotr]
