# Gondor (The South Kingdom)

## Overview

Gondor is the greatest realm of Men in the Third Age — founded at the end of the Second Age by Isildur and Anárion after the Downfall of Númenor, built on the foundations of Númenórean skill and knowledge, sustained for three thousand years against the slow encroachment of Mordor's shadow. Its capital is Minas Tirith, the White City, seven circles of white stone built against the shoulder of Mount Mindolluin. The realm extends from the Anduin in the east to the sea in the west, from the White Mountains in the north to the borders of Near Harad in the south — though much of that extent has been lost over the centuries of decline.

## Key Facts

- **Minas Tirith:** The White City, formerly Minas Anor (Tower of the Sun). Seven concentric walls of white stone, each higher than the last, built against the rock of Mindolluin. The Citadel at the top, with its courtyard and the dead White Tree of Gondor, the symbol of the realm's lost vitality. [book_lotr] [film_return_king]
- **The White Tree:** The tree in the citadel courtyard descends from Nimloth of Númenor, which descends from Celeborn of Tol Eressëa, which descends from Galathilion, the image of Telperion the White Tree of Valinor. When the line of kings fails, the tree dies. It blooms again when Aragorn finds a sapling on the slopes of Mindolluin. [book_lotr]
- **Osgiliath:** The original capital of Gondor — the city on the Anduin, bridge city between east and west banks, now ruined and contested. It fell in the days of civil war (the Kin-strife), was retaken, fell again to the forces of Mordor, and at the time of the War of the Ring is a ruin held tenuously by Gondor's soldiers. [book_lotr]
- **Denethor:** Twenty-sixth and last Steward of Gondor. A man of great intelligence and will, with Númenórean blood stronger than most men of his time, who has been watching the growth of Sauron's power through a palantír — which Sauron has been using to slowly break him. [book_lotr]
- **The Beacons of Gondor:** The signal fires on the peaks of the White Mountains — a chain from Minas Tirith to Edoras — lit when Gondor calls for Rohan's aid. Lighting the beacons is one of the great images of the War of the Ring. [book_lotr] [film_return_king]
- **Minas Morgul:** Formerly Minas Ithil (Tower of the Moon) — Gondor's eastern fortress, lost to the Nazgûl centuries before the War of the Ring. Now a place of sorcery and dread, lit by cold light, the seat of the Witch-king of Angmar. [book_lotr]

## The Siege and Restoration

The Siege of Gondor — the great assault on Minas Tirith, the armies of Mordor breaking the gates, Denethor's despair and death — ends when the Rohirrim arrive on the Pelennor Fields at dawn. The battle is turned. Aragorn arrives with the dead men of the Paths of the Dead and the corsair fleet. The siege is broken. The army of the West marches to the Black Gate as a feint to draw Sauron's attention while Frodo and Sam complete their task.

When the Ring is destroyed, Gondor is restored. Aragorn takes the throne as King Elessar, the White Tree blooms again, and the Reunited Kingdom begins. [book_lotr] [film_return_king]
