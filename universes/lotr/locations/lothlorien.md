# Lothlórien (The Golden Wood)

## Overview

Lothlórien — Lórien of the Blossom — is the greatest Elvish realm remaining in Middle-earth at the time of the War of the Ring, a forest in the angle between the Celebrant and the Anduin, where the mallorn trees grow with silver bark and golden leaves that do not fall in autumn. It is the realm of Celeborn and Galadriel, and it is protected and preserved by Galadriel's ring Nenya — the Ring of Water, the Ring of Adamant — which holds time at bay. To enter Lothlórien is to step out of the Third Age into something older and cleaner.

## Key Facts

- **Galadriel:** Lady of the Golden Wood. She is one of the oldest beings in Middle-earth — a Noldorin Elf who came to Middle-earth from Valinor in the First Age, before the creation of the Sun. She has lived through the full span of Elvish history in exile. She bears Nenya and knows more of Sauron's nature than almost any living being. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]
- **Celeborn:** Lord of Lothlórien, Galadriel's husband. A Sindarin Elf of great age and wisdom. Often underestimated in comparison to Galadriel; he is a lord of genuine power and strategic understanding. [book_lotr]
- **Nenya:** The Ring of Water, the Ring of Adamant. It preserves Lothlórien — the mallorn trees, the absence of decay, the sense that time moves differently within the wood. Its power is bound to Galadriel and, ultimately, to the fate of the One Ring. [book_lotr]
- **The Caras Galadhon:** The city of the Galadhrim — a hill crowned with trees so tall they seem like towers, hung with lanterns of white flame, where Celeborn and Galadriel hold court in a flet (platform) among the highest branches. [book_lotr]
- **The Mirror of Galadriel:** A basin of water in which things may be seen — the past, the present, the possible future. It shows what it chooses. Galadriel offers both Sam and Frodo the chance to look. What it shows may not be what will happen, but it is always true in some sense. [book_lotr]
- **The Gifts:** Before the Fellowship departs Lothlórien, Galadriel gives each member a gift fitted to their nature and need. Frodo receives the phial of Galadriel — a vial of the light of Eärendil's star, which will later save him and Sam in Shelob's lair. Sam receives soil from Lothlórien and the seed of a mallorn tree. [book_lotr]

## After the War

When the One Ring is destroyed and Nenya's power fades, Lothlórien begins to change. The mallorn leaves fall. The timeless quality of the wood departs. Celeborn remains for a time; Galadriel departs to the West with the other Ring-bearers. Eventually Celeborn leaves too, and Lothlórien is empty — the greatest of the Elvish realms of the Third Age, deserted. The trees remain. The leaves fall as they should. [book_lotr]
