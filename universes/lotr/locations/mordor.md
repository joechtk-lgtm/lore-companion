# Mordor

## Overview

Mordor is Sauron's realm — a vast plateau in the southeast of Middle-earth, enclosed on three sides by mountain ranges (the Ered Lithui to the north, the Ephel Dúath to the west and south) that make it nearly impossible to enter without going through the passes Sauron guards. The interior is a wasteland of ash and volcanic desolation, heated from below and starved of rain. The only things that grow there are put there to serve Sauron's purposes. It is not merely hostile; it is a landscape shaped by domination over centuries.

## Key Facts

- **Barad-dûr:** The Dark Tower — Sauron's fortress in Mordor, built with the power of the One Ring in the Second Age. It was thrown down when the Ring was cut from his hand at the end of the Second Age, but rebuilt when he returned to power in the Third Age. It cannot be permanently destroyed while the Ring exists. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]
- **Mount Doom (Orodruin):** The volcano at the heart of Mordor — the only place in the world where the One Ring can be destroyed, because it was made there in its fires. The mountain erupts in direct response to Sauron's power and emotions. [book_lotr]
- **Cirith Ungol:** The pass through the Ephel Dúath that Gollum guides Frodo and Sam through. It is guarded by a great fortress and, in the pass itself, by Shelob — a great spider, last child of Ungoliant, who has lived in the pass since before Sauron came to Mordor and who Sauron tolerates because she keeps the pass cleared of wayfarers. [book_lotr]
- **The Morgai and the Plateau of Gorgoroth:** The interior of Mordor that Frodo and Sam traverse — a land of loose grey ash, jagged rock, and choking sulphurous air. The march to Mount Doom is a march through a landscape that is actively hostile to life. [book_lotr]
- **The Black Gate (Morannon):** The main entrance to Mordor from the west — great iron gates set in a wall across the pass between the Ash Mountains. The place where the army of the West makes its final diversion. [book_lotr] [film_return_king]

## The Fall

When the Ring is destroyed in Orodruin's fires, Mordor collapses. The tower of Barad-dûr falls. The passes break. The ground opens. The armies of Mordor, which had fought because they were compelled, scatter or die. Frodo and Sam, in the story's best-known image, sit on a rock amid the ruin of Mount Doom's eruption and wait for the Eagles. [book_lotr] [film_return_king]
