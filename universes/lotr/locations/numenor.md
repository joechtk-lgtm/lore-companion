# Númenor (Elenna, Atalantë)

## Overview

Númenor — the Land of the Star, Elenna, the Westernesse — was the island kingdom given to the Edain (Men who had fought alongside the Elves against Morgoth) as a reward at the end of the First Age. It stood in the sea between Middle-earth and Valinor, raised from the depths by the Valar, a great island in the shape of a five-pointed star. For over three thousand years the Númenóreans were the greatest civilization of Men the world had known — long-lived, skilled in craft and seamanship, rulers of a sea empire that touched every shore of Middle-earth. Then they fell.

Their fall is one of the central events of Tolkien's mythology — a catastrophe that reshaped the very world.

## Key Facts

- **The Gift:** The Númenóreans were given long life — three to five times the span of ordinary men — but not immortality. The Ban of the Valar forbade them from sailing west toward Valinor. Their long lives and proximity to the Undying Lands made the Ban a source of growing resentment. [book_silmarillion]
- **Ar-Pharazôn:** The last King of Númenor. He seized the throne from his cousin Tar-Míriel by force, took her as his wife against her will, and was the most powerful king Númenor had ever had. He was also the one who broke it. Sauron allowed himself to be captured by Ar-Pharazôn's fleet — it was a calculated surrender — and then corrupted the king from within. [book_silmarillion]
- **Sauron's Corruption:** Once brought to Númenor as a prisoner, Sauron became Ar-Pharazôn's chief counselor within a few years. He turned the king against the Valar, encouraged the breaking of the Ban, and used the king's fear of death to justify atrocity — human sacrifice to Morgoth, burning of the White Tree's sapling, persecution of those faithful to the Valar. [book_silmarillion]
- **The Armada:** Ar-Pharazôn built the greatest fleet ever assembled and sailed west toward Valinor to seize immortality by force. It was the ultimate hubris. [book_silmarillion]
- **The Downfall:** When the armada reached Valinor's shores and Ar-Pharazôn stepped ashore, the Valar laid down their guardianship and called on Ilúvatar directly. The world was changed: Valinor was removed from the circles of the world. Númenor was swallowed by the sea. The seas were bent. Only the Faithful — Elendil and his sons, who had kept their ships in the east — survived. [book_silmarillion] [book_lotr]
- **The Faithful and the Exile:** Elendil led the survivors to Middle-earth and founded the realms in exile — Gondor in the south (with Isildur and Anárion) and Arnor in the north. They carried with them the seedling of the White Tree, the palantíri, the knowledge of Númenor. [book_silmarillion] [book_lotr]

## Legacy

Númenor is gone beneath the sea — "Atalantë" in Elvish, the Downfallen. But its legacy shapes the entire Third Age: Gondor and Arnor are Númenórean realms, their kings are Dúnedain (Men of the West), the Rings of Power were made partly because of the Númenóreans' role in the Second Age conflicts. Aragorn's claim to the throne of Gondor is a claim through the line of Isildur, son of Elendil — the last survivor of the last great Númenórean civilization. The story of the Third Age is in part the long reckoning of Númenor's fall. [book_silmarillion] [book_lotr]
