# Rivendell (Imladris)

## Overview

Rivendell — Imladris in Elvish — is the house of Elrond, established in the Second Age during the War of the Elves and Sauron as a refuge and a fortress. It sits in a deep valley of the Misty Mountains, hidden from outside sight, preserved by the power of Elrond's ring Vilya — the Ring of Air, the greatest of the Three. It is often called the Last Homely House East of the Sea: the last safe harbor before the Misty Mountains, the last place of Elvish refuge and welcome before the wilder world.

## Key Facts

- **Elrond:** Lord of Rivendell, son of Eärendil the Mariner and Elwing. He is half-elven — given the choice between mortal and immortal life, he chose immortality. He is approximately 6,500 years old at the time of the War of the Ring. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]
- **Vilya:** The Ring of Air, borne by Elrond. It is the greatest of the Three Elven Rings and its power sustains Rivendell as a place of preservation and peace. When the One Ring is destroyed, Vilya's power fades. [book_lotr]
- **The Last Homely House:** The phrase conveys what Rivendell is — not a fortress, not a city, but a house. A place where travellers are fed and sheltered and healed and can find counsel. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]
- **The Council of Elrond:** The great council held in Rivendell where the fate of the Ring is decided, representatives of the free peoples gathered, and the Fellowship formed. [book_lotr]
- **Narsil's shards:** The broken sword of Elendil was kept in Rivendell. It is reforged as Andúril for Aragorn before the Fellowship departs. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]

## After the War

When the One Ring is destroyed and Vilya's power fades, Rivendell begins to diminish. The Elves of Rivendell depart — Elrond and his household sail West with the other Ring-bearers. The valley does not simply vanish, but the Elvish preservation that made it remarkable is gone. The Third Age ends and with it the age of Elvish lordship in Middle-earth. [book_lotr]
