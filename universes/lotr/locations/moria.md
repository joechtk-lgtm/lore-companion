# Moria (Khazad-dûm)

## Overview

Khazad-dûm — called Moria by the Elves, a name the Dwarves consider a slander — was the greatest Dwarven city ever built, delved beneath the Misty Mountains over thousands of years by the people of Durin the Deathless. At its height it was a marvel: halls and chambers that took generations to carve, lit by lanterns of cunning craft, containing the richest mithril lodes in Middle-earth. The Dwarves of Khazad-dûm prospered for two Ages of the world.

Then they delved too deep.

## Key Facts

- **Scale:** Khazad-dûm extends through the entire range of the Misty Mountains at the pass of Caradhras. It has levels above ground (the peaks) and levels deep below. The Fellowship passes through only a small portion of it. [book_lotr]
- **Mithril:** The silver-steel found in the deepest veins of Khazad-dûm. Lighter than steel, harder than iron, more valuable than gold. The Dwarves' quest for more mithril drove them ever deeper. Bilbo's coat of mithril rings was made from Khazad-dûm mithril. [book_lotr] [book_hobbit]
- **Durin's Bane:** The Balrog — a Maia corrupted by Morgoth, one of the last of Morgoth's demons surviving into the Third Age — had been sleeping in the deepest roots of the mountain since the War of Wrath. The Dwarves broke into its resting place. It woke. It drove the Dwarves from Khazad-dûm in the late Third Age. [book_lotr] [book_silmarillion]
- **The Record:** In the Chamber of Mazarbul, the Fellowship finds the book of Balin's expedition — a failed Dwarven attempt to retake Khazad-dûm. Its final entry: "We cannot get out. The end comes." [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]

## The Fellowship's Passage

The Fellowship chose Moria because the Pass of Caradhras was broken by weather (possibly by Saruman). They enter through the Doors of Durin — the western gate, sealed, opened only by the password Gandalf eventually remembers: "Speak, friend, and enter." The password is simply Elvish for "friend."

They pass through the mines for three days: the long dark. They find the record of Balin's expedition and understand what became of it. They fight in the Chamber of Mazarbul. They run. On the Bridge of Khazad-dûm, Gandalf holds the Balrog. He falls with it. The Fellowship escapes through the eastern gate without him. [book_lotr] [film_fellowship]
