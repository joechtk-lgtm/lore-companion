# Rohan (The Mark)

## Overview

Rohan — the Riddermark, the Mark — is the great grassland kingdom of the Rohirrim in the northwest of the White Mountains' shadow, stretching from Fangorn Forest in the north to the mountains in the south, from the Isen in the west to the Entwash in the east. It is a land of grass and sky and horses. The Rohirrim are riders above all else — a culture defined by the horse, by the warrior tradition, by the mead-hall and the open plain. They are not Dúnedain; their ancestry is Northmen, but their culture has developed its own particular character over the centuries of settlement.

## Key Facts

- **Théoden:** King of Rohan at the time of the War of the Ring — the seventeenth king of the Mark, of the line of Eorl the Young. He is aged and wearied by Gríma's influence when the Fellowship comes to him, but recovers his full self once Gandalf breaks the hold. He leads the Rohirrim to Helm's Deep and to the Pelennor Fields. [book_lotr]
- **Edoras:** The capital of Rohan — a hill on the plain, with the Golden Hall (Meduseld) at its summit. A hall of wood and stone, hung with tapestries, smelling of smoke and horses. The symbolic center of Rohirric culture. [book_lotr] [film_two_towers]
- **Meduseld:** The Golden Hall — the great mead-hall of the Kings of Rohan. Its thatched roof is threaded with gold so it gleams in sunlight. It is where Théoden sits and where decisions about Rohan's fate are made. [book_lotr]
- **Helm's Deep:** The fortress valley — a narrow gorge ending in a great wall (the Deeping Wall) with Hornburg castle at one end. The refuge of Rohan in times of greatest need, named for King Helm Hammerhand who held it through a long winter against siege. The site of the battle in which the Rohirrim, with Gandalf and Erkenbrand, break the armies of Saruman. [book_lotr] [film_two_towers]
- **Eorl the Young:** The first King of Rohan — he led the Northmen south to aid Gondor at the Field of Celebrant and was given the land of Calenardhon (the future Rohan) as a gift. The Rohirrim's bond with Gondor dates from this founding gift. [book_lotr]
- **The Rohirrim:** A people who have no writing tradition of their own (they use runes they learned from the Dwarves) but a strong oral tradition — their songs and histories are preserved in memory and recitation. They name their horses; a Rohirric rider and their horse are partners. [book_lotr]

## The War and After

The Rohirrim ride to Gondor's aid despite Rohan's own crisis with Saruman. Théoden falls on the Pelennor Fields, slain by the Witch-king — and Éowyn slays the Witch-king, fulfilling the ancient prophecy that no man could. Éomer becomes king. Rohan's losses are severe but its people endure. The alliance between Rohan and Gondor, made by Eorl and honored in both directions through the war, is renewed and deepened.
